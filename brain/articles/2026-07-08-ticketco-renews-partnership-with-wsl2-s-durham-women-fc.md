# TicketCo renews partnership with WSL2’s Durham Women FC

## Summary

TicketCo has renewed its long-term partnership with Durham Women FC, continuing as the Women's Super League 2 club's official ticketing and fan-journey provider. The relationship dates back to 2021, and as part of the renewal the club says it will work with TicketCo to further improve the supporter ticketing journey, with 2026-27 WSL 2 season tickets going on sale soon through the platform. The piece is standard trade-press partnership-renewal coverage, framed around client satisfaction and Durham's identity as one of the few standalone (non men's-club-backed) women's football clubs.

### Key Points
- TicketCo extends its partnership with Durham Women FC on a "long-term basis," continuing as official ticketing & fan journey partner since 2021.
- Durham plays home games at Maiden Castle (Durham University grounds).
- 2026-27 WSL 2 season tickets go on sale soon via TicketCo; match tickets to follow once fixtures are announced.
- Club COO Mark Donnelly cites "overwhelmingly positive feedback" from supporters and visiting fans over five years and a stated intent to "drive further innovation" in the ticketing offer.
- TicketCo VP UK & Ireland David Kenny frames Durham as a standalone women's club (no men's-club backing) that the company is proud to support.

### Highlights
- Renewal is explicitly long-term, not a one-off — TicketCo secures continued season-ticket and match-day ticketing revenue for a growing WSL 2 club through at least the 2026-27 season.
- Both club and vendor publicly commit to "further innovation" and improving the "supporter ticketing journey," signaling planned product/feature investment rather than a static renewal.
- Case example of a primary/independent ticketing platform (TicketCo) retaining a multi-year sports-club client relationship (2021–present) in a segment (community/women's sports) that independents like Etix also compete in.

### Industry Problem
Sports clubs — especially standalone/independent ones without a larger parent organization's resources — are evaluating ticketing vendors on relationship quality, supporter experience, and willingness to keep innovating the fan journey (season tickets, match-day access), not just price; this is the client-retention dynamic behind Q12's "what are competitors shipping" question for primary-ticketing peers.

### Proposed Solution
The item itself proposes no specific new feature, only a stated intent to keep evolving the supporter/fan-journey experience together. Etix should monitor TicketCo's activity in the community and women's-sports club vertical as a competitive signal, and benchmark its own season-ticket and fan-journey tooling for similar sports/club clients to ensure feature and retention parity (#build/#improve).

TheTicketingBusiness News

**TicketCo has renewed its partnership with Durham Women, remaining as the Women’s Super League 2 club’s official ticketing and fan journey partner.**

Durham Women and TicketCo have collaborated since 2021, with the aim of simplifying ticketing for supporters.

As part of the new agreement, the club will explore how to further improve the supporter ticketing journey. Durham plays its home games at Maiden Castle, which is located on the grounds of Durham University in the English city.

“We’re delighted to extend our partnership with TicketCo,” said Mark Donnelly, chief operating officer at Durham Women FC.

“David, Clare and the team at TicketCo have been fantastic partners over recent years and we’re thrilled to be further solidifying the relationship by announcing them as our Official Ticketing & Fan Journey Partner on a long-term basis. As we start a new era for the club, it’s crucial that we work with like-minded brands and partners and TicketCo share our desire to deliver a first-in-class fan experience through technology and innovation.

“Throughout the last five years, the platform has consistently delivered an excellent experience for both our supporters and visiting fans, with overwhelmingly positive feedback across the board.

“We’re excited to continue working closely with TicketCo to drive further innovation, enhancing our ticketing offer and ensuring we provide a seamless, modern and user-friendly experience for all supporters.”

Season tickets for the 2026-27 WSL 2 campaign will go on sale soon via TicketCo, with match tickets following once the new fixtures have been announced.

David Kenny, vice president UK & Ireland at TicketCo, said: “It is a privilege to be extending our partnership with Durham Women FC at such an exciting time for them as they move into their next chapter.

“As one of the only standalone women’s clubs in the country, Durham have shown incredible resilience, building something truly special without the backing of a men’s club behind them. That independence takes courage, and it is a huge part of what makes this club so admirable.”
