# Justin Bieber to co-headline first World Cup halftime show

## Summary

Justin Bieber will co-headline the first-ever FIFA World Cup final halftime show alongside Madonna, BTS and Shakira on July 19 at MetLife Stadium, with Burna Boy, conductor Gustavo Dudamel and a Coldplay-featuring school choir also added — duplicate coverage of the same halftime-show announcement reported by TheTicketingBusiness.

### Key Points
- Bieber joins Madonna, BTS, and Shakira as World Cup final halftime co-headliners, July 19, MetLife Stadium.
- Additional performers: Burna Boy, Gustavo Dudamel, PS22 Chorus featuring Coldplay.
- Billed as aiming for the most-watched 11 minutes of broadcast music performance in history.

## Transcript

Burna Boy, Gustavo Dudamel, and PS22 Chorus ft. Coldplay have also been added to perform alongside Madonna, Shakira, and BTS

Justin Bieber will step up to co-headline the first-ever FIFA World Cup Final halftime show, joining the previously announced trio of Madonna, BTS, and Shakira.

Additionally, Burna Boy, conductor Gustavo Dudamel, and PS22 Chorus ft. Coldplay have been added as performers set to take to the stage during this year’s final at New Jersey’s MetLife Stadium (known as New York New Jersey Stadium during the tournament) on Sunday, 19 July.

It is the first performance for Bieber since he headlined the [Coachella Music & Arts Festival](https://www.iqmagazine.com/2026/04/coachellas-25th-anniversary-edition-proves-momentous/) earlier this year, his first major performance since he [cancelled his *Justice* world tour](https://www.iqmagazine.com/2022/09/justin-bieber-justice-world-tour-health/) in 2022.

The quickfire show will be the first time a halftime entertainment spectacle is being staged at football’s biggest matchup, which drew a record 1.5 billion viewers in 2022, with organisers aiming to create “the most-watched 11 minutes of broadcast music performance in history,” they say.

With the cultural milestone, co-curated by Coldplay’s Chris Martin, co-producers Global Citizen is shining a light on its educational and poverty-ending efforts with its dedicated FIFA Global Citizen Education Fund, which has raised over $30 million (€25.6m) on the path to a $100m (€85.3m) goal. On top of donations, $1 from every World Cup ticket will go to the fund.

Global Citizen’s co-founder and CEO, Hugh Evans, previously hailed the halftime slot as a “once-in-a-generation opportunity” in a [conversation with *IQ*](https://www.iqmagazine.com/2026/01/global-citizen-previews-once-in-a-generation-world-cup-halftime-show/).

“We have a chance to engage the whole world in generating real impact through the FIFA Global Citizen Education Fund – all while creating great entertainment,” Evans said.

Last year, Global Citizen also pioneered what a football halftime show could look like at the [Club World Cup Final](https://www.iqmagazine.com/2025/06/fifa-world-club-club-halftime-show-lineup-revealed/), which utilised a stage within MetLife’s stands in lieu of an on-field one to prevent damage to the pitch and account for a quick turnaround time. While production plans for this year’s show are to be revealed, a similar level of innovation is anticipated.

“There were millions of eyes on us ahead of the 2025 Club World Cup final halftime show, and Global Citizen delivered. The 2026 World Cup final halftime will only be bigger and better,” Evans said.
