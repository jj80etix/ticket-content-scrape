# Harry Styles Sets Wembley Stadium Residency Record With 12 Shows

## Summary

Harry Styles earned a Guinness World Record for the largest stadium residency at Wembley after completing his 12th and final show there on July 4, surpassing previous residency records set by Coldplay (10 shows, 2025) and Taylor Swift (8 shows, 2024). The residency supported his fourth studio album, *Kiss All the Time. Disco, Occasionally.*, and was expanded from an initially planned six shows due to demand.

### Key Points
- Styles set a Guinness World Record with 12 Wembley Stadium shows (June 12 - July 4).
- Surpassed Coldplay's 10-show (2025) and Taylor Swift's 8-show (2024) residency records at the venue.
- Residency supported his album *Kiss All the Time. Disco, Occasionally.*
- Originally announced as six shows, expanded to 12 due to strong demand.

Harry Styles has earned a new Guinness World Record after completing a record-breaking residency at London’s Wembley Stadium.

The singer received the honor following his 12th and final Wembley performance on Saturday, July 4. Styles and members of his touring team were presented with the official Guinness World Records certificate after the show, recognizing the achievement.

The 12-show engagement surpassed previous residency records set by Coldplay, which performed 10 shows at the venue in 2025, and Taylor Swift, who played eight nights in 2024.

Styles’ Wembley concerts supported his fourth studio album, *Kiss All the Time. Disco, Occasionally.*, and began on June 12. Additional performances took place on June 13, 17, 19, 20, 23, 26, 27 and 29, followed by shows on July 1, 3 and the record-setting finale on July 4.

“Wembley Stadium is one of the most iconic live music venues in the world, so to achieve the longest residency by a musician there during a single run is an extraordinary achievement,” Guinness World Records adjudicator Will Munford said in a statement.

Munford said the record reflects the scale of Styles’ live performances and the connection he has built with fans. He also noted that Styles and his team presented official Guinness World Records certificates to members of the touring crew, recognizing the work involved in producing the record-setting residency.

The latest honor adds to several Guinness World Records that Styles has earned throughout his career, including records achieved as a member of One Direction.

Styles is scheduled to resume his tour later this month with four performances at Estadio Morumbis in São Paulo, Brazil, beginning July 17.

He will then travel to Mexico City for a six-show run at Estadio GNP Seguros in early August before launching a 30-night residency at Madison Square Garden in New York. The residency is scheduled to run from August 26 through October 31.
