# KJK to take over Hamburg’s largest outdoor venue

## Summary

German promoter Karsten Jahnke Konzertdirektion (KJK) will take over programming of Hamburg's 40,000-capacity Trabrennbahn Bahrenfeld racecourse from 2027, under an exclusive partnership with site operator Mignon GmbH & Co. KG. The venue, currently run by FKP Scorpio as the Hamburger Kultursommer, has previously hosted Foo Fighters, The Rolling Stones and an 80,000-fan Ed Sheeran show. KJK, Germany's largest independent tour and concert organiser, frames the move as part of an effort to strengthen Hamburg's standing as a major music hub.

### Key Points
- KJK takes over active programming of the Trabrennbahn Bahrenfeld racecourse (40,000 cap.) starting 2027, replacing FKP Scorpio's Hamburger Kultursommer branding.
- Deal is an exclusive partnership between KJK and Mignon GmbH & Co. KG, the racecourse's operating company.
- KJK stages up to 1,500 events a year and already runs Stadtpark Open Air, ELBJAZZ, Grand Soul Supreme, Überjazz, Amplifire, Reeperbahn Festival, MS Dockville and Habitat Festival.
- Venue previously hosted Taylor Swift (with AEG Presents) and an expanded Ed Sheeran show that drew 80,000 fans.
- Goal stated by KJK is to expand the event area and establish the venue on major international touring circuits.

## KJK to take over Hamburg’s largest outdoor venue

The promoter will take the reigns of the Trabrennbahn Bahrenfeld racecourse from 2027 in a bid to "strengthen Hamburg as a music hub"

![](https://www.iqmagazine.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/07/csm_35-2022-8-24_DIe_A__rzte__Trabrennbahn__Hamburg_Mood-1250834_-_24._August_2022-_Sebastian_Madej_ad54370577.jpg)

German promoter Karsten Jahnke Konzertdirektion (KJK) is to take over operations of Hamburg’s largest open-air venue, the 40,000-cap Trabrennbahn Bahrenfeld racecourse, effective from 2027.

The site has previously held concerts by artists including Foo Fighters and The Rolling Stones, as well as Ed Sheeran, who [drew 80,000 fans](https://www.iqmagazine.com/2018/07/ed-sheeran-80000-fans-hamburg/) in an expanded configuration in 2018.

The venue is currently under the management of FKP Scorpio as the Hamburger Kultursommer, with the name set to change in 2027 once KJK take control of the site.

“Mignon GmbH & Co. KG, the operating company of the Bahrenfeld horse racing track, has agreed an exclusive partnership with us,” KJK said today (8 July), adding the goal of the partnership is “to organise events at this venue from the start of 2027”.

“We will therefore shortly be taking over the active programming of concert events at this large venue,” the promoter said.

“The shared aim is to expand the event area at the Bahrenfeld horse racing track in order to enhance the venue’s appeal and establish it in the summer tour schedules of major international and national acts. These measures are designed to strengthen and preserve Hamburg as a music hub,” KJK added.

> “We are really looking forward to the task ahead of further developing this wonderful open-air site together with Mignon”

KJK is Germany’s largest independent tour and concert organiser, and stages up to 1,500 events a year. Its work includes organising the *Stadtpark Open Air* concert series in Hamburg’s Stadtpark, and its portfolio of festival work includes ELBJAZZ, Grand Soul Supreme, Überjazz, and [Amplifire](https://www.iqmagazine.com/2025/12/germanys-kjk-unveils-new-rock-festival/) which is set to make its debut in August this year at Hamburg’s MS Artville site.

It is also involved in festivals such as the Reeperbahn Festival, MS Dockville, and Habitat Festival.

KJK marked the 50th anniversary of the *Stadtpark Open Air* series last year, and the previous year the company worked with AEG Presents to promote Swift’s historic two-night visit to the Volksparkstadion.

“We are really looking forward to the task ahead of further developing this wonderful open-air site together with Mignon GmbH & Co. KG and to being able to host international acts there,” said KJK CEO Ben Mitha.
