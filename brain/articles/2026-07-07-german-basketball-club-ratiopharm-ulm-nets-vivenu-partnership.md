# German basketball club Ratiopharm Ulm nets Vivenu partnership

## Summary

German basketball club Ratiopharm Ulm (BBL) switched to Vivenu's ticketing platform ahead of the new season, seeking a faster, more personalized buying experience along with operational flexibility for upsells, bundles, cross-selling, dynamic pricing, and custom inventory — all configurable without waiting on vendor feature releases. The club is also exploring AI to support day-to-day operations, and the deal follows Vivenu's recent launch of "Vivenu Engage," a built-in marketing/CRM layer for segmenting and targeting ticket buyers.

### Key Points
- Ratiopharm Ulm (Basketball Bundesliga) goes live on Vivenu's platform for the 2026-27 season.
- Club cites need for self-service operational flexibility: upsells, bundles, dynamic pricing, custom inventory, without vendor release cycles.
- Club is exploring AI applications for day-to-day operations.
- Follows Vivenu's June launch of "Vivenu Engage," a native marketing/CRM layer eliminating manual data exports for segmentation and campaigns.

**German basketball club Ratiopharm Ulm has made the switch to Vivenu’s platform, going live with a new ticket shop in time for the new season.**

The Ulm-based club, which plays in the Basketball Bundesliga (BBL) – the highest level of professional basketball in Germany – is targeting a quicker, more simple and personalised buying experience for fans.

For the club itself, the new platform also means operational flexibility: upsells, special offers, bundles, cross-selling, dynamic pricing, and custom inventory configurations can all be set up directly in the purchase flow, with no waiting on new features or releases.

“What sets Ratiopharm Ulm apart is their commitment to pushing forward, especially in the digital space. That is something we share at Vivenu. We are very much looking forward to being part of that journey,” said Maxim Kleimann, director, Central and Eastern Europe, at Vivenu.

Having won its first BBL title in 2023, Ratiopharm Ulm has been exploring ways in which AI can support the club’s day-to-day operations.

Andreas Oettel, chief executive of Ratiopharm Ulm, added: “As our sporting and organisational ambitions grow, so do our requirements for digital infrastructure.

“We needed a platform that fits into our existing systems while giving us the freedom to build and develop things on our own terms. With Vivenu, we found exactly that partner.

“Together, we are laying the foundation to give our fans a modern, comfortable ticketing experience for the long term.”

The 2026-27 BBL season will run from September to June 2027, with Ratiopharm Ulm playing its home games at Ratiopharm Arena, a 6,100-capacity venue in Neu-Ulm.

Last month, Vivenu [launched Vivenu Engage](https://www.theticketingbusiness.com/2026/06/vivenu-engage-launched-to-turn-data-into-valuable-customer-relationship-insights/), a marketing and customer relationship layer built directly into the ticketing system.

It was built to eliminate manual exports, provide event organisers with the ability to segment, target and reward buyers with a native campaign builder, all from a single platform.
