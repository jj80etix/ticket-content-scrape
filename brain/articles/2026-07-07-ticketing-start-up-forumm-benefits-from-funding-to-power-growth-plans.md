# Ticketing start-up Forumm benefits from funding to power growth plans

## Summary

UK ticketing and event-technology start-up Forumm has secured a £250,000 (~$335,000) angel investment under the government's Enterprise Investment Scheme to refresh its platform UX, redesign the purchase journey, and expand into new English markets. Founded in October last year by Dan Marrable and Dr Hilary Young, Forumm has already attracted more than 25,000 active users, mostly in Scotland's arts and independent-events sector, positioning itself as a more personal alternative to "impersonal" mainstream ticketing platforms.

### Key Points
- Forumm raised £250,000 (~€293,000/$335,000) from an angel investor network via the UK's EIS.
- Funding targets platform UI/UX refresh and a redesigned purchase journey.
- Company has 25,000+ active users since its October 2025 launch, concentrated in Scotland's arts/culture/independent sector.
- Plans to expand into key English markets over the next 12 months.
- CEO Dan Marrable frames the pitch as a more personal, less "functional" alternative in the ticketing space.

TheTicketingBusiness News

**Ticketing and event technology start-up Forumm has secured funding to enhance its platform, expand its team and further build the business.**

Funding was secured from an angel investor network, under the UK government’s Enterprise Investment Scheme (EIS), with the company to benefit from a £250,000 (€293,000/$335,000) injection.

Forumm was originally launched by co-founders Dan Marrable and Dr Hilary Young in October last year, and has gone on to attract more than 25,000 active users.

Funding will go towards refreshing the platform’s user interface and user experience, with plans for a redesigned purchasing journey to align with the customers and events Forumm serves.

Forumm would also like to expand into key English markets, having already gained traction across Scotland within the arts, culture and independent sector.

“The ticketing space has become overly functional and impersonal, with many organisers and attendees feeling disconnected from the experience. This investment allows us to take a significant step forward in changing that,” said Marrable, chief executive and co-founder of Forumm, as per [*The Scotsman*](https://www.scotsman.com/business/glasgow-ticketing-disruptor-looking-to-prioritise-user-experience-valued-at-ps22m-after-funding-boost-8782201).

“Over the next 12 months our focus will remain on providing exceptional service to our core users and attracting new event organisers as we continue to build a platform where users feel a stronger connection to the events they attend.

“Our growth to date shows there is real demand for a different approach, and this funding gives us the foundations to scale in a sustainable and measured way.”
