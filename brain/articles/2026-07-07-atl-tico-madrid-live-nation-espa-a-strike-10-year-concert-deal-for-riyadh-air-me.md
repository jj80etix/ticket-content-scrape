# Atlético Madrid, Live Nation España Strike 10-Year Concert Deal for Riyadh Air Metropolitano

## Summary

Atlético de Madrid signed a 10-year strategic agreement with Live Nation España to bring more concerts to Riyadh Air Metropolitano (up to 20/year) and the club's surrounding Ciudad del Deporte development, while also enabling direct concert ticket sales via the stadium's own website alongside other official channels. The deal builds on an existing Barsento joint venture between Atlético, Live Nation España, and Oak View Group for a planned 20,000-capacity arena opening in late 2030, further expanding Live Nation's global stadium footprint amid ongoing noise-related disputes at rival venue Santiago Bernabéu.

### Key Points
- 10-year deal covers Atlético's Riyadh Air Metropolitano stadium and adjacent Ciudad del Deporte development.
- Expected to bring up to 20 concerts per year to the 70,000-capacity venue.
- Concert tickets will be sold directly via the stadium's own website under the same conditions as other official channels.
- Builds on the Barsento JV (Atlético, Live Nation España, Oak View Group) for a 20,000-cap arena opening late 2030.
- Comes as rival Real Madrid's Bernabéu Stadium faces ongoing noise-complaint litigation over concerts, highlighting operational friction in converting football stadiums into concert venues.

**The agreement gives Live Nation a long-term foothold at Atlético’s stadium and wider sports-city development, while adding direct concert ticket sales through the venue’s own website**

Atlético de Madrid has signed a 10-year strategic agreement with Live Nation España to bring more concerts and major live events to Riyadh Air Metropolitano, adding another major European football venue to Live Nation’s expanding global stadium footprint.

The deal covers both Atlético’s home stadium and other spaces within the club’s surrounding Ciudad del Deporte development. [Atlético said](https://en.atleticodemadrid.com/noticias/atletico-de-madrid-reaches-agreement-with-live-nation-espana-to-bolster-its-musical-offer-at-the-riyadh-air-metropolitano) the agreement is intended to cement the Metropolitano’s position as one of Europe’s leading concert venues and as a preferred Spanish stop for major international tours.

Atlético said concert tickets for events at Riyadh Air Metropolitano will now be available directly through the stadium’s own website, “under the same conditions” as other official sales channels. That gives the club’s venue platform a more direct role in live-event ticket sales beyond football, while also tying concert discovery and purchasing into the club’s digital ecosystem.

The deal also gives Live Nation España a long-term position inside one of Madrid’s most important large-scale event venues. Riyadh Air Metropolitano, which Atlético says has a capacity of just over 70,000 for football, has already become a regular home for stadium-scale touring. The Live Nation agreement is expected to bring as many as 20 concerts per year to the venue.

The partnership builds on a broader relationship among Atlético, Live Nation España and Oak View Group. The three companies are already working together through Barsento, a joint venture tied to a planned 20,000-capacity covered music arena next to Atlético’s stadium. Atlético said that venue is expected to open in late 2030, with the stadium and future arena forming what the club described as one of Europe’s most complete entertainment ecosystems.

Óscar Mayo, Atlético’s chief revenue and operations officer, framed the deal as part of a larger push to position Madrid as a cultural destination.

“It makes us so proud and excited to be part of a great project alongside the world’s largest promoter, all of which will help the city of Madrid to keep positioning itself as a leading cultural destination, thereby generating a positive impact for everyone who is part of the city and community,” Mayo said in the club’s announcement.

The agreement lands at a notable moment for Madrid’s live-event market. Across town, Real Madrid’s renovated Santiago Bernabéu Stadium has been caught in a long-running dispute over concert noise, neighborhood complaints and the legal responsibility for ensuring events comply with sound limits. Real Madrid announced in May that a Madrid court had ruled the club and its stadium operating entity were not criminally responsible for alleged noise violations tied to concerts, placing responsibility for compliance on the promoter companies leasing the stadium. In June, the club also issued a separate statement saying a procedural ruling in another matter did not declare Bernabéu concerts unlawful.

Even so, the Bernabéu controversy has underscored the difficulty of turning major football stadiums into year-round concert engines, particularly in dense urban neighborhoods where resident opposition can affect scheduling, permitting and public perception.
