# Diljit Dosanjh nears $50m gross on record-breaking tour

## Summary

Diljit Dosanjh's Aura World Tour has grossed $45m and sold over 317,000 tickets, with its 15-date North American leg becoming the highest-grossing North American tour by a South Asian artist in Billboard Boxscore history, per Live Nation. Strong Canadian markets (Vancouver, Toronto) reflected large South Asian communities.

### Key Points
- $45m gross, 317,000+ tickets sold across the tour to date.
- North American leg: 15 shows, $32m gross, 225,000+ fans — a new record, surpassing his own 2024 Dil-Luminati Tour.
- Live Nation credits growing global demand for South Asian artists.
- Strong Canadian performance tied to large South Asian diaspora communities.

**Dijlit Dosanjh has grossed $45m (£33.5m/€39.4m) and sold more than 317,000 tickets on his Aura World Tour.**

The artist recently completed a 15-date run of shows in the United States and Canada, closing as the biggest [North American](https://www.theticketingbusiness.com/2026/06/diljit-dosanjh-breaks-own-records-in-north-america/) leg of a tour by a Punjabi artist in Billboard Boxscore history.

“The Aura World Tour reflects both his global appeal and the growing demand for South Asian artists worldwide,” said Live Nation’s Nidhish Varughese, as reported by [Billboard](https://www.billboard.com/pro/diljit-dosanjh-aura-world-tour-gross/).

“With this North American leg, he broke his own record, drawing more than 225,000 fans and grossing $32 million across 15 shows, surpassing his record-breaking 2024 Dil-Luminati Tour, which previously stood as the highest grossing North American tour by a South Asian artist.”

Dosanjh made the most of strong South Asian communities in Canada with performances in Vancouver and Toronto.

“Canada has one of the largest South Asian populations in the world, so it’s naturally a very strong market for South Asian artists,” said Varughese.

“At the same time, Diljit has built an exceptionally strong fanbase across Canada, which is reflected in the scale of the venues he’s able to play there.”

Before going to North America, Dosanjh began the tour in [Oceania](https://www.theticketingbusiness.com/2025/11/diljit-dosanjh-confronts-racism-and-breaks-records-on-australian-tour-dates/) with five shows in Australia and one in New Zealand.

That run brought in $13m and sold 90,100 tickets, including stadium stops in Melbourne and Sydney.
