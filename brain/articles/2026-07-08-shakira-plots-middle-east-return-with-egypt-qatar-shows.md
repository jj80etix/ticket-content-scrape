# Shakira plots Middle East return with Egypt, Qatar shows

## Summary

Shakira has confirmed rescheduled Middle East dates — Egypt's Pyramids of Giza (Nov. 28) and Doha's 974 Stadium in Qatar (Nov. 18) — plus a UAE festival slot, after the original March/April shows were scrapped due to a regional war. The dates close out her record-breaking "Las Mujeres Ya No Lloran Tour" and are being read as a signal of confidence in resumed international touring in a region where logistics and safety concerns have disrupted several other artists' shows this year.

### Key Points
- Egypt show moved to Nov. 28 at the Pyramids of Giza, presented by promoter Venture Lifestyle; her first performance there since 2007.
- Qatar debut set for Nov. 18 at the 974 Stadium (44,000 capacity), presented by Visit Qatar.
- Jordan date remains postponed indefinitely; Saudi Arabia shows (tied to F1 Grand Prix) will not happen after those races were cancelled.
- Other Middle East dates align with the rescheduled Offlimits Music Festival in Abu Dhabi (postponed from April 4 to Nov. 21).
- Regional conflict has disrupted international touring all year (André Rieu, Christina Aguilera, Josh Groban shows shelved), though local industry leaders say domestic/regional activity continued largely uninterrupted.
- Before the Middle East dates, Shakira plays a 12-night Madrid stadium residency (Sept. 18–Oct. 11) and co-headlines the FIFA World Cup Final halftime show July 19.

## Transcript

In a vote of confidence for touring in the region, the Latin star is currently set to close her mammoth tour with a trio of performances

![Shakira](https://www.iqmagazine.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/Shakira-©-Chris-Cornejo.png)

Shakira © Chris Cornejo

Shakira has penciled her return to the Middle East, confirming rescheduled dates for shows in Egypt and Qatar alongside a festival headlining slot in the UAE, in a move that potentially signals the resumption of international touring in a region in flux.

The Latin megastar was originally due to bring her mammoth *Las Mujeres Ya No Lloran Tour* to a handful of countries – Jordan, Qatar, Saudi Arabia, the UAE, and Egypt – in March and April, though the events were scrapped due to the outbreak of a regional war at the end of February, with the ongoing conflict continuing to disrupt global travel and touring.

Now, she has moved her show at Egypt’s Pyramids of Giza to 28 November, presented by leading Egyptian promoter Venture Lifestyle. It will mark her return to the historic venue, where she last performed in 2007.

“The November performance will continue that tradition and put one of pop’s most recognisable figures amongst some of humanity’s most enduring landmarks,” says the company. Venture Lifestyle have also helped bring acts like [Anyma](https://www.iqmagazine.com/2025/10/ancient-history-how-anyma-lit-up-the-pyramids-of-giza/) to perform at the ancient wonder.

Additionally, in October, the company will partner with EXIT Festival for the multi-day, five-stage [Starlight Festival at the Pyramids of Giza](https://www.iqmagazine.com/2026/06/exit-rolls-out-pyramids-of-giza-festival-as-world-tour-expands/), with Charlotte de Witte, Micheal Bibi, Adriatique, Vintage Culture, and Pete Tong among the first confirmed artists.

Elseswhere, Shakira will go ahead with her Qatar show on 18 November at Doha’s 44,000-capacity 974 Stadium, making her debut in the country. Visit Qatar, the country’s tourism authority, is presenting the show.

The new dates will align with the rescheduled Offlimits Music Festival in Abu Dhabi, which organisers Theory Eleven postponed from 4 April to 21 November ahead of the event.

As it stands, the Aqaba, Jordan show remains ‘ [postponed indefinitely](https://www.voiceofemirates.com/en/lifestyle/2026/03/22/shakiras-concert-in-jordan-postponed-indefinitely/#:~:text=The%20postponement%20decision%20reflects%20the,that%20meets%20the%20audience's%20expectations.) ‘. She will also not perform in Saudi Arabia, as she was due to headline the country’s Formula 1 Grand Prix’s post-race concert series, though both that race and the Bahrain Grand Prix were cancelled entirely in April.

> “For the international artists to bring all the logistics, all of the heavy equipment, that’s a problem for them to reach here”

The rescheduled dates now make up the concluding part of her [record-breaking run](https://www.iqmagazine.com/2026/01/shakira-sets-guinness-world-record-with-mammoth-tour/), with the Egyptian performance currently set to serve as the finale.

Her return looks to be a vote of confidence in the resumption of international touring in the region, which has largely been put on pause as the regional conflict rages on. Though nothing is certain, given yet another breakdown in the ceasefire this week and US President Donald Trump saying it is “over”, according to reports from the [*BBC*](https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/live/c17y1vnn2qxt).

Earlier this year, the region’s touring business was largely on pause at the time that the conflict broke out due to [Ramadan](https://www.iqmagazine.com/2026/02/what-happens-to-the-live-music-business-during-ramadan/), [travel-related disruptions](https://www.iqmagazine.com/2026/03/middle-east-conflict-prompts-travel-related-show-cancellations/) and logistical issues resulted in shows from the likes of André Rieu, Christina Aguilera, and Josh Groban being [shelved](https://www.iqmagazine.com/2026/03/artists-forced-to-shelve-shows-as-middle-east-conflict-persists/).

But regional industry leaders told *IQ* earlier this year that domestic and regional activity had largely gone [uninterrupted](https://www.iqmagazine.com/2026/03/the-region-is-resilient-middle-east-industry-leaders-on-conflict/), with the international business having to pull back as a result of impacted travel routes, commercial shipping, and safety considerations.

“For the international artists to bring all the logistics, all of the heavy equipment, that’s a problem for them to reach here. It’s not that they don’t want to show up and have a concert,” Cosmin Ivan, CEO of Platinumlist Tickets, told *IQ*. “They are not afraid to be here. It’s just about the logistics that need to be delivered.”

Before she reaches Qatar, she will set up shop in Spain for a 12-night residency at the [bespoke Shakira Stadium](https://www.iqmagazine.com/2026/03/shakira-announces-madrid-residency-in-bespoke-stadium/) in Madrid between 18 September and 11 October.

Live Nation said that the event is “conceived as more than a concert” as the residency “will transform the site into an immersive cultural destination – an ephemeral city within Madrid where music, art, and community come together.”

Shakira is currently touring in the US on an intimate arena run, ahead of her co-headlining slot at the inaugural [FIFA World Cup Final halftime show](https://www.iqmagazine.com/2026/05/madonna-bts-shakira-up-for-inaugural-world-cup-halftime-show/) alongside Madonna and BTS on 19 July.
