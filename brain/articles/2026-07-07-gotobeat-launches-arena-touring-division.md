# Gotobeat launches arena touring division

## Summary

Live entertainment company Gotobeat, which pairs promoter services with AI-driven forecasting technology, is launching an arena touring division, opening with two shows at OVO Arena Wembley (Lucki, TayC). The company reported 400% growth in 2025 and says its proprietary forecasting tools have helped artists earn 30% more per tour on average by reducing planning risk and cost.

### Key Points
- Gotobeat launches an arena touring division, starting with Lucki (July 10) and TayC (Nov 15) at OVO Arena Wembley.
- Company reported 400% growth in 2025.
- Uses AI-driven forecasting technology to inform tour-planning decisions.
- Claims average 30% higher artist earnings per tour in 2025 via its forecasting approach.
- Framed as the next stage of the company's expansion and part of making touring more sustainable.

London-based promoter Gotobeat has launched a new arena touring division, which it said marks a “defining moment” in the company’s growth.

The announcement comes after the promoter announced its first two arena shows at OVO Arena Wembley, which will kick off with American rapper Lucki performing at the venue this week (10 July). This will be followed by French-Cameroonian R&B artist TayC playing the arena on 15 November.

Further arena announcements are to follow soon, Gotobeat said.

Gotobeat partner and senior promoter Pietro Bertini said: “The launch of the Arena Tour Division is a defining moment for Gotobeat. Our mission has always been to rethink how live music tours are planned and delivered, making touring more sustainable and connecting more fans with the artists they love.

“Bringing that approach to arena touring is a significant step forward, and we’re excited to help shape the future of live entertainment at an even greater scale.”

Earlier this year, Gotobeat told *IQ* about the success it had found in the integration of its proprietary [live event data modelling](https://www.iqmagazine.com/2026/03/ai-and-data-must-be-the-core-gotobeat-on-2026-expansion/), as the company said it was on track to increase its live shows from 600 last year to 1,000 in 2026.

> “Our mission has always been to rethink how live music tours are planned”

Speaking of the launch of the arena division, the firm said it will “continue to strengthen its proprietary forecasting technology, which helped artists earn an average of 30 per cent more per tour in 2025.”

The promoter added that this technology allows its team of 20 to “identify audience demand in communities overlooked by traditional promoters” and “forecast ticket sales with greater confidence”. It said it can therefore offer artists the ability to plan tours with reduced financial risk and help venues operate more efficiently.

“Building on the company’s strong growth in 2025, the new division represents the next stage in Gotobeat’s expansion while reinforcing its mission to make touring more sustainable,” Gotobeat said.

The company launched in 2022, and said it has seen particular success in the last year in the UK alternative rap scene and has seen increased demand for European tours.

Up to now, Gotobeat has typically focused on approximately 1,000-cap venues, though it has also staged events in larger London venues such as Roundhouse and Brixton Academy.

The promoter said the launch of the division expands its opportunities to work with larger artists, as well as graduating its existing artists to larger venues. The company stressed its “mission remains unchanged”, naming its ambitions to help artists tour more efficiently and sustainably.
