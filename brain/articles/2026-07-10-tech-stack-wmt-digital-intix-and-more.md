# Tech Stack: WMT Digital, Intix and more…

## Summary

TheTicketingBusiness's weekly tech round-up covers four vendor/partnership stories: WMT Digital built new website/apps for UCLA and Northwestern Athletics; Intix was named ticketing/sponsorship/fan-engagement platform for Scottish club Saltire City FC; white-label platform TicketSocket partnered with StubHub/Viagogo via an "Open Distribution" model letting organizers push inventory directly to resale marketplaces; and Fanbase extended its Chester FC partnership through 2030.

### Key Points
- WMT Digital delivered a new athletics website/app for UCLA (also recently Northwestern).
- Intix named official ticketing/sponsorship/fan-engagement platform for grassroots-to-first-team Scottish club Saltire City FC.
- TicketSocket-StubHub "Open Distribution" partnership lets event organizers distribute tickets directly into StubHub and Viagogo marketplaces.
- Fanbase extends its Chester FC (National League North) partnership to 2030.

### Highlights
- TicketSocket's direct-distribution tie-up with StubHub/Viagogo is a notable primary-to-resale inventory integration pattern worth tracking as a competitive feature.
- Intix and Fanbase wins show continued white-label/challenger platform traction in grassroots and semi-pro sports ticketing.

### Industry Problem
Smaller clubs and organizers need ticketing partners that combine ticketing, sponsorship activation and fan engagement in one platform, and want easy access to secondary-market distribution without building it themselves.

### Proposed Solution
Signals two build/improve directions for a primary platform: (1) a bundled ticketing + sponsorship + fan-engagement offering for grassroots/semi-pro clients, and (2) an optional direct-distribution integration into major resale marketplaces as a client-facing feature.
