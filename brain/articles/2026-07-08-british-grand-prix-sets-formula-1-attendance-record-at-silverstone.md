# British Grand Prix Sets Formula 1 Attendance Record at Silverstone

## Summary

The 2026 British Grand Prix at Silverstone drew an estimated 564,000 attendees over its four-day run (concluded July 5), setting a new Formula 1 attendance record and surpassing the prior mark of roughly 520,000 set at the 1995 Australian Grand Prix in Adelaide. On track, Ferrari's Charles Leclerc won a race that finished behind the safety car, drawing boos from parts of the crowd.

### Key Points
- Approximately 564,000 fans attended across the four-day event, about 64,000 more than the 2025 British Grand Prix.
- Surpasses the previous F1 attendance record of ~520,000, set at Adelaide in 1995.
- Silverstone CEO Stuart Pringle ties the milestone to the circuit's history as host of F1's first-ever World Championship race in 1950.
- Race ended under safety car conditions; Charles Leclerc (Ferrari) took the win, with George Russell second and Lewis Hamilton third.
- Championship leader Kimi Antonelli finished 16th after a mechanical failure.

## Transcript

![](https://www.ticketnews.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/07/British-Grand-Prix-Silverstone-.webp)

The British Grand Prix set a new Formula 1 attendance record after approximately 564,000 fans attended the four-day event at Silverstone Circuit, which concluded on July 5.

The total surpassed the previous record of around 520,000 spectators, set at the Australian Grand Prix in Adelaide in 1995. Attendance at this year’s event was also estimated to be 64,000 higher than last year’s British Grand Prix.

Silverstone CEO Stuart Pringle said the milestone was significant given the circuit’s place in Formula 1 history. Silverstone hosted the first race of the Formula 1 World Championship in 1950.
