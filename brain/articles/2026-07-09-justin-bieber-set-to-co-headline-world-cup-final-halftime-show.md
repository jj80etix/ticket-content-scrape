# Justin Bieber set to co-headline World Cup final halftime show

## Summary

Justin Bieber has joined the lineup for the inaugural FIFA World Cup halftime show on July 19 at MetLife Stadium, co-headlining alongside Madonna, Shakira and BTS, with Burna Boy, Gustavo Dudamel and a Staten Island school choir also performing. The 11-minute show, produced by Global Citizen with Live Nation and curated by Coldplay's Chris Martin, aims to raise $100m for global education access.

### Key Points
- Bieber joins Madonna, Shakira and BTS as World Cup final halftime co-headliners, July 19 at MetLife Stadium.
- Show produced by Global Citizen and Live Nation, curated by Coldplay's Chris Martin; supports a $100m education fund.
- Billed as the largest artist gathering for a cause since Live Aid.

TheTicketingBusiness News

**Justin Bieber has joined the line-up of artists set to perform at the inaugural FIFA World Cup halftime show on July 19 at MetLife Stadium in New Jersey.**

The two-time Grammy Award-winner joins Madonna, Shakira, and BTS as a co-headliner for the event which is scheduled to last for 11 minutes.

Burna Boy, Gustavo Dudamel, and PS22 Chorus, a choir of 4th and 5th graders from a school in Staten Island, featuring Coldplay are also due to perform.

The halftime show will support the FIFA Global Citizen Education Fund, an initiative working to raise $100m (£75m/€87.5m) to expand access to education and football for children worldwide.

“The FIFA World Cup brings the world together in a way nothing else can. I’m grateful to be part of this Halftime Show, and even more grateful knowing it’s already helping expand access to education for children around the world,” said Bieber.

The halftime show is being produced by Global Citizen in partnership with Live Nation and Done + Dusted and is curated by Coldplay’s Chris Martin.

“This is the single largest gathering of artists united for a cause since Live Aid, and it could well be the most-watched 11 minutes of broadcast music performance in history,” said Global Citizen chief executive Hugh Evans.

“This is a moment that will bring the world together through music, hope, and action.

“These extraordinary artists, representing every corner of the globe, are coming together to help create opportunities for children everywhere through the FIFA Global Citizen Education Fund.

“Together, we are calling on world leaders, specifically \[French\] President Macron and \[Spanish\] Prime Minister Sánchez, to seize this moment of global unity by contributing to the Education Fund and ensuring this World Cup sets a new benchmark for lasting impact.”
