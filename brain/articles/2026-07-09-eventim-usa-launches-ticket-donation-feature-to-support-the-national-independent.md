# Eventim USA Launches Ticket Donation Feature To Support The National Independent Venue Foundation

## Summary

Eventim USA (CTS Eventim's U.S. subsidiary) launched a click-through donation feature embedded in every digital ticket receipt, letting fans donate to the National Independent Venue Foundation at the point of purchase, extending its existing support for independent venues.

### Highlights
- Eventim USA integrates a dedicated NIVF donation prompt directly into digital ticket confirmation/email receipts, live immediately.
- Supports grassroots venue funding, workforce development, and emergency relief programs.

### Industry Problem
Independent venues face chronic funding gaps for workforce development and emergency needs; ticketing platforms sit at a natural point-of-purchase moment to channel incremental fan support toward the ecosystem that sustains their inventory.

### Proposed Solution
Eventim built a native checkout-receipt donation feature; Etix could evaluate a similar low-friction, opt-in donation/cause-support add-on at checkout or on digital receipts for its own venue partners, particularly independents.

### Key Points
- New feature embeds an NIVF donation link directly into Eventim USA digital ticket receipts, effective immediately.
- Extends Eventim USA's existing independent-venue support programs.

Eventim USA, the U.S. subsidiary of Europe’s top ticketer and promoter CTS Eventim, announced an alliance with the National Independent Venue Foundation (NIVF) that will help preserve indie live spaces.

The global company is integrating a dedicated click-through donation feature into every digital ticket receipt processed through its American platform, making it seamless for fans to donate to NIVF at the point of purchase.

The new feature is effective immediately, and fans purchasing tickets via Eventim USA will see the NIVF donation portal option featured on their email and digital confirmation receipts.

The rollout of the feature, which will provide critical ongoing support for grassroots stages, workforce development and emergency relief programs across the country, marks an extension of Eventim USA’s foundational history with the independent venue community. The ticketer, previously known as See Tickets, was a major supporter during the formation of the foundation’s sister organization, the National Independent Venue Association. Eventim USA made a corporate contribution to the NIVF Emergency Relief Fund two years ago to provide a financial lifeline to independent stages facing catastrophic, unforeseen challenges.

“Eventim USA has been supporting our mission to support independent venues since our inception,” Laura Wilson, board president of the NIVF, said in a statement. “When our industry was shuttered due to the pandemic, they were the first to donate. This allowed NIVF to contribute more than three million dollars in immediate financial support to independent promoters and festivals nationwide. Eventim’s 2025 donation to the NIVF Emergency Relief Fund directly helps keep doors open and spotlights on. By giving every ticket buyer in the U.S. a simple way to pitch in, Eventim USA is helping us turn fan passion into sustainable, long-term funding for the independent stages that define American culture.”

Funds raised through Eventim USA’s receipt click-through initiative directly benefit NIVF’s core programming, including the Emergency Relief Fund, workforce development and education and community and economic initiatives.

“We know how vital NIVF’s mission is. Independent venues are the lifeblood of the music industry – they are where artists get their start and where communities come together,” said Joe Salem, managing director of Eventim USA. “We are proud to promote the cause to millions of passionate fans to ensure independent stages remain vibrant for generations to come.”
