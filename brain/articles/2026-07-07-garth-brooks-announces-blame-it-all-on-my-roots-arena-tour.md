# Garth Brooks Announces ‘Blame It All On My Roots’ Arena Tour

## Summary

Garth Brooks announced the "Blame It All On My Roots" arena tour, reviving his 1990s in-the-round "Drum Pod" stage that produced the best-selling live album *Double Live*. The tour opens with two shows in Indianapolis on August 21-22, with tickets going on sale July 17 through Ticketmaster with no presales and an eight-ticket order limit.

### Key Points
- Tour opens with two nights at Gainbridge Fieldhouse, Indianapolis (Aug 21-22).
- Revives the in-the-round "Drum Pod" staging from Brooks' 1990s arena tours.
- Performances will be recorded for a new live album, "Killer Live."
- Tickets on sale July 17, 10am ET via Ticketmaster; no presales, 8-ticket limit per order.
- Additional tour dates to be announced.

## Transcript

![](https://www.ticketnews.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/07/Garth-Brooks.webp)

Garth Brooks has announced the “Blame It All On My Roots” tour, a run of performances designed to bring his legendary arena shows back to fans in a more intimate setting.

For the upcoming shows, Brooks will bring back his Drum Pod stage, an in-the-round setup that was a major part of his arena performances during the 1990s. The stage design placed Brooks in the center of the venue, allowing fans on all sides of the arena to have a close view of the concert.

The Drum Pod stage was featured on Double Live, which remains the best-selling live album in music history. Brooks will also record performances from the new tour for an upcoming live release titled “Killer Live.”

“Going back into the arenas is about putting the stadium show in a box,” Brooks said in a statement. “Every seat is a great seat. This is personal.”
