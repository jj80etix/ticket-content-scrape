# Manchester City offers £10 tickets for local residents

## Summary

Manchester City will trial ringfencing 100-500 discounted tickets (£10 adults/£7.50 concessions/£5 under-18) per Premier League match for residents of five wards immediately surrounding the Etihad Campus, starting next season, allocated via a residency-verified ballot after each match is confirmed. The program follows consultation with the club's City Matters fan-engagement group.

### Key Points
- 100-500 tickets per match ringfenced for residents of five specific Etihad-area wards.
- Pricing: £10 adult / £7.50 concession / £5 under-18, across the 62,700-capacity Etihad Stadium.
- Applies to all Premier League matches next season, plus some domestic/European cup matches.
- Allocation via random ballot after match date/kick-off confirmation; requires proof of address.
- Developed in consultation with City Matters fan-engagement programme; misuse triggers sanctions under the club's existing policy.

### Highlights
- Concrete pricing tiers (£10/£7.50/£5) and a defined 100-500-ticket-per-match allocation make this a specific, replicable community-access model.
- Ballot + address-verification mechanism is a notable anti-fraud/anti-resale control paired with an affordability initiative.

### Industry Problem
Rising ticket prices and stadium expansion can price out the local community around a venue, creating reputational and community-relations risk even amid strong demand and record revenue.

### Proposed Solution
A geo-targeted, identity-verified discount-ticket allocation (ballot + address proof) is a concrete pattern a ticketing platform could productize as a configurable "community access" tier for clients wanting to reserve inventory for local/underserved patrons while controlling resale/fraud risk.

TheTicketingBusiness News

**Manchester City is set to trial a new initiative which will see the Premier League club ringfence a number of £10 (€12/$13.50) tickets for local residents.**

Those living close to the Etihad Campus will be able to apply for discounted tickets for every Premier League match from next season, with some domestic and European cup matches also included.

Eligible wards, defined by the English city’s council, include Ancoats & Beswick, Clayton & Openshaw, Gorton & Abbey Hey, Ardwick, and Miles Platting & Newton Heath.

The decision has been announced following consultation with fan engagement programme City Matters, with the club set to ringfence between 100 and 500 tickets for each match, available right across the 62,700-capacity Etihad Stadium.

Tickets will be priced at £10 for adults, £7.50 for concessions and £5 for under-18s.

Following confirmation of the match date and kick-off time, a select number of tickets will be released and randomly allocated via a ballot process. To be eligible, residents will need to provide evidence of their postal address during the registration process.

**Rooted in community**

“Manchester City has always been rooted in its community, and as we grow, we want to ensure people who live closest to the Etihad Campus remain part of everything that happens here,” said Danny Wilson, the club’s managing director of operations.

“We know matchdays bring significant social and economic opportunities to the area, but with more matches, more activities and more visitors than ever before, it’s important that those who live closest to us can continue to enjoy the experience, make lasting memories, and be a part of the journey with us.”

Any misuse of the scheme will result in action in line with the club’s sanctions and pans policy.

Lee Broadstock, chair of City Matters, added: “We’re really pleased to see the Club take proactive steps to help local people watch Manchester City, with thousands of Premier League tickets available for just £10.”
