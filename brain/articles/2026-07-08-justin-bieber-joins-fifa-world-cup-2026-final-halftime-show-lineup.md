# Justin Bieber Joins FIFA World Cup 2026 Final Halftime Show Lineup

## Summary

Justin Bieber has joined the lineup for the FIFA World Cup 2026 Final Halftime Show, co-headlining alongside Madonna, Shakira, and BTS. The 11-minute performance, produced with Global Citizen, Live Nation, and Done + Dusted, takes place July 19 at New York New Jersey Stadium and also features Burna Boy, conductor Gustavo Dudamel, Coldplay with a Staten Island school choir, and appearances from Sesame Street and the Muppets. The show supports the FIFA Global Citizen Education Fund, which has raised over $50 million toward a $100 million goal, partly funded by $1 from every World Cup ticket sold.

### Key Points
- Justin Bieber joins Madonna, Shakira, and BTS as co-headliner of the first-ever World Cup Final Halftime Show.
- Show is July 19 at New York New Jersey Stadium, produced by FIFA, Global Citizen, Live Nation, and Done + Dusted.
- Additional performers: Burna Boy, Gustavo Dudamel, Coldplay (with PS22 Chorus), Sesame Street and the Muppets.
- Event benefits the FIFA Global Citizen Education Fund, targeting $100 million, already past $50 million raised.
- $1 from every 2026 World Cup ticket sold is donated to the education fund.
- Global Citizen calls it the largest artist collaboration for a cause since Live Aid.

## Transcript

![](https://www.ticketnews.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/07/FIFA-World-Cup-Halftime-Show.jpg)

Justin Bieber has been added to the lineup for the FIFA World Cup 2026 Final Halftime Show, FIFA and Global Citizen announced. The singer will join Madonna, Shakira and BTS as a co-headliner for the first-ever halftime performance during the tournament’s final match.

The show is scheduled to take place on July 19 at New York New Jersey Stadium during the FIFA World Cup 2026 Final. The 11-minute performance is being produced as part of a partnership between FIFA, Global Citizen, Live Nation and Done + Dusted.

Bieber said he was honored to be included in the event and highlighted the show’s connection to global education efforts.

“The FIFA World Cup brings the world together in a way nothing else can,” Bieber said in a statement. “I’m grateful to be part of this Halftime Show, and even more grateful knowing it’s already helping expand access to education for children around the world.”

The halftime show will support the FIFA Global Citizen Education Fund, which aims to raise $100 million to expand access to quality education and football programs for children worldwide. The initiative has already raised more than $50 million, according to Global Citizen.

FIFA is also contributing to the effort through ticket sales from the tournament, with $1 from every ticket sold for the 2026 World Cup being donated to the fund throughout the competition.

FIFA President Gianni Infantino said the event will combine music and football while promoting educational opportunities for children.

“FIFA and Global Citizen share the same belief that education is the foundation for opportunity,” Infantino said. “This groundbreaking spectacle will celebrate football, music and our shared values, ensuring a legacy that transcends the final whistle.”

The event will also feature appearances from Sesame Street and the Muppets as part of its focus on children and education.

Global Citizen Co-Founder and CEO Hugh Evans called the performance one of the largest artist collaborations for a charitable cause since Live Aid.

“This is the single largest gathering of artists united for a cause since Live Aid, and it could well be the most-watched 11 minutes of broadcast music performance in history,” Evans shared. “This is a moment that will bring the world together through music, hope, and action.”
