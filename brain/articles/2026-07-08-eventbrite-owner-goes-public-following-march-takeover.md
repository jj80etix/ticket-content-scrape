# Eventbrite owner goes public following March takeover

## Summary

Bending Spoons, the Milan-based tech firm that acquired Eventbrite in a $500m deal completed in March 2026, has now listed publicly on Nasdaq via a $1.7bn IPO. The IPO filings confirm continued headcount cuts across Eventbrite, AOL, and Vimeo (down to "only a few hundred" staff by end of 2026 from over 1,800 acquired) alongside a product push to overhaul discoverability, organizer storefronts, and checkout on Eventbrite. Eventbrite, founded in 2006 and operating in nearly 180 countries, distributed over 83 million paid tickets to 4.7 million events last year, but its non-subscription revenue (driven primarily by Eventbrite) is still only 4% of Bending Spoons' total Q1 2026 revenue.

### Key Points
- Bending Spoons completed its Eventbrite acquisition in March 2026 and followed with layoffs in April; the Nasdaq IPO filing signals further headcount reduction to "a few hundred" staff across Eventbrite, AOL, and Vimeo combined by end of 2026.
- Product roadmap centers on five "core pillars": reliability, creator tools, event reach/discovery, and checkout.
- New/recent features cited: QR-code event-flyer generation, Apple/Google Wallet ticket compatibility, "Top Organiser" accreditation badges, richer organizer storefront profiles, automatic post-event follow-up emails, and embedded ads/marketing tools to convert attention into sales.
- Eventbrite distributed 83M+ paid tickets across 4.7M events last year, spans ~180 countries, and focuses within live music on mid-sized theatre/grassroots venues.
- Eventbrite-driven non-subscription revenue doubled YoY in Q1 2026 but is still only 4% of Bending Spoons' total quarterly revenue.

### Highlights
- $1.7bn Nasdaq IPO for Bending Spoons, following its $500m Eventbrite acquisition (closed March 2026).
- Planned reduction to "only a few hundred" staff across Eventbrite, AOL, and Vimeo by end of 2026, from over 1,800 employees acquired across the three companies.
- New feature set: QR-code flyer generation, Apple/Google Wallet compatibility, organizer storefront rebuild, "Top Organiser" badges, embedded ads/marketing tools, automatic post-event follow-up emails.
- Eventbrite scale: 83M+ paid tickets, 4.7M events, ~180 countries in its most recent full year.

### Industry Problem
Eventbrite, a Tier 1 direct competitor in the primary ticketing space, is publicly signaling that event discoverability, organizer storefronts/branding, and checkout are its biggest unsolved product gaps — the same pain points independent organizers and venues cite when picking a ticketing platform. Its aggressive headcount cuts alongside continued product investment also reflect a broader consolidation dynamic (M&A roll-up of AOL/Vimeo/Eventbrite under one owner) reshaping the competitive field.

### Proposed Solution
Eventbrite's own answer is a discoverability-first platform overhaul: dynamic organizer storefronts, richer media/branding, trust signals like "Top Organiser" badges, and marketing/re-engagement tools (embedded ads, automatic follow-up emails) to convert event traffic into repeat sales. Etix should monitor whether this discoverability/storefront push raises client expectations for organizer branding and post-event marketing tools, and evaluate building comparable organizer-facing discovery and re-engagement features to stay competitive with Tier 1 platforms; the Bending Spoons roll-up/cost-cutting strategy is also worth watching as a signal of consolidation pressure among mid-market ticketing players.

Eventbrite owner Bending Spoons has listed publicly on Nasdaq following its recent purchase of the ticketing platform, as the Milan-based tech firm pledged continued layoffs as well as an effort to overhaul live event discoverability on the platform.

Bending Spoons completed the [acquisition of Eventbrite](https://www.iqmagazine.com/2025/12/eventbrite-to-be-acquired-in-500m-deal/) in March this year. The purchase was followed by significant [layoffs](https://www.iqmagazine.com/2026/04/eventbrite-makes-layoffs-following-bending-spoons-acquisition/) in April, with newly appointed general manager Andrea Parodi saying at the time that a review of the business had “made it clear that a smaller, faster-moving team would serve Eventbrite better.”

The headcount reduction centred on the company’s development of five “core pillars” of improvements to the Eventbrite’s reliability, creator tools, event reach and discovery, and checkout, bosses said.

In the preceding months, Eventbrite had introduced a raft of new features, including QR-code event flyer generation, ticket compatibility with Apple and Google Wallet, and new accreditations for top event organisers.

Founded in 2006, Eventbrite scaled to nearly 180 countries prior to its acquisition by Bending Spoons. Last year, the platform distributed over 83 million paid tickets to 4.7 million events. Within live music, the platform largely focusses on mid-sized theatre and grassroots venue shows.

> “Discoverability is a core focus for us right now”

The strategy employed at Eventbrite mirrors Bending Spoons’ wider approach across a rage of acquisitions of tech-related companies, chief among which are AOL, Vimeo, and WeTransfer. The company noted in its IPO documents that as part of its takeover of Eventbrite, by the end of 2026 it expects “only a few hundred \[staff\] to remain” across all of Eventbrite, AOL, and Vimeo, having taken on over 1,800 people with the purchase of the three companies.

“As we transform AOL, Eventbrite, and Vimeo, and continue to optimise our other businesses, 2026 is shaping up to be strong,” Bending Spoons said in the IPO documents.

In the documents, Bending Spoons also said it considers Eventbrite one of its “main businesses”, but noted it is the only company out of those to significantly contribute to its revenue not to rely on a subscription-based model. Bending Spoons’ non-subscription revenue in Q1 of 2026 doubled year-on-year, driven “primarily” by Eventbrite, but still made up only 4% of the company’s total revenue for the quarter.

Parodi said earlier this month that changes made to the Eventbrite platform since the takeover include efforts to rebuild event organisers’ profiles into “a full storefront fans can follow”, including the addition of automatic post-event follow-up emails to fans for increased sale opportunities, embedded adverts and marketing tools to “convert attention into ticket sales”.

“Discoverability is a core focus for us right now. We’ve rebuilt organiser profiles into more dynamic storefront, with richer media, stronger branding,and clearer ways for fans to move from discovery to purchase. We’ve also improved event listings with higher quality imagery and trusts signals like ‘Top Organiser’ badges to help the best events stand out,” Parodi told *Hypebot.*
