# Mick Jagger Claims The Rolling Stones Plan to Embark on Traditional, Multi-City Tour in 2027

## Summary

Mick Jagger confirmed in a radio interview that The Rolling Stones are planning to tour behind their upcoming album *Foreign Tongues* in 2027, in a traditional multi-city format rather than a residency. Jagger explicitly rejected the residency model that acts like Harry Styles have used, citing cost concerns for fans and his own preference to "move around."

### Key Points
- Jagger confirmed 2027 touring plans in support of new album *Foreign Tongues*.
- No tour expected this year (2026).
- Jagger said he is "ambivalent" about residency-style touring, calling it more expensive for fans.
- He expressed interest in playing Argentina.

## Transcript

![](https://www.ticketnews.com/wp-content/uploads/Untitled-design-23.png)

Mick Jagger has confirmed that The Rolling Stones are planning a 2027 tour — though the run will be traditional, rather than a residency-style run like Harry Styles’ “ [Together Together Tour](https://www.ticketnews.com/2026/05/harry-styles-adds-fourth-melbourne-show-to-together-together-tour/).”

Jagger spoke with the Argentinian radio station La Nacion about their upcoming album, *Foreign Tongues*. During the interview, he noted that “I’d love to tour this album.”

“Can’t wait to sing,” Jagger said. “There’re so many songs on the album that we could do on stage. So, I don’t think we’ll be touring this year, but I hope to do it as soon as possible…And I’d love to come to Argentina, too, obviously!”

When asked about doing a residency-style tour, Jagger said that he is opposed to the idea.

If The Rolling Stones do end up touring next year, it would mark their first trek since the “Hackney Diamonds ’24 Tour,” which supported their 2023 record of the same name.

The Rolling Stones are set to release Foreign Tongues, their 25th studio record, on July 10. The record is set to include features from music icons like Paul McCartney, Robert Smith of The Cure, Red Hot Chili Peppers’ Chad Smith, and Steve Winwood.
