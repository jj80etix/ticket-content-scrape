# Manchester City hit record WSL season ticket sales

## Summary

Manchester City sold a record number of Women's Super League season tickets within two weeks of going on sale, ahead of the WSL's expansion to 14 teams for 2026/27. Adult prices range £95-£125, with expanded discount eligibility (under-18 threshold raised from 16, plus student/over-65/disabled discounts).

### Key Points
- Record WSL season-ticket sales, achieved within two weeks of on-sale.
- 13 guaranteed home league fixtures; sales deadline July 13.
- Adult season prices: £95-£125; under-18: £45 (age threshold raised from 16 to 18).
- Discounts also available for students (18-21), over-65s, and disabled supporters.
- Most games at the 4,998-seat Academy Stadium, with select fixtures at the Etihad.

### Highlights
- Season tickets sold at a record pace within a two-week window — a strong loyalty/demand signal for women's football ticketing.
- Expanded discount tiers (age, student, senior, disability) broaden the addressable season-ticket base ahead of league expansion.

### Industry Problem
This is a positive case study rather than a pain point, but it evidences the broader industry shift toward subscription/membership-style ticketing as a demand-capture and loyalty tool, particularly in growing women's sport.

### Proposed Solution
Illustrates a season-ticket/membership model with tiered, inclusive pricing as a loyalty-building tactic; a relevant reference case for evolving Etix's own subscription/membership ticketing capabilities for club and season-based clients.

TheTicketingBusiness News

**Manchester City has sold the most Women’s Super League (WSL) season tickets in its history, two weeks after putting them on sale.**

Prices range from £95 ($127.50/€110) to £125 for adults while under-18 tickets are set at £45 across all seating categories.

Tickets are still available, with the deadline set for July 13, and will guarantee entry to 13 home league fixtures following the WSL’s expansion to 14 teams for the 2026/27 season.

“An expanded age bracket now means supporters under the age of 18, rather than 16, can benefit from additional seasons at a reduced rate, with discounted offerings also still available for students (18-21), over-65s and disabled supporters,” read a club statement.

The Blues won the WSL and FA Cup double last season for the first time in their history, clinching the league with a game to spare and equalling the team’s best-ever points tally.

Season ticket prices have increased from last year due to the costs incurred from an extra matchday, but the per-match price remains the same.

The team plays the majority of home games at the 7,000-capacity Academy Stadium, which has seating for 4,998.

The Etihad Stadium also hosts select matches, with the last campaign seeing it stage fixtures against Manchester United and Chelsea.
