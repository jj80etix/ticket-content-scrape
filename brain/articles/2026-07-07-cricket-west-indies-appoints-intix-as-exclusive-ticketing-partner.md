# Cricket West Indies appoints Intix as exclusive ticketing partner

## Summary

Cricket West Indies has named Intix its exclusive ticketing partner in a long-term deal central to the organization's digital transformation strategy, adopting a white-label platform to modernize the fan-purchasing experience across its venues. The partnership also gives CWI fan-data capabilities for behavior analysis, audience segmentation, and personalized marketing/notifications.

### Key Points
- Intix becomes Cricket West Indies' exclusive ticketing partner in a long-term agreement.
- Deal centers on a fully integrated, modern white-label ticketing platform.
- Enables fan-behavior data collection, audience segmentation, and personalized marketing across CWI's venues.
- Framed by both parties as core to CWI's fan-experience and revenue-growth strategy.

**Cricket West Indies has appointed Intix as its exclusive ticketing partner in a long-term agreement.**

The partnership is said to be a critical piece of the organisation’s digital transformation strategy as it introduces a fully integrated, modern white-label technology solution designed to elevate the fan experience.

“A robust ticketing system is critical for us,” said Cricket West Indies chief executive Chris Dehring.

“Intix provides a best-in-class platform geared towards strengthening our brand, driving commercial returns, and supporting the fan-first innovation necessary for our sustainable success.

“It is integral that Windies Cricket fans have a smooth ticket purchasing experience that aligns with how our communities buy tickets and like to experience our events. Our partnership with Intix is designed to help us strengthen the relationship between our team and supporters.”

A key aspect of the partnership is giving CWI the ability to collect and utilise fan data.

Through the Intix platform, CWI aims to interpret fan behaviour, from ticket purchasing patterns to stadium usage across each of the venues that the West Indies plays at.

This will allow for live audience segmentation and tailored marketing strategies, delivering personalised notifications to fans before and during the match.

“Caribbean cricket fans are as passionate as any sports fans in the world, and they deserve a ticketing experience that matches their passion,” said Judd Goldstein, CEO at Intix.

“Our goal is to ensure these deeply engaged fans have a smooth, flexible and personalised experience when watching their heroes compete on the international stage.

“Cricket West Indies are on an incredibly exciting digital transformation journey, and our focus is ensuring Intix is a seamlessly integrated piece of the puzzle to unlock audience and revenue growth moving forward.”
