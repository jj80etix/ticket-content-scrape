# Harlequins to launch digital ticketing for all home games

## Summary

Premiership Rugby club Harlequins will move to fully digital ticketing for all men's and women's home fixtures at the 14,800-capacity Twickenham Stoop starting the 2026/27 season, retiring print-at-home tickets entirely. The switch is billed as a response to supporter feedback, aiming to speed up turnstile entry and simplify ticket transfer through the club's official app. The app is positioned as a broader "matchday companion" beyond ticketing, with more features planned.

### Key Points
- Fully digital ticketing rolls out for all home fixtures at The Stoop (14,800 capacity) from the 2026/27 season.
- Driven by supporter feedback; primary goals are faster turnstile scanning and easier ticket transfer.
- Print-at-home tickets are being retired outright, not just deprioritized.
- Everything is managed through the Harlequins Official App, which also delivers app-exclusive content and real-time match updates.
- Club frames digital ticketing as "just the beginning" of an evolving, more personalized in-app matchday experience.

### Highlights
- 14,800-capacity venue going 100% digital for all home games starting 2026/27, with print-at-home eliminated entirely.
- Stated objectives are explicitly speed-of-entry (turnstile scanning) and ease of peer-to-peer ticket transfer.
- Rollout is club-wide (men's and women's fixtures), not a pilot.

### Industry Problem
Clients (venues/clubs) and their patrons are pushing baseline expectations toward digital-only entry, wanting faster gate throughput and frictionless ticket transfer, and are willing to eliminate legacy formats like print-at-home entirely to get there.

### Proposed Solution
Harlequins' own answer is a proprietary all-in-one club app bundling ticketing, transfer, and content. For Etix, this reinforces the case to keep improving mobile-wallet/QR entry speed and native in-app ticket transfer, so client venues can confidently retire print-at-home without hurting throughput or fan experience.
