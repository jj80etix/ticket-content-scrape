# Motorsport Tickets ceases trading

## Summary

UK-based Motorsport Tickets, an authorized reseller for Formula 1, MotoGP, the World Endurance Championship, Le Mans and the Isle of Man TT, has ceased trading and instructed Crowe U.K. LLP to prepare the company for liquidation. The firm, part of American parent Motorsport Network, told customers with existing bookings to pursue refunds or chargebacks through their card issuer rather than the company directly. It operated for more than 20 years, growing through acquisitions (BookF1.com, SportStadion, travel supplier Travel Destinations) and a rebrand from Motorsport Live to Motorsport Tickets.

### Key Points
- Motorsport Tickets has ceased trading and is being prepared for insolvency/liquidation via Crowe U.K. LLP.
- The company states it "is unable to meet any future bookings"; customers are directed to seek refunds/chargebacks from their card issuer rather than the company.
- Operated 20+ years as an authorized seller for F1, MotoGP, WEC, 24 Hours of Le Mans, and the Isle of Man TT.
- Owned by US-based Motorsport Network, which entered ticketing via the BookF1.com acquisition.
- Brand history: BookF1.com to Motorsport Live (2019) to Motorsport Tickets; acquired travel/accommodation supplier Travel Destinations in 2020.

### Highlights
- A 20+ year, acquisition-built motorsport ticketing and travel business has failed outright — insolvency practitioners engaged, not a restructuring, sale, or wind-down with a successor.
- Customers with existing bookings are pushed to card-issuer chargebacks rather than company-issued refunds, indicating no residual funds set aside for direct redress.
- Parent company Motorsport Network appears to be exiting the ticketing business entirely, with no stated buyer or successor for the unit.

### Industry Problem
This is a clean market-exit signal in niche motorsport ticketing resale: even an established, decades-old, acquisition-built authorized reseller can collapse outright, leaving patrons to chase card-issuer chargebacks for redress — the kind of unmanaged fulfillment-guarantee failure that erodes trust in resale/authorized-ticketing channels (Q3 peer distress) and represents a market exit worth tracking (Q13).

### Proposed Solution
The item describes no rescue plan or successor — it's a shutdown, not a sale. For Etix this is monitor-only: watch whether Motorsport Network exits ticketing more broadly, and whether any of Motorsport Tickets' event-rights relationships (F1, MotoGP, Le Mans, Isle of Man TT) come up for grabs for other ticketing partners.

**Motorsport Tickets has instructed insolvency practitioners to prepare the company for liquidation following a decision to cease trading.**

The London-based company stated that customers who have already purchased tickets should contact the issuer of their debit or credit card to check whether they may be eligible for a refund or chargeback.

“We must advise you that the Company is unable to meet any future bookings,” a Motorsport Tickets statement read.

“The Board has instructed Insolvency Practitioners at Crowe U.K. LLP to prepare the Company for liquidation that follows a decision to immediately cease trading the business of the Company.”

Motorsport Tickets operated for more than 20 years and was an authorised ticket seller for the likes of Formula 1, MotoGP, the World Endurance Championship, 24 Hours of Le Mans, the Isle of Man TT and more.

It was part of the American media and technology company Motorsport Network before winding up.

Motorsport Network entered the ticketing market with the acquisition of [BookF1.com](http://bookf1.com/), before further acquiring the Dutch business SportStadion.

BookF1 became Motorsport Live in 2019, before a further rebranding to Motorsport Tickets. In 2020, it was announced that Motorsport Tickets had acquired the accommodation supplier Travel Destinations.
