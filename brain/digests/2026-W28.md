## Regulatory/Legal Watch

- **North Carolina SB 257** (Q9, Q11) — resale/anti-bot law folded into the 2026 budget bill, bypassing standard committee review. Effective **Oct 1, 2026**: mandates all-in pricing, resale-marketplace disclosures, and anti-bot provisions, backed by penalties up to $15,000/day. Live Nation publicly backed it; critics say it entrenches Ticketmaster by omitting transferability/anti-retaliation protections. → [[articles/2026-07-07-north-carolina-pushes-ticket-resale-regulations-through-in-budget-bill-sprint|North Carolina Pushes Ticket Resale Regulations Through in Budget Bill Sprint]]
- **Live Nation/Ticketmaster antitrust — post-trial motions** (Q10) — final reply briefs filed **July 2** seeking to overturn or retry the April 15 monopoly jury verdict; hearing expected **later this month (July 2026)**. DOJ's separate no-divestiture settlement was published in the Federal Register **July 6**, opening the Tunney Act comment period. → [[articles/2026-07-08-live-nation-asks-judge-to-overturn-monopoly-verdict-or-grant-new-trial|Live Nation asks judge to overturn monopoly verdict, or grant new trial]]
- **Live Nation/Ticketmaster — arbitration ruling** (Q10) — SDNY (Judge Subramanian, **June 29** opinion) compelled arbitration in two consumer antitrust suits (Jacobson, Leifer) under revised New Era ADR rules; narrow ruling, does not affect the Popp class action or the government antitrust case. → [[articles/2026-07-09-live-nation-wins-new-era-arbitration-ruling-but-ticketmaster-s-fine-print-fights|Live Nation Wins New Era Arbitration Ruling, But Ticketmaster's Fine Print Fights Continue]]
- **Illinois ticketing reform** (Q11) — headline-only signal from a paywalled Pollstar roundup; specifics unconfirmed, flagged for manual follow-up. → [[articles/2026-07-10-the-biz-illinois-ticketing-reform-eu-proposal-could-cost-us-artists-emmy-noms-fo|The Biz: Illinois' Ticketing Reform; EU Proposal Could Cost US Artists...]]
