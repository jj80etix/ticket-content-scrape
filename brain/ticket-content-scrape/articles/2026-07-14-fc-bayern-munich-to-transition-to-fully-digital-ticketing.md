# FC Bayern Munich to transition to fully digital ticketing

## Summary

FC Bayern Munich will move to fully digital-only ticketing for the 2026/27 Bundesliga season, dropping physical tickets in favor of exclusive distribution through the club's app and the Allianz Arena app. The club frames the move as an anti-fraud measure aimed at curtailing the black market and making forgery "practically impossible," while also easing peer-to-peer ticket transfers for members and fan-club travelers. The change follows Bayern's earlier decision to drop Apple Wallet and Google Wallet support.

### Key Points
- Fully digital ticketing begins with the 2026/27 season; tickets available only via FC Bayern app or Allianz Arena app.
- Stated goals: curtail the black market, make ticket forgery "practically impossible," and improve convenience/sustainability.
- Custom technical solutions were built to let fan-club members transfer digital tickets among group travelers (e.g., fan buses).
- Follows the club's earlier move away from Apple Wallet/Google Wallet support.

### Highlights
- Bayern is going fully digital-only (app-exclusive) starting 2026/27, eliminating physical/printed tickets entirely.
- Stated rationale is explicitly anti-fraud: preventing forgery and curbing black-market resale, not just convenience.
- Club built custom in-app transfer tooling for fan-club groups rather than relying on generic wallet passes.
- Move follows an earlier decision to drop Apple Wallet/Google Wallet support, signaling a shift toward proprietary app-based entry.

### Industry Problem
Physical and loosely-controlled digital tickets remain a vector for forgery and unauthorized resale, and clients increasingly want digital-only entry paired with controlled, trackable transfer mechanisms rather than open wallet-based passes.

### Proposed Solution
Bayern's answer is a proprietary app-exclusive ticket with built-in transfer controls; Etix could evaluate offering venues an optional digital-only entry mode with native (non-wallet) transfer/gifting flows and audit trails as a fraud-reduction and premium-client feature.

**FC Bayern Munich will transition to a fully digital ticketing system for the 2026/27 season with the aim of preventing ticket fraud.**

The German Bundesliga club claimed that the move will make visits to the Allianz Arena easier and more convenient while “ensuring that the black market is significantly curtailed and ticket forgery is practically impossible”.

“With the introduction of digital ticketing, visiting the Allianz Arena will become safer, more transparent and more sustainable,” said Bayern chief executive Jan-Christian Dreesen.

“With this transition, FC Bayern – as a pioneer in club football – will also take a major step towards preventing ticket fraud and actively combating the black market.”

Next season, tickets for FC Bayern will be available exclusively in digital form via the FC Bayern app or the Allianz Arena app.

This follows the club’s decision earlier this year to [drop support for Apple Wallet](https://www.theticketingbusiness.com/2026/05/bayern-munich-to-drop-apple-google-wallet-support/) and Google Wallet.

It will also facilitate the transferring of tickets between fans.

“The Allianz Arena will remain accessible to all Bayern fans in the future,” said Dreesen.

“We’re involving our members and fan clubs in the introduction of digital tickets and providing support wherever necessary.

“Technical solutions were specifically developed for our fan clubs to facilitate the digital transfer of tickets to the numerous fan club members who travel to our home games on fan buses.”
