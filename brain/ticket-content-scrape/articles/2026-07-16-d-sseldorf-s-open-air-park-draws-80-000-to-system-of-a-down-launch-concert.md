# Düsseldorf’s Open Air Park draws 80,000 to System of a Down launch concert

## Summary

Düsseldorf's new Open Air Park venue finally opened on July 10 with a System of a Down concert drawing around 80,000 people, after two earlier planned launch shows were cancelled or relocated. Operator D.Live called the turnout a milestone for live events in North Rhine-Westphalia, and the site will next host OneRepublic and Pitbull on consecutive nights.

### Key Points
- Opening concert: System of a Down, July 10, drew approximately 80,000 attendees.
- Prior launch attempts failed: an Ed Sheeran show in 2018 was cancelled over tree-felling/wildlife objections; an AC/DC show last summer was moved indoors to the 45,000-capacity Merkur Spiel-Arena amid lower-than-expected ticket sales.
- Operator D.Live (CEO Michael Brill) calls the opening a "milestone" for Düsseldorf and North Rhine-Westphalia.
- Next shows at the venue: OneRepublic and Pitbull on consecutive nights, July 18-19.
- D.Live used its own "Event Mobility" platform to manage crowd flow and protect local residents during the concert.
- Partner Live Nation Germany was involved in the venue project and praised the opening.

## Düsseldorf’s Open Air Park draws 80,000 to System of a Down launch concert

The open-air site held its first concert after previous launches fell through, with operators D.Live saying it marks a "milestone" for the region

![](https://www.iqmagazine.com/wp-content/smush-webp/2026/07/open-air-park-system-of-a-down-scaled.jpg.webp)

© D.Live

Düsseldorf’s Open Air Park marked its opening with a concert by System of a Down that drew around 80,000 people to the venue last weekend (10 July).

Operators D.Live said the new site serves as “a strong sign for the future of open-air large events in the state capital” of the state of North Rhine-Westphalia (NRW).

The opening comes at the third time of asking, after operators previously attempted to launch the greenfield site with two concert events that were ultimately called off or relocated.

A scheduled outdoor show by Ed Sheeran in the same location in 2018 was cancelled after objections in relation to the felling of trees and worries over local wildlife, while an AC/DC concert planned for last summer was [switched to the nearby Merkur Spiel-Arena](https://www.iqmagazine.com/2025/05/dusseldorf-open-air-delays-force-ac-dc-indoors/?fbclid=IwY2xjawMvi3NleHRuA2FlbQIxMQBicmlkETFrWnNza3hOUW5GdURZaGVuAR6IYO99j8pWM_Y17kD_8A3M2WD5uHjNt8aAEcRrPeWlbepVjSoDi4h9zAFn2Q_aem_2Rmt8CubmDtvORNbOFHBEw) (45,000-cap) amid reports of lower than expected ticket sales.

“I still have goosebumps – what a special moment,” said Michael Brill, CEO of operator D.Live. “After many years of conception and planning, Germany and North Rhine-Westphalia finally have the space we have been missing. With this unique urban site, we have reached a significant milestone for the live position of Düsseldorf and NRW,” he continued.

> “80,000 guests have impressively demonstrated the potential this new space holds”

The venue will also host OneRepublic and Pitbull on consecutive nights this upcoming weekend (18-19 July), as Brill noted the attendance at the System of a Down opening concert “showed the potential this new space has for international concert productions”.

“Around 80,000 guests have impressively demonstrated the potential this new space holds for international concert productions,” he added.

D.Live also used its own Event Mobility platform at Open Air Park, which the company said enables it to efficiently manage crowd flow and ensure protections for local residents.

Brill thanked partners involved in the project, including Live Nation Germany, who said of the opening: “Live events make lasting memories for fans and bring an essential energy to the host city. Open Air Park Düsseldorf offers artists a unique stage for exceptional international shows.”

The opening concert by System of a Down was first [announced](https://www.iqmagazine.com/2025/09/third-time-lucky-for-dusseldorf-open-air/) in September last year as part of the band’s ongoing European stadium tour. Following the Düsseldorf show, the tour most recently brought the band to London’s Tottenham Hotspur Stadium for two shows, which both drew around 50,000 fans.
