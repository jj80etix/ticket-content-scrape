# Seat Unique partners with Trip.com to power global experiential tourism

## Summary

Premium hospitality platform Seat Unique has announced a global partnership with Trip.com Group to list live-event hospitality and VIP experience packages across Trip.com's travel platforms, marking Seat Unique's first major push into Asia-Pacific. The tie-up offers more than 500,000 official packages spanning music tours, live events, and sporting experiences, building on Trip.com's prior concert-tourism collaboration with Live Nation Asia. The announcement comes alongside data pointing to continued growth in music tourism, including a recent £20 million funding round for Seat Unique.

### Key Points
- Partnership lists premium hospitality/VIP packages across Trip.com Group's platforms (Ctrip, Skyscanner, Qunar), offering 500,000+ official packages.
- Marks Seat Unique's first major Asia-Pacific expansion, connecting its 140+ rightsholder network to Trip.com's travel audience.
- Trip.com survey found 67% of Asia-Pacific travellers have planned vacations around concerts.
- Seat Unique recently raised £20 million and is targeting £175m in sales this year.


## Transcript

The global alliance will bring together the premium hospitality platform and the Singapore-headquartered travel service provider

![Robin Sherry & Bo Sun](https://www.iqmagazine.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/07/Robin-Sherry-Bo-Sun.png)

Robin Sherry & Bo Sun

Premium hospitality platform Seat Unique Group has announced a global partnership with travel service provider Trip.com Group to offer live events, hospitality, and VIP experiences to travellers worldwide, as the music tourism trend only continues growing.

The partnership will see the new category of premium live events listed across Trip.com Group’s platforms, providing customers with more than 500,000 official packages across major music tours, live events, and sporting experiences.

It marks Seat Unique’s first major expansion into the Asia-Pacific region, connecting its network of over 140 rightsholders with a massive international travel audience. According to a consumer survey from Trip.com, over 67% of Asia-Pacific travellers have planned vacations around concerts before, with equal amounts willing to travel internationally to see their favourite artists.

“The way people travel is changing,” says Robin Sherry, Seat Unique Group’s CEO. “Increasingly, it’s no longer just about where you go, but what you experience when you get there. By partnering with Trip.com Group, we’re giving millions of travellers access to official premium experiences at some of the world’s biggest sport, music and entertainment events through a platform they already know and trust.

“This partnership marks a significant milestone for our business. It accelerates our international growth, helps our rightsholder partners reach millions of new premium customers and brings unforgettable live experiences within reach of a truly global audience,” he concludes.

The alliance marks another foray into live entertainment for the Singapore-headquartered Trip.com Group, which comprises package provider Ctrip, flight aggregator Skyscanner, and online travel agency Qunar. The company joined forces with Live Nation Asia last October to offer [concert tourism packages](https://www.iqmagazine.com/2025/10/concert-travel-is-redefining-tourism-across-asia/) in the region, launching with shows from Twice and Blackpink.

> “Travellers are increasingly building trips around the moments that matter most to them”

“Experience-led travel is reshaping how people plan their journeys, and travellers are increasingly building trips around the moments that matter most to them,” says Bo Sun, CMO of Trip.com Group. “Live events are a powerful expression of that shift, and through this partnership, we’re bringing fans closer to some of the world’s most exciting sports, music, and entertainment experiences. This marks another step in our journey to unlock new possibilities between travel and experiences, bringing greater value to travellers and destinations worldwide.”

This new partnership is intended to offer international audiences access to premium experiences through Trip.com’s all-in-one travel platform, with every package sourced directly through Seat Unique’s network, which includes major music players like Co-op Live and KMJ Entertainment.

Recently, Seat Unique posted a [£20 million (€23.5m) funding round](https://www.seatunique.com/blog/seat-unique-raises-20m/), which is poised to support acquisitions, international expansion and continued growth across premium ticketing, hospitality, and travel. The Group says it is on track to hit £175m (€205m) in sales this year, with aims to double in the next three years.

It arrives as music tourism continues to drive record-breaking results for the industry, with the sector tipped to surpass [$400 billion](https://www.iqmagazine.com/2025/10/music-tourism-tipped-to-reach-400-5bn-by-2032/) (€350bn) by 2032.

Last year, the likes of Oasis, Beyoncé, Dua Lipa, Coldplay, Lana Del Rey, and Kendrick Lamar helped drive a [26.8% boost](https://www.iqmagazine.com/2026/07/uk-music-tourism-rose-over-a-quarter-in-2025-report-finds/) to tourism in the UK, drawing a record 24.7 million fans to the market, according to a report from UK Music.

Plus, Gen Z is showing an increasing willingness to travel for live events, with a [*Luminate*](https://www.iqmagazine.com/2026/07/gen-zs-price-sensitivity-declining-despite-concert-costs-rising/) study of US audiences finding that the age group’s live attendees who identified travel cost as a barrier to live shows dropped from 61% in Q1 2024 to just 31% two years later.

As a result, Live Nation has joined forces with travel and tourism platform Skift to launch the [Skift Live Tourism Summit](https://www.iqmagazine.com/2026/07/conference-news-live-nation-announce-new-music-tourism-summit/), taking place in New York later this year. The one-day event will focus on the growing travel and tourism ecosystem surrounding live music experiences.
