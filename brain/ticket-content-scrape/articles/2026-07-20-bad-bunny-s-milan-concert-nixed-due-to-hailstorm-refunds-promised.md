# Bad Bunny’s Milan Concert Nixed Due to Hailstorm, Refunds Promised

## Summary

Bad Bunny's July 18 concert in Milan at the Snai La Maura Hippodrome was interrupted and the roughly 80,000-person crowd evacuated after a violent hailstorm hit the open-air venue. Promoter Live Nation confirmed the interruption and evacuation in a statement and said all ticketholders will receive full refunds. The show was Bad Bunny's second and final Milan date; his tour continues with a stop in Brussels on July 22.

### Key Points
- Concert held at Snai La Maura Hippodrome, Milan, on Saturday, July 18, 2026.
- Approximately 80,000 fans were evacuated due to a violent hailstorm and thunderstorms.
- Live Nation confirmed the show was interrupted and the area safely evacuated.
- All ticketholders will receive full refunds.
- Show was Bad Bunny's second and final Milan concert; next stop is Brussels' King Baudouin Stadium on July 22.

Bad Bunny fans in Italy were unable to catch the Puerto Rican star’s concert in Milan over the weekend due to a violent hailstorm.

The concert was set to take place on Saturday, July 18, however, 80,000 fans rushed out of the open-air show at Snai La Maura Hippodrome.

Videos posted to social media showed fans attempting to leave the venue as large hailstones plummeted around them.

“Due to adverse weather conditions, including thunderstorms and hail, Bad Bunny’s concert scheduled for 18 July at the Snai La Maura Hippodrome in Milan was interrupted and the area was safely evacuated,” Live Nation noted in a statement on Sunday.

The organizer added that all ticketholders will receive full refunds.

The concert marked Bad Bunny’s second and final concert in the city. Bad Bunny is set to perform next at King Baudouin Stadium in Brussels, Belgium on July 22.
