# Black Flag Maps Out Massive 2026 U.S. Tour

## Summary

Black Flag announced an extensive fall 2026 U.S. tour spanning more than 45 cities, kicking off September 3 in Aspen, Colorado and wrapping November 1 in Houston, Texas. The run moves through the Mountain West, Midwest, East Coast, Southeast, Southwest, and California, hitting clubs, theaters, and independent venues. Additional dates in January and April 2027 are planned for the western U.S. Tickets are available via the band's official website.

### Key Points
- Tour launches September 3, 2026 at the Belly Up in Aspen, CO and concludes November 1 at House of Blues in Houston, TX.
- Spans more than 45 cities across the Mountain West, Midwest, East Coast, Southeast, Southwest, and California.
- Current lineup: founding guitarist Greg Ginn, vocalist Max Zanelly, bassist David Rodriguez, and drummer Bryce Weston.
- Additional January and April 2027 dates planned for California, Washington, Oregon, and Colorado.
- Venues are primarily clubs, theaters, and independent spaces rather than large arenas.
- Tickets available through the band's official website.

## Transcript

![](https://www.ticketnews.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/07/black-flag.jpg)

Black Flag will hit the road this fall for an extensive U.S. tour stretching across more than 45 cities.

The legendary punk band will launch the run on September 3 at the Belly Up in Aspen, Colorado, before winding through the Mountain West, Midwest, East Coast, Southeast, Southwest and California. The tour concludes November 1 at the House of Blues in Houston.

Founding guitarist Greg Ginn will lead the current lineup, joined by vocalist Max Zanelly, bassist David Rodriguez and drummer Bryce Weston.

Following recent touring across the United States and Europe, Black Flag returns with a set built around the band’s influential catalog. The upcoming run will bring the group to a mix of clubs, theaters and independent venues, giving longtime followers and newer fans an opportunity to experience the punk veterans in comparatively intimate settings.
