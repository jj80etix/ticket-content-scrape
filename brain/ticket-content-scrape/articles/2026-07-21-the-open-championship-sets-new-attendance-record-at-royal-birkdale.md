# The Open Championship Sets New Attendance Record at Royal Birkdale

## Summary

The Open Championship at Royal Birkdale drew a record 306,000 spectators over four days, surpassing the prior 290,000 mark from the 2022 St Andrews Open. New Zealand's Ryan Fox won his first major.

### Key Points
- New all-time Open Championship attendance record: 306,000.
- Previous record (290,000) was set at St Andrews in 2022.
- Ryan Fox won the Claret Jug, his first major title.

## Transcript

![](https://www.ticketnews.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/07/The-Open-Championship.webp)

The Open Championship attracted a record-breaking crowd of 306,000 spectators during this year’s tournament at Royal Birkdale near Liverpool, establishing a new attendance high for golf’s oldest major championship.

The four-day event concluded on July 19, surpassing the previous record of 290,000 spectators that was established during the 2022 Open Championship at St Andrews in Scotland.

The record-setting championship concluded with New Zealand’s Ryan Fox capturing the Claret Jug, earning his first Open Championship title. The victory marked Fox’s first major championship title and made him the fourth golfer from New Zealand to win a major, joining Bob Charles, Michael Campbell and Lydia Ko.

England’s Tommy Fleetwood, one of the home favorites throughout the week, finished tied for fourth place at seven under par alongside world No. 1 Scottie Scheffler of the United States.

Notably, Royal Birkdale has been one of the championship’s most established venues, hosting The Open on 11 occasions since first welcoming the tournament in 1954. The Southport course has become a regular fixture in The Open’s rotation and has staged many of the championship’s most memorable moments over the decades.

Beyond the men’s championship, Royal Birkdale has also played an important role in women’s golf. The venue is included in the rotation for the Women’s British Open and has hosted that championship six times.
