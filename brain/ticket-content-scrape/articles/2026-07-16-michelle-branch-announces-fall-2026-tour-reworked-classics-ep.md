# Michelle Branch Announces Fall 2026 Tour, Reworked Classics EP

## Summary

Michelle Branch announced an 18-date "Everywhere and Back Again" U.S. tour for fall 2026, tied to a reworked-classics EP of the same name marking the 25th anniversary of her 2001 debut album *The Spirit Room*. The tour runs September 27 in Seattle through November 13 in Lexington, KY, with general on-sale beginning July 17. The EP, due November 6 via BMG, includes a new version of "The Game of Love" featuring New Radicals' Gregg Alexander, who wrote the song originally recorded by Branch with Carlos Santana.

### Key Points
- 18-date tour: September 27 at The Showbox (Seattle) through November 13 at Manchester Music Hall (Lexington, KY).
- Stops include The Wiltern (Los Angeles), Beacon Theatre (New York City), First Avenue (Minneapolis), and House of Blues (Chicago).
- General on-sale begins Friday, July 17; presales already underway.
- *Everywhere and Back Again* EP due November 6 via BMG, featuring reworked versions of six songs including "Everywhere," "All You Wanted," and "Goodbye to You."
- New single is a reworked "The Game of Love" featuring New Radicals; the original (with Carlos Santana, 2002) won Branch and Santana a Grammy for Best Pop Collaboration with Vocals.
- Tour and EP were announced via MBTV, a livestream marking the 25th anniversary of *The Spirit Room* (2001).

## Transcript

![Michelle Branch announced the Everywhere and Back Again tour for fall 2026 this week.](https://www.ticketnews.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/07/michelle-branch-tour-cover.webp)

Michelle Branch announced the Everywhere and Back Again tour for fall 2026 this week.

Michelle Branch will return to the road this fall for an 18-date U.S. tour supporting *Everywhere and Back Again*, a forthcoming EP featuring newly recorded versions of songs from across her career.

The “Everywhere and Back Again Tour” begins September 27 at The Showbox in Seattle and runs through November 13 at Manchester Music Hall in Lexington, Kentucky. The itinerary includes stops at The Wiltern in Los Angeles, the Beacon Theatre in New York City, First Avenue in Minneapolis and the House of Blues in Chicago.

Ticket presales are underway, with the general public on-sale scheduled to begin Friday, July 17.

Branch announced the tour as part of MBTV, a livestream marking the 25th anniversary of her 2001 debut album, *The Spirit Room*. Released when Branch was 18, the album produced the singles “Everywhere,” “All You Wanted” and “Goodbye to You.”

The singer also released the first track from the new EP, a reworked version of “The Game of Love” featuring New Radicals.

Branch originally recorded “The Game of Love” with Carlos Santana for his 2002 album *Shaman*. The song reached the top five of the Billboard Hot 100 and earned Branch and Santana the Grammy Award for Best Pop Collaboration with Vocals.

New Radicals frontman Gregg Alexander wrote the song and recorded its original demo. Alexander reportedly recommended Branch for the Santana recording after other vocalists were considered for the track.

“‘The Game of Love’ has been such a special song in my life made possible by one of my personal musical heroes, Gregg Alexander from New Radicals,” Branch said. “Many people don’t realize that Gregg wrote ‘The Game of Love,’ and being able to sing this together with him is really a full-circle moment and captures the original spirit of the demo.”

Due November 6 through BMG, *Everywhere and Back Again* will include new versions of “Everywhere,” “Breathe,” “Goodbye to You,” “You Get Me,” “All You Wanted” and “Are You Happy Now?” Additional collaborators are expected to be announced ahead of the release.

The tour is divided into two stretches. Branch will play 10 shows in the western U.S. between late September and mid-October before returning for eight dates in the Midwest and Northeast during November.

The announced schedule does not currently include dates in the Southeast, Texas or Canada.
