# Spilt Milk festival set to move to Kardinia Park

## Summary

Australian touring music festival Spilt Milk will relocate to Kardinia Park Stadium and Precinct in Geelong, Victoria, where it plans to stay through at least 2030. The festival, previously staged in Ballarat since its 2019 Victoria debut, has grown into Australia's largest regional touring music festival and has injected more than A$46.7m into the state's economy. This year's lineup has not yet been announced, but a presale signup is open ahead of the event's expected December date.

### Key Points
- Spilt Milk moves to Kardinia Park Stadium and Precinct in Geelong, with the deal running through at least 2030.
- The festival previously held four editions in Ballarat after arriving in Victoria in 2019.
- It has generated more than A$46.7m (£24.4m/$32.5m/€28.6m) for the Victorian economy to date.
- Past lineups have included Kendrick Lamar, Doechii, Post Malone, Lorde, Flume, Childish Gambino, and others.
- A 2025 attendee survey found 100% of local Victorian attendees said the festival enhances community life.
- The move is backed by Visit Victoria and the City of Greater Geelong; producer is Kicks Entertainment.
- 2026 lineup is unannounced; a presale signup is open ahead of the festival's expected December date.

TheTicketingBusiness News

**Australian touring music festival Spilt Milk is set to make Kardinia Park Stadium and Precinct in Geelong its new home until at least 2030.**

The event first arrived in Victoria in 2019, with four editions staged in Ballarat before the move, and has injected more than A$46.7m (£24.4m/$32.5m/€28.6m) into the state’s economy.

“Geelong is the perfect stage for the festival’s next five years, and we want to thank Visit Victoria and the City of Greater Geelong for backing what Spilt Milk can do – their support means we can keep proving that a world-class festival doesn’t need a capital city and keep bringing tens of thousands of young people to regional Victoria every December,” said Ryan Sabet, director and co-founder of Kicks Entertainment, which produces the festival.

Since launching in 2016, Spilt Milk has grown into Australia’s largest regional touring music festival, featuring artists such as Kendrick Lamar, Doechii, Post Malone, Lorde, Flume, Childish Gambino, Khalid, Dom Dolla, Latto, and Steve Lacy.

In 2025, 100% of local Victorian attendees said Spilt Milk enhances community life, while 97% rated music, arts and cultural events as extremely or very important to quality of life in their city.

“Bringing Spilt Milk to Geelong is a fantastic opportunity for our region, most importantly, for our young people,” said Member for Geelong, Christine Couzens.

“These kinds of experiences give them the chance to connect with friends, create lasting memories, and enjoy world-class music close to home.

“I’m proud that Geelong is attracting these major events.”

The line-up for this year’s edition of the festival is yet to be announced, but fans can sign up to the ticketing presale before the event is expected to take place in December.
