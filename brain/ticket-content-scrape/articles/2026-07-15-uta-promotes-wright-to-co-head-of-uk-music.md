# UTA promotes Wright to co-head of UK Music

## Summary

Talent agency United Talent Agency (UTA) has promoted agent James Wright to co-head of its UK Music group alongside Neil Warnock, a year after Darnell Strom's appointment to lead UTA's London office.

### Key Points
- James Wright, at UTA since 2015 (via its acquisition of The Agency Group) and previously an agent at Elastic Artists and A&R manager at Candid Records, becomes co-head of UK Music alongside Neil Warnock.
- Wright was also recently made a UTA partner alongside Jules de Lattre.
- Global Music co-heads Sam Kirby Yoh and David Zedek called the London team "a cornerstone" of UTA's worldwide music network and cited it as key to identifying international touring talent.
- UTA's roster includes Bad Bunny, Rosalía, Karol G, FKA twigs, Halsey, Take That, David Byrne, and others.
- Wright is set to appear as a panelist for the opening session of International Festival Forum London (IFF London) on 8 September 2026 at Outernet London.

## Transcript

The agent will join Neil Warnock at the head of the department at what he called a "pivotal time" for the London team

![](https://www.iqmagazine.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/07/James-Wright-UTA.png)

James Wright © UTA

United Talent Agency (UTA) has promoted James Wright to co-head of its UK Music group, with the agent set to join Neil Warnock at the helm of the London-based team.

The agency said Wright will be responsible, alongside Warnock, for “steering the department’s short and long-term strategies and overall industry positioning”.

Wright joined UTA in 2015 following the company’s acquisition of The Agency Group, having previoulsy served as an agent at Elastic Artists. Before moving into the live music sector in 2009, Wright also worked as A&R manager for Candid Records.

Global Music co-heads Sam Kirby Yoh and David Zedek said: *“* UTA’s global music business is built on collaboration across genres, disciplines, and territories, and London is a cornerstone of that network.”

They added the London team is “critical to everything from identifying the next wave of international touring talent to executing complex global runs for established stars”, explaining that the bolstering of the deparment represents a further investment in London as “a strategic base for our worldwide touring ambitions and unlocking even more opportunity for our artists.”

> “Leading the London music group with Neil at such a pivotal time is incredibly exciting”

Wright said of his promotion: “Leading the London music group with Neil at such a pivotal time is incredibly exciting. Our focus is on signing exceptional artists and continuing to build a team culture that is inclusive, collaborative and accountable, where a colleague’s win or an artist’s success genuinely feels like a win for everyone.”

Wright was also recently made a partner at UTA along with Jules de Lattre. His promotion also comes a year on from the [appointment of Darnell Strom as leader of UTA’s London office](https://www.iqmagazine.com/2025/06/uta-appoints-uk-head-in-new-chapter-for-agency/), which the company said would herald “the next phase of international growth and continued expansion into key markets across Europe, the Middle East, and Africa”.

Wright was also announced yesterday as a panelist for the opening session of the [International Festival Forum London (IFF London)](https://www.iqmagazine.com/2026/04/iff-launches-in-la-and-moves-into-new-home-in-london/), titled ‘The Festival Season 2026’, set for 8 September at Outernet London.

UK Music co-head Neil Warnock said of Wright’s promotion that it has “been a privilege to watch James grow into not only a first-class agent but a thoughtful, trusted leader”.

“His intelligence, self-deprecating humour and razor‑sharp instincts will be instrumental in shaping the next chapter of UTA’s global music business,” Warnock added.

UTA’s roster currently includes Bad Bunny, Rosalía, Karol G, Sombr, FKA twigs, David Byrne, Sammy Virji, Take That, Jon Batiste, Giveon, Halsey, Arlo Parks, Underworld, Megan Moroney, and more are impacting the international touring marketplace.
