# LA28 Sets July 22 Deadline for Second Olympic Ticket Draw, Visa Presale

## Summary

LA28 set a July 22 registration deadline for its second Olympic ticket draw ("Drop 2") and a separate Visa-cardholder presale draw, part of a phased, randomized registration-and-time-slot system for allocating 2028 Los Angeles Olympics tickets. Selected registrants get a 48-hour purchase window — August 10-20 for Drop 2 or July 29-31 for the Visa presale — with notifications going out July 27 (Visa) or August 6-7 (Drop 2). Purchase limits are cumulative across all LA28 drops and presales: 12 Olympic session tickets, up to 12 for soccer, and 4 for ceremonies.

### Key Points
- Registration for LA28's second Ticket Draw and Visa Presale Draw both close 11:59 p.m. PT on July 22, 2026.
- Visa Presale runs July 29-31, with notifications July 27; Drop 2 general purchase runs August 10-20, with notifications August 6-7.
- Each purchase window stays open 48 hours; availability and pricing vary by sport, session and venue.
- Registrants who missed Drop 1 or didn't reach their ticket limit are automatically rolled into the second draw.
- Purchase caps are cumulative across all drops/presales: 12 Olympic session tickets, up to 12 for soccer, 4 for ceremonies.
- Paralympic Games tickets go on sale separately starting in 2027; registrants must be 18+ and subject to eligibility verification.

### Highlights
- LA28 uses a multi-phase randomized draw plus scheduled 48-hour purchase windows — rather than a live first-come queue — to manage massive demand across Olympic session tickets.
- A dedicated early-access tier (Visa cardholders) sits ahead of the general draw, with mutually exclusive slot assignment (a Visa slot precludes a Drop 2 slot).
- Purchase limits are enforced cumulatively across all drops/presales (12 Olympic tickets, 4 ceremony tickets) to control per-buyer allocation across multiple sales phases.

### Industry Problem
Extremely high-demand onsales risk queue meltdown and unfairness when sold via simultaneous open sale; LA28 illustrates the structural alternative — pre-registration, randomized selection, and scheduled individual purchase windows — to spread load and blunt bot/scalper advantage at the moment of sale.

### Proposed Solution
LA28's own approach is registration-gated, randomized, time-boxed access tied to cumulative per-buyer caps. Etix could improve/build similar phased-draw tooling — registration windows, randomized slotting, cross-phase cumulative limits, and tiered sponsor presales — as a configurable onsale mode for high-demand client events, reducing reliance on live queue infrastructure alone.

Fans hoping to purchase tickets for the 2028 Los Angeles Olympic Games [have until July 22 to register for the next phase](https://la28.org/en/newsroom/register-for-la28-ticket-draw-by-july-22-for-chance-at-drop-2-olympic-tickets-and-visa-presale.html) of LA28 ticket sales, including a separate early-access draw for qualifying Visa cardholders.

Registration for the second LA28 Ticket Draw closes at 11:59 p.m. Pacific time on July 22. Selected registrants will receive an opportunity to purchase tickets during Drop 2, which is scheduled to run from August 10 through August 20.

LA28 plans to notify fans selected for Drop 2 time slots by email between August 6 and August 7. Each purchasing window will remain open for 48 hours, although ticket availability and pricing will depend on the sport, session and venue.

Fans who registered for the initial ticket draw but either did not receive a purchase window or did not reach their ticket limit during Drop 1 will automatically be entered into the second draw.

LA28 is using a randomized registration and time-slot process rather than opening ticket sales to all buyers simultaneously. Registering for either draw does not guarantee that a fan will receive a purchase window, and receiving a time slot does not guarantee that tickets for a particular event will remain available.

Purchasers may buy as many as 12 Olympic session tickets, along with up to 12 tickets for the Olympic soccer tournament. Ceremony tickets are limited to four per purchaser and count toward the general 12-ticket Olympic limit.

The purchase maximum is cumulative across LA28 Olympic ticket drops and presales, meaning tickets purchased during an earlier sales phase will count against a buyer’s allowance in later rounds.

Tickets for the 2028 Paralympic Games are expected to go on sale separately beginning in 2027.

Registration for the Olympic ticket draws is free, although entrants must be at least 18 years old and remain subject to eligibility verification and the official draw terms.
