- Co-op Live (Manchester, 23,500 cap.): Europe's largest indoor arena by capacity; five direct-access loading bays.

**Coca-Cola Arena** | Dubai, UAE

*Concert capacity*: 17,000  
*Operator*: Legends Global

As one of the Middle East’s leading multi-purpose venues, Coca-Cola Arena has strengthened its position on the international touring circuit through major recent upgrades.

The venue has invested in a d&b audiotechnik line array sound system, incorporating new technologies to deliver consistent, high-quality audio coverage throughout the building. From a 2,000-capacity family show to a full-scale, 17,000-capacity concert, any level of production will benefit from the arena’s adaptable design.

Operational efficiency remains central to the venue’s appeal for all kinds of touring productions. In-house assets are key to supporting the rapid changeovers between concerts, sports, conferences, and live entertainment events, with the arena regularly accommodating multiple configurations every week.

Touring productions also benefit from streamlined loading access, flat-surface dock design, minimal push distances to the arena floor, with a roof load capacity of 190 tonnes, allowing the venue to accommodate the biggest productions. Positioned within the global hub of Dubai, Coca-Cola Arena continues to benefit from the city’s strategic role as a gateway between Europe, Asia, and Africa.

**Co-op Live** | Manchester, UK

*Concert capacity*: 23,500  
*Operator*: Oak View Group

A symbol of the next generation of live venues, Co-op Live made a notable splash when it came online in 2024. Designed as a fully acoustically treated black box venue, the arena places sound quality and audience experience at the centre of its design. As Europe’s largest indoor arena by maximum capacity, Co-op Live combines scale with flexibility through configurable seating and draping systems capable of accommodating a wide range of event formats.

Back-of-house infrastructure has been engineered for the demands of modern productions, with five loading bays offering direct arena-floor access for production vehicles, alongside a state-of-the-art artist village complete with an on-site gym. Located within Manchester’s Etihad Campus, the venue benefits from extensive surrounding space for external activations and streamlined touring logistics, while prioritising accessibility and fan wellbeing through dedicated respite areas and accessible viewing platforms on every level.

Sustainability is deeply integrated into the building’s operation, including 100% renewable energy usage, rainwater harvesting, reusable cup systems, and a zero-waste-to-landfill policy. Recent additions such as Europe’s first integrated autonomous self-serve market and the new Canalside fan zone further reinforce Co-op Live’s position at the centre of Manchester’s rapidly expanding entertainment district.

**Etihad Arena** | Abu Dhabi, UAE

*Concert capacity*: 18,000  
*Operator*: Ethara

Set along the picturesque Yas Bay Waterfront on Yas Island, Etihad Arena has quickly established itself as one of the Middle East’s largest and leading live entertainment destinations.

Opened in 2021, the state-of-the-art venue was designed to host everything from global concert tours and UFC events to NBA games, theatre productions, and corporate gatherings, scalable up to 18,000 people. Purpose-built for flexibility, the arena features modular staging, adaptable seating configurations, luxury suites, and a 3,565 sqm main bowl capable of transforming from an intimate theatre setting into a full-scale arena.

A gem in the Middle Eastern touring circuit, the venue is well-connected to Yas Island’s entertainment district, benefiting from seamless access to hotels, dining, and major entertainment attractions. Sustainability is also central to the arena’s long-term vision. Etihad Arena was developed to Estidama \[sustainability\] standards and incorporates energy-efficient cooling, heat-deflecting architectural design, digital ticketing systems, and venue-wide recycling initiatives aimed at reducing waste and environmental impact.

---

**Uber Arena** | Berlin, Germany

*Concert capacity*: 17,000  
*Operator*: AEG

Opened in 2008 as part of the Uber Platz development, the arena combines high-capacity infrastructure with modern production capabilities, supported by continually updated facilities and premium artist, crew, and fan amenities. With a maximum capacity of 17,000, it ranks among Germany’s largest indoor arenas and remains a key stop on the global touring circuit.

Engineered for operational speed, the venue is designed to easily transition between concerts, ice hockey, basketball, and other live entertainment formats. Strong roof and ceiling load capacities support complex rigging requirements, while flexible seating and staging configurations allow each production to optimise every patron’s experience. Premium hospitality has also become a notable feature, with dedicated entrances, exclusive lounges and suites, elevated dining options, and attentive in-seat service among the high-end offerings.

Externally, the venue benefits from exceptional transport links, supporting sustainable audience travel patterns. Their efforts in sustainability continue to be a priority after achieving AGF certification in 2024 and introducing a dedicated green rider for tours.
