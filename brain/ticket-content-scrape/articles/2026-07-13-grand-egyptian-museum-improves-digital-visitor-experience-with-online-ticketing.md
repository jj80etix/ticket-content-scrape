# Grand Egyptian Museum improves digital visitor experience with online ticketing

## Summary

The Grand Egyptian Museum integrated its official ticketing website into the mobile apps of Egypt's four telecom operators (Vodafone Egypt, Orange Egypt, e& Egypt, WE), letting visitors book tickets directly through carrier apps. The stated goals are protecting visitors from fraudulent ticketing sites and fake links, and the museum is separately exploring mobile wallet payments through the same telecom partners' payment platforms to simplify checkout.

### Key Points
- GEM's official ticketing site is now embedded in four Egyptian telecom carriers' mobile apps (Vodafone, Orange, e&, WE).
- Stated purpose: combat fraudulent ticketing websites/fake links and improve cybersecurity for online ticket bookings.
- Museum is evaluating mobile wallet payments via telecom operators' digital payment platforms as a follow-on step.
- Framed by Egypt's tourism ministry as part of a broader push for secure, government-backed digital ticketing channels.

### Highlights
- Distribution channel innovation: ticketing embedded directly in telecom carrier apps rather than a standalone site/app, reaching a built-in mobile user base.
- Explicit anti-fraud framing — protecting buyers from fake ticketing sites/links via a trusted carrier-app channel.
- Mobile wallet payment integration flagged as the next step, tied to the same telecom partnerships.

### Industry Problem
Ticket-buyer fraud from lookalike/fake ticketing sites is a recurring patron pain point, especially for high-demand attractions and events; carriers' branded apps offer a trust signal that a museum or venue's own site may lack, particularly for less tech-savvy or mobile-first visitors.

### Proposed Solution
The museum's solution is a distribution partnership (embedding official ticketing in trusted carrier apps) plus planned mobile-wallet checkout. For Etix, this points to two watchable/buildable patterns worth tracking: partnering with trusted third-party distribution channels (telecom, banking apps) to reduce fraud exposure and reach patrons where they already are, and prioritizing mobile wallet payment options as a fraud-reduction and conversion lever.

TheTicketingBusiness News

**The Grand Egyptian Museum (GEM) has made its official website accessible through the mobile applications of Egypt’s four telecommunications operators, meaning users are able to book museum tickets online.**

The museum is located in Giza, Egypt, and is dedicated to ancient Egypt and its culture, society and artefacts.

Launched by the GEM in cooperation with Egypt’s telecom companies, the initiative integrates the museum’s official website into the applications of Vodafone Egypt, Orange Egypt, e& Egypt and WE.

The move is designed to facilitate safe online ticket bookings, protecting visitors from fraudulent websites and fake links. It also forms part of a wider effort to enhance cybersecurity, and improve the overall digital visitor experience.

Additionally, the museum is looking into the introduction of mobile wallet payments through the telecom operators’ digital payment platforms. This would offer visitors greater flexibility and simplify the online booking process.

Egypt’s Minister of Tourism and Antiquities, Sherif Fathy, said that “the initiative reflects the ministry’s commitment to expanding cooperation between government institutions and the private sector to deliver secure, reliable digital services and ensure visitors can easily access official online platforms”.

Ahmed Ghoneim, chief executive of the GEM Authority, explained that the initiative was part of the museum’s strategy “to build an integrated digital ecosystem that prioritises visitor security and seamless access to services”.
