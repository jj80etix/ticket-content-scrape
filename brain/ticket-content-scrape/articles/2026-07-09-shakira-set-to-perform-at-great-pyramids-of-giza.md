# Shakira set to perform at Great Pyramids of Giza

## Summary

Shakira will return to Egypt for the first time in 19 years with a show at the Great Pyramids of Giza on November 28, rescheduled after her original March/April Middle East dates were postponed due to the 2026 Iran war; her Qatar debut at Doha's 974 Stadium is still set for November 18. Tickets range from roughly $140 to $140+ premium pricing (E£7,055 to E£15,055).

### Key Points
- Shakira plays Great Pyramids of Giza, Egypt, November 28 — her first Egypt show in 19 years.
- Originally postponed Middle East leg (Jordan, Qatar, Saudi Arabia, UAE, Egypt) due to the 2026 Iran war.
- Qatar debut at Doha's 974 Stadium still scheduled for November 18.

TheTicketingBusiness News

**Shakira is set to perform in Egypt for the first time in 19 years with a show at the Great Pyramids of Giza on November 28.**

The Colombian’s last performance in the country in 2007 also took place at the pyramids in what is described as “one of the most memorable international concerts ever staged in Egypt”.

“I’m very happy to be performing in Egypt, at the Pyramids of Giza. I love you all,” she said.

Ticket prices start at E£7,055 (£105/$140/€125) and rise to E£15,055 for premium seats.

The event is being organised by Egyptian company Venture Lifestyle which has also arranged performances from the likes of Katy Perry, John Legend, and Demi Lovato in the Middle East.

Shakira was initially due to bring her Las Mujeres Ya No Lloran tour to Jordan, Qatar, Saudi Arabia, the UAE, and Egypt in March and April.

However, the events were postponed due to the outbreak of the 2026 Iran war at the end of February.

“The November performance will put one of pop’s most recognisable figures amongst some of humanity’s most enduring landmarks,” read a statement from Venture Lifestyle.

Shakira is set to go ahead with her Qatar show on November 18 at Doha’s 44,000-capacity 974 Stadium, marking her debut performance in the country.

The new dates will align with the rescheduled Offlimits Music Festival in Abu Dhabi in the UAE, which organisers Theory Eleven postponed from April 4 to November 21 ahead of the event.

As it stands, the Jordan show remains “postponed indefinitely”. Shakira will also not perform in Saudi Arabia, as she was due to headline the country’s Formula 1 Grand Prix’s post-race concert series, though both that race and the Bahrain Grand Prix were cancelled entirely in April.
