# Chicago Stars FC, StubHub Announce Ticketing Partnership Through End of 2026 Campaign

## Summary

NWSL club Chicago Stars FC named StubHub its official fan marketplace and Open Distribution partner through the end of the 2026 season, the latest in StubHub's push into women's sports (following deals with the Chicago Sky and Athletes Unlimited Softball League).

### Key Points
- Partnership covers Chicago Stars home matches at Martin Stadium through the Nov. 21 season close.
- StubHub serves as both official marketplace and Open Distribution partner, letting the club sell through StubHub while retaining its own primary ticketing.
- Extends StubHub's women's-sports partnership roster (Chicago Sky, Athletes Unlimited Softball League).

### Industry Problem
Women's sports properties are seeking additional distribution reach and fan-data/brand control simultaneously — a tension the Open Distribution model is designed to resolve without requiring a full primary-ticketing handover.

### Proposed Solution
Another data point in StubHub's rapid build-out of "coexist with the incumbent" distribution deals, concentrated in fast-growing women's sports; worth tracking as a signal of where StubHub is prioritizing its secondary-market land grab.

Chicago Stars FC has announced a new partnership with StubHub, naming the ticket marketplace its official fan marketplace and open distribution partner for the remainder of the 2026 National Women’s Soccer League season.

The agreement is designed to expand ticket access for supporters attending Chicago Stars home matches at Martin Stadium in Evanston through the end of the current campaign, which concludes on November 21. The partnership will provide fans with additional options and greater flexibility when purchasing tickets for upcoming matches.

“Woman’s soccer is experiencing unprecedented growth, and our responsibility is to ensure that every fan who wants to be part of that movement has an easy and convenient way to access our matches,” said Sara Arnold, vice president of partnerships for Chicago Stars FC.

“StubHub has demonstrated a meaningful commitment to investing in women’s sports and expanding opportunities for fans to engage with their favourite teams,” Arnold continued. “We’re excited to partner with an organisation that shares our vision for growing the game and creating memorable experiences for supporters across Chicago and beyond.”

Under the agreement, StubHub will serve as both the club’s official fan marketplace and open distribution partner. The arrangement enables the Stars to make tickets available through StubHub’s marketplace while using the company’s open distribution network to broaden the club’s ticket reach.

StubHub said its open distribution model is designed to help sports organizations expand ticket sales while maintaining ownership of their customer relationships and brand identity.

“Open Distribution lets the Chicago Stars use StubHub’s marketing reach and audience to sell tickets while keeping control of their brand, data and fan relationships,” said Alexa Fuentes, director of soccer partnerships at StubHub.
