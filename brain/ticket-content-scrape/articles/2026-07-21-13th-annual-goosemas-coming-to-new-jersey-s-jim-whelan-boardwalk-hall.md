# 13th Annual Goosemas Coming To New Jersey’s Jim Whelan Boardwalk Hall

## Summary

Jam band Goose announced its 13th annual "Goosemas" holiday shows, a two-night December 11–12 stand at Atlantic City's Jim Whelan Boardwalk Hall closing out its 2026 touring year. Presales begin July 22, general on-sale July 24.

### Key Points
- Goosemas 2026 dates: Dec. 11–12 at Jim Whelan Boardwalk Hall, Atlantic City.
- Presale July 22–23; general on-sale July 24 at 10 a.m. local.
- $1 per ticket benefits the band's Western Sun Foundation.

Goosemas is back this December. Goose unveiled dates for its 13 <sup>th</sup> annual holiday celebration, and tickets go on sale this week.

The band will take over the historic Jim Whelan Boardwalk Hall in Atlantic City, New Jersey on Dec. 11 and 12, marking its final two shows of 2026. Goose is on the road from August through November, performing in venues like The Greek Theatre in Los Angeles, Seattle’s WAMU Theater, Red Rocks Amphitheatre near Denver, The Midland Theatre in Kansas City, Nashville’s The Truth and Citizens Live at the Wylie in Pittsburgh.

Fans can sign up for the presale at [goosetheband.com/tour](https://www.goosetheband.com/tour). They will be given a unique code that grants access to the band’s official presale beginning July 22 at 10 a.m. local time. Local presales begin the same day at noon local time, and all presales end July 23 at 10 p.m. Tickets will be made available to the general public July 24 at 10 a.m. local time.

One dollar from every ticket sold for Goosemas will benefit Western Sun Foundation, a nonprofit established by the band that provides support to small and mid-sized organizations that aim to improve music education, music therapy, the safety, health and well-being of women and children, and climate action.

Goose are managed by 11E1ven Management and booked by Roam Artists’ Ethan Berlin and Karl Morse in the U.S., Canada, Mexico, South America, Asia, Africa and the Middle East.

The quartet from Wilton, Connecticut, played two sold-out nights at New York’s Madison Square Garden in June, grossing $2,890271 off 28,455 tickets, according to Pollstar Boxoffice.
