# Rock-It Company expands touring logistics services to Abu Dhabi

## Summary

Touring logistics firm The Rock-It Company is opening a new regional headquarters in Abu Dhabi, partnering with the Abu Dhabi Investment Office, consolidating its freight, storage and tour-logistics divisions under one roof. The move comes as Abu Dhabi ramps up concert activity tied to its Formula One Grand Prix and other entertainment investments, even as broader Middle East touring remains dampened by regional conflict.

### Key Points
- Rock-It (formerly Global Critical Logistics) establishes second Middle East HQ, in Abu Dhabi.
- Partnership with Abu Dhabi Investment Office supports the emirate's live-entertainment ambitions.
- Move coincides with F1 Grand Prix-linked concerts (Imagine Dragons, Andrea Bocelli) and a planned Sphere venue on Yas Island.
- Middle East touring market remains affected by the ongoing US-Iran conflict, though some artists (Shakira) are returning to the region.

Touring logistics player The Rock-It Company (Rock-It) has expanded its presence in the Middle East with the establishment of a new regional headquarters in Abu Dhabi.

The expansion comes as part of a partnership with the Abu Dhabi Investment Office, with the company saying the move supports the city’s ambition to become “a world-leading destination for luxury experiences, cultural attractions, and global events”.

The New York-based company, formerly known as Global Critical Logistics, provides bonded storage and logistical services across core areas live entertainment and sport, as well as fine art, film and television, and luxury automotive. The move into Abu Dhabi will see the company unite all of these divisions in one headquarters.

The firm’s [live touring business](https://www.iqmagazine.com/2021/01/rock-it-cargo-sound-moves-join-forces/) provides services in air, ground, and ocean freight, as well as handling ATA carnets and flight charters and tour route planning advice for live tours.

“Abu Dhabi’s growth as a centre for sport, art, and entertainment aligns perfectly with Rock-It’s global expertise,” said Daniel Rosenthal, Rock-It president and CEO.

This growth includes a recent string of concerts announced in Abu Dhabi, many of which are organised around Abu Dhabi’s Formula One Grand Prix, including shows by [Imagine Dragons](https://www.iqmagazine.com/2026/05/imagine-dragons-to-headline-abu-dhabi-gp-concert/) and [Andrea Bocelli](https://www.iqmagazine.com/2026/06/andrea-bocelli-up-for-new-abu-dhabi-grand-prix-concert-series/). Meanwhile, the city’s government also unveiled plans in May to [build a Sphere](https://www.iqmagazine.com/2026/05/sphere-confirms-1-7bn-abu-dhabi-expansion/) venue on Yas Island, the chief entertainment and tourism district of the emirate.

The news comes amid a dampened Middle Eastern touring market, as the ongoing US-Iranian war continues to pose risks to the region’s live entertainment sector.

However, last week, in a potential sign of revival for the regional market, [Shakira penciled her return to the Middle East](https://www.iqmagazine.com/2026/07/shakira-plots-middle-east-return-with-egypt-qatar-shows/) with rescheduled dates for shows in Egypt and Qatar alongside a festival headlining slot in the UAE, though the announcement came as US strikes against Iran recommenced.
