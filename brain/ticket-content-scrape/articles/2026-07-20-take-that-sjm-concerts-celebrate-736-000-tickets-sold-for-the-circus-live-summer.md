# Take That, SJM Concerts celebrate 736,000 tickets sold for The Circus Live – Summer 2026

## Summary

Take That's "The Circus Live – Summer 2026" stadium tour sold 736,000 tickets across 17 UK and Ireland dates, promoted by SJM Concerts as a reimagining of the band's 2009 stadium tour. The Manchester show at Etihad Stadium was live-streamed on Amazon Music on June 20, becoming the most-watched UK live-streamed performance on record. The band also reported a 100% increase in UK/Ireland streams and over 100 million views across owned social content.

### Key Points
- The Circus Live sold 736,000 tickets across 17 UK and Ireland stadium shows.
- The tour was promoted by SJM Concerts, which also promoted Take That's original 2009 stadium tour.
- The June 20 Etihad Stadium (Manchester) show was live-streamed on Amazon Music, becoming the most-watched UK live-streamed performance ever.
- UK/Ireland streams for the band rose 100%, with a 70% growth in active listeners (46% aged 18-34).
- The tour visited stadiums including St Mary's (Southampton), Coventry Building Society Arena, Stadium of Light (Sunderland), Hampden Park (Glasgow), Principality Stadium (Cardiff), Etihad Stadium (Manchester), London Stadium, and Aviva Stadium (Dublin).
- The band reported over 100 million views across owned social content, reaching 14.4 million people.

TheTicketingBusiness News

**Take That’s ‘The Circus Live – Summer 2026’ sold 736,000 tickets across 17 UK and Ireland stadium shows, according to SJM Concerts.**

The string of dates concluded earlier this month, with The Circus Live acting as a reimagining of the British band’s iconic 2009 stadium tour. The tour retained the spectacle and theatricality of the original tour while interpreting it for a contemporary audience.

As well as the 736,000 tickets sold, The Circus Live at Etihad Stadium in Manchester was streamed live on June 20 on Amazon Music, breaking records to become the most watched UK live-streamed performance.

Take That headed to the likes of St Mary’s Stadium in Southampton, Coventry Building Society Arena in Coventry, Sunderland’s Stadium of Light, Glasgow’s Hampden Park, Cardiff’s Principality Stadium, Etihad Stadium in Manchester and London Stadium, before concluding at Dublin’s Aviva Stadium.

The UK and Ireland run was promoted by SJM Concerts, which also promoted the original 2009 tour.

Featuring Gary Barlow, Howard Donald and Mark Owen, the band also saw a 100% increase in UK and Ireland streams, with a 70% growth in active listeners. Some 46% of those listeners were aged between 18-34.

Take That also celebrated more than 100 million views across the band’s owned social content, reaching 14.4 million people.

“The Circus Live 2026 was always going to come with high expectations because the original tour meant so much to so many people,” said Chris Dempsey, founder and chief executive of Corduroy Artists, and manager of Take That.

“What Gary, Mark and Howard achieved was to honour that legacy while creating something that felt fresh, ambitious and relevant for today’s audience. The numbers are fantastic, but they only tell half the story. What I’ll remember most is the atmosphere inside the stadiums every night and seeing audiences of all ages sharing those moments together.

“That’s what makes Take That such a special live band and why they’ve continued to connect with people for more than three decades. Thank you to every promoter, partner, creative collaborator, member of the cast and crew, creator and supplier who helped make this tour possible. Most of all, thank you to the fans, whose support continues to make moments like this possible.”
