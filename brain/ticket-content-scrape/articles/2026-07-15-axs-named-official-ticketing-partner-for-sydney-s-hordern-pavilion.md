# AXS Named Official Ticketing Partner for Sydney’s Hordern Pavilion

## Summary

AXS has been named the official ticketing partner for Sydney's Hordern Pavilion, starting August 10, extending AEG-owned AXS's Asia-Pacific venue portfolio. The deal brings AXS's full technology stack — Mobile ID rotating barcodes, mobile ticket management, customer analytics, marketing tools, and integrated primary/secondary marketplaces — to the century-old Moore Park venue operated by Playbill Venues.

### Key Points
- AXS becomes Hordern Pavilion's primary ticketing provider effective August 10, 2026, covering concerts and other events.
- Package includes AXS Mobile ID (rotating digital barcodes), mobile ticket management, customer analytics, integrated marketing tools, and customer support.
- AXS operates both primary and secondary marketplaces and reports 1,600+ clients and 70M+ tickets processed annually worldwide.
- Deal extends AXS's broader Asia-Pacific expansion; other cited AXS clients include The O2 (London), Red Rocks Amphitheatre, Coachella/Stagecoach, and the LA28 Olympics.
- AXS is owned by AEG; Hordern Pavilion is operated by Playbill Venues, part of the Playbill Group.

### Highlights
- AXS's Mobile ID uses rotating digital barcodes specifically to reduce ticket duplication and unauthorized copying — a concrete anti-fraud feature shipping at scale (70M+ tickets/year, 1,600+ clients).
- The Hordern deal bundles primary ticketing with built-in resale/transfer, customer analytics, and marketing tools — positioning AXS as a full venue-management platform, not just a transactional ticketing vendor.
- Deal reflects continued AXS/AEG Asia-Pacific venue-portfolio growth (adds to The O2, Red Rocks, Coachella/Stagecoach, LA28 clients already on the platform).

### Industry Problem
Venues increasingly select ticketing partners based on bundled capabilities — fraud-resistant access control, first-party resale, and integrated marketing/analytics — rather than ticketing alone, raising the bar for what an independent primary platform must offer to win and retain venue accounts.

### Proposed Solution
AXS's response is to package rotating-barcode fraud protection with native resale and analytics into one venue-facing platform; Etix could benchmark its own barcode/anti-duplication tech and consider bundling first-party resale and venue-facing analytics/marketing tools to stay competitive for venue RFPs against Tier 2 consumer majors.

AXS has been selected as the official ticketing partner for Sydney’s Hordern Pavilion, adding one of Australia’s best-known live entertainment venues to the company’s growing Asia-Pacific portfolio.

Beginning August 10, AXS will provide primary ticketing technology and services for concerts and other events at the venue, which is located in Sydney’s Moore Park entertainment precinct.

The agreement will bring the company’s mobile ticketing, marketing and data products to the Hordern Pavilion. The technology package includes AXS Mobile ID, mobile ticket management, customer analytics, integrated marketing tools and customer support services.

AXS said the platform will give the Hordern Pavilion and its promoter partners additional tools to manage ticket distribution, engage attendees and evaluate event performance, while providing fans with a mobile-based purchasing and ticket management experience.

The Hordern agreement represents another step in AXS’s expansion across Australia and New Zealand, where the company has been building a portfolio of venue, festival, sports and entertainment clients.

The company has increasingly positioned its technology as a broader venue-management and fan-engagement platform rather than solely a transactional ticketing service. Its products cover ticket issuance and access control as well as customer data, mobile transfers, resale, marketing and event analytics.

AXS Mobile ID uses rotating digital barcodes designed to reduce ticket duplication and unauthorized copying. Fans can access, transfer and, where enabled, resell tickets through their AXS accounts.

The company operates both primary and secondary ticket marketplaces and is owned by global sports and entertainment company AEG. Its international client portfolio includes The O2 in London, Red Rocks Amphitheatre, the Coachella and Stagecoach festivals and the LA28 Olympic and Paralympic Games.

AXS says it works with more than 1,600 clients across North America, Europe and the Asia-Pacific region and processes more than 70 million tickets annually.

The Hordern Pavilion is operated by Playbill Venues, part of the broader Playbill Group of entertainment companies. The partnership gives AXS a prominent presence in Sydney while providing the Hordern with a ticketing system that can support both venue operations and the needs of outside concert promoters using the building.
