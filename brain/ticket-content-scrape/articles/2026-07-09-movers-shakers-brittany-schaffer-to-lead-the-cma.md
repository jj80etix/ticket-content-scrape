# Movers & Shakers: Brittany Schaffer to lead the CMA

## Summary

IQ Magazine's Movers & Shakers roundup leads with Brittany Schaffer's appointment as the Country Music Association's next CEO, succeeding retiring Sarah Trahern, alongside other personnel moves including new Legends Global VPs and a WME Group promotion for Marie Sheehy.

### Key Points
- Brittany Schaffer named next CEO of the Country Music Association, succeeding Sarah Trahern.
- Legends Global adds two live-entertainment VPs; WME Group elevates Marie Sheehy; ROSTR boosts its leadership team.

## Transcript

Plus: Legends Global adds two live entertainment VPs, WME Group elevates Marie Sheehy, ROSTR boosts leadership team, plus more...

![Brittany Schaffer](https://www.iqmagazine.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/07/Brittany-Schaffer.png)

Brittany Schaffer

The Country Music Association (CMA) has named Brittany Schaffer as its next chief executive officer, to succeed Sarah Trahern, who is [retiring](https://www.iqmagazine.com/2026/01/country-music-association-ceo-to-retire-at-end-of-2026/) after 13 years at the helm.

She joins the CMA from her role as dean of Belmont University’s Mike Curb College of Entertainment & Music Business. Prior to her time at the Nashville university, she served as head of artist & label partnerships, Nashville, at Spotify, helping lead the strategy for country and Americana genres during the shift to streaming. She began her career as a music and entertainment attorney at Loeb & Loeb LLP, advising artists, creators and Fortune 500 companies on entertainment and IP deals.

“Country Music meets people where they are, and it changes lives every day,” Schaffer says. “That starts with songwriters and artists and everyone who carries this music to fans around the world. Serving them is the honour of a lifetime. Sarah Trahern will leave CMA and this community stronger than she found it. I’m grateful for her mentorship and friendship, and for a transition marked by the same professionalism and intentionality that defined her tenure.

“Together with CMA’s talented staff and members, we’ll build on that foundation. We’ll reinforce the connectivity within this community, bring renewed energy to our signature events, and find new ways to connect Country Music with fans around the world. And if we get that right, somewhere a song will find somebody who needs to hear it. That’s the heart of this work, and I can’t wait to get started,” she concludes.

She will join the CMA on 8 September for a transition period and will formally step into the role on 1 January 2027.

Outgoing CEO Trahern adds: “Leading this organisation for 13 years has been the honour of my life, and I have this community to thank for that. I’m grateful to our search committee for the time, care and integrity they brought to this process, and I trust the thoughtfulness they poured into it. I wish Brittany every success as she steps into this role, and I look forward to cheering this entire industry on for many years to come.”

> “They are ideal partners as we continue to grow our content and booking capabilities”

**Legends Global  
**Venue management giant Legends Global has appointed Tom Alexander and Jenn Sonick as vice presidents of live entertainment.

In their new roles, Alexander and Sonick will join Legends Global’s central content and booking team that partners with top promoters to book concerts, family shows, entertainment events, and live sporting events across the company’s network of 450 venues globally.

Alexander brings more than 30 years of experience across touring, business, and live promotion, with stints at PACE Concerts, AEG, and Live Nation, while Sonick brings a deep expertise in live music booking, talent buying, and multi-venue programming from roles at ATG Entertainment and AEG Presents.

Jason Rio, SVP of live entertainment and content development, says: “Tom and Jenn each bring a remarkable depth of relationships and expertise that will strengthen our ability to deliver world-class programming across our venue network.

“Tom’s decades of experience on the touring and arena booking side, combined with Jenn’s track record of curating exceptional live experiences at iconic venues, make them ideal partners as we continue to grow our content and booking capabilities,” he says.

**WME Group  
**Marie Sheehy has been promoted to EVP and head of corporate communications for WME Group, which includes the WME talent agency, IMG Licensing, and Pantheon Media Group.

WME Group’s lead spokesperson, Sheehy, leads the team that handles internal, external, and executive communication across the company. She joined William Morris Agency in 2005, and has been involved in major milestones for the company, including the agency’s takeover by Endeavor in 2009 and the formation of [WME Group](https://www.iqmagazine.com/2025/03/endeavor-goes-private-wme-group-formed/) last year.

“Marie has been instrumental to WME for two decades and has helped steer the reputations of our company and our clients through countless milestones and constant evolution,” says Christian Muirhead and Richard Weitz, co-chairmen of WME Group. “Her strategic counsel, steady leadership and trusted relationships across the media and entertainment industries have made her an invaluable partner as we position WME Group as the premier destination for talent representation.”

> “I am excited that we have the right global team in place to lead our next stage of growth”

**ROSTR  
**Industry tech platform ROSTR has added two to its leadership team, with Jess O’Donnell joining as head of growth and partnerships and Santiago Restrepo Abisambra as head of Latin America.

The new additions come as ROSTR aims to accelerate its growth across the live music ecosystem.

O’Donnell joins from a seven-year tenure with Apple’s growth marketing team, bringing expertise in performance marketing to help the platform expand its user base.

Restrepo previously led business development at Last Tour, a leading Spanish independent promoter. He also previously served as A&R for ONErpm’s Andean and Caribbean region. His appointment will help ROSTR launch a significant presence in the Latin American market.

“ROSTR is the connective tissue for the music industry,” says Vivian Wang Donohue, chief growth officer. “Jess’s hire deepens that connection in our existing markets, while Santiago extends it into new ones, both driven by our mission of bringing fairness and transparency to the music industry.”

**ATG Entertainment  
**Live entertainment company ATG Entertainment will add Charlotte Longstaff to its team in September, when she’ll join as London programming director after seven years at LW Theatres.

She will work alongside head of London programming Holly Mirams to secure shows for the company’s West End venues, including the Harold Pinter Theatre, Duke of York’s, and Savoy. Stuart Griffiths will continue in his role as director of UK programming, working with Nicky Monk and the UK regional programming team.

Meanwhile, global chief content officer Michael Lynas will increase his focus on the company’s global programming and production investment strategy.

“We are always looking to bring the best shows to our theatres, and today’s announcement further strengthens the team that focuses on this content and manages the relationships with producers,” says global CEO [Melanie Smith](https://www.iqmagazine.com/2026/02/movers-shakers-atg-entertainment-bmac/).

“I am excited that we have the right global team in place to lead our next stage of growth, with Michael focusing on the big strategic content objectives that add value for our theatres, audiences and producers,” she adds.
