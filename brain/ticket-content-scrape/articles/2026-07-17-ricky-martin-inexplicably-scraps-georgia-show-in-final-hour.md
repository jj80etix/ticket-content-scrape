# Ricky Martin ‘inexplicably’ scraps Georgia show in final hour

## Summary

Ricky Martin canceled his debut Georgia concert one day before the show for reasons his promoter, Park Live, called "inexplicable," with ticket refunds to be processed at the original point of purchase. He remains scheduled to play Almaty, Kazakhstan the next day as part of a two-city regional run.

### Key Points
- Show was set for Rustavi International Motorpark (20,000 cap.) near Tbilisi, Georgia.
- Promoter Park Live gave no explanation for the cancellation; refunds go through original purchase points.
- Martin's Almaty, Kazakhstan show remains on schedule for the following day.
- Part of a broader government-backed push (Starring Georgia) to build the country's live-music market.

## Ricky Martin ‘inexplicably’ scraps Georgia show in final hour

The show's promoter, Park Live, said the Latin star cancelled his debut show in the transcontinental country one day out

![Ricky Martin](https://www.iqmagazine.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/07/Ricky-Martin.jpg)

Ricky Martin

Latin star Ricky Martin cancelled his debut concert in Georgia a day ahead of the show for “reasons inexplicable” to the show’s promoter, Park Live.

The Puerto Rican star was due to play his first show in the transcontinental market yesterday (16 July) at the 20,000-capacity Rustavi International Motorpark. The venue is located roughly 20 km outside the capital city of Tbilisi.

“For reasons inexplicable to us, the artist \[made\] the decision to cancel the concert,” the promoters [said](https://www.instagram.com/p/DaydbTMO5Ec/). “The return of monetary funds for the purchased tickets will be carried out at the place of their purchase.”

Martin’s performance in Georgia was part of a two-city run in the region, announced back in March by promoter Park Live. He is still due to play in Almaty, Kazakhstan, at the 54,500-capacity Spartak Stadium tomorrow (18 July). Natalie Imbruglia will support.

Live music has been positioned as a powerful cultural initiative for Georgia in recent years, with the government throwing its weight behind the Starring Georgia programme in 2023, which has since brought artists like Bruno Mars, OneRepublic, The Killers, Imagine Dragons, Justin Timberlake, and Guns N’ Roses to the [Caucasus](https://www.iqmagazine.com/2025/08/the-caucasus-the-next-frontier-for-touring/) country.

As it stands, Moby is due to perform at the same venue, the Rustavi International Motorpark, later this month on 30 July, with Park Live also promoting a pair of Bryan Adams shows in Georgia/Kazakhstan next June.

The promoter is also gearing up for its three-day festival, Park Live Almaty, in Kazakhstan from 21-23 August. Jack White, Gorillaz, and The Offspring are set to headline, with Cee Lo Green, Bloodywood, The Kills, Glassjaw, and De La Soul to join.

Park Live originated as an international music festival in Moscow, Russia, in 2013, founded by the independent Melnitsa Concert Agency promoter. Following the 2022 invasion of Ukraine, the festival was [relocated](https://www.iqmagazine.com/2024/07/moscow-park-live-relocates-kazakhstan/) to Almaty, Kazakhstan.
