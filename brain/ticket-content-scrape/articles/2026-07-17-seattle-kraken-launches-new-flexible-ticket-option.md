# Seattle Kraken launches new flexible ticket option

## Summary

The Seattle Kraken launched "Build-A-Pack," letting fans assemble custom 3-to-9-game ticket plans starting at $45/game, up to 32% cheaper than the $65.50 single-game minimum. The NHL club is also shifting most weeknight home games to a 6:40 p.m. PT start in response to fan feedback.

### Key Points
- Build-A-Pack: fans choose any 3-9 games; starts at $45/game, up to 32% off single-game price.
- Full/half-season memberships include a 25% food & beverage discount and playoff priority.
- Weeknight (Mon-Thu) games moved to 6:40 p.m. PT starts per fan feedback.
- Season opens Oct. 4 vs. Calgary at Climate Pledge Arena.

### Highlights
- Flexible 3-9 game self-build packaging, priced below single-game rates, is a direct response to demand for schedule-flexible commitment tiers.
- The start-time shift was explicitly attributed to season-ticket-member feedback.

### Industry Problem
Fans and season-ticket holders increasingly want ticket commitment levels between full-season plans and single-game purchases; rigid all-or-nothing packaging leaves that middle demand unmet.

### Proposed Solution
A flexible, self-service "build your own mini-plan" tool with tiered per-game discounts is a concrete build/improve target for a primary platform serving team/venue clients who want to offer this without custom development.

The team finished sixth in its division last season with 34 wins, 35 losses, and 11 overtime defeats.
