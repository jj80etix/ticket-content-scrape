# Shane Gillis Shatters Two Guinness World Records at Sold-Out Philadelphia Stadium Show

## Summary

Comedian Shane Gillis set two Guinness World Records at a sold-out July 17 Lincoln Financial Field show in Philadelphia: largest audience for a comedian (73,946) and most tickets sold for a solo comedy show (77,047).

### Key Points
- Records verified on-site by a Guinness adjudicator.
- Surpassed the prior comedian-audience record of 67,733 (Mario Barth).
- Show featured surprise guests Dave Chappelle and Meek Mill, among others.

## Transcript

![Shane Gillis | Press Photo](https://www.ticketnews.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/07/shane-gillis.jpg)

Shane Gillis | Press Photo

Shane Gillis turned his biggest show yet into a record-breaking homecoming.

The Pennsylvania-born comedian set two Guinness World Records during his sold-out July 17 performance at Lincoln Financial Field in Philadelphia, drawing an official audience of 73,946 and selling 77,047 tickets for the massive stadium event.

The crowd broke the record for the largest audience for a comedian, surpassing the previous mark of 67,733 set by Mario Barth. Gillis also claimed the record for the most tickets sold for a comedy show by a solo comedian.

A Guinness World Records adjudicator was on site to verify both achievements.

“Tonight’s event was a powerful reminder that record-breaking achievements continue to capture people’s imagination around the world,” said Guinness World Records adjudicator Andy Glass. “Congratulations on achieving new Guinness World Records titles for ‘Largest audience for a comedian’ and ‘Most tickets sold for a comedy show by a solo comedian.’ You’re OFFICIALLY AMAZING®!”

The hometown celebration featured a stacked lineup of surprise guests. Dave Chappelle, Jay Oakerson and Matt McCusker appeared as opening acts, while Meek Mill and Three 6 Mafia delivered musical performances.

Philadelphia Eagles stars Jason Kelce, Cooper DeJean and Jordan Mailata also joined the festivities, welcoming Gillis back to Pennsylvania and introducing performers throughout the night.

The Lincoln Financial Field show capped a massive two-year run for Gillis. Across more than 100 headline performances, the comedian has sold over 1 million tickets and established 34 venue attendance and sales records across North America. Those milestones include the most tickets sold for any live event at Toronto’s Scotiabank Arena, the highest-attended event in the history of Seattle’s Climate Pledge Arena and the largest comedy crowd ever recorded at Philadelphia’s Wells Fargo Center.

Gillis has also set records at Chase Center in San Francisco, Tucson Arena, BJCC Legacy Arena in Birmingham, Nationwide Arena in Columbus, TD Garden in Boston and Golden 1 Center in Sacramento.

The record-breaking evening also supported organizations in Gillis’ hometown community. Special-edition hats featuring Philadelphia coffee shop Nour were sold at the stadium and online, with proceeds benefiting Nouri’s Place. Nour, co-owned by Kait Gillis, trains and employs neurodivergent and neurotypical individuals. Nouri’s Place provides resources for families and creates inclusive opportunities for people of all abilities.

The celebration continued after the show through a partnership with Bud Light. The promotion referenced Gillis’ story about spending his final $9 on a Bud Light during an Eagles game at Lincoln Financial Field years earlier, with participating Philadelphia bars offering complimentary drinks to eligible fans following the performance.

Gillis’ momentum will continue throughout the remainder of 2026. The third season of his Netflix comedy series *Tires*, which recently earned its first Emmy nomination, is scheduled to premiere Aug. 13. He will also appear in the upcoming film *Madden* alongside Nicolas Cage, Christian Bale, John Mulaney and Kathryn Hahn this November.
