# Atlas Arena sets attendance record

## Summary

Poland's Atlas Arena in Łódź drew a record 250,000+ visitors across 32 events in the first half of 2026, six of them sellouts, with acts including Kings of Leon, Deftones, and Akon. The venue also reported outsized social media growth, with its TikTok following up 345% over the same period.

### Key Points
- Atlas Arena hosted 32 events in H1 2026, drawing over 250,000 attendees, a new venue record.
- Six shows were complete sellouts.
- Notable acts included Kings of Leon, André Rieu, Deftones, Akon, Wu-Tang Clan and Raye.
- TikTok following grew 345% in H1 2026, the venue's highest growth rate on record.
- Second-half 2026 lineup includes Marilyn Manson, Scorpions, Deep Purple and Pitbull.

![TheTicketingBusiness News](https://www.theticketingbusiness.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/04/TBN-LOGO-reversed-white.png)

TheTicketingBusiness News

**More than 250,000 people visited Łódź’s Atlas Arena in the first six months of this year, marking a new record for the Polish venue.**

From January until the end of June, the arena hosted 32 events with six being completely sold out.

It has hosted concerts from the likes of Kings of Leon, André Rieu, Deftones, Alphaville, Ne-Yo, Akon, Wu-Tang Clan, and 5 Seconds of Summer.

Raye also launched the European leg of her “This Tour May Contain New Music” tour at the 14,728-capacity venue.

The popularity of the events also translated into online results.

“In the first half of 2026, the Atlas Arena community on TikTok grew by as much as 345 percent, marking the highest growth rate in the venue’s history,” read an Atlas Arena statement.

“The venue’s social media profiles, including Instagram and Facebook, currently reach up to two million users per month.

“The most popular content included concerts by international stars, sold-out events, and behind-the-scenes footage of the organisation of major productions.”

The second half of the year is set to see shows from Marilyn Manson, Scorpions, Deep Purple, Placebo, Hollywood Vampires, Jungle, Pitbull, A$AP Rocky, and more.

“After a record-breaking first half of the year, all signs point to 2026 potentially being one of the best years in Atlas Arena’s history in terms of attendance, and Łódź will further solidify its position as one of Poland’s most important centres for music and sports events,” the statement continued.
