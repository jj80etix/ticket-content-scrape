# Latin party events leader Bresh Global acquires Grupo Polenta

## Summary

Bresh Global, a Latin-focused experiential music company, has acquired a majority stake in Argentina-based Grupo Polenta, marking Bresh's first add-on acquisition and expanding its Gen Z/young-millennial-focused Latin music and nightlife portfolio across Latin America, Europe, and the US.

### Highlights
- Bresh Global's first add-on acquisition: majority stake in Grupo Polenta (Argentina), terms undisclosed.
- Grupo Polenta produced ~120 events in 2024 (club nights, festivals, sponsor activations) across multiple continents.
- Grupo Polenta continues operating under its own brand with founders remaining as leadership.

### Industry Problem
Consolidation among experiential/nightlife live-event operators signals continued market maturation and roll-up activity in adjacent live-entertainment segments beyond traditional ticketed concerts.

### Proposed Solution
No product implication directly; Monitor posture — track whether experiential-event roll-ups like this create new multi-market ticketing/venue-partner opportunities as these portfolios scale.

### Key Points
- Bresh Global acquires majority stake in Grupo Polenta, its first add-on deal.
- Expands Latin-music/nightlife experiential portfolio across Latin America, Europe, and the US.
- Financial terms undisclosed; Grupo Polenta founders remain in leadership.

Latin-focused experiential music company Bresh Global has acquired a majority stake in Grupo Polenta, folding in the Argentina-based operation’s DJ-driven music experiences into its global events portfolio.

The alliance, Bresh Global’s first add-on transaction, will expand the company’s portfolio of entertainment IP rooted in community and live experiences, primarily across Latin music for Gen Z and young millennial audiences. Financial terms were not disclosed.

Founded in Argentina, Grupo Polenta is a market leader in its home market and has expanded across Latin America, Europe, and the United States. In 2024, the brand produced roughly 120 events, including club nights, festivals, and sponsor-backed activations.

It will continue to operate under its own brand and be led by co-founders Marina “Pol4ca” Frohmann and Ignacio Elizalde, who will remain as CEO.

“We always wanted the dance floor to be a place of freedom and expression – a place with a story to tell, where music reconnects you with memories while introducing you to something new,” say the co-founders. “That’s the energy and community we built Polenta around. At its core, Polenta has always had one purpose: to bring light to the night. It may sound simple, but we believe nightlife is ready for a revolution.”

The acquisition of Grupo Polenta will expand the reach of Bresh Global, which says it produces over 500 events in over 30 countries for over one million attendees annually.

> “Polenta is a natural addition to the Bresh Global platform”

Bresh Global was formed in September 2024, when the Buenos Aires-founded Bresh brand partnered with investment firm Carroll Street Capital to launch an international media and live events platform.

The Bresh party brand has appeared at festivals including Coachella, Lollapalooza, Tomorrowland, and Arenal Sound.

“Bresh was born from the belief that happiness lives inside every person, and that music, emotion, and community can help awaken it,” says Jaime James, founder of Bresh. “Polenta has built a world with that same energy: its own sound, its own spirit, and a community that comes together to feel alive. We are excited to bring our communities together, help Polenta reach more people, and create more moments of happiness.”

Bresh Global is backed by the holding company Carabela, which is focused on scaling entertainment businesses.

“Polenta is a natural addition to the Bresh Global platform,” says Eduardo Garcia Fernandez, Carabela founder and managing partner. “It brings a complementary audience, adds new experiential formats, and fits how we build asset-light IP that travels across markets.”
