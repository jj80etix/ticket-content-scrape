# Napster AI agent guides F1 fans through Belgian Grand Prix buying purchase

## Summary

Napster's agentic AI concierge "Jack," deployed for the Belgian Grand Prix at Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps, handled over 17,000 fan conversations in its first month, generating an estimated $285,000 in incremental revenue for race promoter Spa Grand Prix. Built on the Napster API, Jack guides fans in their own language through multi-day packages, parking, camping, hospitality tiers, and seat selection directly on the ticketing site, including a 360-degree seat-view tool. Napster and Spa Grand Prix are framing the deployment as one of the first uses of agentic AI in live motorsport ticketing, and are extending Jack into 2027-season sales.

### Key Points
- Jack launched in June 2026 as a multi-year collaboration between Napster and Spa Grand Prix.
- In its first 30 days, Jack held 17,407 conversations with fans across 21 languages.
- Napster estimates the deployment generated $285,000 (£214,000/€251,000) in additional revenue.
- Clickthrough-to-checkout improved by 70% following Jack's deployment.
- More than 1,200 hours of customer service time were saved between June 2 and July 2.
- Nearly 700 fans used Jack's 360-degree seat view to check sightlines before purchasing.
- Spa Grand Prix has opened 2027 ticket sales, with Jack set to support the full season's inventory.

### Highlights
- 17,407 fan conversations in 30 days across 21 languages, directly on the ticketing site.
- 70% improvement in clickthrough-to-checkout attributed to the AI concierge.
- $285,000 in estimated incremental revenue and 1,200+ staff hours saved in one month.
- ~700 fans used an AI-surfaced 360-degree seat view before committing to a purchase tier.

### Industry Problem
International fans face a complex, high-friction buying decision (multi-day packages, hospitality tiers, parking, seat selection) compounded by language barriers, which historically both suppresses checkout conversion and consumes heavy human customer-service capacity for the promoter.

### Proposed Solution
Napster's answer is an embedded, multilingual agentic AI concierge that walks fans through package/seat decisions in real time and surfaces tools like 360-degree seat previews, reducing support load while lifting conversion. Etix could benchmark its own Assistant against this model — piloting a similar on-site AI concierge for complex, tiered events (festivals, motorsport, packages with upsells) that offers multilingual guidance and seat-preview integration to cut checkout abandonment and support ticket volume.
