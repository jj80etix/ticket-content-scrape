# Wiz Khalifa’s arrest warrant leads to Czech festival cancellation

## Summary

Czechia's Clout Festival (Prague edition) was cancelled after headliner Wiz Khalifa scrapped his entire European tour, following a Romanian arrest warrant tied to a 2024 onstage marijuana conviction. Organizers could not find a replacement headliner on short notice for the 10,000-capacity debut international edition. Ticketholders are being offered full refunds or the option to transfer to the festival's Polish edition, which proceeds as planned in Warsaw.

### Key Points
- Clout Festival's Prague debut (Areál 7, up to 10,000 cap., 4-5 July) was cancelled after Wiz Khalifa pulled out of his whole European run on 25 June.
- Cancellation stems from a Romanian arrest warrant following Khalifa's December conviction/nine-month sentence for onstage cannabis possession at 2024's Beach, Please! Festival.
- EU-wide arrest warrant validity forced cancellation of Khalifa's other European dates (Türkiye, Norway, Switzerland, Estonia) as well.
- Organizers announced the Czech cancellation four days after Khalifa's withdrawal, citing inability to secure a replacement headliner.
- Ticketholders offered full refunds or transfer to Clout Festival's Polish edition (Warsaw, 10-11 July, up to 60,000 cap.), which proceeds as scheduled.

## Wiz Khalifa’s arrest warrant leads to Czech festival cancellation

Clout Festival was called off after Khalifa axed his entire European tour, as Romanian authorities seek his arrest for drug possession

![Wiz Khalifa](https://www.iqmagazine.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/06/wiz-khalifa.jpg)

Wiz Khalifa © Tore Sætre

Czechia’s Clout Festival has been called off after headliner Wiz Khalifa cancelled his European tour, as he faces charges in Romania following his conviction over smoking marijuana onstage at a festival in 2024.

The rap and hip-hop festival was due to expand to Prague, its first international edition outside its Polish flagship event, last weekend (4-5 July) at Areál 7, which can accommodate up to 10,000 people. Artists including Quavo, Yzomandias, Rich Amiri, Ski Mask the Slump God, HXG, and NobodyListen were also lined up to perform.

Khalifa was confirmed as a headliner on 19 June, but cancelled his entire European run days later on 25 June, just over a week before the festival was due to begin.

“This is out of his hands and out of ours. We’re as shocked as you are,” organisers said at the time. This isn’t something we saw coming, and it’s not something we could control. But it’s something we have to deal with, and we’re already on it,” they said.

Four days later (29 June), organisers announced the Czech event would not go ahead after they could not find a “replacement headliner on such short notice”.

The news came just over a month after [reports](https://www.romania-insider.com/wiz-khalifa-wanted-person-romania-may-2026) surfaced that Romanian police had issued an arrest warrant for the American rapper, after he was [sentenced to nine months in prison](https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/articles/c93wxp2p05xo) in December for convictions related to an appearance at the Beach, Please! Festival in 2024.

During his set, he allegedly smoked cannabis on stage and was later charged and convicted of possession of drugs for personal use. In the country, recreational cannabis is strictly illegal and is classified as a high-risk drug.

In the European Union, an arrest warrant issued by one member country is legally valid across the entire territory. Khalifa cited the “ongoing legal situation” for the cancellation of the tour, which also included dates at Türkiye’s Istanbul Congress Center, Norway’s Slottsfjell festival, Switzerland’s Openair Frauenfeld, and Estonia’s Beach Grind.

He was also due to perform at Clout Festival’s Polish edition, which will go ahead this weekend from 10-11 July at Warsaw’s Bemowo Airport, which can host up to 60,000 people. Artists still set to perform at this weekend’s event include Ken Carson, Don Toliver, Swae Lee, Skepta, Fakemink, DestroyLonely, and Quavo, among others.

Ticketholders for the Czech event are due to receive full refunds, though organisers announced they could transfer their ticket to the Polish event.

Since it launched in 2022, organisers say Clout Festival has welcomed more than 70,000 attendees and over 100 artists.
