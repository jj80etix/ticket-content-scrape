# Tasmania proposes live music rebate for small venues

## Summary

Tasmania's Acting Premier Bridget Archer has proposed a pilot rebate program that would reimburse pubs, clubs and other small venues for 50% of verified expenditure on live music performances. The initiative aims to boost local musician employment and revive foot traffic at hospitality venues by making it more financially viable to book live acts. The proposal has been sent to the Australian federal government for consideration.

### Key Points
- Tasmanian government proposes a "Live Music Excise Rebate Pilot" for small venues.
- Rebate would cover 50% of verified live-music expenditure at pubs, hotels and clubs.
- Goal is to increase gig frequency, support musicians/sound technicians, and boost local economies.
- Proposal has been sent to the Australian government; not yet approved.

**The Tasmanian government has proposed a financial incentive for pubs and clubs to host live music in order to create more opportunities for the region’s musicians, and encourage more people back in local venues.**

Acting Premier Bridget Archer has written to the Australian government proposing a Tasmanian Live Music Excise Rebate Pilot that would provide participating venues with a rebate worth 50% of their verified expenditure on live music.

Pubs, hotels and clubs and other small venues would be able to claim back half of what they spend on live performers and associated live music costs, with the goal of encouraging more gigs and making hosting live music economically viable.

“This initiative would support the hospitality industry and back Tasmania’s live music scene,” said Archer.

“Tasmanians love live music and they love their local pub – this proposal is about supporting both. We want to see local venues rewarded for investing in musicians, creating local jobs and bringing communities together.

“This incentive means the more a venue invests in live music, the more support it will receive. More live music means more people through the door, more customers in town, more activity in our regions and more opportunities for local businesses.

“It means jobs for musicians, work for sound technicians and stronger local economies.”
