# Row erupts over Heritage Live cancellation following refund offers

## Summary

Heritage Live's promoter GCE Live and ticketing partner See Tickets are locked in a public dispute over who owes fans refunds after three UK Heritage Live festivals (Sandringham, Englefield, and Audley End) were cancelled. See Tickets, which claims GCE Live never returned ticket-sale proceeds owed to it, announced on 15 July it would self-fund full refunds to fans anyway; other agents involved in selling the events (Ticketmaster, AXS, Skiddle, Ticketline, Gigantic) also confirmed refunds without clarifying who ultimately bears the cost. HeritageLive says the episode exposes a structural problem: independent promoters have little leverage over the small number of major ticket agents that hold and control customer funds.

### Key Points
- Three Heritage Live 2026 festivals cancelled: Sandringham Estate (19-23 Aug, with Michael Bublé, Janet Jackson, Christina Aguilera, Eric Clapton, Lionel Richie, Ricky Martin), Englefield Estate (Richard Ashcroft, UB40 ft. Ali Campbell, Ministry of Sound Classical), and Audley End Estate (6-9 Aug, with Scissor Sisters, Bananarama, Faithless).
- See Tickets (part of Eventim) said on 15 July it would issue full refunds "irrespective of the legal position," despite maintaining that promoters GCE Live/HeritageLive failed to return ticket-sale proceeds it says it is owed.
- HeritageLive disputes responsibility, arguing the "longstanding convention" is that the ticket agent refunds customers first and recovers costs from the promoter afterward; it directed fans to Ticketmaster, AXS, Skiddle, and Ticketline as well as See Tickets.
- Ticketmaster confirmed on 14 July it would issue full refunds but declined to say who is funding them or whether it is owed money by GCE Live.
- HeritageLive says the dispute evidences a wider industry issue: as an independent promoter it has "little choice but to rely on a small number of major ticket agents" to process transactions and safeguard customer funds.

### Highlights
- Fans of the Sandringham/Englefield/Audley End festivals were bounced between See Tickets and HeritageLive, each pointing to the other as responsible for refunds.
- See Tickets ultimately self-funded refunds "irrespective of the legal position" rather than leave fans unpaid.
- HeritageLive publicly stated it has "little choice" as an independent promoter but to rely on a small number of major ticket agents to safeguard customer funds.
- Five separate agents (See Tickets, Ticketmaster, AXS, Skiddle, Ticketline, Gigantic) were entangled in selling/refunding the same cancelled events.

### Industry Problem
When an event cancels, ambiguity over whether the ticket agent or the promoter is contractually and financially responsible for refunds leaves patrons confused and stuck chasing multiple parties, while independent promoters are exposed to reputational damage from a dispute they have limited leverage to control, since they depend on a handful of major agents that hold their sales proceeds.

### Proposed Solution
The article describes only an ad hoc, goodwill refund by See Tickets rather than a systemic fix; for an independent primary platform like Etix, this evidences demand for contractually clear, pre-agreed cancellation/refund-liability terms with promoter clients plus fast, platform-funded refund mechanisms that protect the promoter relationship — a differentiator against agents seen as slow or adversarial in a crisis, and grounding material for support-facing AI on how refund responsibility actually works.

## Transcript

The promoter of the cancelled festivals said it was "hugely disappointed" after See Tickets told fans the event had failed to pay back its ticket revenue

![](https://www.iqmagazine.com/wp-content/smush-webp/2026/07/Michael-Buble-Sandringham-scaled.jpg.webp)

Michael Buble performing at Sandringham © HeritageLive

Promoters of the cancelled Heritage Live music festivals in the UK have hit back at ticketing partner See Tickets for claiming it is owed money by the events while offering refunds to fans.

This comes after See Tickets today (15 July) confirmed it would issue full refunds to ticket holders despite claiming promoters had failed to pay ticket proceeds it says it is owed relating to the Heritage Live events.

The cancellation of the three festival events was [announced on Monday](https://www.iqmagazine.com/2026/07/heritage-live-cancels-three-uk-festival-events-citing-spiralling-costs/), with organisers telling fans to contact ticket agencies about refunds. However, reports soon emerged of fans contacting ticket sellers only to be told to seek money back from HeritageLive.

Heritage Live is a series of festival events staged at stately home estates in the UK, promoted by GCE Live. The cancellation means three separate festival events will no longer take place. The [Sandringham Estate festival](https://www.iqmagazine.com/2026/06/festival-news-edc-reveals-lineup-for-colombia-debut/) had been due to host performances by Janet Jackson, Christina Aguilera, Eric Clapton, Lionel Richie, and Ricky Martin, across 19-23 August.

A separate festival at the Englefield Estate in Berkshire was due to host Richard Ashcroft, UB40 featuring Ali Campbell, and Ministry of Sound Classical, while a third at the Audley End Estate in Essex was set for a four‑night run on 6–9 August featuring Scissor Sisters, Bananarama, Gok Wan, The Streets, Pete Tong, and a Faithless headline set.

One ticket holder for the Sandringham event told *IQ:* “I purchased tickets including VIP packages and was informed by SeeTickets about it, but was told they aren’t responsible for refunds. HeritageLive are saying the same thing and Citizens Advice are saying refunds are with who took the money for the tickets.”

Earlier today (15 July), ticketing partner See Tickets emailed fans to say the firm would be issuing full refunds, despite claiming GCE Live and HeritageLive had failed to pay proceeds from the on-sale to See Tickets.

However, a HeritageLive spokesperson told *IQ*: “We are hugely disappointed by the position taken by See Tickets for these events, although we are glad to see that See Tickets customers are starting to be refunded.

“All promoters experience the cancellation of shows from time to time, and the longstanding convention has always been that the ticket agent refunds the customer in the first instance and then recovers those costs from the promoter over time.”

“The responsibility to refund customers sits with the ticket agent, not the promoter, and customers should never be left carrying the weight of an inter-company arrangement they were never party to,” they added.

> “Our position remains that responsibility for customer refunds rests with the promoter”

Despite See Tickets, which is part of Eventim, saying it was the sole “authorised” ticketing agent for the festivals, in its statement on the events’ cancellation, HeritageLive told fans to seek refunds from a range of ticketing agencies, including Ticketmaster, AXS, Skiddle, and Ticketline.

A See Tickets spokesperson told *IQ: “* See Tickets acted solely as the authorised ticketing agent for the Heritage Live 2026 events at Englefield, Audley End and the Royal Sandringham Estate. The decision to cancel these events was taken solely by the event promoter, GCE Live.

“Our position remains that responsibility for customer refunds rests with the promoter, since they have failed to return the ticket sale proceeds to us to enable this.”

They continued: “However, we do not want customers to be left out of pocket while these issues remain unresolved. We have therefore decided to fund customer refunds ourselves in the first instance, irrespective of the legal position. We will be reaching out to them shortly with instructions on how to claim their refunds. We remain committed to providing the best possible service to our customers, even in exceptionally difficult circumstances.”

> “as an independent promoter, HeritageLive has little choice but to rely on a small number of major ticket agents”

Meanwhile, a Ticketmaster spokesperson told *IQ* that fans had been emailed yesterday (14 July) and informed they would receive full refunds. However, they declined to say if Ticketmaster or HeritageLive would be funding these payments. Ticketmaster also did not disclose when asked by *IQ* if it is owed ticket revenue from GCE Live.

HeritageLive told *IQ* the row “also speaks to a wider issue in the live events industry”, adding that “as an independent promoter, HeritageLive has little choice but to rely on a small number of major ticket agents to process transactions and safeguard customer funds”.

“When one of those ticket agents is slow to meet its obligations, it’s the fans and promoters who are left managing the fallout, despite having no control over how, or when refunds are issued.

The promoter confirmed that customers who purchased through Ticketmaster, AXS, Gigantic, Skiddle or Ticketline “should already have received their refund, or be in the process of receiving one”.

“We can only add how terribly sorry we are for all the grief that this has caused and apologise most sincerely for all the trouble people are facing gaining their refunds from See Tickets. However, we are glad to see that this is now being resolved,” HeritageLive concluded.
