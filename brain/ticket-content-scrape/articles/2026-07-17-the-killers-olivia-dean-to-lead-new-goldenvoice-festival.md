# The Killers, Olivia Dean to lead new Goldenvoice festival

## Summary

Goldenvoice is launching a new two-day beachside festival, Ocean Way Festival, in Santa Monica (Sept. 26-27), headlined by The Killers and Olivia Dean alongside Jack White, Khruangbin, and Sublime. The inaugural AEG Presents event expects up to 35,000 attendees, with tickets going on sale July 23 ($399+ for two-day GA, $794+ for premium pit) after a local-resident presale.

### Key Points
- New AEG Presents/Goldenvoice festival debuts Sept. 26-27 in Santa Monica.
- Headliners: The Killers, Olivia Dean; also Jack White, Khruangbin, Sublime, Alvvays, SG Lewis.
- Expected attendance up to 35,000; plans to run annually.
- Tickets on sale July 23 ($399+ GA, $794+ premium pit) after a July 22 local-resident presale.
- Separately: Australia's Fridayz Live festival is taking a year off, aiming to return in 2027.

## Transcript

Plus: Australia's Fridayz Live to take another year off, Austria's Snowbombing reveal first 2027 names, and SEMA Fest plots one-day event...

![The Killers & Olivia Dean](https://www.iqmagazine.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/07/The-Killers-Olivia-Dean.png)

The Killers & Olivia Dean

Leading the latest festival announcements, The Killers and Olivia Dean are set to lead Goldenvoice’s new Ocean Way Festival, set to make its debut in September in Santa Monica.

The two-day event, set for 26-27 September, is billed as the [first major festival](https://www.iqmagazine.com/2025/12/goldenvoice-to-launch-new-santa-monica-festival/) on the Los Angeles coastal city’s beach, bringing live music to one of the state’s most picturesque coastlines.

Alongside the headliners, Jack White, Khruangbin, Sublime, Durand Jones and the Indications, Alvvays, SG Lewis, and more are set to perform, with the event promising attendees a mixture of rock, soul, indie, and house across two stages for an all-ages event.

The inaugural event from the AEG Presents subdivision is expected to attract up to 35,000 people, with further plans to host the festival annually. It joins the likes of Coachella, Stagecoach, Head in the Clouds, Portola, and Camp Flog Gnaw in Goldenvoice’s festival portfolio.

“Santa Monica has an incredible music history, and Goldenvoice has been fortunate to be part of that story,” says Nic Adler, Goldenvoice’s VP of regional festivals.

“From legendary shows at the Civic Auditorium to the origins of Ocean Way Recording just steps from the beach, there’s a creative legacy here that’s unlike anywhere else. Ocean Way Festival is our opportunity to celebrate that history while building something that feels authentically Santa Monica for years to come,” he says.

Tickets go on sale next Thursday, 23 July, with a local residents presale launching the day prior. Two-day general admission passes start at $399 (€350), with premium pit passes starting at $794 (€654).

In another blow to Australia’s festival scene, the team behind **Fridayz Live** has announced the travelling festival will take a break this year, with the aim of returning in 2027.

It is the second time the festival has been called off in recent years, having gone on hiatus in 2024 before returning to [great acclaim](https://www.iqmagazine.com/2025/10/australias-fridayz-live-makes-triumphant-return/) last year with 160,000 tickets sold.

> “We can’t wait to show you what we’re working on when the time is right”

“In recent years, we’ve stepped away from an annual schedule,” organisers said. “After our 2025 series raised the bar, the goal was to return only when we could match that experience – and that is going to be in 2027.”

“We can’t wait to show you what we’re working on when the time is right. Fridayz is built on creating an unforgettable experience rather than a predictable one.”

Presented by Mushroom Group’s MG Live and Hit Network, last year’s lineup featured Mariah Carey, Pitbull, Wiz Khalifa, Lil Jon, Eve, Tinie Tempah, and Jordin Sparks, who sold out Brisbane’s Showgrounds, Sydney’s Engie Stadium, Perth’s Langley Park, and Melbourne’s Marvel Stadium on back-to-back weekends.

The news follows a similar pattern to that of Secret Sounds’ [Harvest Rock](https://www.iqmagazine.com/2026/07/festival-news-secret-sounds-harvest-rock-called-off-again/), which was recently called off for the second time in its five-year history. The two-day Adelaide event launched in 2022 but took a ‘strategic hiatus’ in 2024, only to return in 2025 and be called off again this year.

Austrian alpine music festival **Snowbombing** has started rolling out names for its 2027 event, to be led by Bicep and Fatboy Slim.

The Superstruct-backed event transforms the mountain town of Mayrhofen into a week-long music event, mixing world-class electronic music, alpine adventure, and après experiences in the scenic Austrian Alps.

> “SEMA Fest represents where our industry is headed”

Also appearing on the first wave is one of the UK’s Rossi, with many more artists to be announced in the coming months.

The festival will return from 5-10 April 2027, with full price passes available starting at £529 (€620).

And over in Las Vegas, **SEMA Fest** will return amidst the annual trade automotive aftermarket event, SEMA Show, on 6 November.

The Offspring are due to headline the one-day event, held at the Las Vegas Convention Center, and will be joined by Jimmy Eat World, 311, and The Alive.

SEMA Fest serves as the official “closing ceremony” of SEMA Week, transforming the world’s premier automotive trade show into a massive public celebration. Tickets are on sale for the event now.

“SEMA Fest represents where our industry is headed, at the crossroads of innovation, lifestyle, and cultural relevance,” says Tom Gattuso, SEMA VP of events.

“By bringing together the world’s premier automotive gathering with iconic music, immersive automotive experiences, and enthusiast access, we are creating a powerful platform that expands the reach of car culture, strengthens engagement with new audiences, and sends SEMA Week out on a high-energy, high-impact note.”
