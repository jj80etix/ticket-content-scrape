# Tour news: Elton John set for Mexico farewell

## Summary

A tour-news roundup covering several major artist announcements: Elton John's first Mexico shows in 14 years at the newly renovated 90,000-cap Estadio Banorte; Benson Boone's Australian arena dates; Garth Brooks teasing a "world tour" starting with two Indianapolis shows; Sam Smith's London Coliseum residency as part of a UK tour; Jonas Brothers' two-night Madison Square Garden stand marking Camp Rock's 20th anniversary; and Violet Grohl expanding her UK tour with a venue upgrade in Manchester.

### Key Points
- Elton John returns to Mexico City for two "farewell" shows (2-3 Oct) at the renovated, 90,000-cap Estadio Banorte, produced by AEG Presents, Cardenas Marketing Network, and Enso Music.
- Benson Boone's Wanted Man tour adds Australian arena dates in Brisbane, Melbourne, and Sydney (Sept-Nov).
- Garth Brooks announces two Indianapolis Gainbridge Fieldhouse dates (21-22 Aug) as the opening stop of a teased "world tour."
- Sam Smith's To Be Free UK tour includes an eight-night London Coliseum residency (8-19 Sept), promoted by SJM Concerts.
- Jonas Brothers announce a two-night Madison Square Garden stand (Live Nation-promoted) marking Camp Rock's 20th anniversary.
- Violet Grohl expands her UK tour, upgrading her Manchester show to the larger Band on the Wall venue due to demand.

## Transcript

Plus: Benson Boone to hit Australian arenas, Garth Brooks hints at 'world tour' after upcoming Indianapolis stand, Sam Smith set for London residency, and more

![](https://www.iqmagazine.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/07/Elton-John.png)

**Elton John** has confirmed he will return to Mexico City for what promoters call two “farewell” shows at Estadio Banorte on 2 and 3 October, leading the latest tour news round-up.

It will be the superstar’s first appearance in the country for 14 years, having played his first Mexican shows at the Azteca Stadium in 1992.

The Estadio Banorte [reopened in March](https://www.iqmagazine.com/2026/03/mexico-citys-estadio-banorte-reopens-after-e175m-refresh/) this year following a large-scale renovation which increased its capacity to 90,000. The singer said: “Mexico City has always held a special place in my heart”.

“I was truly disappointed that the pandemic kept me from touring Latin America during my *Farewell* tour which makes this return especially meaningful. I’m thrilled to finally share this very special moment with my fans after all these years.”

The performances, which will be produced by AEG Presents, Cardenas Marketing Network, and Enso Music, will follow a month after John is set to perform at Rock in Rio on 7 September.

In other tour news, **Benson Boone** will bring his *Wanted Man* tour to arenas in Australia in November, it was announced this week.

Three dates at the Brisbane Entertainment Centre will take place between 6 and 8 September, before three nights at Melbourne’s Rod Laver Arena on 12-14 November, and ending with Sydney’s Afterpay Arena between 17 and 19 November.

> “Going back into the arenas is about putting the stadium show in a box”

**Garth Brooks** has also revealed his *Blame it all on my Roots* tour will begin in August by revealing the run’s two opening arena dates. Though only two dates are so-far announced, Brooks referred to the run in a social media post as a “world tour”, with further dates expected to be announced soon.

Promoters also said the tour would mark “a full-scale return to the arenas that made him a legend”.

The only known dates so far are at Indianapolis’ Gainbridge Fieldhouse on 21 and 22 August. Brooks will also record the tour for a new live album, and the shows will feature end-on staging as well as in-the-round staging.

“Going back into the arenas is about putting the stadium show in a box,” says Brooks. “The excitement gets multiplied by the intimacy. Every seat is a great seat. This is personal,” Brooks said.

In the UK, **Sam Smith** has announced their *To be Free* UK tour which will include an eight-night London residency at theatre venue London Coliseum, promoted by SJM Concerts.

The run, which spans ten dates in total will begin with two shows on 3 and 4 September at Manchester’s Albert Hall, before heading to London for the residency that will run between 8 and 19 September.

The *To be Free* shows debuted with *To Be Free: New York* in Brooklyn’s Warsaw in October 2025, spanning 24-nights, before continuing to San Francisco, where Smith became one of the first artists to perform on the newly restored stage of the Castro Theatre.

This August will see Smith performing four dates of *To Be Free: Mexico City* at The Auditorio Nacional, before kicking off the UK run.

In the US, **Jonas** **Brothers** have announced a two-night stand at New York’s Madison Square Garden, as part of the *Burning Up Tour All Over Again,* promoted by Live Nation.

The shows are timed to mark the 20th anniversary of the *Camp Rock* film which marked a breakthrough for the band. The tour is a revival of the group’s 2008-2009 *Burning Up Tour* which followed the film.

“When we looked at everything happening this summer, it felt like the right moment to celebrate one of the most exciting times of our lives,” the band said.

Finally, back in the UK, **Violet Grohl** has expanded her tour of the country, including a venue upgrade for her Manchester show due to “overwhelming demand”.

The tour has also added a 8 October Brighton concert, in addition to upgrading the 25 August Manchester set to the 520-cap Band on the Wall Venue, having first been scheduled for the 260-cap Manchester Deaf Institute.
