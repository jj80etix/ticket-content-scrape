# Barcelona to support small venues with €1 million grant scheme

## Summary

Barcelona is launching the En Viu BCN program, a joint initiative from the city's Night Office and the Barcelona Institute of Culture (ICUB), allocating €1 million in grants to support more than 100 small live-music venues (under 250 capacity) across the city. Applications open in October, with awards up to €8,000 per venue, and the program also introduces a "seal of recognition" for qualifying venues.

### Key Points
- €1 million total funding pool for sub-250-capacity Barcelona venues, launching the En Viu BCN program.
- Run jointly by Barcelona's Night Office and the Barcelona Institute of Culture (ICUB).
- Applications open in October; awards up to €8,000 per venue, pro-rated if demand exceeds the pot.
- Eligibility requires at least 40 concerts/year, location in Barcelona, and under-250 capacity.
- New "seal of recognition" for participating venues, developed with ASACC and AMJM.
- Follows a separate ICUB grant round (March-April 2026) that distributed €270,000 to venues under 150-capacity.

Barcelona will allocate €1 million to support sub-250 capacity venues in the city, with over 100 performance spaces set to benefit.

The funding marks the launch of the En Viu BCN program, a joint project from the city’s Night Office and the Barcelona Institute of Culture (ICUB), and will be allocated through the City Council.

Subsidy applications can be submitted from October according to the dates established by the call, with a maximum available of up to €8,000 per venue. Requirements for applications include having a regular live music programme with at least 40 concerts a year, being located in the municipality of Barcelona, and having a capacity of under 250 spectators.

Applications will be awarded subsidies until the €1 million amount available is used up, though the City Council said in the event that subsidy applications exceed this total allocation, qualifying venues will all receive an equal share of the pot.

> “ Beyond their cultural function, these venues contribute to revitalising neighbourhood life”

The program will also create a “seal of recognition” for venues that offer live music in the city, which will be distributed by the Night Office.

The seal has been created in conjunction with the Association of Catalonian Concert Venues (ASACC) and the Catalonian Association of Jazz and Modern Musicians (AMJM). Organisers say the distinction “aims to help city venues engaging in \[live music\] to flourish and to get others on board”. The Night Office will also publish a list of awarded venues on its website.

“With this program, Barcelona reinforces institutional recognition of small-format venues, considered an essential part of the live music ecosystem for their ability to promote emerging talent, sustain a continuous program and bring culture closer to the different neighbourhoods of the city,” said APM, the association of Spanish promoters.

It added the program aims to recognise these venues’ “role as key drivers of creativity, consistent programming, and the development of emerging artists”.

“Beyond their cultural function, these venues contribute to revitalising neighbourhood life and maintaining a diverse and accessible musical offering,” APM said.

The funding programme comes after ICUB held separate applications for grant funding for music venues between March and April this year, which offered a total of €270,000 to venues, including those below 150-capacity.
