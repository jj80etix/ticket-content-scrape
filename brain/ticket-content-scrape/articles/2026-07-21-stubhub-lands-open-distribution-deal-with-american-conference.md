# StubHub Lands Open Distribution Deal With American Conference

## Summary

StubHub signed a three-year Open Distribution deal naming it the American Conference's official secondary-ticketing partner through June 2029, covering football, basketball, and other championship inventory — the first such deal with an NCAA Division I conference. It extends a run of similar StubHub partnerships (Athletes Unlimited Softball, Danny Wimmer Presents festivals, Chicago Sky) into college athletics.

### Key Points
- Three-year deal (through June 2029); StubHub becomes the conference's Official Open Distribution Partner.
- Model lets rights holders keep their existing primary ticketing platform while adding StubHub as a supplemental sales channel.
- Follows a string of 2026 StubHub Open Distribution deals (Athletes Unlimited, Danny Wimmer Presents, Chicago Sky).
- First Open Distribution partnership with a Division I conference, covering all member-school championship events.

### Industry Problem
Rights holders want incremental distribution reach and sell-through without handing over their entire primary ticketing relationship — a gap StubHub's "Open Distribution" wrapper is designed to fill across an expanding roster of sports properties.

### Proposed Solution
StubHub is positioning its resale marketplace as a bolt-on distribution layer rather than a replacement for primary ticketing; worth monitoring how far this "coexist with the incumbent" model spreads into properties Etix serves, since it reframes resale as complementary infrastructure rather than a competitor to primary platforms.

StubHub is expanding its push into direct ticket distribution through a three-year agreement with the American Conference, which the [parties describe as the first Open Distribution partnership involving an NCAA Division I athletic conference](https://theamerican.org/news/2026/7/19/general-american-conference-stubhub-announce-first-ever-open-distribution-partnership-in-college-sports.aspx).

The sponsorship and ticketing agreement names StubHub the conference’s Official Open Distribution Partner through June 2029. Championship ticket inventory for football, men’s and women’s basketball and other conference sports will be connected with StubHub’s marketplace under the arrangement.

Unlike an exclusive primary ticketing contract, StubHub’s Open Distribution model is intended to provide rights holders with an additional sales channel while allowing them to preserve their existing ticketing relationships and broader distribution strategy.

StubHub will be integrated into the conference’s championship ticket-sales operation, including through links and promotion on TheAmerican.org, conference digital and social channels and signage at football and basketball championship events. The company will also provide distribution, pricing and sales-strategy services intended to increase ticket sell-through and conference revenue.

That allows a rights holder to reach additional buyers without turning over its entire ticketing operation or signing an exclusive primary-ticketing agreement.

Earlier this month, StubHub and viagogo announced an integration with white-label ticketing provider TicketSocket. That deal allows TicketSocket clients to distribute inventory through the two marketplaces while maintaining their existing ticketing systems, branded purchasing environments and control over customer relationships. Inventory is synchronized between the platforms to reduce the risk that the same ticket will be sold through multiple channels.

StubHub has also been applying the strategy through direct partnerships with sports properties and event producers.

In June, the company became the Official Open Distribution Partner and Official Ticket Marketplace of the Athletes Unlimited Softball League. Tickets can be offered simultaneously through the league’s primary platform and StubHub, providing AUSL with an additional channel as the professional women’s softball league expands into six home markets.

StubHub also secured its largest festival partnership to date with Danny Wimmer Presents, becoming the producer’s Official Open Distribution Partner across a portfolio that includes Welcome To Rockville, Sonic Temple, Aftershock, Bourbon & Beyond and Louder Than Life.

A separate multi-year agreement with the WNBA’s Chicago Sky named StubHub the team’s Official Open Distribution Partner and Official Ticket Resale Marketplace, adding another prominent women’s sports property to the company’s partnership roster.

The American Conference deal extends that strategy into college athletics and gives StubHub a relationship covering championship events across an entire conference, rather than an individual team or venue.

The conference includes 14 full or football-only member institutions and sponsors 20 sports. It was known as the American Athletic Conference before adopting the shortened American Conference name in 2025.
