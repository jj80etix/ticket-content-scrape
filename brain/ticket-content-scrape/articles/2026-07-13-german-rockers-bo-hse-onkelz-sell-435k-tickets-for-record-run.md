# German rockers Böhse Onkelz sell 435k tickets for record run

## Summary

German rock band Böhse Onkelz wrapped their 11-date "Mitten Unter Euch" stadium tour having sold over 435,000 tickets, setting attendance records across German and Austrian stadiums. The tour, promoted by DEAG subsidiaries LiveGeist Entertainment and Wizard Live, used an in-the-round production with eight rotating 25-metre towers. It's a follow-up to the band's 45th-anniversary arena run in 2025 and adds to DEAG's record year (12M+ tickets sold, €490M revenue, +33% YoY).

### Key Points
- 435,000+ tickets sold across 11 dates from 10 June to 11 July 2026, spanning Leipzig, Vienna, Nuremberg, Frankfurt, Oberhausen, and Gelsenkirchen.
- Over 400,000 tickets sold on the first day of general on-sale.
- Stadium attendance records set in nearly every city, including two shows at the 50,000-cap Red Bull Arena and a closing show at the 80,000-cap Veltins-Arena.
- Novel in-the-round stage: eight 25-metre towers around a rotating octagonal platform, rotating every two songs.
- Tour promoted by DEAG subsidiaries LiveGeist Entertainment and Wizard Live (fully acquired by DEAG earlier in 2026).
- DEAG reported over 12 million tickets sold last financial year and €490M revenue, up 33%+ from 2024.

## Transcript

With the tour, a collaboration between LiveGeist Entertainment and Wizard Live, the veteran band set attendance records in several German stadiums

German rock veterans Böhse Onkelz wrapped up their *Mitten Unter Euch Tour* with over 435,000 tickets sold, alongside a handful of attendance records due to an ambitious in-the-round stadium production.

The 11-date *In Your Midst* outing, promoted by LiveGeist Entertainment GmbH, launched on 10 June in Leipzig, Germany, with a warm-up show at Haus Auensee followed by two record-breaking shows at Red Bull Arena (cap. 50,000). It wrapped up just over a month later, on Saturday (11 July) at Gelsenkirchen’s Veltins-Arena, which can accommodate up to 80,000 people.

Along the way, the band played a mix of intimate shows and blockbuster stadium dates, also making stops in Vienna, Nuremberg, Frankfurt, and Oberhausen, with the rockers setting stadium attendance records in nearly every city.

“What made this tour so special was not only the scale of the stage, the height of the towers or the number of fans in attendance,” says Marco Matthes, band spokesperson. “It was the willingness of the four Onkelz to take on something entirely new after 46 years as a band.”

That willingness was well-received from the outset, with over 400,000 tickets sold on the first day of general sale alone, demonstrating the power of the German-speaking rockers in their core markets.

For the stadium performances, the band utilised an in-the-round production for the largest outing in their nearly five-decade career, which helped boost attendance in the German-speaking markets. It was comprised of eight 25-metre towers surrounding an octagonal platform, which rotated every two songs.

> “Böhse Onkelz challenged themselves with an entirely new stage concept”

The tour was a combined effort by Deutsche Entertainment AG (DEAG) subsidiaries LiveGeist and Wizard Live, the latter of which was [fully acquired earlier this year](https://www.iqmagazine.com/2026/01/deag-finalises-wizard-live-takeover/).

“It takes courage for a band to reinvent itself after 46 years,” Oliver Hoppe, MD of Wizard Live, says. “Böhse Onkelz challenged themselves with an entirely new stage concept, and 435,000 fans responded by filling stadiums across Germany and Austria. There is no stronger endorsement than that.”

In 2025, the band celebrated their 45th anniversary with 17 arena shows across Germany, Austria, and Switzerland, with this year’s outing a notable step up for the storied group.

Hoppe adds: “Record attendance at every German stadium show. Eleven performances across two countries. Not a single cancellation. That’s not luck. It’s the result of meticulous planning, experience, and an outstanding team.”

The successful tour will add to DEAG’s continued success in the live entertainment space. Last financial year, DEAG reported it had sold over [12 million tickets](https://www.iqmagazine.com/2026/04/deag-raises-revenue-by-a-third/) for the first time, posting a year-end revenue of €490 million (33%+ growth from 2024).
