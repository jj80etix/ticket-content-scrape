# WWE Survivor Series: WarGames Heads to Houston’s Daikin Park for 40th Anniversary

## Summary

WWE will bring Survivor Series: WarGames to Houston's Daikin Park on November 28 for the event's 40th anniversary, marking its first Houston appearance since 2017. General on-sale is Friday, August 7 via Ticketmaster, preceded by a general presale on August 6 and Club WWE early access on August 5. Premium hospitality packages will be sold separately through On Location.

### Key Points
- Survivor Series: WarGames comes to Houston's Daikin Park on Saturday, Nov. 28, 2026 — the event's 40th edition.
- It is WWE's first Houston Survivor Series since 2017 and first Premium Live Event at Daikin Park since the 2020 Royal Rumble.
- General public on-sale begins Friday, Aug. 7 at 11 a.m. ET via Ticketmaster; general presale starts Aug. 6, Club WWE early access starts Aug. 5.
- Last year's WarGames at Petco Park (San Diego) reportedly set gate and attendance records for the event.
- On Location will offer Premium Survivor Series Pass packages with premium seating and hospitality; deposits are open now.
- Houston First and the Houston Astros are event partners; Daikin Park is the Astros' home stadium.

## Transcript

![](https://www.ticketnews.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/07/wwe-survivor-series.jpg)

WWE is bringing one of its biggest annual events back to Houston for a milestone celebration.

Survivor Series: WarGames will take over Daikin Park on Saturday, Nov. 28, marking the 40th edition of Survivor Series and the event’s first visit to Houston since 2017.

Tickets head on sale to the general public Friday, Aug. 7, at 11 a.m. ET/10 a.m. CT through [Ticketmaster](https://www.ticketmaster.com/wwe-tickets/artist/807358?camefrom=CFC_WWE_PR_20260713). A general presale begins Thursday, Aug. 6, while Club WWE members will receive early access beginning Wednesday, Aug. 5. Fans can register for presale information through WWE’s Survivor Series [website](https://www.wwe.com/survivor-series-2026).

“Houston is the perfect host city to celebrate this milestone chapter in Survivor Series’ storied history, and we look forward to partnering with Houston First and the Houston Astros to deliver an unforgettable event,” WWE Chief Content Officer Paul “Triple H” Levesque said in a press release.

The event will mark the second consecutive year Survivor Series: WarGames has been staged inside a major sports stadium. Last year’s edition at San Diego’s Petco Park reportedly set new gate and attendance records for the event.

It will also be WWE’s first Premium Live Event at Daikin Park since Royal Rumble in 2020.

“We are proud to be able to help bring one of the world’s premier entertainment brands to Houston for what promises to be an unforgettable holiday weekend,” Michael Heckman, president and CEO of Houston First, said in a statement. “Hosting WWE Survivor Series will bring thousands of passionate fans to our city and generate a significant economic impact for community. Houston has earned a reputation for delivering world-class entertainment events, and we look forward to welcoming the WWE Universe to our destination.”

Daikin Park, home of the Houston Astros, has hosted a growing slate of major sporting and entertainment events alongside its Major League Baseball schedule.

Rachel Quan, vice president of special events for the Houston Astros, said that this will mark “just the second time this world class event will be held in a Major League stadium.”

“The Houston Astros and Daikin Park take great pride in showcasing our city through big events – from concerts, to the World Baseball Classic and beyond,” Quan added. “Having a WWE event in our park for the first time since 2020 is another special event our team is excited to bring to our ballpark and our city.”

Premium Survivor Series Pass packages will also be offered through [On Location](https://onlocationexp.com/survivorseries). Packages are expected to include premium seating, pre-show hospitality featuring WWE Superstar appearances, ringside photo opportunities and other perks. Fans can place deposits now for early package access.

Houston has long been one of WWE’s major event markets. The city previously hosted WrestleMania in 2001 and 2009, Royal Rumble in 1989 and 2020, and Survivor Series in 2017, along with numerous episodes of Monday Night Raw and Friday Night SmackDown.
