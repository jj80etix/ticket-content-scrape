# Live Nation Takes Festival Building to Roblox With Music Festival Tycoon

## Summary

Live Nation has partnered with gaming studio Livewire to launch "Music Festival Tycoon," a Roblox experience where players book artists, design stages and build their own virtual music festivals, modeled on the 200+ real festivals Live Nation produces. The game has already logged 3.5 million-plus visits and 23 million-plus in-game performances, and it links directly to real-world programming: K-pop group CORTIS is the platform's first featured artist (timed to its Lollapalooza debut), and Lollapalooza itself is the first real festival rendered in-game, complete with a branded stage, mascot and merch stand. Live Nation frames the game as much as a sponsorship/brand-activation channel as a fan-engagement product.

### Key Points
- Roblox experience "Music Festival Tycoon," built with Livewire, has logged 3.5 million-plus visits and 23 million-plus in-game performances since launch.
- Players handle lineup booking, stage design, merchandise and fan activations for their own virtual festivals.
- CORTIS is the platform's first featured artist, timed to the group's real-world U.S. festival debut at Lollapalooza.
- Lollapalooza is the first real festival integrated in-game, including its main stage, lollipop mascot and Chicago-style hot dog vendor.
- Live Nation is positioning the game as a new channel for brand sponsorships — branded virtual stages, virtual merchandise and creator collaborations.
- The initiative comes from Live Nation Media & Sponsorship (with Roblox's music-partnerships team), placing it in marketing/fan-engagement rather than core ticketing operations.

### Highlights
- Live Nation (Ticketmaster's parent) is investing in gamified, pre-onsale fan engagement rather than a ticketing-checkout feature — a different kind of competitive "shipping" than a fee-model or redesign change.
- 3.5M+ visits / 23M+ in-game performances is a meaningful early-adoption signal for a fan-acquisition funnel tied directly to a real onsale (Lollapalooza/CORTIS).
- The sponsorship-activation angle (branded virtual stages/merch) suggests Live Nation is testing a new revenue line, not just a marketing spend.

### Industry Problem
Major promoters compete for fan attention and pre-onsale engagement long before tickets go on sale, and conventional marketing channels don't create the kind of habitual, gamified engagement that keeps a fanbase warm between a lineup announcement and the actual sale.

### Proposed Solution
Live Nation's answer is a branded Roblox game that turns festival-building into a habitual, pre-onsale engagement loop tied directly to real festivals and sponsors. The actionable takeaway for Etix isn't building a metaverse game, but borrowing the mechanic at a lighter weight — e.g., a simple interactive "build your lineup" or festival-planner feature on client event pages that captures fan interest/wishlists ahead of onsale, feeding demand-forecasting and conversion signal back to venue and promoter clients.

Live Nation is taking festival production into the virtual world.

The concert giant has teamed with Livewire to launch **Music Festival Tycoon**, a new Roblox experience that lets players book artists, build stages and create their own music festivals.

The game is already drawing a crowd, with more than 3.5 million visits and over 23 million in-game performances to-date. Inspired by the more than 200 festivals Live Nation produces worldwide, Music Festival Tycoon gives players control of the entire festival experience, from lineup building and stage design to merchandise and fan activations.

The platform’s first featured artist is CORTIS, while Lollapalooza becomes the first real-world festival to enter the game. CORTIS will headline virtual performances, appear through exclusive member-inspired avatars and receive a dedicated stage that players can add to their own festivals. The rollout coincides with the group’s U.S. festival debut at Lollapalooza.
