# Open Championship sets new attendance record

## Summary

The Open Championship at Royal Birkdale drew a record 306,000 attendees across its four days, surpassing the previous benchmark of 290,000 set at St Andrews in 2022. New Zealand's Ryan Fox won the Claret Jug by one stroke over Cameron Young, becoming the country's fourth major champion. Home favorite Tommy Fleetwood finished joint-fourth, praising the crowd atmosphere.

### Key Points
- Royal Birkdale (near Liverpool) hosted The Open Championship, golf's oldest major.
- Attendance hit 306,000 over four days, a new record beating 2022's 290,000 at St Andrews.
- Ryan Fox won by one stroke over Cameron Young, becoming New Zealand's fourth major winner.
- Tommy Fleetwood finished joint-fourth at seven under par alongside Scottie Scheffler.
- Royal Birkdale has hosted The Open 11 times since 1954 and the Women's British Open six times.

## Transcript

![TheTicketingBusiness News](https://www.theticketingbusiness.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/04/TBN-LOGO-reversed-white.png)

TheTicketingBusiness News

**The Open Championship, held at Royal Birkdale near Liverpool, set a new attendance record of 306,000 for golf’s oldest major.**

The four-day event, which concluded yesterday (July 19), beat the previous benchmark of 290,000 set in 2022 at St Andrews in Scotland.

Ryan Fox secured the Claret Jug by one stroke over American Cameron Young to become New Zealand’s fourth major winner alongside Bob Charles, Michael Campbell and Lydia Ko.

Home favourite Tommy Fleetwood finished in joint-fourth place alongside Scottie Scheffler of the USA with seven under par.

“The eighth was pretty emotional,” Fleetwood said.

“It was just very, very special. There’s only one person that gets to win it, and my dream of winning The Open will carry on and I’ll continue to chase it.

“But stuff like that, walking down 18 and the atmosphere out there, that was beyond stuff that I’ve dreamt of.

“That was just stuff that I never imagined. The crowds were amazing, and they made it the most special week ever for me. I can’t thank them enough.”

Royal Birkdale has hosted the Open 11 times since 1954 and is also in the rotation to stage the Women’s British Open, which it has held on six occasions.
