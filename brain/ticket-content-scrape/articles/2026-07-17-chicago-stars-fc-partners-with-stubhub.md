# Chicago Stars FC partners with StubHub

## Summary

Chicago Stars FC (NWSL) has named StubHub its official fan marketplace and Open Distribution partner for the rest of the 2026 season, letting the club sell primary tickets through StubHub's marketplace and audience reach while retaining its own brand, data, and fan relationships. It's StubHub's latest women's-sports deal, following existing partnerships with the Chicago Sky and Athletes Unlimited.

### Key Points
- Deal covers primary ticket distribution through StubHub's Open Distribution product plus its resale marketplace.
- Club retains control of brand, data, and fan relationships under the Open Distribution model.
- Runs through the NWSL season's Nov. 21 conclusion.
- Extends StubHub's existing footprint in women's sports (Chicago Sky, Athletes Unlimited).

### Highlights
- Open Distribution lets a secondary marketplace sell primary inventory while the rights holder keeps first-party data, blurring the primary/secondary line.
- Third StubHub women's-sports partnership named in the piece, suggesting a deliberate category push.

### Industry Problem
Rights holders want secondary marketplaces' reach and marketing scale for primary sales without giving up first-party fan data or brand control, a tension StubHub's Open Distribution is explicitly designed to resolve.

### Proposed Solution
Worth monitoring whether StubHub's Open Distribution model gains further traction with primary-rights clients, since it directly competes with the value proposition of a full-service primary platform like Etix (audience reach plus data ownership in one place).

TheTicketingBusiness News

**Chicago Stars FC has named StubHub its official fan marketplace and open distribution partner for the remainder of the 2026 National Women’s Soccer League season.**

The partnership aims to provide fans with greater access and flexibility when purchasing tickets to Chicago Stars matches at Martin Stadium in Evanston for the rest of the current campaign, which concludes on November 21.

“Women’s soccer is experiencing unprecedented growth, and our responsibility is to ensure that every fan who wants to be part of that movement has an easy and convenient way to access our matches,” said Chicago Stars FC vice president of partnerships Sara Arnold.

“StubHub has demonstrated a meaningful commitment to investing in women’s sports and expanding opportunities for fans to engage with their favourite teams.

“We’re excited to partner with an organisation that shares our vision for growing the game and creating memorable experiences for supporters across Chicago and beyond.”

The agreement sees StubHub’s presence across women’s sports grow, following existing partnerships with organisations including the Chicago Sky and Athletes Unlimited.

Through StubHub’s marketplace and open distribution network, the Stars hope to reach new audiences and provide supporters with additional pathways to experience the NWSL in person.

“Open Distribution lets the Chicago Stars use StubHub’s marketing reach and audience to sell tickets while keeping control of their brand, data and fan relationships,” said Alexa Fuentes, director of soccer partnerships at StubHub.

“Women’s soccer is one of the fastest-growing categories on our platform, and we’re proud to help the Stars turn that momentum into fans in seats at Martin Stadium.”
