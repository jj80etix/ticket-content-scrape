# Detroit City FC Breaks Ground On AlumniFi Field

## Summary

Detroit City FC broke ground on AlumniFi Field, a $150M+, 15,000-seat, privately funded soccer stadium anchoring a $200M mixed-use development on the former Southwest Detroit Hospital site, targeting a 2028 opening.

### Key Points
- Michigan's only privately owned major sports stadium; nearly 6,000 seats already deposited.
- Project includes affordable housing, a parking garage, and $2.27M in community benefits.
- Targeted to open in 2028.

Detroit City FC, Mayor Mary Sheffield and project partners broke ground July 15 on AlumniFi Field, the HOK-designed, 15,000-seat stadium rising on the site of the former Southwest Detroit Hospital.

The $150 million-plus venue will be the city’s first soccer-specific stadium and the state’s only privately owned major sports stadium. The stadium anchors a $200 million mixed-use development designed to make the under-utilized area a year-round sports and entertainment destination. The project also includes a 421-space parking garage and a 104-unit residential building with 76 affordable housing units.

“As a club built from the ground up by Detroiters, this moment reflects years of work, belief and investment from our supporters and partners,” said Sean Mann, co-founder and CEO of Detroit City FC at the groundbreaking ceremony. “Breaking ground on AlumniFi Field brings us one step closer to creating a forever home for our supporters and a development that reflects the pride, history and future of this city.”

The groundbreaking follows the Detroit City Council’s unanimous approval of the project in November 2025. The project includes more than $2.27 million in community benefits, among them $1.2 million for neighborhood organizations, 3,000 free tickets for neighbors each year, a $17-an-hour minimum wage, local hiring priorities and a $200,000 revolving loan fund for small businesses.

Supporters have already placed deposits on nearly 6,000 seats ahead of the stadium’s planned 2028 opening.

The development will also honor the legacy of Southwest Detroit Hospital, which opened in 1973 as the first hospital in Detroit to accredit African American doctors and nurses. Artistic installations, historical markers and storytelling elements will be incorporated throughout the site.
