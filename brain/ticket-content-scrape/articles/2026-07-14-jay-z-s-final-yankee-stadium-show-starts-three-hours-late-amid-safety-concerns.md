# Jay-Z’s Final Yankee Stadium Show Starts Three Hours Late Amid Safety Concerns

## Summary

Jay-Z's third and final Yankee Stadium concert began nearly three hours late after roughly 10,000 people without tickets gathered outside, with some breaching security checkpoints and pushing past ticketholders. Stadium gates were closed while NYPD and security stabilized the scene before reopening them; Jay-Z addressed the crowd after taking the stage past midnight. The show ultimately ran until nearly 3 a.m. with numerous surprise guests including Beyoncé, Rihanna, Usher, and Pharrell.

### Key Points
- Roughly 10,000 ticketless people gathered outside the stadium; some breached security checkpoints and pushed past legitimate ticketholders.
- All stadium gates were closed for an extended period while NYPD and security worked to stabilize the situation before reopening them.
- Jay-Z addressed the crowd directly, explaining the delay was for fan safety, and performed until nearly 3 a.m.
- The show closed a three-night run marking the 25th and 30th anniversaries of The Blueprint and Reasonable Doubt.

Jay-Z’s final concert at Yankee Stadium began nearly three hours late Sunday night after groups of people without tickets overwhelmed entrances and caused officials to temporarily close the venue’s gates.

The rapper addressed the delay after taking the stage shortly after midnight Monday morning. He told the crowd that the decision was made to protect fans inside and outside the stadium.

“Let me explain the delay to you guys. It was like 10,000 people outside, and we closed all the doors, and somebody rushed the door,” Jay-Z said. “They closed the door for you guys’ safety and everyone’s safety outside.”

Jay-Z said he did not want the concert to begin while such a large crowd remained gathered near the entrances.

“There’s 10,000 people outside; I don’t want to start the music and people get trampled,” he said. “I’m really sorry for the inconvenience, but I had to make sure everyone was OK. I appreciate your patience.”

The New York Yankees, Roc Nation and Live Nation released a joint statement providing more details about the incident. The organizations said hundreds of people who did not have tickets arrived in large groups and pushed past legitimate ticketholders at several stadium entrances.

Some individuals also breached security checkpoints, according to the statement. Officials closed all stadium gates for an extended period while security personnel and the New York Police Department worked to stabilize the situation.

Additional guests included Jermaine Dupri, Fat Joe, Jadakiss, Clipse, Jeezy and Swizz Beatz.

The concert closed Jay-Z’s three-night run at Yankee Stadium. The opening shows included appearances from Beyoncé, Blue Ivy Carter, Nas, Alicia Keys, Eminem, Slick Rick and Pharrell.

The weekend celebrated the 25th anniversary of *The Blueprint a* nd the 30th anniversary of Jay-Z’s debut album, *Reasonable Doubt*. The concerts marked his first major run of performances since the “4:44 Tour” in 2017.

Jay-Z is scheduled to perform September 4 at Tottenham Hotspur Stadium in London, September 10 at Stade de France in Paris and October 23 at SoFi Stadium in Inglewood, California.
