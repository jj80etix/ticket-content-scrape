# New York State Liquor Authority Drops Suit Against MSG Over Facial Recognition Technology, Issues $30K Fine

## Summary

The New York State Liquor Authority has dropped its multi-year suit against Madison Square Garden Entertainment over venue access, settling instead for a $30,000 fine ($10,000 each against MSG, Radio City Music Hall, and the Beacon Theatre) tied to an unrelated "unauthorized corporate-change" violation. The SLA said the resolution is not an endorsement of MSG's facial-recognition or exclusion practices and that it may still review premises-access rules going forward. The settlement comes as MSG separately pursues a lawsuit against WIRED over a report on an internal risk-database system and fended off a since-dismissed class action tied to a data breach of its surveillance systems.

### Key Points
- The SLA's original 2022 case alleged MSG violated liquor-license rules requiring public access by barring attorneys from firms litigating against MSG.
- Under the settlement, MSG, Radio City, and the Beacon Theatre each pay the maximum $10,000 penalty for an "unauthorized corporate-change charge" unrelated to the facial-recognition dispute.
- SLA spokesperson Jade Kraft said the resolution is not an endorsement of any exclusion policy or facial-recognition practice, and the agency continues reviewing whether its rules should address premises-access practices.
- An MSG spokesperson said the fine "pertains to an administrative error and has nothing to do with MSG's biometric practices."
- MSG sued WIRED and its journalists over a report alleging an internal database categorized celebrities and other guests by sexual orientation, race, and perceived risk.
- A separate proposed class action over an alleged data breach tied to MSG's surveillance/facial-recognition systems was voluntarily dismissed July 1 before its claims were adjudicated.

James Dolan, the executive chair of the Knicks and CEO of Madison Square Garden, has received a slap-on-the-wrist and a $30,000 fine by the New York State Liquor Authority over MSG’s use of facial recognition technology to track and remove potential threats from the premises.

Dolan has been at odds with the New York State Liquor Authority [since 2022](https://www.ticketnews.com/2023/03/msg-faces-liquor-ban-tax-issues-over-facial-recognition-fight/); the authority claimed Dolan violated state rules that require liquor license holders to allow public access to their venues by [barring attorneys](https://www.ticketnews.com/2023/01/ny-lawmakers-ag-warn-msg-over-its-continued-ban-of-lawyers/) at firms that were litigating against MSG.

According to , those charges were dropped last week as part of an agreement with MSG, Radio City Music Hall, and the Beacon Theatre — which are owned and operated by MSG Entertainment. Liquor Authority spokesperson Jade Kraft told the publication that all three venues will pay the maximum allowed penalty of $10,000 each for “unauthorized corporate-change charge.”

Kraft added that the access charges “should not be read as an endorsemment of any exclusion policy, facial-recognition practice, or admission practice” and does not prevent the agency from reviewing future complaints. Separately, the agency is reviewing whether its rules should “address licensed-premises access pracitces,” Kraft said.
