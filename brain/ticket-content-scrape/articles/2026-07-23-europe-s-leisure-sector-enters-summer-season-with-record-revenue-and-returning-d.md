# Europe’s leisure sector enters summer season with record revenue and returning demand

## Summary

European leisure and attraction operators posted record revenue in the second quarter of 2026, according to booking-platform Bookingkit's Leisure Market Index. Ticket volumes and prices both climbed through spring and into summer, with Bookingkit urging operators to extend spring offers, keep all sales channels active, and open availability deep into the season to capture the momentum.

### Key Points
- Q2 2026 was Bookingkit's strongest quarter on record for its Leisure Market Index, surpassing both 2024 and 2025, with May and June each setting new monthly highs.
- Ticket sales volume overtook 2025 levels in Q2 but has not yet caught up to the historic 2024 peak.
- Price per ticket rose to +4.3% year-on-year in Q2, rebounding after cooling through 2025 and dipping near zero in Q1 2026.
- Bookingkit CEO Lukas Hempel said revenue, visitor numbers, and prices are all rising simultaneously for the first time in a while.
- Bookingkit's recommendation to operators: extend strong spring offers into summer, keep every sales channel live, and open availability deep into the season.

TheTicketingBusiness News

**European leisure providers have entered the summer in their strongest position in a number of years, according to data collated by booking and administration solution Bookingkit.**

Results for June and the full second quarter of 2026 surpassed 2024 and 2025, with Q2 setting a new revenue record. Ticket sales edged past 2025 following a recovery, and price per ticket returned to +4.3% year-on-year after a slower start to the year.

According to Bookingkit, the second quarter of this year is the strongest it has recorded as part of its Leisure Market Index. After a steady first quarter, revenue overtook both 2024 and 2025, with May and June each setting a new monthly high.

The company suggests that operators utilise this momentum, by extending their best spring offers into summer, keep every sales channel live, and open availability deep into the season.

Ticket sales climbed steadily through spring, matching prior-year levels right as summer began. In the second quarter, volumes overtook 2025 but have not yet caught up to the historic 2024 record. Bookingkit said this is where the opportunity is – the demand is there, but now needs to be converted into sales.

However, price per ticket rose sharply again in the second quarter to +4.3% after cooling through 2025 and dipping to near zero in the first quarter of 2026. With the summer season now in full swing, prices and visitor numbers are continuing to climb.

“The second quarter was our strongest ever, and the decisive weeks are still ahead. For the first time in a while, revenue, visitor numbers and prices are all rising at once,” said Lukas Hempel, founder and chief executive of Bookingkit.

“That is the best possible position an operator could ask for at the start of the season. Those who are set up cleanly now, with offering, availability and pricing under control, will win the season. The demand is there, prices are supportive, and the task now is to use both levers with discipline.”
