# Oak View Group and Live Nation strike Saudi stadium joint venture

## Summary

Oak View Group (OVG) and Live Nation have formed Radia, a new joint venture with Saudi Arabia's Public Investment Fund (via its SURJ Sports Investment arm) to build and operate stadium infrastructure across the Kingdom, spanning both sports and concerts. The venture, to be led by CEO Xavier Campbell, builds on Live Nation's presence in Saudi Arabia since 2017 and targets projects including the under-construction Prince Mohammed bin Salman Stadium in Qiddiya. It marks continued expansion of major Western live-entertainment and venue operators into the Gulf region's entertainment infrastructure buildout.

### Key Points
- New joint venture named Radia, formed by Oak View Group, Live Nation, and Saudi PIF's SURJ Sports Investment company.
- Xavier Campbell named CEO of Radia; OVG CEO Chris Granger and Live Nation EMEA chairman John Reid both quoted on the deal.
- Live Nation says it has invested and operated in Saudi Arabia since 2017.
- Target infrastructure includes the under-construction 47,000-capacity Prince Mohammed bin Salman Stadium in Qiddiya, part of the Vision 2030 megaproject in Riyadh.
- Concerts are described as "key" to Radia's strategy alongside sporting events and existing-venue servicing.

### Highlights
- OVG, Live Nation, and Saudi PIF's SURJ Sports Investment jointly launched "Radia" to build and service stadium infrastructure across Saudi Arabia.
- The venture spans both sport and concert programming, with Xavier Campbell as CEO and targets like the 47,000-cap Prince Mohammed bin Salman Stadium.
- Live Nation frames this as an extension of a Saudi commitment dating to 2017, not a one-off deal.
- Two of the largest global venue/promotion operators are combining forces with a sovereign wealth fund to control a new national venue portfolio.

### Industry Problem
This reflects continued vertical and geographic consolidation among the dominant live-entertainment conglomerates (Live Nation, OVG), extending their venue-ownership and promotion reach into a new international market — the kind of scale and market concentration independent ticketing platforms compete against globally.

### Proposed Solution
The item itself proposes no ticketing product, only infrastructure investment; for Etix, the signal is to monitor whether Radia's Saudi venues get tied into Live Nation/Ticketmaster ticketing exclusivity as they open, since that would preview how the majors extend venue lock-in into emerging markets, and to watch for any independent-venue relationships that emerge as new operators enter outside the existing Ticketmaster ecosystem.

Oak View Group (OVG) and Live Nation have announced a new joint venture with the Saudi Public Investment Fund (PIF) to support the growth of stadium infrastructure in the Kingdom, with a view to boosting both live music and sporting facilities in the country.

The venture will be known as Radia, and comes as a result of a partnership between the two live industry giants and the PIF’s SURJ Sports Investment company. It will aim to grow Saudi Arabia’s portfolio of “world-class stadiums” as well as servicing existing infrastructure, the companies said.

“Sports and live entertainment have the unique ability to bring people together, inspire communities, and create lasting memories,” OVG CEO Chris Granger told *IQ.*

He continued: “The opportunity in Saudi Arabia is unlike anywhere else in the world, and we’re proud to partner with SURJ and Live Nation to help create a world-class, multi-purpose destination that can host a diverse range of experiences—from international sporting events and global concert tours to community programming and beyond.”

> “our complementary expertise creates a unique opportunity to develop the next generation of venues”

Meanwhile, John Reid, Live Nation’s chairman of EMEA, said: “Live Nation has been working and investing in Saudi Arabia since 2017, and this partnership builds on that long-term commitment. By bringing our expertise in programming venue operations and live events, we’ll help build venues and experiences that work for artists, athletes, audiences, and the wider industry.”

OVG’s Granger added the companies’ “complementary expertise” will allow Radia to develop the next generation of venues, and the infrastructure which he said is “needed to deliver unparalleled fan experiences, welcome the world’s biggest events, support touring artists, expand opportunities locally, and fuel the continued growth of Saudi Arabia’s live entertainment ecosystem”.

Radia will be led by Xavier Campbell, a seasoned sporting executive now named CEO of the new venture. While the company launches with an explicit focus on sport, *IQ* understands concerts are also central to the strategy behind developing the Kingdom’s multi-purpose stadiums.

Saudi Arabia has made its ambitious infrastructure plans clear over recent years with a series of entertainment and event projects announced or rumoured. Among the long-touted venues are the under-construction 47,000-cap Prince Mohammed bin Salman Stadium located in Qiddiya, an entertainment and tourism megaproject in Riyadh unveiled as part of the Vision 2030 masterplan.
