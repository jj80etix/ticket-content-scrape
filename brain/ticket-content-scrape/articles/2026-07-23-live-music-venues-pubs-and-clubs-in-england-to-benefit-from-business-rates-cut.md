# Live music venues, pubs and clubs in England to benefit from business rates cut

## Summary

Pubs, clubs, and live music venues in England will get a 20% cut to business rates starting April 2027, new UK Prime Minister Andy Burnham announced, on top of an existing 15% relief already in place for 2026/27. The roughly £100m-a-year package is expected to save the typical pub about £1,100 next year and will benefit nearly 32,000 pubs, clubs, and live music venues, though the largest venues are excluded. Music Venue Trust and the Night Time Industries Association welcomed the move but called for the eligibility criteria to be widened to cover all grassroots venues, including in Wales, Scotland, and Northern Ireland.

### Key Points
- 20% business rates cut for pubs, clubs, and live music venues in England begins April 2027.
- It stacks on top of an existing 15% relief for the 2026/27 bill announced in January.
- The package is worth about £100m a year and is expected to save the typical pub roughly £1,100 next year.
- Nearly 32,000 pubs, clubs, and live music venues are expected to benefit; the largest live music venues are excluded.
- Funding comes from reviewing reliefs for businesses like vape shops and cracking down on online marketplace sellers avoiding tax obligations.
- Music Venue Trust CEO Mark Davyd called it "an encouraging first step" but flagged implementation gaps and urged wider eligibility and matching support from Wales, Scotland, and Northern Ireland.
- Night Time Industries Association CEO Michael Kill welcomed the inclusion of clubs alongside pubs and music venues.

## Transcript

![TheTicketingBusiness News](https://www.theticketingbusiness.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/04/TBN-LOGO-reversed-white.png)

TheTicketingBusiness News

**Pubs, clubs and live music venues in England will receive a 20% cut in business rates from April, it has been announced by new UK Prime Minister Andy Burnham.**

It is estimated that the cut in business rates would save the typical pub an estimated £1,100 (€1,288/$1,470) next year.

Burnham, who succeeded Sir Keir Starmer as UK Prime Minister earlier this week, said that “governments have stood by while cherished venues have disappeared from our local high streets”.

He added: “This government will back the businesses that people want to see in their communities. I said I would protect pubs and local high streets – the beating heart of our communities – and that’s what we will do. What we’re announcing today is just the start as we work to bring back hope across the country.”

Chancellor of the Exchequer John Healey said: “Pubs, clubs and live music venues are at the heart of communities across the UK. They help make a place what we love. They bring people together, support local jobs and help keep high streets and town centres busy – which is why we will back them all the way.

![](https://www.theticketingbusiness.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/07/Andy_Burnham_on_13_August_2024_cropped-225x300.jpg)

UK Prime Minister, Andy Burnham. Credit: Scottish Government, CC BY 2.0, via Wikimedia Commons

“We are determined to bring hope back, give businesses the support they need and generate growth in every postcode.”

The package is worth around £100m a year, with the 20% cut for pubs, clubs and live music venues from 2027/28 coming on top of the existing 15% relief off their 2026/27 bill that was announced in January.

The government said that the new business rate cuts will benefit nearly 32,000 pubs, clubs and live music venues. Support will be targeted, meaning that the largest live music venues will be excluded, according to a report from the [*Guardian*](https://www.theguardian.com/business/2026/jul/23/business-rates-to-be-cut-by-20-for-england-pubs-clubs-and-music-venues).

Funding for the cuts will come through the review of reliefs for businesses that are deemed not to make a positive contribution to local communities, such as vape shops. The government said it would also crack down on businesses that sell through online marketplaces but do not comply with their tax obligations which it said puts them “at an unfair advantage” over businesses that “play by the rules”.

Revenue raised from these reforms will be reinvested in improvements to the business rates system.

**The reception**

In response to today’s (Thursday’s) news, the Music Venue Trust’s chief executive Mark Davyd said: “MVT warmly welcomes this announcement from the government, and its recognition that grassroots music venues are essential to our local communities. The 20% additional reduction on Business Rates from April 2027 is an encouraging first step in a range of opportunities available to Andy Burnham’s new team to not just protect and secure live music, but begin to restore its central role at the heart of our towns and cities.

“There remain some issues of implementation of previous reliefs, and we will work with colleagues in government to ensure that all grassroots music venues in England are recognised and eligible. We call on Senedd, Holyrood, and the Northern Ireland Assembly to swiftly confirm they will match this much needed support through Barnett formula consequentials. Venues in Wales, Scotland and Northern Ireland must be confident of a level playing field of economic conditions for touring across the UK.

“Live music is an ecosystem, and we strongly urge the government to reconsider the limit to the eligibility criteria so that all live music spaces of all sizes qualify, supporting jobs, local economies and communities to access live music.”

The Night Time Industries Association’s CEO Michael Kill also welcomed the news, and said it was “encouraging to see a positive outcome from genuine engagement with the sector” and that the “inclusion of clubs alongside pubs and live-music venues is particularly important and demonstrates a broader recognition of the vital economic, cultural and social contribution made by the night-time economy”.
