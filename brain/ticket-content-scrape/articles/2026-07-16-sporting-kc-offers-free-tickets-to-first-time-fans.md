# Sporting KC offers free tickets to first-time fans

## Summary

Sporting Kansas City launched a "First Game On Us" promotion offering up to two free tickets to first-time attendees for each of the club's final 10 home games this season. The Major League Soccer team is aiming to capitalize on interest in the sport generated by this year's World Cup to build its fan base at its 18,491-capacity Sporting Park stadium.

### Key Points
- Offer: up to two free tickets per first-time attendee, valid for any of the club's remaining 2026 home games.
- Seating choices are the Supporters Section or the more family-friendly South Stand.
- Sporting Park has an 18,491-seat capacity.
- The club is explicitly targeting fans new to the sport, citing World Cup-driven interest as the rationale.
- Sporting KC is currently last in its division with three wins this season, and holds a five-season record of 49 wins, 37 draws, and 74 losses.

TheTicketingBusiness News

**Sporting Kansas City has launched its “First Game On Us” initiative, which offers two free tickets to first-time match attendees for the club’s final 10 home games this season.**

The Major League Soccer side is aiming to capitalise on interest in the sport sparked by this year’s World Cup to attract new fans to its 18,491-capacity Sporting Park stadium.

“We want to make Sporting KC games an experience that all of Kansas City can be a part of,” read a club statement.

“That’s why, for the remainder of the season, fans who have never been to a game can take advantage of our First Game On Us program and claim up to two free tickets to any remaining 2026 home game.

“Tickets are available in either the Supporters Section—for fans who want to be part of the stadium’s most electric atmosphere—or the South Stand, which offers a more family-friendly environment.

“Whether it’s a social outing with friends or a fun day with family, we want to be the reason you experience your first Sporting KC game. Don’t miss your chance to feel the energy, excitement, and community—on us!”

Currently, Sporting KC is in last place in its division with three wins this season and holds a five-season record of 49 wins, 37 draws, and 74 losses.
