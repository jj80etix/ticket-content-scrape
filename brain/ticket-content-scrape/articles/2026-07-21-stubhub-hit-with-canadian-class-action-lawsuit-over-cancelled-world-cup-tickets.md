# StubHub Hit With Canadian Class Action Lawsuit Over Cancelled World Cup Tickets

## Summary

A Vancouver man filed a proposed Canadian class action against StubHub, viagogo, and affiliates alleging he paid over $19,000 for World Cup tickets that were never delivered, was repeatedly given false availability promises, and was denied comparable replacement seats despite StubHub's "FanProtect Guarantee." It's the second class action to hit StubHub this month, after a July 13 U.S. suit over CEO Eric Baker's ties to reseller Andro Capital.

### Key Points
- Plaintiff Mark Gallagher paid ~$19,184 total for four World Cup tickets across two matches; none were delivered despite repeated "available within hours" assurances.
- Lawsuit alleges violations of BC's Business Practices and Consumer Protection Act and the Ticket Act, and that StubHub "regularly declines to honour" its FanProtect Guarantee.
- Covers proposed class members in BC, Alberta, Saskatchewan, Quebec, and Ontario since March 31, 2025.
- Follows a separate July 13 U.S. class action alleging StubHub misled consumers about its "neutral marketplace" status.
- None of the allegations have been tested in court; StubHub has not yet responded.

### Industry Problem
Marketplace resellers can list and sell inventory they don't actually possess or can't guarantee, leaving buyers with unfulfilled purchases, unreliable support escalation, and guarantee programs that (per the suit) aren't consistently honored — a core patron trust and delivery-reliability failure mode of the secondary market.

### Proposed Solution
The suit itself proposes no fix beyond damages; for a primary platform, the takeaway is the value of guaranteed, verifiable inventory (tickets tied to real, deliverable seats) and transparent, honored refund/guarantee policies as a trust differentiator against marketplace resale risk.

StubHub has been hit with a proposed class action lawsuit after a Vancouver man purchased tickets to the World Cup that were never delivered.

The Vancouver-based Westpoint Law Group announced the proposed class action on Friday, July 17, naming defendants StubHub Inc., StubHub Canada Ltd., viagogo Entertainment Inc. and viagogo GmbH — all subsidiaries of StubHub Holdings Inc.

According to the class action, Mark Gallagher purchased two sets of tickets to the Canada vs. Qatar match in Vancouver on June 18, totaling $11,407.11 and $2,011.28. He also paid for two tickets to the June 24 Canada vs. Switzerland match in Vancouver for $5,765.15. None of the purchases included exact seat numbers; the Switzerland tickets only specified a section and row.

On June 14, Gallagher was notified that the two Qatar tickets were ready for delivery, but when he followed the instructions, he was unable to obtain the tickets. He called StubHub’s helpline and was told the tickets would be available the next day, though they were not. He continued to call StubHub multiple times leading up to the event and was told the tickets would be available within “two to three hours” the day-of the event. However, the tickets never materialized. Right before the match, StubHub help line staff told Gallagher the tickets he purchased were not available.

In the lawsuit, Gallagher claims that StubHub did not provide alternative seats comparable to the ones he purchased “despite there being comparable tickets available.”

According to a news release from Westpoint Law Group, the lawsuit alleges that the defendants “implemented a scheme to sell event tickets they either knew did not exist when advertised, or were procured in breach of the laws of the respective place where an event would occur.” Additionally, the lawsuit claims the defendants “provided a guarantee, at least parts of which they knew would not or could not be honored.”

The suit noted that if Gallagher knew StubHub was not in possession of the tickets or was unable to deliver them, he never would have purchased them in the first place. It added that while StubHub has a “FanProtect Guarantee,” the company “regularly declines to honour the Guarantee.”

Gallagher is seeking a declaration from StubHub that they have contravened the Business Practices and Consumer Protection Act, as well as the Ticket Act, and all class members are entitled to monetary damages and/or restitution. He is also seeking general and special damages.
