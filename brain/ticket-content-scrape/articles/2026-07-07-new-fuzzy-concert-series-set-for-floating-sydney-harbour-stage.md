# New Fuzzy concert series set for floating Sydney Harbour stage

## Summary

Australian promoter Fuzzy Operations, marking its 30th anniversary, announced "The Point," a new six-concert series on a floating stage in Sydney Harbour between October and November, at the waterfront Fleet Steps venue it has used since 2003 for its long-running Harbourlife festival. The lineup includes Icehouse, Missy Higgins, The Teskey Brothers, and the Hot Dub on the Harbour and Harbourlife festival editions.

### Key Points
- "The Point" is a six-show series on a floating barge stage at Fleet Steps, Sydney Harbour (Oct-Nov).
- Venue holds up to 3,000 in unseated configuration, backed by Sydney Harbour Bridge/Opera House.
- Lineup: Icehouse (Sept 30), Missy Higgins & The Teskey Brothers (Oct 1-2), plus Hot Dub on the Harbour and Harbourlife editions (Nov 20-21).
- Fuzzy has produced events at this location since 2003; became part of Superstruct in 2018.

## Transcript

As Fuzzy marks its 30th anniversary, the promoter steps up its programming for the home of its Harbourside festival

![](https://www.iqmagazine.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/07/Fuzzy-Australia.png)

© Fuzzy Operations

Australian promoter Fuzzy Operations has announced a six-concert series known as The Point, which will see artists perform on a floating stage in Sydney Harbour between October and November this year.

The series will take place at the waterfront Fleet Steps in the city’s Botanic Gardens, which is capable of holding up to 3,000 in unseated concert configurations. The stage will be located on a floating barge in the harbour, against a backdrop of Sydney Harbour Bridge and Sydney Opera House.

The series will host popular Australian acts, kicking off with veteran rock band Icehouse performing the special concert *ICEHOUSE at the Harbour* on 30 September.

Missy Higgins and The Teskey Brothers will follow on 1 and 2 October at, before festivals Hot Dub on the Harbour and Harbourlife stage editions on 20 and 21 November. Harbourlife has been staged by Fuzzy at the Fleet Steps since 2003.

> “the magic of this location has never worn off”

“For more than two decades, Fuzzy has produced live music events at Yurong and Mrs Macquarie’s Point, creating moments that have become part of Sydney’s cultural calendar. The Point builds on that history,” Fuzzy said.

“The Point brings a seasonal program of standalone performances on a floating stage in Sydney Harbour. Each event is individually curated, bringing together artists from across genres in one of Australia’s most distinctive outdoor settings,” the promoter added.

Fuzzy recently marked its 30th anniversary, and used the milestone to invite fans to respond to a survey about their experience at the promoter’s events, which it said would “inform the next chapter of Fuzzy”. The company became part of Superstruct in 2018, while it says all profits from Fuzzy-led events or festivals are reinvested into the music business.

“We have been hosting shows at Fleet Steps since Harbourlife launched here in 2003 and the magic of this location has never worn off,” Fuzzy CEO Adelle Robinson said, via *Rolling Stone Australia*. “As Fuzzy approaches 30 years, launching The Point feels like the perfect way to mark that milestone, opening up this extraordinary setting to even more artists and audiences. We cannot wait for Sydney to experience what we have planned.”
