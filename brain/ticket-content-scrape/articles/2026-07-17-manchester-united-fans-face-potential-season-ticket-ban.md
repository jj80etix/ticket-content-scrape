# Manchester United fans face potential season ticket ban

## Summary

Manchester United has suspended a number of season-ticket accounts over suspected touting-related "sustained activity," giving affected fans until July 19 to verify all devices used to access their accounts or lose their tickets entirely. Several fans, including one 27-year season-ticket holder, say the club has provided no specific evidence and criticized the process as vague, though the club maintains the sweep is necessary to curb ticket touting even at the cost of some false positives.

### Key Points
- Suspended accounts must confirm every device (phone, tablet, laptop, desktop) used to access them.
- Appeal deadline: July 19; no appeal or an unresolved appeal means cancellation.
- Club acknowledges the anti-touting approach may catch innocent fans.
- Some fans who responded have had accounts restored; others' suspensions remain despite disputing wrongdoing.

### Highlights
- Enforcement mechanism is device/access-pattern based ("sustained activity"), not a specific named violation.
- Appeal window is only a few days (through July 19).
- Club explicitly concedes false positives are an accepted cost of the anti-touting sweep.

### Industry Problem
Touting-detection systems built on account/device activity signals can flag legitimate long-tenure fans, and vague, low-transparency suspension notices leave patrons unable to meaningfully contest the decision — a direct patron-trust cost of anti-fraud enforcement.

### Proposed Solution
A primary platform's bot/touting detection should pair enforcement with clearer, evidence-specific notices and a longer, more structured appeal path, since the reputational cost of wrongly flagging loyal fans can outweigh the fraud caught — an area where Etix could differentiate on transparency.
