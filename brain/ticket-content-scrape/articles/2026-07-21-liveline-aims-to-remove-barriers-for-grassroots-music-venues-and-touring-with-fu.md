# Liveline aims to remove barriers for grassroots music, venues and touring with funding

## Summary

Music Venue Trust, with Save Our Scene and the Association of Independent Promoters, opened applications for "Liveline," a UK funding initiative — financed partly by Coldplay's tour-proceeds donation and the LIVE Trust — aimed at reducing the financial risk that keeps grassroots venues and promoters from booking shows.

### Key Points
- Four funding streams: an 80%-loss-guarantee scheme, a tour-extension/regional-access fund, a local-scene development fund, and a genre-specific access fund.
- Funded by 10% of Coldplay's 2025 UK stadium tour proceeds plus the LIVE Trust (funded by arena/stadium ticket-sale contributions).
- Targets grassroots venues, independent promoters, and artist representatives across the UK.

### Industry Problem
Grassroots venues and independent promoters face financial risk substantial enough to prevent tours, local shows, and new music nights from happening at all — a structural funding gap for the smallest tier of the live-events client base.

### Proposed Solution
Liveline addresses the gap with underwriting/guarantee mechanisms rather than grants. This is grounding context on the financial fragility of the smallest venue/promoter segment of the client base — useful for understanding pain points at the grassroots end of the market, even though it's a UK-specific third-party fund rather than something Etix would build directly.

TheTicketingBusiness News

**Applications have now opened for the latest round of funding from Liveline, an initiative set up to help grassroots music venues, independent promoters and artists across the UK.**

Delivered by Music Venue Trust, in partnership with Save Our Scene and the Association of Independent Promoters, Liveline was developed to tackle one of the biggest structural challenges facing grassroots music, which is the growing financial risk that is preventing tours, local shows and new music nights from taking place.

Liveline is open to grassroots music venues, independent promoters, in-house venue programmers and artist representatives – including managers, booking agents and even artists themselves.

Financing can be used to support individual shows, tour dates, regular venue programmes, promoter development initiatives, and longer-term activity that strengthens local live music ecosystems.

Funding has been provided by British band Coldplay, who gave 10% of the proceeds from their record-breaking 2025 UK stadium tour to support grassroots music, and the LIVE Trust, the industry’s funding initiative that channels contributions from arena and stadium ticket sales back into the grassroots sector.

Liveline identifies specific barriers preventing shows, tours and local programming, rectifying the situation through targeted, incentive-led support, as opposed to a conventional grant programme. The aim is to give venues, promoters and artists the confidence to programme more live music.

“The collapse of touring across the UK requires a robust and coordinated response from venues, bookers, promoters, agents, managers and artists,” said Mark Davyd, chief executive of Music Venue Trust.

“With Liveline, we are all working together to bring truly revolutionary support to the market. Liveline means everyone involved in creating, producing, developing and delivering a show can move forward with confidence, get shows happening, and be focused on the need to reignite towns and cities across the country with the thrill of extraordinary live music. We are a music nation, and music is written deep into our history. Liveline ensures music remains part of our future.”

**Four funding streams**

Applications are open across four areas, which have been designed to remove a specific barrier, whether that is the cost of touring, developing the local music scene or supporting underserved genres.

The Guarantorship Scheme underwrites up to 80% of approved losses on eligible shows, reducing financial risk for venues and promoters while allowing them to retain any profits from successful events.

The Tour Extension and Regional Access Fund helps artists and promoters extend tours into communities that are usually bypassed either because of travel costs, accommodation expenses or geographical challenges. The goal is to reconnect underserved parts of England, Scotland, Wales and Northern Ireland with the national touring circuit.

The Local Scene Development Fund supports venues and promoters building sustainable local music ecosystems through regular programming, while the Genre Specific Safety and Access Fund supports genres, scenes and communities.

“Today marks a landmark moment for UK music. Liveline applications are now open! If you are a venue, a promoter or an artist who has been struggling to make it work, this fund was built for you,” said George Fleming, CEO of Save Our Scene.

“For too long, we have watched the pipeline that produces world class talent shrink and Liveline is our answer to that. We are so proud to be working alongside a small but amazing team of people bringing that answer to life”.
