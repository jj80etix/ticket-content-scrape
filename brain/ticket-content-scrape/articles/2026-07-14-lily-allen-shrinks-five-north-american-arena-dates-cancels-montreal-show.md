# Lily Allen Shrinks Five North American Arena Dates, Cancels Montreal Show

## Summary

Four months after Live Nation promoted Lily Allen's fall "West End Girl" tour as a career-defining arena expansion, five of the twelve dates are being downsized into smaller theaters or amphitheaters, and the Montreal arena date has been canceled outright. Affected ticketholders will receive automatic refunds and priority presale access to the replacement shows.

### Key Points
- Five arena dates (Philadelphia, Chicago, Bay Area, LA, Vancouver) move to venues holding a fraction of original capacity; Montreal's Bell Centre date is canceled.
- Fans with tickets to affected dates get automatic refunds within 5-7 business days and presale access from July 22 ahead of the July 24 general on-sale.
- Reports point to soft ticket sales and criticism of the show's unconventional hour-long, no-banter format as likely drivers, though neither Allen nor Live Nation confirmed this.
- Several dates (MSG, Boston, Toronto, Detroit, Minneapolis, Red Rocks) remain unchanged.


## Transcript

***Live Nation announced the fall “West End Girl” run as the biggest headlining tour of Allen’s career, but stops in Philadelphia, Chicago, Los Angeles, the Bay Area and Vancouver are moving into substantially smaller venues***

Four months after Live Nation promoted [Lily Allen’s fall North American tour](https://www.ticketnews.com/2026/03/lily-allen-announces-fall-2026-north-american-dates-for-west-end-girl-tour/) as a career-defining expansion into arenas, the “West End Girl” run is being substantially scaled back.

Allen [announced Monday](https://www.instagram.com/p/DavS6SvDKEd/?utm_source=ig_web_copy_link&igsh=MzRlODBiNWFlZA==) that five concerts will move from arenas into smaller theaters or amphitheaters, while her September 8 appearance at Montreal’s Bell Centre has been canceled outright. A new Seattle theater date has been added to the itinerary.

The tour will still open September 3 at Madison Square Garden in New York, but half of the 12 dates included in Live Nation’s original announcement have now been altered. When the fall leg was unveiled in March, the promoter described it as Allen’s “largest headlining shows to date,” citing “overwhelming demand” for her sold-out spring theater run.

The Philadelphia show will remain scheduled for September 6, but moves from Xfinity Mobile Arena to the outdoor TD Pavilion at Highmark Mann.

In Chicago, Allen will trade the United Center for the 3,600-seat Chicago Theatre on September 13. The Bay Area concert moves from San Francisco’s 18,064-seat Chase Center on September 23 to the approximately 3,000-seat Paramount Theatre in Oakland one day earlier.

Allen’s September 25 Kia Forum concert in Inglewood has been replaced by a September 21 performance at the Greek Theatre in Los Angeles. The Vancouver show, originally scheduled for September 21 at Rogers Arena, will now take place September 28 at the 2,672-seat Orpheum Theatre.

The reworked West Coast route also adds a September 24 performance at Seattle’s Paramount Theatre.

Allen did not publicly explain why the tour was being reconfigured, saying only that organizers had “a few updates” to the schedule. She told Montreal fans that the tour had “tried our hardest” but would not be able to visit the city.

The scope of the changes nevertheless represents implies that sales were not strong enough in impacted markets to justify the size of the rooms originally booked for the tour. While concert capacities vary with production configuration, the Chicago, Bay Area, Los Angeles and Vancouver changes replace major arenas with rooms holding only a fraction of their original audiences.

The San Francisco Chronicle attributed the Bay Area move to low ticket sales and online criticism of the production, although neither Allen nor Live Nation has publicly connected the broader restructuring to either factor.

Fans holding tickets for affected arena dates will receive automatic refunds within five to seven business days. Those buyers will also be entered into a presale for the replacement shows beginning July 22, ahead of the general on-sale July 24.

The downsizing comes shortly after Allen defended the unconventional format and running time of “Lily Allen Performs West End Girl.” The production features Allen performing the 14-track album in sequence as a theatrical narrative, with limited interaction between the singer and the audience.

Some concertgoers criticized the show following Allen’s recent O2 Arena dates, pointing to its roughly hour-long runtime and ticket prices. Allen responded that the album presentation and lack of stage banter were deliberate artistic choices intended to preserve the production’s “fourth wall.”

“I don’t want anyone to feel ripped off,” Allen wrote, adding that she was proud of the show and the work behind it.

Several dates remain unchanged, including Madison Square Garden, Boston’s MGM Music Hall at Fenway, Toronto’s RBC Amphitheatre, Detroit’s Fox Theatre, the Armory in Minneapolis and Colorado’s Red Rocks Amphitheatre.

The revised tour remains a significant North American outing, but its venue profile now more closely resembles the theater and amphitheater circuit on which Allen initially found success with the “West End Girl” production than the arena expansion Live Nation announced in March.
