# Luke Combs raises the bar for country in the Nordics

## Summary

Luke Combs drew a record 60,826 fans to Gothenburg's Ullevi Stadium, the largest country-music concert ever held in the Nordic region, as he launched the European leg of his "My Kind of Saturday Night Tour." The show underscores country music's rapid rise in Scandinavia, with Live Nation-backed promoter Luger noting growing appetite across artist tiers in the market.

### Key Points
- Combs drew 60,826 fans to Ullevi Stadium, Gothenburg — a new Nordic country-music attendance record.
- Follows his sold-out 2023 Avicii Arena (17,000-cap) show, illustrating the genre's rapid regional growth.
- Sweden now regularly hosts country acts including Lainey Wilson, Zach Top, and Midland.
- Tour launched March 21 at Las Vegas's Allegiant Stadium before its European leg.

Luke Combs has set a new benchmark for country music in the Nordic region, drawing over 60,800 people to Gothenburg’s Ullevi Stadium over the weekend.

The American star launched the European leg of his *My Kind of Saturday Night Tour* in Sweden on Saturday (4 July), setting a new attendance record at 60,826 fans as the largest country concert held in the region.

The Live Nation-promoted show followed three years after his sell-out performance at Stockholm’s [Avicii Arena](https://www.iqmagazine.com/featured-list/arena-stars-2026/avicii-arena-stockholm/) (cap. 17,000) in 2023, demonstrating the remarkable rise of country music on a global scale.

That show was initially booked in a smaller venue, but due to overwhelming demand, it moved to the arena level and swiftly sold out, Niklas Herrström of Live Nation-backed Luger [told *IQ* earlier this year.](https://www.iqmagazine.com/2026/05/these-boots-are-made-for-tourin-country-genre-report-part-2/)

Sweden now hosts a broad spectrum of country acts, including Lainey Wilson, Randall King, Willow Avalon, Cameron Whitcomb, Midland, Max McNown, Dasha, and Zach Top, who sold out his first Stockholm show outside the US within days.

“We work with acts of all sizes,” Herrström said, highlighting the market’s diversity and appetite for talent at all levels. “US acts that may previously have skipped our market are now coming over and building strong, long-lasting audiences.”

Combs’ *Saturday Night* tour launched on 21 March at Las Vegas’s Allegiant Stadium (65,000), with the star performing 13 total shows before hopping over the pond for the UK/EU leg.

Today (7 July), he will headline Paris’ Accor Arena (20,300), continuing through Amsterdam’s Johan Cruyff Arena (71,000), with two nights lined up at both Ireland’s Slane Castle (80,000) and Edinburgh’s Murrayfield Stadium (67,144), and three shows at London’s Wembley Stadium (90,000) to [end the tour](https://www.iqmagazine.com/2025/10/country-star-luke-combs-announces-stadium-run/) on 2 August.
