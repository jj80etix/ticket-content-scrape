## KMJ’s Stuart Galbraith hits 100 shows at London’s The O2

The promoter earned the prestigious Gold Brick Award with Italian composer Ludovico Einaudi's first show at the arena

![](https://www.iqmagazine.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/07/IQ-News-Image.png)

Stuart Galbraith and Ludovico Einaudi (second and third from left) were honoured at The O2

With 100 shows at London’s The O2 arena under his belt, KMJ Entertainment Group CEO Stuart Galbraith has earned the venue’s prestigious Gold Brick Award.

On Saturday (11 July), the promoter was awarded alongside Italian composer Ludovico Einaudi, who was presented with the venue’s First Time award in honour of his debut show at the venue. The headline show marked the classical artists’ biggest reception in the UK in their over four-decade career.

“What an honour and a privilege to be awarded a Gold Brick on the Promoter Wall of Fame, and for it to be triggered by Ludovico’s debut performance at London’s The O2 Arena,” Galbraith says. “Both Ludovico and I have enjoyed careers spanning over 45 years in the entertainment industry, and it’s incredibly special to be able to share this milestone evening with him.”

Galbraith’s Gold Brick will go up on the venue’s backstage display, which honours major milestones at the iconic venue. Last year, he was also honoured with the [LIVEtime Achievement Award](https://www.iqmagazine.com/2025/12/galbraith-roam-communion-one-among-live-awards-winners/) for his accomplishments in the live music industry.

Einaudi’s O2 debut followed his being honoured with the Innovation in Music Award at the 50th O2 Silver Clef Awards in London’s Royal Albert Hall last week, demonstrating the power of classical music in today’s touring industry.

Kilimanjaro Live, which sits under the DEAG-backed KMJ Entertainment umbrella, has been championing the demand for classical music, having recently taken Lord of the Rings Orchestral into larger venues in the UK and Australia and launching an arena tour for Royal Albert Hall’s official organist, Anna Lapwood.

In previous years, the promoter has also worked with Andrea Bocelli, Hans Zimmer, Lang Lang, and Jeff Wayne, placing Kilimanjaro Live at the forefront of modern classical performance.

The KMJ Entertainment portfolio, which comprises over a dozen companies, spans a wide variety of entertainment, including concert promotion, spoken word, comedy, immersive experiences, family, and theatre. Last year, it sold 4.5 million tickets and recorded a record-breaking turnover of £200 million (€230m), doubling from 2024.
