# Gotobeat launches arena tour division

## Summary

Live entertainment company Gotobeat, which combines promoter services with AI-driven forecasting technology, is launching an arena touring division, starting with two shows at OVO Arena Wembley. The company reported 400% growth in 2025 and says its proprietary forecasting tools have helped artists earn an average of 30% more per tour by reducing risk and cost in tour planning.

### Key Points
- Gotobeat launches an arena touring division, opening with Lucki (July 10) and TayC (Nov 15) at OVO Arena Wembley.
- Company reported 400% growth in 2025.
- Uses AI-driven forecasting technology to inform tour routing/planning decisions.
- Claims its forecasting tools helped artists earn 30% more per tour on average in 2025.
- Positions the move as reducing risk and cost while making touring more sustainable.

TheTicketingBusiness News

**Live entertainment company Gotobeat is expanding to launch a new arena touring division, starting with two shows at OVO Arena Wembley.**

The shows will be headlined by Lucki on July 10 and TayC on November 15 as part of a move that has been described as a “defining moment” for the company which combines the promoter experience with AI technology.

“We’re excited to help shape the future of live entertainment at an even greater scale,” said partner and senior promoter at Gotobeat, Pietro Bertini, as reported by [MusicWeek](https://www.musicweek.com/live/read/gotobeat-expands-with-launch-of-arena-tour-division/094420).

“Our mission has always been to rethink how live music tours are planned and delivered, making touring more sustainable and connecting more fans with the artists they love.

“Bringing that approach to arena touring is a significant step forward, and we’re excited to help shape the future of live entertainment at an even greater scale.”

Gotobeat, which reported 400% growth in 2025, stated that the new division represents the next stage in its expansion while reinforcing its mission to make touring more sustainable.

The company aims to reduce risk, lower costs and support more sustainable touring through the use of AI tools to inform decision-making.

By working across a broader range of venues and audiences, Gotobeat plans to “continue to strengthen its proprietary forecasting technology”, which it says helped artists earn an average of 30% more per tour in 2025.
