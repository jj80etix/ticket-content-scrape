# Live Nation faces possible investigation in UK as CMA reportedly lines up meetings

## Summary

The UK's Competition and Markets Authority has begun contacting Live Nation critics and industry figures to line up meetings, a step that typically precedes a formal investigation into the company's market dominance. It follows a May UK parliamentary committee finding that the CMA-investigation threshold had been met, and comes after the CMA already secured pricing-transparency commitments from Ticketmaster over the 2024 Oasis on-sale.

### Key Points
- CMA reportedly lining up meetings with Live Nation critics ahead of a possible formal probe.
- May 2026 UK Business and Trade Committee report concluded the CMA's investigation threshold was met, citing Live Nation's 23 UK venue and 26 festival stakes.
- Live Nation disputes the committee's findings as based on "inaccurate data."
- Follows the CMA's 2024–25 Oasis on-sale investigation, which produced Ticketmaster pricing-transparency undertakings.

### Industry Problem
Regulators in a second major market (after the U.S. DOJ case) are scrutinizing whether Live Nation's vertically integrated venue/festival/ticketing ownership structure limits competition and transparency for fans and independent promoters.

### Proposed Solution
No remedy proposed yet — this is pre-investigation positioning. Worth tracking as a leading indicator: if the CMA opens a formal probe, expect renewed pressure on pricing transparency and possibly ticketing-exclusivity practices, which would sharpen the competitive opening for independent, non-integrated platforms like Etix.

**The UK’s Competition and Markets Authority (CMA) has begun writing to industry figures to line up meetings, paving a way for an investigation into the country’s live music sector.**

According to a report from [*The Times*](https://www.thetimes.com/business/companies-markets/article/ticketing-live-nation-investigation-g26kwpjlm), the CMA has reached out to Live Nation critics as well as other members of the industry, potentially ahead of a formal probe.

[In May](https://www.theticketingbusiness.com/2026/05/live-nation-faces-calls-for-investigation-into-market-dominance-in-the-uk-market/), a report from the UK government’s Business and Trade Committee on competition and market in the live music industry concluded that the threshold for investigation by the CMA into Live Nation’s ‘dominance’ had been met.

The Committee said it had concerns that the scale and integrated nature of Live Nation’s business model made it difficult for artists and managers to operate independently of its ecosystem in the UK.

Following the report, Live Nation said that it misrepresented the UK live music industry by “relying on inaccurate data and unsupported conclusions”.

A spokesperson added: “Live Nation competes every day for tours, venues and artists in a highly competitive market. We will engage constructively with any process that benefits artists, fans and the wider industry, but debate about the sector must be based on evidence, not allegation and hearsay.”

According to *The Time* s, Live Nation owns stakes in 23 venues and 26 festivals in the UK.

It is not the first time Live Nation has come under fire in the UK, with subsidiary Ticketmaster facing hostility after fans were left angered by the Oasis reunion tour onsale. Many believed that dynamic pricing had been installed for standing tickets, but it was later revealed that the ticket seller had instead used a tiered pricing system.

The competition watchdog then officially [launched an investigation](https://www.theticketingbusiness.com/2024/09/uk-watchdog-launches-investigation-into-ticketmasters-oasis-on-sale/) into whether Ticketmaster had breached consumer rights rules, as it was also not clear what ‘platinum’ tickets (which were 2.5 times the price of standard entry) actually offered, if anything at all.

The CMA [secured formal commitments](https://www.theticketingbusiness.com/2025/09/ticketmaster-to-make-changes-after-uk-cmas-oasis-investigation/), known as undertakings, from Ticketmaster to make sure fans have the information they need when purchasing tickets. Ticketmaster said it would make it clear to fans what they will get for their money, and offer more information around different ticket prices while fans are waiting in the online queues.
