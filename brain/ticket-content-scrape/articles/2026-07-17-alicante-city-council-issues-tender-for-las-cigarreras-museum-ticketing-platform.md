# Alicante City Council issues tender for Las Cigarreras Museum ticketing platform

## Summary

Alicante City Council has opened a tender for a two-year ticketing management contract (worth ~€19,341) for the new Las Cigarreras International Museum of Temporary Exhibitions, set to open later this year with general admission priced at €10 (€5 reduced). The council frames the museum, hosting exhibitions from artists like Modigliani, Balenciaga, and Chagall, as a cultural anchor for the city.

### Key Points
- Two-year ticketing services tender worth ~€19,341 (~$22,100).
- Planned pricing: €10 general, €5 reduced (families, children, seniors).
- Museum spans 1,600 sqm within the Casa de la Misericordia; opens later in 2026.
- Six exhibitions already lined up, including Modigliani, Balenciaga, Chagall, and Sorolla.

TheTicketingBusiness News

**Alicante City Council has launched a tender for a new ticketing platform for the Las Cigarreras International Museum of Temporary Exhibitions.**

The local governing board has approved the call for an open procedure to contract the management service for issuing and selling tickets for the museum with two-year contract worth €19,341 (£16,450/$22,100).

“With this tender, we are taking another step towards finalizing everything necessary for the opening of the Las Cigarreras International Museum of Temporary Exhibitions, which is destined to be a landmark for our city,” said Councillor for Culture, Nayma Beldjilali.

“The exhibitions that this new cultural facility will host are of exceptional quality, and its opening will undoubtedly revitalise the cultural life of our city and, above all, will bring energy to the neighbourhoods of Carolinas, El Pla, and San Antón.”

When the new system is operational, the Council wants to set the general price of a ticket at €10 as well as a reduced price of €5 for large families, children, and people over 65 years old.

The new museum, spanning 1,600 square metres within the Casa de la Misericordia, is set to open later this year.

It already has six exhibitions lined up featuring artists and designers such as Amedeo Modigliani, Cristobal Balenciaga, Marc Chagall, and Joaquín Sorolla among others.
