# Lily Allen downsizes tour venues

## Summary

Lily Allen has downsized several North American stops on her West End Girl tour, canceling arena dates in Los Angeles, Chicago, San Francisco, Vancouver and Philadelphia in favor of medium-sized venues, while dropping the Montreal show entirely with no replacement booked. The North American leg, running since March 2, resumes July 24. Ticketholders for the canceled shows will be automatically refunded and rolled into a July 22 presale for the new dates, ahead of a July 24 general onsale.

### Key Points
- Cancelled arena dates: Los Angeles' Forum, Chicago's United Center, San Francisco's Chase Center, Vancouver's Rogers Arena, and Philadelphia's Xfinity Mobile Arena.
- Replacement venues: the Greek Theatre (LA), Paramount Theatre, Chicago Theatre, TD Pavilion, and Orpheum.
- The Montreal show at Bell Centre was cancelled outright with no alternative venue booked.
- Existing ticketholders for the affected shows will be automatically refunded within 5-7 business days.
- Affected fans are auto-entered into a presale opening July 22 at 10 a.m. local time, ahead of a general onsale July 24 at 10 a.m. local.
- The tour has been running since March 2 in support of Allen's West End Girl album.

TheTicketingBusiness News

**Lily Allen has cancelled arena shows to book medium-sized venues for North American shows on her West End Girl tour.**

The tour has been ongoing since March 2 and is set to revisit the United States and Canada from July 24.

However, arena shows at Los Angeles’ Forum, Chicago’s United Center, San Francisco’s Chase Center, Vancouver’s Rogers Arena, Philadelphia’s Xfinity Mobile Arena and Montreal’s Bell Centre have been cancelled.

New shows have been booked at medium-sized venues in five of the six affected cities, while the Montreal concert has been cancelled without any alternative venue being booked.

Those dates have now been moved to the Greek Theatre, the Paramount Theatre, the Chicago Theatre, the TD Pavilion and the Orpheum.

Ticketholders have been notified that they will automatically receive refunds in five to seven days and be entered into a presale that goes live on July 22.

“For Philly, Chicago, LA, San Francisco, Vancouver, we’ve had to make some adjustments,” Allen wrote on social media.

“If you’ve already bought tickets to these shows, you will be refunded in 5-7 working days and automatically entered into a presale that goes live July 22, 10AM local. General on sale will go live July 24, 10AM local.

“Montreal, I am so sad to say we tried our hardest but will not be able to make it to you this time.”
