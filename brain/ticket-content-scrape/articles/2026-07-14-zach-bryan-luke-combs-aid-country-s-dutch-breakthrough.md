# Zach Bryan, Luke Combs aid country’s Dutch ‘breakthrough’

## Summary

Dutch promoter Greenhouse Talent describes 2026 as a breakthrough year for country music in the Netherlands, with Luke Combs and Zach Bryan each headlining their first stadium shows in the market (50,000 and 30,000 attendees respectively). The piece also notes strong growth for the C2C festival's Rotterdam stop and the return of Greenhouse's Once In A Blue Moon Festival, framing the genre's rapid rise from club shows to stadiums across Europe.

### Key Points
- Luke Combs drew 50,000 fans to Amsterdam's Johan Cruijff ArenA; Zach Bryan drew 30,000 to Eindhoven's Philips Stadion — first international country stadium shows in the Netherlands.
- C2C: Country to Country's Rotterdam edition saw 45% attendance growth year-over-year (7,500 fans) and returns 5-6 March 2027.
- Greenhouse is on track to promote more than 60 country artists in the Dutch market this year.
- Combs and Bryan continue touring other European markets (Sweden, France, UK, Germany, etc.).

## Zach Bryan, Luke Combs aid country’s Dutch ‘breakthrough’

Greenhouse Talent has hailed the growth of the genre, as the pair of international stars step up to the market's stadium level

![Luke Combs in the Netherlands](https://www.iqmagazine.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/07/Luke-Combs-in-the-Netherlands-©-Martin-Hols.png)

Luke Combs in the Netherlands © Martin Hols

The Netherlands’ Greenhouse Talent have hailed the growth of country music in the market, with the promoter calling 2026 a ‘breakthrough’ year for the genre as Zach Bryan and Luke Combs step up to the stadium level.

In recent weeks, Luke Combs drew 50,000 people to Amsterdam’s Johan Cruijff ArenA on his *My* *Kind of Saturday Night Tour,* and Zach Bryan attracted 30,000 fans to Eindhoven’s Philips Stadion during his *With* *Heaven on Tour*, marking the first stadium shows for international country stars in the market.

“We’ve been promoting country music in the Netherlands for more than a decade, and we’ve seen the audience grow year after year,” says Wouter de Wilde, MD of Greenhouse Talent Netherlands. “But 2026 has been a true breakthrough.

“Seeing artists like Zach Bryan and Luke Combs headline Dutch stadiums is something that would have seemed unimaginable just a few years ago. It shows that country music has firmly established itself as a major live genre in the Netherlands, and we’re proud to have contributed to that journey,” he shares.

Once considered a niche genre, [country music](https://www.iqmagazine.com/iq-magazine/issue-143/) is now one of the fastest-growing musical genres in the world. The genre’s success in the Netherlands underscores its rapid growth, up from intimate club shows all the way up to large-scale gatherings.

In addition to the pair of shows, [C2C: Country to Country](https://www.iqmagazine.com/2026/03/c2c-festival-100-000-attendees-uk-editions/) festival’s Rotterdam stop also attracted 45% more visitors than last year, totalling 7,500 fans for its fifth Dutch edition. It is set to return from 5-6 March 2027, alongside stops in Berlin, London, Glasgow, and Manchester.

“This year marked the strongest edition of C2C in the Netherlands to date,” de Wilde said previously. “Alongside the significant growth in attendance, the atmosphere and fan experience stood out as highlights. It feels like a milestone moment for the festival and an exciting foundation for future growth.”

Greenhouse’s Once In A Blue Moon Festival is also set to return this year after a successful revival in 2025. Led by The White Buffalo and The 502s, the two-day boutique roots festival is set to be staged from 4-5 September at Zuiderparktheater in The Hague.

Across the market, Greenhouse is promoting swathes of talent in the country genre spectrum, with the promoter on track to promote more than 60 country artists in the market this year.

Combs is hitting a variety of European markets on his tour this year, including [Sweden](https://www.iqmagazine.com/2026/07/luke-combs-raises-the-bar-for-country-in-the-nordics/), France, Ireland, and the UK, while Bryan is also due out across the UK, Spain, Germany, Norway, and Denmark.
