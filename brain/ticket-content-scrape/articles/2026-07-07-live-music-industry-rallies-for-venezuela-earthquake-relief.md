# Live music industry rallies for Venezuela earthquake relief

## Summary

The global live-music industry has mobilized relief efforts for Venezuela following two major earthquakes on June 24 that killed an estimated 3,500 people and injured over 16,700. Latin American promoter Move Concerts joined regional peers MD Productions and Jogo to match donations for relief group Dividendo Voluntario para la Comunidad, while Metallica donated $100,000 through its charitable arm to Direct Relief.

### Key Points
- Two magnitude-7+ earthquakes hit Venezuela's Yaracuy state on June 24, killing ~3,500 and injuring 16,700+.
- Move Concerts, MD Productions, and Jogo are matching public donations to Dividendo Voluntario para la Comunidad.
- Metallica donated $100,000 via All Within My Hands to Direct Relief for emergency aid and search-and-rescue support.
- Rush and conductor Gustavo Dudamel are also cited among industry figures supporting relief efforts.

Metallica, Rush, Gustavo Dudamel, and Move Concerts are among the industry activists leading the way to support the victims

![](https://www.iqmagazine.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/Metallica-©-Live-NationRoss-Halfin.png)

Metallica are among those leading the charge © Live Nation/Ross Halfin

Metallica, Rush, Gustavo Dudamel, and a trio of concert promoters are among the industry activists leading the way to support the victims of Venezuela’s recent earthquakes, which killed over 3,500 people and injured thousands.

On 24 June, two massive earthquakes with magnitudes of over seven hit the northwestern portion of Venezuela, with both epicentres hitting the state of Yaracuy, causing widespread damage across the country and in cities like the capital of Caracas and La Guaira.

The earthquakes, which hit a minute apart from one another, resulted in the death of an estimated 3,500 people, with reports of another 16,700 individuals injured and tens of thousands more missing, according to reports from [*Reuters*](https://www.reuters.com/world/americas/death-toll-venezuela-quakes-rises-3535-thousands-remain-displaced-2026-07-06/).

In response, Latin America’s leading independent promoter, Move Concerts, has united with other regional promoters, MD Productions and Jogo, who have all pledged to match the amount collected from donations. People who would like to support the initiative can donate [here](https://causematch.com/Venezuela?utm_source=ig&utm_medium=social&utm_content=link_in_bio).

“We have joined forces with other concert promoters across the region to raise funds for the victims of the recent earthquakes in Venezuela,” Move’s EVP Andres Guanipa tells *IQ*.

“All proceeds will be donated to Dividendo Voluntario para la Comunidad, one of Venezuela’s most respected charitable organisations, which has been leading relief efforts for more than 60 years and is providing emergency assistance to families affected by the disaster,” he says.

Elsewhere, Metallica have donated $100,000 through their charitable organisation All Within My Hands, earmarked for Direct Relief to deliver emergency aid to those affected and support search-and-rescue operations. Direct Relief is also helping organise first-aid supplies, medicine, and other healthcare for those affected by the earthquakes.

> “This concert at the Hollywood Bowl is an invitation to stand together and transform our compassion into action”

Canadian rockers Rush have also taken up arms to support the cause, teaming up with their frequent collaborator, Venezuelan director David Calcano, to create a limited-edition T-shirt, with sale proceeds to be donated to the Hogar Bambi foundation.

“All proceeds go directly to Hogar Bambi, which since 1996 has sheltered Venezuela’s orphaned and abandoned children, and is now racing to care for the youngest victims of this disaster, turning every dollar into food, medicine, and shelter,” reads the shirt description.

They are available for purchase [here](https://www.hellomerch.com/products/rush-venezuela-earthquake-relief-t-shirt) for $40.

Gustavo Dudamel and the Los Angeles Philharmonic will stage a benefit concert to raise funds, taking place on 23 August at the Hollywood Bowl (cap. 17,500), with full programming to be announced.

Donations will go to Dudamel’s Earthquake Recovery to Support Venezuelan Communities fund, in partnership with the United Nations Development Programme, and the Development Bank of Latin America and the Caribbean (CAF) fund.

The performance will also mark the Venezuelan-born conductor’s last as music & artistic director of the LA Philharmonic.

“Venezuela will always be my home, and every moment, my thoughts are with the families whose lives have been forever changed by this tragedy,” he said. “The suffering is immense, but so is the strength and resilience of our people. This concert at the Hollywood Bowl is an invitation to stand together and transform our compassion into action.”
