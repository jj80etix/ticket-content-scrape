# HeritageLive festivals cancelled over costs, low ticket sales

## Summary

Three UK HeritageLive festivals (at Englefield, Audley End, and Sandringham estates), featuring headliners like Christina Aguilera, Eric Clapton, and Lionel Richie, were cancelled at the eleventh hour after an investment/equity package fell through. Organizer GCE Live cited high supplier, artist, and staffing costs driven up by "huge multi-nationals," combined with the cost-of-living crisis and weaker-than-average ticket sales, as making the events unsustainable; refunds will go through partner ticket agents.

### Key Points
- Three country-estate festivals cancelled last-minute after a planned investment/equity deal collapsed.
- Organizer GCE Live explicitly blames cost pressure from "huge multi-nationals" driving up supplier, artist, and staffing costs industry-wide.
- Combined with cost-of-living-driven weak ticket sales, the promoter said it would have been "irresponsible" to proceed without certainty of covering costs.
- Refunds to be processed via partner ticket agents; GCE Live frames itself as one of the last independent British promoters.

### Industry Problem
Independent promoters are being squeezed between rising costs (partly attributed to consolidation among large multi-national competitors) and softer consumer demand amid cost-of-living pressure — a structural pain point for smaller/independent event organizers, Etix's client base, that can end in cancellations and refund events even after tickets are sold.

### Proposed Solution

TheTicketingBusiness News

**Three summer music festivals in the UK, hosted on historical country estates, have been cancelled due to high costs and low ticket sales.**

HeritageLive festivals were due to take place at the Englefield Estate in Berkshire, the Audley End Estate in Essex and royal residence Sandringham Estate in Norfolk.

Artists such as Christina Aguilera, Eric Clapton and Lionel Richie were scheduled to play at Sandringham, while Scissor Sisters were due to perform at Audley End Estate. Richard Ascroft was among the headliners at Englefield Estate.

Organiser GCE Live said that it had been working “desperately hard” to conclude an investment and equity package to ease the burden “of an extraordinarily tough year”, but this had fallen through resulting in the cancellations at the eleventh hour.

In a statement, GCE Live said: “As one of the few remaining British independent promoters, it’s become almost impossible to compete in what has become an increasingly saturated festival market.

“The might of huge multi-nationals has had the adverse effect of driving up supplier, artist and staffing costs and whilst it’s still just about manageable during a normal year, the cost-of-living crisis combined with general financial uncertainty, has resulted in several shows having far lower than average ticket sales, making it impossible to survive in such a competitive industry, with so many rising costs.

“It would therefore be irresponsible and wrong of us to proceed without the certainty that we’d be able to meet all of our supplier, artist and crew costs.”

GCE Live added that the HeritageLive team was “absolutely devastated” and thanked everyone for their support over the last 10 years, and that refunds would be available via partner ticket agents.

The company’s chief executive Giles Cooper is also chairman of the Royal Variety Charity, which is behind the Royal Variety Performance.
