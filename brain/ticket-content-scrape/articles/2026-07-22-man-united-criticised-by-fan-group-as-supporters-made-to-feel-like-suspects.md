# Man United criticised by fan group as supporters made to feel like ‘suspects’

## Summary

Manchester United's crackdown on season-ticket touting has drawn a joint rebuke from the club's official Fans' Forum, MUST, The 1958, and Red Army, who say legitimate long-time supporters are being treated as suspects without due process. The groups aren't disputing the club's right to act against professional touts and automated purchasing, but are demanding the suspension and cancellation process pause while the club and supporters negotiate a fairer approach.

### Key Points
- Joint statement from four Manchester United supporter organizations criticizes the touting crackdown's fairness, not its underlying goal.
- Supporters say the club's approach lacks clear evidence, due process, or a meaningful opportunity to respond before sanctions.
- MUST notes this is the first time supporter groups across the fanbase have united behind one position.
- Groups are calling for a pause on further suspensions/cancellations pending discussion with the club.

### Highlights
- Four supporter organizations issued a unified joint statement — described as unprecedented cross-fanbase alignment.
- Explicit ask: pause further suspensions and season-ticket cancellations during talks.
- Club maintains it's targeting "professional touting, automated purchasing" specifically.

### Industry Problem
Anti-touting enforcement that lacks transparent evidence and appeal processes risks alienating a club's most loyal, longest-tenured fans — the same due-process gap seen whenever fraud-detection systems flag legitimate users as bad actors.

### Proposed Solution
Clubs and primary platforms doing bulk anti-touting sweeps should pair enforcement with a published, transparent evidence standard and pre-restriction notice/appeal window, rather than the "ban first" sequence supporters are objecting to here — a design point directly relevant to Etix's own fraud/anti-touting tooling.

**Manchester United has been criticised by several supporter groups after some fans had their** [**season tickets restricted**](https://www.theticketingbusiness.com/2026/07/manchester-united-fans-face-potential-season-ticket-ban/)**.**

The club’s official fans’ forum, Manchester United Supporters Trust (MUST), The 1958 and The Red Army are among the groups to speak out against the Premier League club, which is aiming to crack down on the illegal resale of tickets.

However, the Red Devils’ approach has been castigated for taking a “ban first and ask questions later” approach.

“Professional touting, automated purchasing and those who exploit supporters for financial gain have no place at Manchester United and deserve robust action,” read a joint statement from the groups.

“However, that action must be fair, proportionate and transparent. Long-standing supporters who have followed Manchester United for decades, often across multiple generations of the same family, should never feel they are being treated as suspects without clear evidence, due process or a meaningful opportunity to respond to the allegations made against them.

“The current approach has created a growing perception among supporters of a ‘Ban first and ask questions later’ process. That is not how trust is built, nor is it consistent with the standards of fairness that loyal supporters should expect from their football club.”

The Fans’ Forum has called on the club to pause the issuing of further suspensions and to halt any season ticket cancellations.

MUST stated that this is the first time that supporter organisations from across the fanbase have come together to support a common position.
