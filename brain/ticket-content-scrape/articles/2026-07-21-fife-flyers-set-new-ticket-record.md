# Fife Flyers set new ticket record

## Summary

Scottish ice hockey club Fife Flyers set a new season-ticket sales record ahead of the 2026/27 Elite Ice Hockey League season, despite finishing last in the prior campaign.

### Key Points
- Record season-ticket sales at Fife Ice Arena, surpassing last year's mark.
- New EIHL season opens with the Challenge Cup Sept. 12.
- Regular season runs through April 4, 2027.

## Transcript

![TheTicketingBusiness News](https://www.theticketingbusiness.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/04/TBN-LOGO-reversed-white.png)

TheTicketingBusiness News

**Elite Ice Hockey League team Fife Flyers has set a new season ticket record, after surpassing last season’s benchmark.**

The high uptake at Fife Ice Arena comes despite the Flyers finishing in last place in the most recent league campaign.

“This is a landmark moment for our club. To know that more supporters than ever before have committed to the Fife Flyers before a puck has even been dropped is both humbling and incredibly motivating,” said Flyers chief executive John Brown.

“It gives us greater confidence as we continue to build the club and plan for the season ahead, knowing that our fans are right behind us. I’ve said it before and I’ll say it again – there is no question that we have the best and most loyal supporters in British ice hockey. Time and time again they answer the call, and once again they have shown exactly what this club means to them.

“Every single season ticket purchased is an investment in the future of the Fife Flyers and gives us the very best opportunity to succeed. On behalf of everyone at the club, thank you. We can’t wait to welcome you back to Fife Ice Arena for what promises to be a hugely exciting season.”

The 2026/27 EIHL season is set to begin with the Challenge Cup on September 12 before the regular season concludes on April 4 next year.
