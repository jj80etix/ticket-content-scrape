# Arena Stars 2026: Latin America

## Summary

Part four of IQ Magazine's 2026 "Arena Stars" series profiles three Latin American venues — Arena CDMX, Movistar Arena Argentina, and Movistar Arena Bogotá — as part of an industry-nominated list of the world's 20 best arenas. Coverage is largely descriptive/promotional, highlighting each venue's capacity, hospitality amenities and production infrastructure.

### Key Points
- Arena CDMX (Mexico City): 23,000 capacity, operated by Zignia Live; extensive premium hospitality (127 suites).
- Movistar Arena Argentina (Buenos Aires): 15,100 capacity, ~240 shows and 2.5m visitors/year since 2019.
- Movistar Arena Bogotá: 14,000 capacity; investing in digital ticketing systems and venue connectivity upgrades.
- Full list published in IQ's 2026 Global Arena Guide with Legends Global.

**Arena CDMX** | Mexico City, Mexico

*Concert capacity*: 23,000  
*Operator*: Zignia Live

One of Latin America’s leading entertainment hubs, Arena CDMX has solidified its reputation for hosting the globe’s biggest tours. The venue features some of the world’s largest HD screens, integrated into a central chandelier configuration with four massive displays, alongside a fully illuminated exterior façade that comprises over 6,600 LEDs.

Fans are at the forefront of Arena CDMX’s driving mission, with the venue housing expansive hospitality offerings, including 127 premium suites, court-level VIP bars, two super boxes with private bars, a 400-person terrace, and over 60 F&B service locations. No matter where you’re at in the space, you’ll never miss a moment of the action, with 864 HD screens dotted throughout the venue.

Operationally, the venue is equipped from top to bottom, with an extensive security and surveillance system ensuring safety. Two helipads on the venue’s roof are a unique offering, with fans benefiting from ergonomic seating designed for comfort. Arena CDMX often ranks among the world’s top arenas for ticket sales, reinforcing its position as one of the Americas’ most commercially successful venues.

---

**Movistar Arena Argentina** | Buenos Aires, Argentina

*Concert capacity*: 15,100  
*Operator*: Buenos Aires Arena S.A.

As Argentina’s first purpose-built arena created exclusively for live entertainment, Movistar Arena has helped to transform one of the world’s most passionate markets. Since opening in 2019, the venue has become a cornerstone of international touring in South America, hosting around 240 shows and welcoming more than 2.5m visitors annually.

Designed for flexibility, the arena offers capacities ranging from 4,000 to 15,100, with excellent sightlines, strong acoustics, and touring infrastructure tailored for high-demand productions. A newly installed L-Acoustics sound system delivers uniform audio coverage throughout the building, while a modular staging system, two high-capacity grids (capacity up to 90 tonnes) and four loading docks enable fast turnarounds for all kinds of events. Backstage, facilities include production offices, dressing rooms with private amenities, and a dedicated catering facility with direct vehicle access.

The venue has become a benchmark for guest experience in Latin America, with 20 F&B outlets and premium hospitality areas on offer, with recent renovations improving the guest areas. Movistar Arena boasts an Instagram following of over 1.3 million followers, underlining the strength of its communal reach, unmatched anywhere else in the region.

---

**Movistar Arena Bogotá** | Bogotá, Colombia

*Concert capacity*: 14,000  
*Operator*: Movistar Arena

In the heart of Bogotá sits Movistar Arena, one of Latin America’s premier touring destinations. Through a combination of flexible design, advanced production infrastructure, and strong audience demand, the venue has established itself as an unskippable destination for major tours. Designed with adaptability at its core, the venue features a modular configuration capable of rapid turnarounds, with efficient load-in/out systems, advanced rigging capabilities, and technical infrastructure designed to support complex productions.

From back- to front-of-house offerings, the arena is fully equipped with state-of-the-art systems, with its sound/lighting and hospitality systems tailored to global standards. Beyond production, Movistar Arena continues to invest in fan experience and sustainability initiatives to power its future, including improvements to digital ticketing systems, venue connectivity, waste management, and energy reduction programmes. It is currently investing in developments around audience experience and operational innovation, setting up its future as a market-leading venue.

With a proven track record of sell-out shows, Movistar Arena has contributed to Bogotá’s reputation as a gateway for the Latin American touring circuit.
