# Resurrection Fest draws 140,000 fans to biggest ever edition

## Summary

Spain's Resurrection Fest drew a record 140,000 fans across its four-day, four-stage 2026 edition featuring headliners Iron Maiden, Limp Bizkit, Sabaton, and Marilyn Manson. Organizers say the 21st edition generated over 1,500 direct and 4,000 indirect jobs, with a total economic impact exceeding €110 million for host town Viveiro.

### Key Points
- Resurrection Fest 2026 drew a record 140,000 attendees over four days, its largest edition yet.
- Headliners included Iron Maiden, Limp Bizkit, Sabaton, and Marilyn Manson across ~100 acts.
- Generated 1,500+ direct and 4,000+ indirect jobs; socioeconomic impact over €50M plus €60M in media impact (€110M+ total).
- Promoted by Bring the Noise; credited by the Spanish promoters' association (APM) as Spain's biggest rock festival.

## Transcript

The Association of Spanish Promoters credited the festival, now in its 21st year, as a primary economic engine driving the local region

![](https://www.iqmagazine.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/07/Resurrection-Fest.jpeg)

Resurrection Fest 2026 © Resurrection Fest

Spain’s Resurrection Fest has welcomed its largest ever edition, as it attracted 140,000 fans to its four-day event which ended on 4 July.

The metal festival staged around 100 acts on its four stages, with headline sets coming from Iron Maiden, Limp Bizkit, Sabaton, and Marilyn Manson.

“This year’s edition once again broke attendance records within the festival grounds. Thursday became the day with the highest attendance in Resurrection Fest history, reflecting the enormous interest generated by a lineup that brought together some of the biggest bands on the planet,” organisers said.

Organisers said the 2026 edition generated more than 1,500 direct jobs and more than 4,000 indirect jobs, in addition to a socioeconomic impact exceeding €50 million, saying this demonstrates that host town Viveiro “can be the site of one of Europe’s major music events.”

“This is in addition to an estimated media impact of more than €60 million, placing the overall impact of the festival above €110 million,” organisers said of the economic impact figures.

> “Resurrection Fest leaves behind some of the most spectacular images ever seen in live music in our country”

Promoted by Bring the Noise, Resurrection Fest marked its twentieth anniversary in 2025, with APM, the association of Spanish music promoters, now calling it “the biggest rock festival in Spain and one of the main European benchmarks of the genre”. Announcing the attendance figures, APM credited Bring the Noise for “preserving the identity with which \[the festival\] was born in 2006”.

“Beyond the musical lineup, Resurrection Fest once again demonstrated its importance as an economic driver for Viveiro and Galicia,” said APM.

Speaking to *IQ* about the festival’s 20-year milestone last year, Bring the Noise CEO Iván Méndez said the festival’s consistent draw of fans from a wide range of countries was “something only a few festivals in our country have been able to reach so far”.

“We have a very loyal audience that religiously attend the festival every year and come to Viveiro – the village where the festival takes place. It’s a social event, a holiday destination and also a cultural celebration,” he said.

Speaking after the conclusion of the 2026 event, Bring the Noise said: “The 2026 edition was once again held without any significant incidents, leaving behind some of the most spectacular images ever seen in live music in our country.”
