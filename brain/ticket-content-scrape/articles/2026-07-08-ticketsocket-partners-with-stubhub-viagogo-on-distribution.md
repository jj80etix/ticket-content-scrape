# TicketSocket Partners With StubHub, viagogo on Distribution

## Summary

White-label ticketing platform TicketSocket has added StubHub and viagogo as new distribution partners, letting event organizers push primary inventory directly into both marketplaces via StubHub's "Open Distribution" model. The integration syncs ticket availability automatically across channels while organizers keep their existing ticketing system, branding, and customer data — the transaction itself is handled by whichever marketplace makes the sale. The deal reflects StubHub/viagogo's broader push to position themselves as official primary-distribution channels, not just resale destinations, for a wide range of event types (promoters, festivals, sports, performing arts, universities, fairs, conferences).

### Key Points
- TicketSocket (white-label ticketing/registration platform) integrates with StubHub and viagogo's Open Distribution program, announced 8 July 2026.
- Organizers get access to a marketplace audience described as 125M+ fans across 200+ countries/territories without migrating ticketing systems or entering an exclusive agreement.
- Inventory sync is automatic; sales made via StubHub/viagogo are managed by the marketplace, while availability updates flow back through TicketSocket to limit oversells.
- Quoted: Shaun Stewart (VP of Open Distribution, StubHub) and TicketSocket CEO Mark Miller, both emphasizing organizers retain brand/data/customer-relationship ownership.
- No specific launch clients or commercial terms disclosed; integration is described as quick to activate for existing TicketSocket customers.

### Highlights
- StubHub/viagogo's Open Distribution model is being marketed explicitly as a primary-distribution channel (not just resale) — a direct competitive vector into the primary-ticketing space.
- TicketSocket, a white-label primary platform, is the vendor integrating — a Tier-adjacent competitor shipping a channel-distribution feature (Q12 signal).
- Marketplace reach cited at 125M+ fans across 200+ countries as the value proposition for organizers considering secondary-market distribution deals.
- Structure explicitly preserves organizer ownership of brand, data, and customer relationship — a design choice addressing organizer wariness of resale-channel lock-in.

### Industry Problem
Secondary marketplaces are moving up the value chain to capture primary distribution and demand aggregation, which changes the competitive line between "resale platform" and "primary ticketing partner" that independent primary platforms like Etix compete within (Q6, Q12). Organizers want broader sales reach without sacrificing control of branding, data, or checkout — a want that white-label competitors are now serving through third-party marketplace integrations rather than building the audience themselves.

### Proposed Solution
TicketSocket's answer is a plug-in distribution integration that syncs inventory to StubHub/viagogo while leaving the organizer's core ticketing stack untouched. Etix should monitor how StubHub/viagogo's Open Distribution program evolves as a primary-channel competitor (#monitor) and evaluate whether a similar white-label-friendly marketplace-distribution integration is worth building to offer clients incremental reach without displacing Etix as system of record (#build/#improve).

***The white-label ticketing platform says clients will be able to push inventory directly into StubHub and viagogo’s global marketplaces without changing ticketing systems or giving up control of their brand and customer data.***

TicketSocket is adding StubHub and viagogo as new distribution partners, giving event organizers that use the enterprise white-label ticketing platform a direct path to sell inventory through two of the largest ticket marketplaces in the world.

The partnership, announced Wednesday, is built around StubHub and viagogo’s Open Distribution model, which allows rights holders, venues, promoters and other event organizers to list tickets across the marketplaces while continuing to operate on their existing primary ticketing systems. For TicketSocket clients, the pitch is straightforward: broader distribution and incremental sales without a platform migration, exclusive ticketing agreement, or loss of control over the organizer’s own ticketing operation.

Through the integration, TicketSocket inventory can be distributed directly to StubHub and viagogo, with ticket availability synchronized automatically across channels. When a ticket is purchased through StubHub or viagogo, the transaction is managed through the marketplace while inventory is updated through TicketSocket, a structure intended to reduce oversells and limit additional operational burden for organizers.

The companies said the arrangement gives TicketSocket users access to StubHub and viagogo’s marketplace audience of more than 125 million fans across more than 200 countries and territories.

“Through Open Distribution, organizers keep full ownership of their brand, their data, and their fan relationships, and gain direct access to StubHub and viagogo’s marketplaces,” said Shaun Stewart, VP of Open Distribution at StubHub. “This partnership puts that reach inside the TicketSocket platforms organizers already run.”

TicketSocket CEO Mark Miller framed the deal as an extension of the company’s focus on helping organizers sell more tickets while preserving ownership of the customer relationship.

“This partnership reflects our commitment to helping event organizers maximize every opportunity to sell tickets while maintaining complete control of their brand and customer relationships,” Miller said.

The agreement fits into a broader strategic push by StubHub and viagogo to position their marketplaces not only as resale destinations, but also as official distribution channels for primary ticket inventory. The companies have increasingly described Open Distribution as a way for event organizers to access global demand without handing over control of pricing, branding, data, or the broader fan experience.

That positioning makes sense for TicketSocket, which markets itself as a white-label ticketing and registration platform. The company’s clients include organizations that want to maintain their own branded checkout and customer experience, rather than routing fans through a third-party ticketing brand as the primary point of sale.

For organizers, the appeal is access to another sales channel without restructuring their existing ticketing stack. The companies said the integration is designed for a wide range of event businesses, including independent promoters, festivals, sports organizations, attractions, performing arts venues, universities, fairs, conferences and enterprise event producers.

The announcement does not identify the first TicketSocket clients expected to activate the integration, nor does it provide specific commercial terms. The companies said the integration can be activated quickly, with more events expected to appear across StubHub and viagogo as TicketSocket clients opt into the distribution channel.
