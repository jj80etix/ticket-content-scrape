# BTS set new attendance record at Tottenham Hotspur Stadium

## Summary

BTS set a new attendance record at Tottenham Hotspur Stadium with two sold-out shows this week, drawing roughly 130,000 fans combined as part of their Arirang World Tour — the largest per-show audience at the venue since it opened in 2019. The group next heads to Munich to become the first Korean artists to headline Allianz Arena, while separately facing a stadium-safety dispute over three planned Chile shows.

### Key Points
- ~130,000 fans across two nights, largest per-show crowd at Tottenham Hotspur Stadium since 2019.
- Part of the Arirang World Tour; next stop Munich's Allianz Arena July 11-12.
- Three planned Santiago, Chile shows remain in dispute over stage load concerns on the playing surface.

TheTicketingBusiness News

**BTS has set a new attendance record with their two sold-out concerts at Tottenham Hotspur Stadium this week.**

The K-pop stars entertained roughly 130,000 fans on Monday and Tuesday, drawing the largest per-show audience for any concert since the stadium opened in 2019, according to [reports](https://www.koreaherald.com/article/10803986).

The dates made up part of the group’s Arirang World Tour.

It is not the first time BTS have made history in London, after the group – which consists of RM, Jin, Suga, J-Hope, Jimin, V and Jung Kook – became the first Korean artists to perform at Wembley Stadium in 2019.

BTS will next perform in Munich on July 11-12, where they will also become the first Korean artists to headline Allianz Arena.

Last week, BTS fans in Chile [protested](https://www.theticketingbusiness.com/2026/07/bts-move-closer-to-being-able-to-perform-in-chile/) a recent decision to halt the K-pop group’s three scheduled concerts at Santiago’s Estadio Nacional in October.

The National Sports Research Institute, which is responsible for signing off on the use of the stadium, argued that the group’s 360-degree stage setup would place a load of 600 tonnes on the field, potentially damaging the playing surface. The government also took issue with tickets being sold before the relevant authorisation for the concerts was obtained.

The Chilean government then went on to say the door was open to holding the concerts after promoter DG Medios presented a modified technical proposal, but it is still yet to be confirmed.
