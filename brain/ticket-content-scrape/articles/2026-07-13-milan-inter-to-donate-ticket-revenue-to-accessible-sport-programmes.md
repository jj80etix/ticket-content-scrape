# Milan, Inter to donate ticket revenue to accessible sport programmes

## Summary

AC Milan and Inter Milan will donate proceeds from disability-supporter ticket sales — including a €140,000 lead allocation — to grassroots accessible-sport programmes via a public call for proposals from the City of Milano. The initiative prioritizes inclusive youth sport activities and free summer programming for under-18s.

### Key Points
- €140,000 (largest share) of disability-ticket-sale proceeds funds grassroots accessible-sport organizations.
- Allocated through a City of Milano public call for proposals.
- Priority given to inclusive youth sport programmes and free summer activities for under-18s.
- Remainder managed directly by the two clubs for broader "sport for all abilities" projects.

TheTicketingBusiness News

**Italian football clubs AC Milan and Inter Milan have revealed plans to donate money generated from ticket sales to sports programmes for people with disabilities.**

The funds come from the sale of tickets to supporters with disabilities and the largest share, amounting to €140,000 (£120,000/$160,000), will support grassroots sports organisations across the city.

The funding will be allocated through a public call for proposals launched by the City of Milano, aimed at supporting initiatives with an educational and social inclusion focus through sport.

“The call will be open in particular to organisations that promote inclusive sport activities and integrated sports programmes for young people, encouraging the full participation of people with disabilities,” read a joint statement from the two clubs.

“Priority will also be given to projects offering free summer activities for children and young people under the age of 18, with a particular emphasis on sport, including outdoor activities.

“The remaining share of the proceeds will be managed directly by AC Milan and FC Internazionale Milano to support projects dedicated to sport for all abilities.

“Further information on the sale of season tickets and individual match tickets for supporters with disabilities for the 2026/27 season will be announced in due course through the Clubs’ official channels. As in previous years, the proceeds will once again be dedicated to social projects.”
