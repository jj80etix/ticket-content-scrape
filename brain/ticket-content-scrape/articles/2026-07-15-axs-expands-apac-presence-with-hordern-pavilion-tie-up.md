# AXS expands APAC presence with Hordern Pavilion tie-up

## Summary

AXS has been named the new official ticketing partner for Sydney's 5,500-capacity Hordern Pavilion, taking over from incumbent Ticketek beginning August 10, 2026. The deal extends AEG-owned AXS's push into the APAC region, following its Melbourne Park venue win earlier in 2026, its 2025 acquisition of Singapore's SISTIC, and its Lawson Entertainment joint venture in Japan. AXS will bring its full technology suite — including AXS Mobile ID, mobile ticket management, data/analytics, and marketing tools — to the venue.

### Key Points
- Hordern Pavilion (Sydney, 5,500-cap, opened 1924) switches ticketing provider from Ticketek to AXS effective Aug. 10, 2026.
- AXS also won Melbourne Park (Rod Laver Arena, AAMI Park, John Cain Arena, Margaret Court Arena) earlier in 2026.
- Recent AXS APAC moves: acquired SISTIC (Singapore, 2025) and formed a joint venture with Lawson Entertainment (Japan, 2025).
- AXS now serves 1,600+ clients globally, delivering 70M+ tickets annually.
- Tech bundle includes AXS Mobile ID, mobile ticket management, data/analytics, integrated marketing, and customer support.

### Highlights
- Venue win is a direct incumbent-to-challenger switch: Ticketek (Australia's dominant ticketer) loses a marquee 5,500-cap Sydney venue to AXS, effective Aug. 10, 2026.
- Follows a string of 2025-2026 AXS APAC moves: Melbourne Park (Apr 2026), SISTIC acquisition (Singapore, 2025), Lawson Entertainment JV (Japan, 2025).
- AXS scale cited: 1,600+ clients, 70M+ tickets/year globally.
- Venue and AXS leadership cite "fan-first technology" and focus on fans/promoter partners as deciding factors, though no specific complaint about the outgoing provider is detailed.

### Industry Problem
Venues actively re-shop and switch ticketing partners based on perceived technology and fan-experience advantages, even absent a stated grievance with the incumbent — evidence that account retention is contestable on product capability, not just price or relationship.

### Proposed Solution
The item names no specific new product, but it shows a fully bundled, mobile-first ticketing/ID/analytics/marketing suite is a real switching driver; Etix should ensure its own offering is competitively packaged and marketed when courting venues weighing a switch, and should track win/loss reasoning behind comparable competitor account moves to sharpen its own pitch.

The global ticketing firm will take over ticketing duties for the famed Sydney venue from Ticketek in August

Australia’s Hordern Pavilion has selected AXS as its next official ticketing partner, expanding the global ticketing company’s foothold in the Asia-Pacific region.

The AEG-owned ticketing outlet will take over duties at the 5,500-capacity Sydney venue beginning 10 August, taking over duties from Ticketek.

Originally opening in 1924, the Hordern Pavilion in Sydney’s Moore Park precinct has hosted generations of the world’s biggest artists and events, with Louis Tomlinson, Zara Larsson, Lamb of God, James, Bailey Zimmerman, Death Cab for Cutie, and Two Door Cinema Club all set to pass through this year.

Through the partnership, AXS will provide the venue with its full suite of technology, including AXS Mobile ID, mobile ticket management, data and analytics tools, integrated marketing capabilities, and customer support.

“The Hordern Pavilion has played an iconic role in Australia’s live entertainment landscape for generations,” says Andrew Travis, CEO, AXS Australia & New Zealand.

“We’re incredibly excited to welcome The Hordern to the AXS family. It’s an honour to partner with such an iconic venue and bring our fan-first technology to support its continued success while delivering great fan experiences,” he says.

The partnership strengthens US-headquartered AXS’s growing foothold in the Australia and New Zealand markets, with the company appointed as [Melbourne Park](https://solutions.axs.com/us/2026/04/22/new-ticketing-provider-appointed-at-melbourne-park/) ‘s ticketing provider earlier this year. This includes ticketing at the Rod Laver Arena, as well as sports-focused venues AAMI Park, John Cain Arena, and Margaret Court Arena.

Elsewhere in the APAC market, AXS has also recently expanded its presence in Singapore by [acquiring SISTIC](https://www.iqmagazine.com/2025/10/axs-acquires-singapores-leading-ticketing-firm/)  and in Japan with a joint venture with [Lawson Entertainment](https://www.iqmagazine.com/2025/10/axs-expands-ticketing-operations-in-japan/).

Globally, AXS serves more than 1,600 clients across North America, Europe and Asia-Pacific, delivering more than 70 million tickets annually.

“The Hordern Pavilion has always evolved with the live entertainment industry while staying true to what makes it special,” says Michael Nebenzahl, CEO, Playbill Venues. “Partnering with AXS is our next, natural step in that evolution.

“Their technology and global experience, combined with a genuine focus on fans and our promoter partners, made them the right fit for The Hordern, and we’re excited to work together,” he concludes.
