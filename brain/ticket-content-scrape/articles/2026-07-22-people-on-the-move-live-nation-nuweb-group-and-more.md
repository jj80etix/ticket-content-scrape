# People on the move: Live Nation, Nuweb Group and more…

## Summary

TheTicketingBusiness's personnel roundup: Nuweb Group's head of product departs after six years; Live Nation hires a new SVP for Middle East/Africa venues; the NFL promotes its EMEA ticketing/hospitality lead; and PlayOn Sports names a new CPO and CFO.

### Key Points
- Live Nation adds Pete Griffin (ex-Trivandi) as SVP venues, Middle East & Africa.
- NFL promotes Helen Thomas-Boland to VP, EMEA ticketing and global hospitality sales.
- PlayOn Sports (high-school sports ticketing/streaming) names Dave Bottoms CPO and Celia Poon CFO.

TheTicketingBusiness News

**We round up the latest movements from the ticketing sector and associated industries…**

Nuweb Group’s head of product, **Anthony Escott-Lawrence** has announced he is leaving the company after six years.

In a post on Linkedin, Escott-Lawrence said he would be “enjoying some much-needed downtime” with his family before embarking on his next project.

University of South Dakota Athletics has appointed **Ryan Williams** as director of ticket sales and operations.

Williams joins the Coyotes from Sonoma State, where he was a ticket sales and operations specialist, helping to increase ticket sales through new season ticket and group ticket packages.

Elsewhere, **Helen Thomas-Boland** has been promoted to vice president, EMEA, ticketing and global hospitality sales at the National Football League (NFL).

Based in London, Thomas-Boland has spent over 19 years with the NFL.

**Pete Griffin** has joined Live Nation as senior vice president for venues in the Middle East and Africa.

Griffin was previously executive director at Trivandi, having first joined the company in 2022 as director of strategy and commercial advisory.

PlayOn Sports, a US company that supports high school athletics through its brands which focus on ticketing, streaming, highlights, scores and stats, has announced a new chief product officer and chief financial officer.

**Dave Bottoms**  has joined as chief product officer, while  **Celia Poon** has taken on the role of chief financial officer.

“PlayOn powers how millions of fans and families experience high school sports, from discovering scores and highlights to buying tickets and streaming games,” said Perkins Miller, chief executive of PlayOn Sports.

“Dave and Celia’s deep expertise building category-leading consumer technology platforms will help us accelerate innovation, strengthen the connections between schools and their communities, and expand the reach and impact of high school sports.”
