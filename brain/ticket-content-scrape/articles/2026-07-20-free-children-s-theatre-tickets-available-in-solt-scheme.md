# Free children’s theatre tickets available in SOLT scheme

## Summary

The Society of London Theatre (SOLT) and UK Theatre have launched Kids Week, offering more than 200,000 free tickets for children aged 17 and under accompanied by a full-paying adult, running through August 31. Founded in 1998 by Emma D'Souza, the scheme is now reaching a second generation of theatre fans. Participating shows include Alice In Wonderland, Beetlejuice The Musical, Bluey's Big Play, and The Gruffalo Live On Stage, among others.

### Key Points
- SOLT and UK Theatre launched Kids Week, running until August 31, 2026.
- More than 200,000 free tickets are available for children 17 and under accompanied by a full-paying adult.
- The initiative was founded in 1998 by Emma D'Souza, executive director at SOLT and UK Theatre.
- Participating productions include Alice In Wonderland, Beetlejuice The Musical, Bluey's Big Play, The Cat In The Hat, The Comedy About Spies, The Enormous Crocodile The Musical, and The Gruffalo Live On Stage.

TheTicketingBusiness News

**The Society of London Theatre (SOLT) and UK Theatre has launched Kids Week, an initiative that sees children aged 17 and under able to go to the theatre for free.**

Running until August 31, the scheme has made more than 200,000 tickets available for children that are accompanied by a full-paying adult.

“A trip to the theatre is something everyone should have the opportunity to enjoy – and programmes like Kids Week play a vital role in ensuring as many young people as possible have early access to the arts,” said Kids Week founder and executive director at SOLT and UK Theatre, Emma D’Souza.

“Having founded the initiative in 1998, I’m incredibly proud that Kids Week is now inspiring a second generation of theatre fans, with many who attended as children in the earlier years now returning to share the experience with their own children.

“With thousands of tickets still available, there’s no better time to book something special to enjoy with the family this summer – especially if you can be flexible with dates and shows. Some of the best Kids Week memories are created in those unexpected moments, by those who are willing to try something new!”

Tickets for shows such as Alice In Wonderland, Beetlejuice The Musical, Bluey’s Big Play, The Cat In The Hat, The Comedy About Spies, The Enormous Crocodile The Musical, and The Gruffalo Live On Stage are all available with the initiative.
