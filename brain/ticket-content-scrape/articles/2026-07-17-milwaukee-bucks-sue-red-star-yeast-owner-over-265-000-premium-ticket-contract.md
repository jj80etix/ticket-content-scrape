# Milwaukee Bucks Sue Red Star Yeast Owner Over $265,000 Premium Ticket Contract

## Summary

The Milwaukee Bucks have sued Lesaffre Yeast Corp. (Red Star Yeast) for nearly $265,000 after the company defaulted on a multiyear premium loge-table membership at Fiserv Forum. The suit seeks not just the unpaid current-season balance but the full remaining value of a contract running through 2028-29, illustrating how premium seating packages function as long-term commercial contracts rather than renewable season tickets.

### Key Points
- Complaint filed June 26 in Milwaukee County Circuit Court.
- Lesaffre signed the four-season loge-table agreement in February 2025; total contract value ~$265,000 through 2028-29.
- Unpaid 2025-26 balance is $10,412, but the Bucks are pursuing the entire multi-season commitment.
- Package included four loge seats, VIP arena entry, and premium parking.

The Milwaukee Bucks have sued a corporate premium-seat customer over an alleged payment default, seeking nearly $265,000 under a multiyear ticket agreement extending through the 2028-29 NBA season.

A complaint filed June 26 in Milwaukee County Circuit Court alleges that Lesaffre Yeast Corp., the parent company of the Red Star Yeast brand, failed to complete payments on a premium loge table membership at Fiserv Forum. The Bucks contend that the default entitles the team to collect not only the remaining balance for the recently completed season, but all payments scheduled over the following three seasons.

Lesaffre reportedly signed the agreement in February 2025. The package included four seats at a loge table for Milwaukee Bucks home games and other events held at Fiserv Forum, along with VIP arena entry and a premium parking pass.

Loge tables are positioned around the perimeter of the arena’s main concourse and are marketed as one of the Bucks’ premium seating products. Unlike an ordinary season-ticket purchase that may be renewed annually, the agreement described in the lawsuit committed Lesaffre to escalating payments across four NBA seasons.

For the 2025-26 season, Lesaffre was required to pay a total of $74,000, structured as monthly payments of $6,727.28. The complaint alleges that the company paid $63,588, leaving an unpaid balance of $10,412.

The contract then called for annual payments of $79,180 during the 2026-27 season, $84,723 in 2027-28 and $90,654 in 2028-29. Monthly payments would have risen to $7,554.50 by the final year of the agreement.

Added together, the outstanding first-year amount and the three future seasons total $264,969 — the amount the Bucks are seeking in the lawsuit.

“Despite demands for payment, defendant refuses or neglects to pay plaintiff,” the complaint states, according to reporting from the Milwaukee Journal Sentinel.

The case transforms what the Bucks describe as a relatively modest first-season shortfall into a six-figure dispute because the team is seeking to enforce the remaining value of the entire agreement. The complaint’s allegations illustrate how premium seating memberships, loge products and arena suites can operate as long-term commercial contracts rather than conventional season-ticket subscriptions.

Teams and venue operators generally use multiyear commitments to secure predictable premium-seat revenue, particularly from corporate customers purchasing tickets for employee benefits, client entertainment and business development. In exchange, buyers may receive preferred locations, hospitality benefits, parking and access to events beyond the team’s regular-season schedule.

For purchasers, however, those contracts may leave substantially less flexibility to cancel or reduce inventory when business needs, budgets or demand change. In this case, the Bucks allege that Lesaffre remains responsible for three full future seasons despite defaulting during the first year.

Lesaffre is a global fermentation and baking-ingredients company with North American operations serving commercial bakeries. Red Star Yeast is among the company’s consumer and commercial baking brands.

The Journal Sentinel reported that attempts to obtain comment from a Lesaffre representative were unsuccessful.

The Bucks are seeking judgment for the remaining contract balance and enforcement of the premium seating agreement.
