# StubHub named in US class action over ‘deceptive practices’

## Summary

StubHub and CEO Eric Baker face a $5 million class action lawsuit in New York alleging deceptive practices, after SEC filings revealed Baker is managing partner of Andro Capital, a major seller on StubHub's own platform. The suit, brought by plaintiff Louis Sanquini, argues StubHub misrepresented itself as a neutral fan-to-fan marketplace while its leadership held an undisclosed financial stake in large-scale resale operations supplying much of its inventory. The revelation follows StubHub's recent $10 million FTC settlement over undisclosed fees and reports that a handful of large sellers dominate its UK arena listings.

### Key Points
- Class action filed in New York alleges StubHub concealed CEO Eric Baker's role as managing partner of seller Andro Capital.
- SEC filings show StubHub earned ~$100,000 in fees from Andro-listed tickets in both 2022 and 2023, and paid Andro over $4 million in costs since 2008.
- Lawsuit references StubHub's cancellation of numerous World Cup tickets in the US without notifying holders.
- StubHub US previously paid $10 million to settle FTC charges over undisclosed mandatory fees (announced April 2026).
- Reporting indicates three large-scale sellers supply roughly three-quarters of StubHub UK's arena concert listings.

### Highlights
- $5M class action (Louis Sanquini v. StubHub) filed in New York over alleged deceptive "fan-to-fan" marketing claims.
- SEC filings confirm CEO Eric Baker is managing partner of Andro Capital, a large seller on StubHub since 2008; StubHub paid Andro over $4 million in costs.
- StubHub US already paid a $10M FTC settlement (April 2026) for undisclosed mandatory fees.
- Reported concentration: three sellers supply ~75% of StubHub UK's arena ticket inventory.

### Industry Problem
The case evidences a structural conflict-of-interest problem in secondary ticketing: platforms marketed as neutral peer-to-peer marketplaces are heavily supplied and financially intertwined with large professional resellers, compounding existing fee-transparency and deceptive-pricing regulatory exposure.

### Proposed Solution
Monitor the litigation and any resulting disclosure mandates as a bellwether for resale-market regulation; the trigger for a posture change would be a ruling or settlement forcing seller-identity/conflict-of-interest disclosure, which Etix should preempt by maintaining transparent fee and seller-relationship practices as a primary-market differentiator from resale platforms.

## StubHub named in US class action over ‘deceptive practices’

The news comes days after SEC filings revealed CEO Eric Baker is also managing partner of a seller on the platform

![](https://www.iqmagazine.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/07/StubHub-1.png)

© StubHub

StubHub and its CEO Eric Baker have reportedly been named in a $5 million class action lawsuit for alleged ties to large-scale scalpers, only days after it was revealed Baker is part owner of one such large seller on the ticketing platform.

Filed in New York, the class action reportedly relates to “deceptive practices” alleged by plaintiff Louis Sanquini, whose complaint relates to tickets bought for a KISS concert at Madison Square Garden in 2023, among other sporting events.

StubHub routinely says it is a platform for fan-fan resale of tickets, but has a [long history of criticism](https://www.iqmagazine.com/2025/11/cma-investigates-secondary-ticketing-platforms/) from regulators and live events industries over companies listing tickets, often in large numbers, for profit on its site.

The class action filed in New York represents an undisclosed number of fans in the wake of multiple reports that StubHub recently cancelled significant numbers of World Cup tickets in the US without notifying their putative holders. The lawsuit also reference the recent revelations of CEO Baker’s ties to Andro Capital, an entity known to be selling tickets on StubHub.

“Plaintiff and members of the proposed class purchased tickets on StubHub believing they were buying from individual fans through a neutral marketplace, when in fact StubHub’s own leadership has a direct financial stake in, and StubHub itself helps finance, the large-scale resale operations that supply much of the platform’s inventory,” the lawsuit says, via Billboard. “Defendants’ failure to disclose this conflict of interest, while affirmatively marketing StubHub as a fan-to-fan marketplace, deceived the plaintiff.”

Last week, SEC filings from the company from StubHub also confirmed CEO Baker is managing partner of Andro.

> “StubHub’s own leadership has a direct financial stake in, and StubHub itself helps finance, the large-scale resale operations that supply much of the platform’s inventory”

“Andro Capital is a seller on our platform and, in the normal course of business, has engaged us to support ticket management and list and fulfil its tickets on its behalf since 2008. Eric H. Baker, our founder and chief executive officer, is currently the managing partner of Andro,” the filing says.

It reveals that StubHub generated around $100,000 in fees from tickets sold by Andro on its platform in each of. 2022 and 2023, and that StubHub paid Andro over $4 million dollars in costs relating to ticket sales some time between the end of 2022 and mid-2025.

Earlier this month, *IQ* also revealed that three quarters of tickets listed for UK arena concerts on StubHub UK, operated as a separate entity from StubHub US by StubHub International, came from just three large-scale sellers.

StubHub US was also recently forced to [pay $10 million](https://www.iqmagazine.com/2026/04/stubhub-fined-10-million-in-undisclosed-fee-row/) (€8.52m) to settle charges brought by the US Federal Trade Commission, after the regulator found the company violated federal law by advertising ticket prices without clearly disclosing mandatory fees upfront.

StubHub has repeatedly said it is only a technology platform, that it does not control the behaviour of sellers on its site, and reiterated that its business model is legal.
