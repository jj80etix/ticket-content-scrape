# CMA Fest Taps SeatGeek as Primary Ticketing Partner

## Summary

SeatGeek signed a new multi-year deal to become the primary ticketing partner for CMA Fest, adding one of country music's largest annual festivals to its expanding Nashville portfolio alongside existing deals with Nissan Stadium and the Tennessee Titans. The partnership will carry over when CMA Fest moves to Nissan Stadium's new venue in 2027, and public on-sale begins August 14 with SeatGeek's Buyer Guarantee coverage.

### Key Points
- SeatGeek becomes CMA Fest's primary ticketing partner under a new multi-year agreement.
- Builds on SeatGeek's existing Nashville presence (Nissan Stadium, Tennessee Titans).
- Deal is designed to persist through CMA Fest's 2027 move to Nissan Stadium's new home.
- Public on-sale begins Aug. 14, 2026, with mobile entry and SeatGeek Buyer Guarantee protection.

### Highlights
- New multi-year primary-ticketing deal: SeatGeek + CMA Fest.
- Continuity clause: partnership carries into the 2027 Nissan Stadium relocation.
- SeatGeek framing: "connecting the entire fan journey from discovery to the festival gates."

### Industry Problem
Major festivals are consolidating ticketing under a single primary partner to unify discovery, purchase, and entry — reducing fragmentation that frustrates both organizers (multiple systems) and fans (multiple accounts/apps).

### Proposed Solution
SeatGeek is competing on end-to-end fan-journey integration and buyer guarantees; Etix should track how many Tier 2 competitors are winning multi-year "system of record" deals with major recurring festivals, since that's a durable, hard-to-dislodge revenue category worth targeting for comparable clients.

## Transcript

![](https://www.ticketnews.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/07/seatgeek.jpg)

SeatGeek is strengthening its foothold in Nashville with a new multi-year deal to become the primary ticketing partner of CMA Fest.

The agreement adds one of country music’s biggest annual events to SeatGeek’s growing Nashville portfolio, which already includes official ticketing partnerships with Nissan Stadium and the Tennessee Titans. SeatGeek will begin supporting CMA Fest at the current Nissan Stadium before continuing the partnership when the festival moves to the venue’s new home in 2027.

Through the platform, CMA Fest attendees will be able to browse ticket options, make purchases and manage their tickets from one place, with mobile entry available at the festival. Tickets purchased through SeatGeek will also be verified and covered by the company’s Buyer Guarantee.

“Nashville is one of the great live event markets in the country, and we’ve already seen what a connected ticketing platform can do here with the Titans and Nissan Stadium,” said SeatGeek Co-founder and President of Supply Russ D’Souza. “CMA Fest brings that same opportunity to one of the city’s biggest weekends, connecting the entire fan journey from discovery to the festival gates.”
