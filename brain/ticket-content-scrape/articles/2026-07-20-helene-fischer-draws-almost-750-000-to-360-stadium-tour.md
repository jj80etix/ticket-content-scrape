# Helene Fischer Draws Almost 750,000 To “360° Stadium Tour”

## Summary

Helene Fischer wrapped her "360° Stadium Tour" on July 17 at Munich's Allianz Arena, the last of 15 stadium shows across Germany, Austria, Switzerland, and the Netherlands that drew almost 750,000 fans combined. Live Nation GSA produced and promoted the tour — its second full Fischer tour after her 2023 arena run — and reports record attendance at several venues including Berlin's Olympic Stadium and Hamburg.

### Key Points
- Tour spanned 15 stadium shows in Germany, Austria, Switzerland, and the Netherlands, closing July 17 in Munich.
- Almost 750,000 total attendance across the run, per Live Nation.
- Attendance records reported at Berlin's Olympic Stadium (90,000), Hamburg (120,000 across two shows), and Cologne (85,000).
- Production featured a large floating LED cube stage, 20+ dancers, and a 12-piece band.
- Live Nation GSA also produced Fischer's 2023 "Live" arena tour, which moved over 700,000 tickets.

German superstar Helene Fischer brought her “360° Stadium Tour” to a close on Friday, July 17, at Allianz Arena in Munich – the final of a total 15 performances across stadiums in German, Austria, Switzerland, and the Netherlands.

Almost 750,000 fans came to see the show in total. According to a press release from Live Nation, which produced and promoted the show, it was one of the biggest stadium productions in the world.

A gigantic, floating LED cube formed the center piece of the stage, with multiple walkways connecting Fischer to her fans all around her. On stage, she was supported by more than 20 dancers, including her husband Thomas Seitel, and a 12-piece band, celebrating two-and-a-half hours with all the hits from her 20-year career.

According to Live Nation, the show broke attendance records at several stadiums, including at Berlin’s Olympic Stadium, June 13, in front of 90,000; Hamburg (120,000 across two shows, July 3-4); or Cologne, (85,000, June 26-27).

It’s the second time Live Nation GSA took on production and promotion for an entire Helene Fischer tour, following her “Live 2023” arena tour through Germany, Austria, Switzerland, and the Netherlands. Back then, Fischer performed [mini-residencies across the region’s most popular arenas, moving more than 700,000 tickets](https://news.pollstar.com/2023/04/04/biggest-arena-tour-of-the-year-helene-fischer-sells-more-than-700000-tickets-in-gsa/?_gl=1%2A7sakl4%2A_ga%2AMTM4MzMyNDM3Ny4xNzgyOTc2ODgw%2A_ga_5B6XRECYRM%2AczE3ODQ1MzMxMjckbzQ5JGcxJHQxNzg0NTMzMTM2JGo1MSRsMCRoMA..).

During five shows at Zurich’s Hallenstadion, Sept. 19-24, for instance, Fischer moved a total 45,859 tickets, grossing $6,744,910 in the process, according to the boxoffice reports submitted to Pollstar for the shows.

The reports for her 2026 “360° Stadium Tour” hadn’t been submitted at the time of this writing.

Helene Fischer is managed and represented by Hamburg-based Kanthak Artist Management.
