# Latin stars line up for Venezuela benefit concert

## Summary

IQ Magazine reports on Unidos Por Venezuela, a benefit concert set for August 16 at Miami's Kaseya Center featuring Marc Anthony, Feid, Ricardo Montaner, Jay Wheeler, Elena Rose and Silvestre Dangond, raising funds for victims of Venezuela's recent earthquakes (an estimated 3,500 dead, 16,700+ injured). The event is one of several live-music-industry relief efforts, alongside a Gustavo Dudamel/LA Philharmonic Hollywood Bowl benefit and donations from Metallica and Rush.

### Key Points
- Unidos Por Venezuela plays the 20,000-capacity Kaseya Center on August 16; tickets go on sale July 17 via Ticketmaster.
- Organized by Cardenas Marketing Network, Maestro Cares Foundation, TelevisaUnivision, and Pimiento Entertainment, with iHeartRadio and Spotify support; paired with a live telethon.
- Gustavo Dudamel and the LA Philharmonic will hold a separate benefit at the Hollywood Bowl (cap. 17,500) on August 23 — also Dudamel's final show as the orchestra's music & artistic director.
- Metallica donated $100,000 through All Within My Hands; Rush created a limited-edition charity T-shirt to raise funds.
- Latin American promoters Move Concerts, MD Productions and Jogo are jointly fundraising, with proceeds directed to Dividendo Voluntario para la Comunidad.

Marc Anthony and Feid are among the artists lining up for *Unidos Por Venezuela*, a benefit concert at Miami’s Kaseya Center aiming to raise funds for the victims of Venezuela’s recent earthquakes, which killed over 3,500 people and injured thousands.

The *United For Venezuela* benefit concert, which will also feature Ricardo Montaner, Jay Wheeler, Elena Rose, and Silvestre Dangond, is set for 16 August at the 20,000-capacity US arena, taking place alongside a live telethon set to support the South American country’s recovery and reconstruction.

Cardenas Marketing Network (CMN), Maestro Cares Foundation, TelevisaUnivision, and Pimiento Entertainment are helping facilitate the events, with the support of iHeartRadio, Spotify, Creative Link, EB Public Relations, and Arco Entertainment. Both efforts will be broadcast live across the United States, Latin America, and digital platforms.

Donations will be accepted via QR codes, text messages, digital platforms, and corporate contributions, and will be “administered by recognised international humanitarian organisations to address the needs that arise throughout the different phases of recovery,” say organisers.

The industry’s humanitarian efforts are in response to two massive earthquakes ripping through Venezuela last month, which caused widespread damage across the country and in cities like the capital of Caracas and La Guaira.

The earthquakes, which hit a minute apart from one another, resulted in the death of an estimated 3,500 people, with reports of another 16,700 individuals injured and tens of thousands more missing, according to reports from [*Reuters*](https://www.reuters.com/world/americas/death-toll-venezuela-quakes-rises-3535-thousands-remain-displaced-2026-07-06/).

Tickets will go on sale tomorrow, 17 July, on [Ticketmaster](https://www.ticketmaster.com/event/0D0064EA9A88501D?irgwc=1&afsrc=1&clickid=1H0VCjy1kxyZWNd1KZS4xSxfUkuV%3AZy-TxZKwM0&camefrom=CFC_BUYAT_10078&impradid=10078&REFERRAL_ID=tmfeedbuyat10078&wt.mc_id=aff_BUYAT_10078&utm_source=10078-Skimbit%20Ltd.&impradname=Skimbit%20Ltd.&utm_medium=affiliate&ircid=4272).

It is the latest in a [series of efforts](https://www.iqmagazine.com/2026/07/live-music-industry-rallies-for-venezuela-earthquake-relief/) from the live music industry to help address the aftermath of the twin earthquakes.

> “The suffering is immense, but so is the strength and resilience of our people”

Famed Venezuelan conductor Gustavo Dudamel and the Los Angeles Philharmonic previously announced a benefit concert at the Hollywood Bowl (cap. 17,500), set for 23 August. The performance will also mark the Venezuelan-born conductor’s last as music & artistic director of the LA Philharmonic.

“Venezuela will always be my home, and every moment, my thoughts are with the families whose lives have been forever changed by this tragedy,” he said. “The suffering is immense, but so is the strength and resilience of our people. This concert at the Hollywood Bowl is an invitation to stand together and transform our compassion into action.”

Donations will go to Dudamel’s Earthquake Recovery to Support Venezuelan Communities fund, in partnership with the United Nations Development Programme, and the Development Bank of Latin America and the Caribbean (CAF) fund.

Additionally, Latin America’s leading independent promoter, Move Concerts, has united with other regional promoters, MD Productions and Jogo, to raise funds for victims. People who would like to support the initiative can donate [here](https://causematch.com/Venezuela?utm_source=ig&utm_medium=social&utm_content=link_in_bio).

“We have joined forces with other concert promoters across the region to raise funds for the victims of the recent earthquakes in Venezuela,” Move’s EVP Andres Guanipa told *IQ*.

“All proceeds will be donated to Dividendo Voluntario para la Comunidad, one of Venezuela’s most respected charitable organisations, which has been leading relief efforts for more than 60 years and is providing emergency assistance to families affected by the disaster,” he says.

In addition, Metallica have donated $100,000 through their charitable organisation All Within My Hands, while Rush has created a limited-edition T-shirt to generate donations.
