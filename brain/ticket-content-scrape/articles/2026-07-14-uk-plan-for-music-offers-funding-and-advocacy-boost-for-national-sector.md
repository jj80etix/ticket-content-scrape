# UK ‘Plan for Music’ offers funding and advocacy boost for national sector

## Summary

The UK government unveiled a "Plan for Music" bringing £15 million in new funding and appointing former MP Michael Dugher as the country's first Music Champion, alongside a further £1 million tranche from the LIVE Trust's voluntary ticket-contribution scheme supporting grassroots venues. The plan also includes regulatory relief for festival licensing and event-day allowances.

### Key Points
- £15 million Music Growth Package includes £12.5m for "music lending libraries" and £10m for creative mentoring for care-experienced youth.
- Licensing reforms increase Temporary Event Notices per venue from 15 to 20 annually and total event days from 21 to 26.
- LIVE Trust's £1 voluntary per-ticket contribution (applied to shows above 5,000 capacity) has now distributed £1.5m total, aiding 372 venues, 211 shows, and 81 festivals.
- Industry bodies (Night Time Industries Association, Council of Music Makers) welcomed the plan and Dugher's appointment.

## UK ‘Plan for Music’ offers funding and advocacy boost for national sector

The Plan accompanies a further £1 million funding tranche from the LIVE Trust and will see the government's first Music Champion appointed

![](https://www.iqmagazine.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/06/Crowd_Oasis__CREDIT_Catarina_Walter_Ticketmaster_Brasil-1-scaled.png)

© Catarina Walter

The UK government’s new Plan for Music will bring forward £15 million in funding for the country’s music industry and see the appointment of former MP Michael Dugher to the industry advocacy role of the government’s Music Champion.

Alongside this, £1 million has been unveiled as the latest tranche of funding from the country’s voluntary ticket contribution to support the grassroots live ecosystem.

Dugher will serve a one year unpaid term as the government’s new Music Champion, and will act as a conduit between government and the sector to increase the reach of the government’s new Plan for Music, the government said. After leaving parliament, Dugher was also previously CEO of UK Music and a board member for the Ivors Academy Trust.

As part of the Plan, the Music Growth Package offers a £15 million cash injection to the UK’s music industry, bringing the government’s total support behind 2,000 music projects up to £45 million.

Within this, £12.5 million will be set aside for libraries to be transformed into ‘music lending libraries’, with free studio spaces and live performance opportunities.

Meanwhile, £10 million is reserved for creative mentoring for young people who have spent time in the care system, which the government said will aim to “help them build trusted relationships with creative practitioners through one-to-one support”.

Also part of the package is a cut to “red tape for music festivals”, longer licences to provide security for organisers supporting live outdoor music events, and an increase to the the number of Temporary Event Notices from 15 to 20 per year and the total event days from 21 to 26.

> “It is extremely encouraging to see the Government deliver a long term strategy that recognises music as one of the UK’s greatest cultural and economic assets”

“From artists and fans to venues, festivals, labels and music businesses, *Turn It Up: Our Plan for Music* will create the conditions for the sector, already worth at least £8 billion to the economy, to grow, innovate and succeed,” the government said. “The new strategy reaffirms the Government’s commitment to ensure the UK is the best place in the world to create, experience and invest in music.”

This comes as the LIVE Trust also announces a £1m funding boost for UK grassroots live venues, which takes the total amount distributed by the Trust to £1.5m. The funding is paid out of £1 voluntary ticket contribution, which many venues apply to each ticket at shows above 5,000 capacity.

Earlier this year, LIVE Trust [distributed £500,000](https://www.iqmagazine.com/2026/01/live-trust-distributes-first-tranche-of-funding/) through its Phase One funding programme to support artists, venues, festivals, promoters, and others across the live music industry. That funding has already helped 372 venues, 211 shows and 81 festivals across the UK.

“Since launching in January 2025, LIVE Trust has secured more than 6 million ticket pledges, working  
with 10 promoters across more than 100 tours. Less than a year since LIVE Trust’s first meeting, the  
latest figures highlight the rapid and continuing growth in support for an industry-led funding model  
for grassroots live music,” LIVE Trust said.

The announcement was broadly welcomed by UK live sector figures. Night Time Industries Association CEO Michael Kill said: “It is extremely encouraging to see the Government deliver a long term strategy that recognises music as one of the UK’s greatest cultural and economic assets. We have been proud to work alongside colleagues from across the industry to help shape this plan and it is positive to see that collaboration translate into meaningful action.”

Meanwhile, the Council of Music Makers, a coalition of industry bodies The Ivors Academy, Musicians’ Union, Music Managers Forum, Music Producers Guild, and the Featured Artists Coalition, said: “We welcome the publication of the government’s Music Plan and the appointment of Michael Dugher to the role of Music Champion, and look forward to working with him and the government to deliver this plan in a way that delivers maximum value and support for British music-makers.

“Music-makers interact with government and policy-makers on a wide range of issues, including copyright, streaming, AI, ticketing, touring, visas, education and the need for an accessible, safe, equitable and inclusive music industry infrastructure, and an ecosystem that proactively and fairly supports the many businesses that music-makers work with and the businesses that they run.”
