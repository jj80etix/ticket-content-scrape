# Charlotte FC ends sale of permanent seat licenses

## Summary

Charlotte FC (MLS) is ending the sale of permanent seat licenses (PSLs) — a one-time upfront fee required to buy season tickets — after fan focus groups convened by parent company Tepper Sports & Entertainment last month. The club will credit its roughly 15,000 existing PSL holders 100% of what they paid, applied against season-ticket purchases over the next two seasons, and will cut average season-ticket prices about 6% next year. The reversal follows a stretch of declining demand: despite ranking fourth in MLS average attendance (28,043/game), Charlotte FC has seen home attendance fall 16% since 2023 and season-ticket sales drop 15-25%.

### Key Points
- Charlotte FC was the only MLS club selling PSLs; PSL prices ranged $450-$900 plus a $550 one-time fee.
- Roughly 15,000 PSL holders get a 100% account credit, applied over the next two seasons' season-ticket purchases.
- Average season-ticket price is being cut about 6% for next season (a mix of cuts, flat pricing, and some increases).
- Home attendance fell 16% between 2023 and 2025; season-ticket sales dropped 15-25% over the same period.
- The pricing reversal followed fan focus groups run by parent company Tepper Sports & Entertainment last month.
- Charlotte FC currently ranks fourth in MLS average attendance at 28,043 fans/game despite the decline.

### Highlights
- Upfront PSL fees ($550 one-time plus $450-900 licensing) coincided with a 15-25% season-ticket sales decline and a 16% attendance drop over two years.
- The club is refunding 100% of PSL cost as account credit to ~15,000 holders — unwinding the fee model rather than tweaking it.
- Fan focus-group feedback directly drove the pricing-model reversal, paired with a 6% average season-ticket price cut.

### Industry Problem
Upfront licensing fees layered on top of season-ticket prices created enough patron resistance to measurably erode both attendance and renewal rates for a top-quartile-attendance club, showing legacy fee structures can suppress demand even at an otherwise healthy franchise.

### Proposed Solution
Charlotte FC's fix was to scrap the PSL fee entirely and refund it as account credit; for Etix, this evidences client demand for flexible, low-friction season-ticket/package pricing tools — configurable products without punitive upfront licensing, plus buyer-sentiment surveying features — that help venue/team clients catch and correct fee-model problems before losing renewals.
