# College athletics platform Impact Tickets launches its own resale marketplace with Nuweb Group

## Summary

College-athletics ticketing provider Impact Tickets has launched native, verified resale functionality built on its existing technology partnership with Nuweb Group, letting fans relist tickets through the same purchase flow used for primary sales. The move keeps resale transactions, customer data, and revenue inside the organizer's own platform rather than routing fans to third-party resale marketplaces.

### Key Points
- Impact Tickets serves college athletics clients including Jackson State University, Alcorn State University, and Mississippi Valley State University.
- Nuweb Group, already Impact Tickets' ticketing technology partner, built the new resale layer on the same system used for primary sales.
- Organizers retain control over resale pricing, rules, fees, and policies under the new functionality.
- CEO James Covington says resale is meant to reduce refund requests and chargebacks when customers can't attend, while keeping fans off third-party marketplaces.
- Nuweb's Head of Product, Anthony Escott-Lawrence, frames the launch as supporting "fair resale" without encouraging price inflation or speculative reselling.

### Highlights
- A direct primary-ticketing competitor (Impact Tickets, college-athletics segment) shipped first-party resale built into its core purchase flow rather than outsourcing resale to a third party — a concrete feature launch.
- Stated business rationale: keeping resale in-house is meant to cut refund requests/chargebacks and retain resale revenue, data, and the fan relationship within the organizer's own ecosystem.
- Organizers keep full control of resale pricing, rules, and fees rather than ceding that to third-party marketplaces, positioning the feature against speculative/inflated resale.

### Industry Problem
Organizers lose revenue, data, and control of the fan relationship — and absorb avoidable refund/chargeback costs — when ticket holders who can't attend are pushed onto third-party secondary marketplaces instead of a sanctioned in-platform resale path.

### Proposed Solution
Impact Tickets/Nuweb's answer is embedded, organizer-controlled resale inside the primary purchase flow; Etix could build or expand a comparable native resale/transfer feature so clients keep resale revenue and data in-platform and reduce refund/chargeback load, matching a shipped capability from a direct primary-ticketing peer.

Impact Tickets has launched native resale functionality powered by Nuweb Group, enabling verified ticket resale directly within its ticketing platform.

The US-based ticketing provider, which works with college athletics clients including Jackson State University (JSU), Alcorn State University, and Mississippi Valley State University, will offer resale through the same system used for primary ticketing.

[Nuweb Group](https://www.nuwebgroup.com/?utm_campaign=impact-resale&utm_medium=featured&utm_source=ticket-news), which already serves as Impact Tickets’ technology partner, is extending the partnership to include resale.

The functionality allows ticket holders to relist tickets through the original purchase journey, while organizers retain control over pricing, rules, fees, and policies.
