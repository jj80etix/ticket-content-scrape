# ‘Weird Al’ Yankovic Says He Turned Down Six-Figure Deal to Perform at Saudi Arabia Comedy Festival

## Summary

"Weird Al" Yankovic revealed in a TribLIVE interview that he turned down a seven-figure offer to perform at the 2025 Riyadh Comedy Festival in Saudi Arabia, citing discomfort with the engagement despite it being the largest single-show payday he's ever been offered. He declined to criticize peers like Dave Chappelle, Pete Davidson, Bill Burr, Louis C.K. and Aziz Ansari who did perform there, while noting other comedians (Marc Maron, Shane Gillis, Zach Woods) publicly criticized those who accepted, citing Saudi human rights concerns. Yankovic separately mentioned turning down a lucrative AI-related commercial deal earlier this year. He remains on the road with his 90-city "Bigger & Weirder Tour" through October.

### Key Points
- Yankovic turned down a seven-figure offer to perform at the September 2025 Riyadh Comedy Festival.
- Riyadh Comedy Festival headliners included Dave Chappelle, Pete Davidson, Bill Burr, Louis C.K. and Aziz Ansari; Marc Maron, Shane Gillis and Zach Woods publicly criticized performers over Saudi human rights issues.
- Yankovic also declined a separate paid commercial deal after learning it involved AI.
- He is currently on his 90-city "Bigger & Weirder Tour" across the US and Canada, running from May through October 2026.

## Transcript

“Weird Al” Yankovic has spoken out about his decision to turn down a six-figure deal to perform at the Saudi Arabia Comedy Festival last year.

During an interview with [*TribLIVE*](https://triblive.com/aande/music/weird-al-yankovic-gets-bigger-and-weirder-in-a-tour-headed-to-pittsburgh/), Yankovic explained that at this point in his career, he’s in a position where he can turn down appearances that don’t resonate with him, telling the Pittsburgh-area newspaper that “I wish people would offer me things that I didn’t feel bad about accepting.”

“I don’t think I’ve mentioned this anywhere, but I was invited to the Riyadh Comedy Festival, which is something that I obviously did not want to do,” Yankovic told the publication. “They offered me, I’m not going to say how much, but it was seven figures to do one show, which is way more than I’ve ever been offered in my life to do a show. But I just couldn’t be part of that.”

The Riyadh Comedy Festival, held in September 2025, featured headliners Dave Chappelle, Pete Davidson, Bill Burr, Louis C.K., and Aziz Ansari. While the former all defended their appearances, Marc Maron, Shane Gillis, and Zach Woods publicly criticized those who performed, pointing to the Saudi Arabian government’s prior human rights abuses and restrictions of free speech.

Yankovic noted that he’s “not dissing anybody that took the money because I’m sure everybody that went there got a lot of money,” adding, “I’m sure they had their own reasons and justifications for doing it.”

“But I’m doing enough that I don’t need to take any amount of money if it makes me feel icky, and that kind of did,” Yankovic said.

This isn’t the first time Yankovic has spoken out about a matter he feels strongly about; earlier this month, [he rejected](https://www.syracuse.com/entertainment/2026/06/weird-als-secret-hes-just-a-normal-guy-who-loves-fun-interview-syracuse-concert-preview.html) a “nice pile of money” to appear in a commercial for a business software that he later found out was connected to AI. While he initially accepted the offer, he learned a week before the shoot that it’s AI, noting: “I’m not down with that.”  
  
Yankovic is currently crossing North America on his “Bigger & Weirder Tour,” which was extended with a whopping 90-city schedule across the U.S. and Canada. The massive run, which kicked-off in May, will wrap-up in October.
