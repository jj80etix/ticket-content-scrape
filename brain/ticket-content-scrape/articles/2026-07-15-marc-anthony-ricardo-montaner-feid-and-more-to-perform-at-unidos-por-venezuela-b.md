# Marc Anthony, Ricardo Montaner, Feid And More To Perform At ‘Unidos Por Venezuela’ Benefit Concert

## Summary

Latin music stars including Marc Anthony, Ricardo Montaner, Feid, Jay Wheeler, and Elena Rose will headline "Unidos Por Venezuela," a benefit concert and telethon at Miami's Kaseya Center on August 16, raising funds for earthquake recovery in Venezuela. The show, produced by Cárdenas Marketing Network with TelevisaUnivision and other partners, follows a pair of devastating earthquakes that struck Venezuela on June 24.

### Key Points
- Benefit concert "Unidos Por Venezuela" at Kaseya Center, Miami, Aug. 16, 2026; 14+ artists confirmed, more to be announced.
- Lineup includes Marc Anthony, Ricardo Montaner, Feid, Jay Wheeler, Elena Rose, and others.
- Produced by Cárdenas Marketing Network, TelevisaUnivision, PIMIENTO Entertainment, and Maestro Cares Foundation; supported by Spotify, iHeartRadio, EB Public Relations, and Arco Entertainment.
- Proceeds go to foundations for Venezuela's earthquake recovery and reconstruction.
- Tickets go on sale via Ticketmaster on July 17 at 10 a.m. local time.
- Context: June 24 earthquakes (7.2 then 7.5 magnitude) left a death toll of 4,734, with 16,740 injured and nearly 18,000 homeless.

Some of Latin music’s biggest names are gathering at Kaseya Center in Miami to raise money for those affected by the devastating earthquakes in Venezuela.

Marc Anthony, Ricardo Montaner, Feid, Jay Wheeler and Elena Rose are among the 14 artists confirmed to perform at “Unidos Por Venezuela,” a benefit concert and national telethon on Aug. 16, with additional acts to be announced soon.

Other artists on the bill include Silvestre Dangond, Gente De Zona, Mau y Ricky, Olga Tañon, Lasso, Sanluis, Piso 21, Alleh, Zhamira Zambrano and Free Cover.

Cárdenas Marketing Network, one of the top Latin music promoters in the business, is putting on the event alongside TelevisaUnivision, PIMIENTO Entertainment and Maestro Cares Foundation. The benefit show is also supported by Spotify, iHeartRadio, EB Public Relations and Arco Entertainment.

All proceeds from the concert will go to foundations committed to the recovery and reconstruction of Venezuela, CMN said in the show’s announcement.

Tickets go on sale on [Ticketmaster](https://www.ticketmaster.com/event/0D0064EA9A88501D?CAMEFROM=CFC_601BISCAYNE_KCCOM&promo_id=kcdotcom&promo_name=unidos-por-venezuela_2026&promo_creative=button&promo_position=event_details&_gl=1*1yxrulu*_gcl_au*MTk1NTU0MjE2OS4xNzg0MTI2NTM5*_ga*MTMzNTUxOTk3MC4xNzg0MTI2NTM5*_ga_3YQTEV8J3F*czE3ODQxMjY1MzkkbzEkZzAkdDE3ODQxMjY1MzkkajYwJGwwJGgxMTUzODA4NTY4) July 17 at 10 a.m. local time.

A 7.2-magnitude earthquake, followed by a magnitude 7.5 quake less than a minute later, affected Northwestern and Central Venezuela on June 24. The death toll from the massive earthquakes is now up to 4,734, according to [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/world/americas/death-toll-venezuela-quakes-rises-4734-government-says-2026-07-14/), and 16,740 were left injured while nearly 18,000 remain homeless.
