# The Eagles Extend Las Vegas Sphere Residency Through December 2026

## Summary

The Eagles have added four more Las Vegas Sphere performances (December 4, 5, 11, and 12, 2026), bringing their total residency count to 68 shows and making them Sphere's longest-running residency. The extension follows earlier expansions of a run that began in September 2024, alongside the band's separate "Long Goodbye, Act III" farewell tour dates elsewhere in the US earlier this year. Artist presale (via Seated) opens July 15, with general onsale through Ticketmaster on July 17.

### Key Points
- Four new December 2026 Sphere dates added (12/4, 12/5, 12/11, 12/12), bringing the residency to 68 total performances.
- Eagles are now Sphere's longest-running residency; the engagement first launched in September 2024.
- Prior 2026 dates were already scheduled for September and November.
- The band also played separate farewell shows in Georgia, Florida, Texas, and Tennessee as part of "The Long Goodbye, Act III."
- Artist presale (via Seated) begins July 15 at 10 a.m. PT; general public onsale via Ticketmaster begins July 17 at 10 a.m. local time.

## Transcript

![](https://www.ticketnews.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/07/The-Eagles-Sphere-December-2026.webp)

The Eagles are extending their Las Vegas residency once again, announcing four additional performances at Sphere that are set for December 4, 5, 11, and 12, 2026.

The newly announced concerts increase the band’s total number of Sphere performances to 68, making the Eagles the venue’s longest-running residency. The latest dates extend the engagement through December after previously announced shows scheduled for September and November 2026.

The Eagles first launched their Sphere residency in September 2024. Since opening the engagement, the band has repeatedly expanded the schedule in response to demand, adding multiple rounds of performances that have steadily increased the length of the run.

While the Las Vegas residency has remained the band’s primary focus, the Eagles also performed several farewell concerts outside Nevada earlier this year as part of “The Long Goodbye, Act III” tour. Those shows brought the group to Georgia, Florida, Texas, and Tennessee before returning to Sphere.
