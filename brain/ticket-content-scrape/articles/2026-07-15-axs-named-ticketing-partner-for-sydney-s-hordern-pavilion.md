# AXS named ticketing partner for Sydney’s Hordern Pavilion

## Summary

AXS has become the official ticketing partner for Sydney's Hordern Pavilion, effective August 10, taking over from Ticketek Entertainment Group. The AEG subsidiary will supply its full technology suite — including AXS Mobile ID, ticket management, data/analytics, marketing tools and customer support — to the historic 5,500-capacity venue operated by Playbill Venues, itself majority-owned by AEG.

### Key Points
- AXS becomes exclusive ticketing partner for Hordern Pavilion starting August 10, replacing incumbent Ticketek Entertainment Group.
- Deal includes AXS Mobile ID, mobile ticket management, data/analytics, integrated marketing, and customer support.
- AEG holds a 90% stake in Playbill Venues, the venue's operator, per an Australian Financial Review report.
- Hordern Pavilion (5,500 capacity, opened 1924) has hosted acts including Fleetwood Mac, Arctic Monkeys, and Oasis.

### Highlights
- AXS wins the Hordern Pavilion contract effective August 10, displacing incumbent Ticketek.
- Win is tied to AEG's ownership stake (90%, per AFR) in the venue operator Playbill Venues, an internal-ecosystem consolidation play.
- Package bundled includes AXS Mobile ID, analytics, marketing, and support — a full-suite competitive offer, not just ticketing.

### Industry Problem
Consumer-major platforms are winning venue contracts partly by leveraging ownership/affiliate ties to venue operators and bundling mobile ID, analytics and marketing into a single package, raising the bar for independent platforms competing on venue deals.

### Proposed Solution
Watch for similar AEG/AXS-affiliated venue wins as a pattern (ownership-driven lock-in); Etix should ensure its own bundled offering (mobile ID/analytics/marketing/support) is packaged competitively when pitching venues that lack an affiliated major behind them.

TheTicketingBusiness News

**AXS has been selected as the official ticketing partner for Sydney’s Hordern Pavilion starting from August 10.**

The collaboration will see AXS power ticketing for concerts and events at the Australian venue which is located in Sydney’s Moore Park precinct.

Through the new partnership, AXS will provide the Hordern Pavilion with its full suite of ticketing technology, including AXS Mobile ID, mobile ticket management, data and analytics tools, integrated marketing capabilities, and customer support.

It marks another loss for Ticketek Entertainment Group, which previously held a partnership with the venue’s operator [Playbill Venues](https://www.theticketingbusiness.com/2024/08/australias-playbill-group-joins-forces-with-frontier-touring-and-aeg-presents/). AXS is a fully owned subsidiary of AEG, which first agreed a partnership with Playbill in 2024, alongside Frontier Touring (AEG formed a joint venture with [Frontier Touring](https://www.theticketingbusiness.com/2019/04/aeg-presents-frontier-touring-team-asia-pacific-growth/) back in 2019).

While financial terms of the 2024 deal were not disclosed, according to a report from the *[Australian Financial Review](https://www.afr.com/companies/media-and-marketing/us-ticketing-giant-buys-sydney-venue-to-win-contract-from-ticketek-20260714-p60f6b)*, shareholding details with the country’s corporate regular show AEG has a 90% stake in Playbill.

“The Hordern Pavilion has always evolved with the live entertainment industry while staying true to what makes it special,” said Michael Nebenzahl, chief executive of Playbill Venues.

“Partnering with AXS is our next, natural step in that evolution. Their technology and global experience, combined with a genuine focus on fans and our promoter partners, made them the right fit for The Hordern, and we’re excited to work together.”

Hordern Pavilion is claimed to have played a defining role in Australia’s live entertainment scene, hosting some of the world’s biggest artists and events since its opening in 1924.

The 5,500-capacity venue has welcomed the likes of Fleetwood Mac, Arctic Monkeys, Oasis, The Kinks and more over the years.

“The Hordern Pavilion has played an iconic role in Australia’s live entertainment landscape for generations,” said Andrew Travis, CEO of AXS Australia & New Zealand.

“We’re incredibly excited to welcome The Hordern to the AXS family.

“It’s an honour to partner with such an iconic venue and bring our fan-first technology to support its continued success while delivering great fan experiences.”
