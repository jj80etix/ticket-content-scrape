# Conference news: Live Nation announce new music tourism summit

## Summary

Live Nation and travel-intelligence firm Skift are launching the Skift Live Tourism Summit in New York on September 22, targeting senior executives at the intersection of live events and travel. The article also rounds up programming news for BIME Bilbao, Iceland Airwaves' first London showcase, and the DJ Awards' 25th-anniversary edition.

### Key Points
- Skift Live Tourism Summit debuts Sept. 22 at NYC's North Javits Center, with speakers including Live Nation's Russell Wallach and tourism executives from Visit Philadelphia and Las Vegas.
- Live Nation cites nearly six in ten music fans traveling for live music annually, an estimated 40 billion miles traveled for live music in 2025.
- BIME Bilbao's 2026 edition (27-30 October) confirms early speakers/artists including Jorge Drexler and YouTube/Activision executives.
- Iceland Airwaves holds its first London showcase (10-12 September); DJ Awards marks its 25th anniversary at Amnesia Ibiza on Oct. 7.


## Transcript

The promoter will team up with tourism industry firm Skift for the September event, while BIME Bilbao reveals 2026 programme, and Iceland Airwaves announces UK showcase

![Nubank Parque](https://www.iqmagazine.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/Nubank-Parque-©-Live-Nation.png)

Nubank Parque © Live Nation

Live Nation has unveiled a new conference, known as the Skift Live Tourism Summit, set to take place in New York on 22 September this year, and which is aimed at senior executives in the live event tourism space.

The new conference is the result of a partnership between Live Nation and Skift, a travel and tourism industry intelligence platform, and will focus on the growing travel and tourism ecosystem surrounding live music experiences.

Speakers so-far announced for the inaugural event at the North Javits Centre in New York include Russell Wallach, Live Nation global president of media and sponsorship, as well as tourism figures including Visit Philadelphia CEO Angela Val and Steve Hill, CEO of La Vegas Convention and Visitors Authority, with more to be announced.

“Concerts, festivals, sports and cultural events now influence how people choose destinations, when they travel and how cities compete for visitors,” organisers said.

“This creates a massive ripple effect across flight bookings, lodging, local experiences, and distribution. As travellers increasingly flock to these massive spectacles, the strategic question facing the industry is: how do you convert these moments into lasting demand and growth?,” they added.

Live Nation noted in its announcement on the conference that close to six in ten music fans now travel for live music every year, and that in 2025 music fans travelled an estimated 40 billion miles for live music.

**BIME Bilbao  
**In other conference news, BIME Bilbao has announced its program for its 2026 edition set to take place from 27 to 30 October.

Among the first confirmations for the event are artists Jorge Drexler and Ángeles Toledano, and Rocío Márquez, as well as producer Raül Refree, AJ Ramos, head of music content partnerships for YouTube in EMEA, Brandon Young, senior director of music affairs at Activision, intellectual property lawyer JJ Sunday, composer Yassir, and cultural economics expert Justin O’Connor.

“ The BIME Pro program will once again be structured around four main thematic axes: Creation, Music Industry, Development and Connections, addressing some of the main challenges and opportunities facing the sector,” organisers said.

**Iceland Airwaves  
**Iceland Airwaves Conference has also announced a musical showcase for its first ever London event, which will bring Icelandic artists to East London venue Colours.

The conference will hold a London edition for the first time between 10 and 12 September, following a history of touring the event around cities in the US, Canada, Germany, and Japan. Artists set for the showcase include Spacetime and Virgin Orchestra.

“Across the week Taste of Iceland London will offer a range of events showcasing the very best that Iceland has to offer, from Icelandair’s Iceland by the World’s Worst Photographer exhibition and an enthralling conversation on Iceland Noir with Ragnar Jónasson and Yrsa Sigurðardóttir to a Skin Science Masterclass with Blue Lagoon, as well as multiple chances to win exclusive prizes via the Wheel of Prizes, Presented by Icelandair,” organisers said.

**DJ Awards**  
Finally, the DJ Awards has announced its 25th anniversary edition at Amnesia Ibiza on 7 October this year, which it says will “honour the executives, festivals, icons, lifetime achievers, social innovators and artists shaping the electronic sound of now across a range of genres.”

The event will reward figures from brands and businesses in the electronic music space, while a separate public vote will decide awards for DJ artists. Last year’s awards attracted 250,000 votes, the largest number in the event’s history.
