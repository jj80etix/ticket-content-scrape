# Tour news: Young Thug reveals first headline tour since 2019

## Summary

A roundup of newly announced tour dates across multiple artists: Young Thug's 24-date New Generation Tour (his first headline run since 2019, kicking off September 1 in Rogers, Arkansas, promoted by Live Nation), Bob Dylan's UK/EU leg of the Rough and Rowdy Ways Tour, Thundercat's 32-date Distracted AF tour, Ken Carson's 14-date US run, Kasabian's five UK arena dates, Jordan Rakei's biggest-ever European/UK run, Jimmy Eat World's Bleed American anniversary Australian shows, and adjustments to Lily Allen's West End Girl Tour (several US dates downsized to theatres, a Montreal show cancelled).

### Key Points
- Young Thug's New Generation Tour: 24 dates across US/Europe starting September 1 in Rogers, Arkansas; promoted by Live Nation with Young Stoner Life Records.
- The Young Thug tour will use Spotify's Reserved feature, which sets aside tickets for an artist's top Spotify fans ahead of general sale.
- Bob Dylan's UK/EU leg launches October 17 in Oslo and wraps December 8, including five nights at London's Royal Festival Hall.
- Thundercat's Distracted AF tour: 32 dates, October 2 – December 11, including Kings Theatre (Brooklyn) and Hollywood Palladium.
- Kasabian's Live Nation-promoted UK arena run starts December 11 in Birmingham, ending December 19 at Manchester's Co-op Live, with The Vaccines supporting.
- Lily Allen downsized several West End Girl Tour dates (Philadelphia, Chicago, LA, San Francisco, Vancouver) to theatres and cancelled a Montreal show without rescheduling; her September 3 Madison Square Garden date is unaffected.

## Transcript

Plus Bob Dylan extends run, US dates announced by Thundercat and Ken Carson, while Kasabian and Jordan Rakei unveil UK shows, and more

![](https://www.iqmagazine.com/wp-content/smush-webp/2026/07/DESKTOP-scaled.jpg.webp)

Leading the latest round of tour news, **Young Thug** has announced a 24-date *New Generation Tour* across the US and Europe in September and October, his first headlining run since 2019.

Promoted by Live Nation and presented in association with Young Stoner Life Records, the tour will kick off on 1 September in Rogers, Arkansas, and go on to visit theatres and amphitheatres in cities across the US. This is followed by a four-date date European leg spanning Amsterdam’s AFAS Live, Düsseldorf’s PSD Bank Dome, Łódź’s Atlas Arena, and Paris’ Adidas Arena.

Highlights of the US leg include the Aragon Ballroom in Chicago, MGM Music Hall at Fenway in Boston, and SummerStage in Central Park in New York.

The tour will make use of Spotify’s recently launched [Reserved feature](https://www.iqmagazine.com/2026/06/role-model-up-first-for-spotifys-reserved-tour-ticket-initiative/), meaning the artists’ top fans on Spotify will have tickets set aside for them before general sale begins.

Bob Dylan will continue his *Rough and Rowdy Ways Tour* later this year, announcing a slate of UK/EU dates to follow his run across the US.

The outing launches on 17 October at Oslo’s Spektrum, with the touring veteran set to perform in Sweden, Denmark, Germany, Belgium, Luxembourg, Switzerland, Italy, Slovenia, Austria, and the UK, wrapping up on 8 December.

The ITB-repped artist will play a mix of venue sizes, lining up dates at arenas like Milan’s new Unipol Dome and London’s Royal Festival Hall, where he is due to play five nights.

Back in North America, **Thundercat** will hit the road, with the bassist and songwriter revealing his *Distracted AF* tour. It will begin on 2 October at Miami Beach Bandshell and conclude at Austin’s Holiday Hootenanny on 11 December.

The run spans 32 dates in total, with Canadian stops in Montreal and Canada, and will be highlighted by stops at the Kings Theatre in Brooklyn and the Hollywood Palladium.

Also announcing a US run is **Ken Carson**, with the rapper revealing a 14-date tour set to begin on 26 August at Chicago’s Huntington Bank Pavilion. The tour will run across amphitheatres in cities including Washington DC, Miami, Atlanta, Austin, and San Francisco, before concluding in Los Angeles on 3 October.

The US leg will follow the artist’s ongoing European run, which has one remaining date set for Austria’s Frequency Festival next month.

Back in the UK, **Kasabian** have unveiled five arena dates across the country to follow the band’s performance to 45,000 at London’s Finsbury Park earlier this month.

The band will kick off the Live Nation-promoted tour at the Utilita Arena in Birmingham on 11 December, followed by dates at the OVO Hydro in Glasgow, Utilita Arena in Cardiff, The O2 in London, before finishing at Manchester’s Co-op Live on 19 December. The Vaccines will join the bill as a support act.

Meanwhile, **Jordan Rakei** will also feature a UK run as part of his largest UK and European run to-date, culminating in what will be the biggest concert of his career at Alexandra Palace on 24 April 2027.

Three UK dates, including the Alexandra Palace show, will also see the artist visit the Bristol Beacon and Albert Hall, Manchester, and will follows a 14-night European run, which will begin in Stockholm at 27 March and end at Cologne’s Stadthalle on 14 April.

Elsewhere, **Jimmy Eat World** have announced their  *Bleed American* 25th anniversary tour of Australia, with four shows in Sydney, Melbourne, Adelaide, and Brisbane between 10 and 15 December.

Finally, **Lily Allen** has downsized several shows on the upcoming North American leg of her *West End Girl Tour* to theatre-sized venues. The changes affect dates in Philadelphia, Chicago, Los Angeles, San Francisco, and Vancouver. A Montreal show has also been cancelled without a replacement date.

The 3 September Madison Square Garden concert will go ahead as originally planned, and the singer-songwriter has also added a Seattle date at the city’s Paramount Theatre. This comes after a three-night run at London’s O2 Arena as part of the UK leg of the tour.
