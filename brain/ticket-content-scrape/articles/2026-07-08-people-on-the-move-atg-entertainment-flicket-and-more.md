# People on the move: ATG Entertainment, Flicket and more…

## Summary

TheTicketingBusiness rounds up personnel moves across the ticketing and live-entertainment sector. ATG Entertainment strengthened its UK theatre programming team, adding Charlotte Longstaff as London programming director; Live Nation hired a new SVP of global programming for family/immersive formats; Superstruct Entertainment UK named a new managing director; and event-commerce platform Flicket added a CFO and expanded its client-success team with hires from SeatGeek, AXS, and Ticketek.

### Key Points
- ATG Entertainment strengthens UK theatre programming: Charlotte Longstaff (ex-LW Theatres) returns as London programming director; Michael Lynas shifts focus to global content strategy.
- Live Nation hires Jonathan Shank as SVP of global programming, focused on family entertainment and non-traditional immersive formats.
- Superstruct Entertainment UK appoints Jacqui Harris (ex-AEG Presents UK) as managing director.
- Legends Global names Matthew Lazarus-Hall general manager of Sydney's Afterpay Arena.
- Flicket adds a CFO and expands its global client-success team with hires poached from SeatGeek, AXS, and Ticketek.

TheTicketingBusiness News

**We round up the latest movements from the ticketing sector and associated industries…**

ATG Entertainment has strengthened its UK theatre programme team, in a bid to provide an enhanced service to producers and bring the best shows to its London and regional venues.

**Michael Lynas**, global chief content officer, will increase his focus on guiding ATG Entertainment’s global programming and production investment strategy, while  **Stuart Griffiths**  will continue his role as director of UK programming, working with  **Nicky Monk** and the UK regional programming team.

**Charlotte Longstaff**  is set to rejoin ATG in September as London’s programming director, after spending seven years at LW Theatres. Longstaff will work closely with  **Holly Mirams**, who will continue in her role as head of London programming to secure outstanding shows for the company’s venues in the West End.

“We are always looking to bring the best shows to our theatres and today’s announcement further strengthens the team that focuses on this content and manages the relationships with producers. I am delighted that Charlie Longstaff is returning to ATG and look forward to working with her,” said Melanie Smith, global chief executive of ATG Entertainment.

“I am excited that we have the right global team in place to lead our next stage of growth, with Michael focussing on the big strategic content objectives that add value for our theatres, audiences and producers.”

**Jonathan Shank** has joined Live Nation as senior vice president of global programming, focusing on expanding family entertainment and non-traditional immersive live formats.

Elsewhere, Superstruct Entertainment UK has appointed **Jacqui Harris** as managing director.

Harris joined Superstruct following 18 years at AEG Presents UK, where she served as vice president and general manager of UK touring.

Legends Global has named **Matthew Lazarus-Hall**  as the new general manager of Sydney’s [recently renamed](https://www.thestadiumbusiness.com/2026/05/20/qudos-bank-arena-to-become-afterpay-arena-in-five-year-deal/) Afterpay Arena.

Lazarus-Hall originally took on the role earlier this year in an interim capacity, and has been with Legends Global (APAC) since 2024. After joining the company, his initial focus was on Kai Tak Sports Park in Hong Kong, which opened last year.

Event commerce platform Flicket has strengthened its leadership team with the appointment of **Andrew Dalziel** as chief financial officer.

As a qualified lawyer, Dalziel also assumes responsibility for the company’s legal function.

With growth across New Zealand, Australia, the US and the UK, Flicket has also expanded its global client success team.

**Nicholas Hobbs**  has joined the team in the US, while  **Rodney Nielsen** has been appointed in Australia.

Hobbs has more than a decade of experience with the likes of SeatGeek and AXS, while Nielsen joins from Ticketek, where he most recently served as head of entertainment – NSW.

Olympia Events, managed by Legends Global, has appointed **Carlo Zoccali** as its new event operations director.

Zoccali has over 20 years of industry experience across the events, venues, exhibitions, conferences and sports sectors.
