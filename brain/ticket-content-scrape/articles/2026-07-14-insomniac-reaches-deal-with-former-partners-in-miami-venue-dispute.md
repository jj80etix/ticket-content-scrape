# Insomniac Reaches Deal With Former Partners in Miami Venue Dispute

## Summary

Insomniac Events has settled a lengthy legal dispute with its former Miami nightlife business partners over ownership and control of Club Space and Factory Town. The parties reached agreement outside of court after litigation and countersuits that began in 2024 over revenue splits, decision-making authority, and event-booking disputes, including allegations involving Insomniac founder Pasquale Rotella.

### Key Points
- Insomniac settles dispute with Sinopoli, Danese and Coloma Cano over Club Space and Factory Town in Miami.
- Insomniac will remain involved in Club Space and Factory Town; former partners retain Jolene and plan independent projects.
- Dispute originated in 2024 over revenue share and control, escalating to dueling lawsuits in 2025.
- Countersuit cited a CircoLoco booking offer 44% higher than the prior year and personal allegations against CEO Rotella.


## Transcript

![](https://www.ticketnews.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/07/insomniac-events.webp)

Insomniac Events has resolved its legal dispute with three prominent Miami nightlife operators, ending months of litigation over the ownership, management and programming of Club Space and Factory Town.

The dance music promoter and its former business partners, David Sinopoli, Davide Danese and Jose Gabriel Coloma Cano, announced the settlement in a joint statement released Friday. The parties said they had resolved their disagreements outside of court and established a path forward for their Miami nightlife businesses.

Under the agreement, Insomniac will remain involved in the operation of Club Space alongside Sinopoli. The company will also continue to participate in Factory Town, the open-air entertainment complex that has become a major destination for electronic music events in Miami.

Danese and Coloma Cano will continue overseeing Jolene, another Miami nightlife venue, in partnership with Sinopoli. The pair also indicated that they intend to develop additional projects independently.

The settlement preserves Insomniac’s presence in Miami, where the company first expanded in 2019 by acquiring an interest in Club Space. Sinopoli, Danese and Coloma Cano had previously operated the downtown Miami venue together.

The partnership later grew to include Factory Town, which opened in 2022. Located in the Hialeah area, the venue has hosted large-scale dance music events tied to Miami Music Week, Halloween and Art Basel.

The business relationship deteriorated in 2024 as the parties disagreed over control and financial arrangements connected to Factory Town. Insomniac alleged that its partners sought a larger share of the venue’s revenue and additional decision-making authority.

Sinopoli, Danese and Coloma Cano disputed that account. They claimed Insomniac reduced their ownership position while continuing to rely on them to handle operational responsibilities and financial exposure associated with the venue.

The conflict entered mediation in June 2025, resulting in a proposed agreement under which Insomniac would pay $3 million to acquire the operators’ interest in Factory Town.

The proposed resolution unraveled by August, when Insomniac filed a lawsuit accusing the three operators of violating the agreement. The complaint alleged that they continued presenting themselves as having authority over Factory Town and interfered with event planning and promoter relationships.

The operators filed a countersuit the following month. They argued that the mediation agreement required both sides to cooperate on major programming decisions, including the Hocus Pocus Halloween event and Factory Town’s Art Basel schedule.

Their complaint accused Insomniac of moving forward with bookings without their approval. It cited an offer to CircoLoco that was reportedly 44% higher than the event brand’s payment during the previous year.

The countersuit also included personal allegations concerning Insomniac founder and CEO Pasquale Rotella. Insomniac rejected those accusations, arguing that they were unrelated to the contractual issues and were intended to harm Rotella’s reputation.

Settlement discussions resumed during the fall. Attorneys notified the judge in June that the parties had reached an agreement, clearing the way for the litigation to be formally dismissed.
