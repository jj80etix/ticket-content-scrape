# BTS ticket scam ring busted by Thai police

## Summary

Thai police arrested five suspects, aged 18-27, in Bangkok and Surat Thani Province for running a counterfeit-ticket scam ring tied to BTS's Arirang World Tour, defrauding more than 100 fans of over THB1.8m (£39,650/$53,500/€46,700). Victims bought fake tickets advertised on social media for the tour's December stop at Bangkok's Rajamangala National Stadium, amid demand that reportedly outstripped official supply by up to 15x across Southeast Asia. The arrests followed a wave of formal complaints to Thailand's Consumer Protection Police Division, and add to a regional total the BBC put at over $100,000 in ticket-scam losses.

### Key Points
- Five suspects (ages 18-27) arrested in Bangkok and Surat Thani Province after a series of warrants issued last week.
- 100+ fans defrauded of a combined THB1.8m+ ($53,500/€46,700), averaging THB17,700 (~$490) lost per victim.
- Counterfeit tickets were sold via social media accounts for BTS's Arirang World Tour, three-night Bangkok stop in December.
- Charges include public fraud, entering false data into a computer system, and unauthorized use of bank/payment accounts.
- BBC reports Southeast Asian fans lost over $100,000 total in scams tied to the tour, with demand exceeding availability by 15x.
- Arirang World Tour spans 80+ dates across 34 regions, running through South Korea, Japan, North America, Europe, South America, and back to Asia by November.

### Highlights
- 100+ fans lost an average of THB17,700 (~$490) each to unofficial social-media ticket sellers for a high-demand tour.
- Regional demand outstripped official ticket supply by as much as 15x, pushing fans toward unverified resale channels.
- Arrests came only after 100+ formal complaints reached Thailand's Consumer Protection Police Division — reactive enforcement, not a preventive platform fix.

### Industry Problem
When official on-sale supply can't meet demand (15x oversubscription here), patrons turn to unofficial social-media resale and counterfeit sellers, exposing them to fraud with no recourse until losses are incurred and law enforcement eventually intervenes.

### Proposed Solution
The item shows only after-the-fact policing with no platform-side fix; an independent primary platform like Etix could invest in verified ticket/QR authentication, official waitlists or lotteries for oversubscribed onsales, and clearer in-app steering away from unverified social-media resale to cut counterfeit exposure before it happens.
