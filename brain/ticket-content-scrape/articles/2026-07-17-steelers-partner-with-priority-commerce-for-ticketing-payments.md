# Steelers partner with Priority Commerce for ticketing payments

## Summary

The Pittsburgh Steelers have selected payments company Priority Commerce to handle ticketing payment processing and treasury orchestration, aiming to optimize payment costs and improve visibility into daily cash flow. The multi-year deal also grants Priority Commerce in-stadium branding and hospitality rights, adding another major sports franchise to its client roster.

### Key Points
- Multi-year agreement covers payment processing plus "passport" treasury orchestration.
- Stated goals: lower payment costs, daily transaction monitoring, better cash-flow/treasury visibility.
- Priority Commerce receives marketing rights, in-stadium branding, and hospitality assets.
- Steelers cited service quality and a "collaborative approach" as deciding factors alongside the technology itself.

### Highlights
- Deal is explicitly framed around payment-cost optimization and treasury visibility, not just transaction processing.
- Priority Commerce positions this as part of a "growing list" of pro-sports clients.

### Industry Problem
Even well-resourced clients like an NFL franchise are actively shopping for better payment-cost management and cash-flow visibility, signaling this remains an underserved need beyond basic transaction processing.

### Proposed Solution
Worth checking whether Etix's own payment stack surfaces comparable treasury/cash-flow visibility and cost-optimization tooling for clients, since dedicated payments vendors are winning share on exactly that gap.

**The Pittsburgh Steelers have selected Priority Commerce to provide ticketing payment processing.**

The company will also use its passport treasury orchestration solution to help the Steelers optimise payment costs, monitor daily transactions, and improve visibility into cash flow and treasury operations.

“Great teams know success comes down to execution,” said Tom Priore, CEO of Priority Commerce.

“This partnership reflects an increasing recognition that payments and treasury solutions have become strategic drivers of operational performance.

“We’re proud the Steelers have joined a growing list of professional sports franchises that have chosen Priority Commerce to advance that vision.”

As part of the multi-year agreement, Priority Commerce will also receive marketing rights and other partnership assets, including in-stadium branding, hospitality and participation in select Steelers business events.

“We were looking for a partner that could help us optimize payment costs, provide greater visibility into our payment operations and deliver the level of service we expect,” said Doug Stuver, vice president of finance for the Pittsburgh Steelers.

“Priority Commerce offered the combination of payment technology, passport treasury orchestration and collaborative approach we were looking for, making them the clear choice.”
