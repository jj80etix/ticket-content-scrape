# The Stray Cats Extend 2026 Tour With Fall Dates

## Summary

The Stray Cats (Brian Setzer, Lee Rocker, Slim Jim Phantom) added fall theater dates running November 9 (Grand Prairie, TX) through December 6 (Foxwoods, CT). General tickets go on sale July 17, with a fan-club presale running July 14-16.

### Key Points
- New fall leg: Nov. 9 (Grand Prairie) through Dec. 6 (Foxwoods).
- Stops include Nashville's Ryman Auditorium, Atlanta's Fox Theatre, and Louisville Palace.
- General on-sale July 17; fan-club presale July 14-16.
- All three original members (Setzer, Rocker, Phantom) confirmed for the run.

The Stray Cats are extending their tour into the fall, adding shows in November and December in theaters across the country.

The band, comprising original members Brian Setzer (guitar, vocals), Lee Rocker (upright bass, vocals) and Slim Jim Phantom (drums, vocals), will kick off its fall run Nov. 9 at Texas Trust CU Theatre in Grand Prairie. The tour includes stops at Beau Rivage Resort & Casino in Biloxi, Kentucky’s Louisville Palace, Ryman Auditorium in Nashville, Caesars Virginia in Danville, Hard Rock Live Orlando in Florida, Atlanta’s Fox Theatre, Harrah’s Cherokee Casino in North Carolina and Bethlehem’s Wind Creek Event Center before wrapping up Dec. 6 at Premier Theater at Foxwoods Resort Casino in Mashantucket. See full routing below.

“It’s good to be healthy and strong again. There’s something about the unique sound of The Stray Cats that keeps drawing me in,” Setzer said in a statement. “The Gretsch guitar, the acoustic bass, and the stand-up drums still sound pure and fresh today. Come on out and have some fun with us this summer.”

Tickets for the fall shows go on sale July 17 at 10 a.m. local time, and an artist fan club presale runs from July 14 at 10 a.m. through July 16 at 10 p.m. local time. For more information about tickets and to sign up for the presale, visit the band’s website at [straycats.com](https://straycats.com/).

“The Stray Cats are back and ready to blast off! It’s an amazing feeling to join up with Brian and Slim, hit the stage and Rock across America,” Rocker said. “No one can do it like us Cats; …nobody comes close.”

The trio is on tour this summer through Aug. 16 and returns to U.S. stages less than three months later.

“With The Stray Cats, there’s always some extra magic around the summertime shows,” Slim Jim Phantom said. “It’s the perfect time for the Cats & Kittens to get dolled up, put the top down & cruise to the greatest Rockabilly show on earth!”

The Stray Cats are booked by WME and managed by Dave Kaplan Management. The band had a successful outing in 2024 at The Rooftop at Pier 17 in New York City, grossing $223,117 off 3,080 tickets sold, according to Pollstar Boxoffice.
