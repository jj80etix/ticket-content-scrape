# Friendly Fire celebrates sold-out debut of Slot Zeist Live

## Summary

Dutch promoter Friendly Fire's new boutique outdoor concert series, Slot Zeist Live, sold out all four shows in its debut run (Katie Melua, dEUS, Angus & Julia Stone, Tamino) at a 17th-century castle estate near Utrecht. The promoter calls the 4,000-capacity series a "valuable addition" to its portfolio.

### Key Points
- All four debut shows (8-11 July) sold out.
- 4,000-capacity outdoor series staged at the Slot Zeist castle estate.
- Lineup included Katie Melua, dEUS, Angus & Julia Stone, Tamino.
- Promoter plans to continue the series annually.

## Transcript

The Dutch promoter and booking agency has hailed the 4,000-capacity outdoor series as a "valuable addition" to its portfolio

![Katie Melua at Slot Zeist Live ](https://www.iqmagazine.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/07/Katie-Melua-at-Slot-Zeist-Live-©-Alicia-Karsonopoero.png)

The Netherlands’ Friendly Fire has hailed the sell-out success of its new boutique outdoor concert series, Slot Zeist Live, calling it a “valuable addition” to its events portfolio.

The Dutch promoter and booking agency launched the annual 4,000-capacity series this year, staging a series of shows in the gardens of Slot Zeist outside a 17th-century castle near Utrecht. All four concerts on its debut calendar from 8-11 July sold out, which included performances by Katie Melua, dEUS, Angus & Julia Stone and Tamino.

“We are incredibly happy with this venue and with the response from audiences,” says Friendly Fire promoter Sjef Athmer. “The fact that all four shows sold out has surpassed all of our expectations. It clearly demonstrates that a concert series at this capacity and in a location like Slot Zeist adds real value to the Dutch outdoor summer season.

“At the same time, it has proven to be a valuable addition to Friendly Fire’s portfolio. From the outset, we felt this place offered something unique, and seeing audiences embrace the concept so enthusiastically gives us great confidence for the future.”

The Baroque-style château estate, while host to some small-scale gatherings, does not have a large history with concerts. With the calm, intimate setting, Athmer previously told [*IQ*](https://www.iqmagazine.com/2026/01/friendly-fire-reveals-new-boutique-concert-series/) that the team hopes the series will be a major attraction for artists and audiences alike.

> “Hopefully in the future, we can build more”

“There’s nothing like this. There are bigger and smaller options in the Netherlands, but not at this capacity with this unique atmosphere. It felt like a sweet spot to find something in this range; It really adds something special,” he said.

While it launched with four days this year, the team is aiming for a strong addition to the open-air concert circuit that will expand in the years to come.

“The goal of this year is to show the market, both on the audience and business sides, what we can do here and what we’re capable of at this site. We’ll start with four days, but hopefully in the future, we can build more,” Athmer previously said.

Alongside the event, FKP Scorpio subsidiary Friendly Fire also presents roughly 40 outdoor concerts at the 1,500-cap Live At Amsterdamse Bos series alongside large-scale concerts in Zuiderpark, The Hague (20,000). The company also recently sold out the latest edition of the Best Kept Secret Festival, which welcomed Gorillaz, Jack White, and Nick Cave & The Bad Seeds as headliners.

Following the success of the inaugural edition, Slot Zeist Live is confirmed to return late June 2027, with details to be announced at a later date.
