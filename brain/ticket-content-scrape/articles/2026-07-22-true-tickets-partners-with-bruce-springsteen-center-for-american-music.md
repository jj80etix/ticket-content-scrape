# True Tickets Partners With Bruce Springsteen Center for American Music

## Summary

True Tickets is now powering admission for the newly opened Bruce Springsteen Center for American Music at Monmouth University, using its mobile-first digital ticket delivery platform to manage exhibitions, concerts, and educational programs. The partnership extends True Tickets' push into museums and cultural institutions, following a pattern of landing venues that want tighter distribution control and reduced unauthorized resale.

### Key Points
- The Bruce Springsteen Archives' new public center (opened June 13) will use True Tickets for all ticketed programming.
- True Tickets pitches the deal around three benefits: distribution control, limiting unauthorized resale, and a smoother visitor experience.
- The center also gets audience-insight data from the platform to build long-term relationships with visitors.
- Adds another museum/cultural-institution client to True Tickets' roster, a vertical it's been building out beyond traditional concert venues.

### Highlights
- Client win: Bruce Springsteen Center for American Music (Monmouth University), opened June 13, 2026.
- Vendor: True Tickets — mobile-first digital ticket delivery, anti-resale positioning.
- Expands True Tickets' footprint in the museum/cultural-institution vertical.

### Industry Problem
Cultural institutions and museums increasingly need the same ticketing tools as concert venues — controlled distribution, resale limits, and audience data — but are underserved by ticketing vendors built primarily for touring/concert use cases.

### Proposed Solution
True Tickets is positioning mobile-first, resale-resistant ticketing as the answer; for Etix, this is a signal that non-traditional venue types (museums, cultural centers, archives) are a viable adjacent market to court with the same anti-resale/audience-data pitch.

## Transcript

![](https://www.ticketnews.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/07/true-tickets.jpg)

True Tickets is bringing secure digital ticketing to the newly opened Bruce Springsteen Center for American Music at Monmouth University, helping the New Jersey institution manage admission for exhibitions, concerts, educational programs and special events.

The Center, which opened to the public on June 13, houses the Bruce Springsteen Archives and serves as the official repository for materials connected to Springsteen and the E Street Band. Its collection includes historic photographs, memorabilia, oral histories, recordings and other archival items.

Beyond chronicling Springsteen’s career, the Center explores the wider history of American music through exhibitions, live performances and educational programming focused on music’s role in culture, storytelling and community.

Under the new partnership, the Center will use True Tickets’ mobile-first digital ticket delivery platform. The technology is designed to give organizers greater control over ticket distribution, limit unauthorized resale and create a smoother experience for visitors traveling from around the world.

The system will also give the Center more insight into its audiences, helping the institution build stronger long-term relationships with concertgoers, students and museum visitors.

“As someone who has spent decades listening to Bruce Springsteen’s music, attending performances, and supporting the Center as a donor, this partnership is incredibly meaningful to me on a personal level,” Ken Lesnik, chief commercial officer at True Tickets, said in a press release.

“But what excites me most is what the Center represents,” Lesnik said. “It is preserving one of the most important musical archives in America while creating a place where future generations can explore the broader story of American music. We’re honored to support that mission and help create a secure, seamless experience for the audiences who will visit from around the world.”

The agreement adds another museum and cultural institution to True Tickets’ growing client roster, extending the company’s reach among organizations that host a mix of exhibitions, performances and high-demand events.
