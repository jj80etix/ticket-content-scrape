# Dice partners with Broadwick Group on two London venues

## Summary

DICE has become the primary ticketing partner for two Broadwick Group venues in London — 26 Leake Street (capacity 1,150) and Exhibition White City (capacity 2,800) — extending an existing relationship that already covers Broadwick venues in London and New York. Since going live two months ago, the two venues have generated nearly 100,000 event views and over 4,000 waitlist sign-ups on DICE's platform. The partnership is pitched around DICE's fan-first feature set: all-in pricing, personalized gig recommendations, and anti-touting technology.

### Key Points
- DICE is now primary ticketing partner for 26 Leake Street and Exhibition White City, two Broadwick Group venues in London.
- Combined, the venues have generated ~100,000 event views and 4,000+ waitlist sign-ups in two months on DICE.
- 26 Leake Street (South Bank) holds 1,150; Exhibition White City (a converted Victorian engine house) holds 2,800.
- DICE already partners with Broadwick venues in London and New York.
- Partnership is framed around DICE's all-in pricing, personalized recommendations, and anti-touting technology as differentiators for venue operators.

### Highlights
- DICE (Tier 1 primary-platform competitor) is actively winning/expanding venue partnerships, landing two new London venues from an existing operator relationship.
- Product features cited as the draw: all-in (no hidden fee) pricing, personalized gig recommendations, and anti-touting technology bundled into the venue partnership pitch.
- Early traction metrics disclosed (100,000 views / 4,000 waitlist signups in two months) — a data point on DICE's ability to drive demand for partner venues.

### Industry Problem
Venue operators want a ticketing partner that combines transparent pricing with active demand generation (discovery, personalization, waitlists) and touting/bot control, not just transaction processing — a bundle DICE is using to win exclusive venue deals from competitors.

### Proposed Solution
DICE's solution is a packaged offer of all-in pricing plus personalization and anti-touting tooling positioned as core value for venue partners. For Etix, this is a competitive signal to benchmark: independent primary platforms should ensure their own all-in pricing options, recommendation/discovery features, and bot/resale mitigation are positioned just as clearly in venue-facing sales pitches, since these are becoming table-stakes differentiators in competing for exclusive venue contracts.

TheTicketingBusiness News

**Dice has collaborated with Broadwick Group to become the primary ticketing partner for the 26 Leake Street and Exhibition White City venues.**

Both venues are now live and on sale through Dice’s platform and, since launching two months ago, they have generated nearly 100,000 event views and over 4,000 waitlist sign-ups.

“Broadwick venues are built to work seven days a week, and that only happens if getting through the door is as good as what’s happening inside,” said Simeon Aldred, director of strategy at Broadwick Group.

“Dice has backed our venues across London and New York, and we’re looking forward to building on that with 26 Leake Street and Exhibition White City.”

For Broadwick Group, the partnership aims to bring Dice’s fan-first approach to its audiences with all-in pricing, personalised gig recommendations and anti-touting technology.

It is said to be a “combination designed to deepen the connection between Broadwick’s venues and the people who fill them”.

“This partnership reflects our deep commitment to the UK music scene and to the operators pushing it forward,” said Sophie Doherty, senior director of music partnerships at Dice.

“Broadwick has built some of the most exciting venues in the country, and together we’ll give fans more access to the diverse spaces that are shaping live music right now.”

Located on London’s South Bank, 26 Leake Street has a capacity of 1,150 while Exhibition White City is restored from a Victorian engine house and boasts a capacity of 2,800.
