# D.C. caps ticket resale prices

## Summary

The DC Council unanimously passed the RESALE (Restricting Egregious Scalping Against Live Entertainment) Amendment Act, capping resale prices for live-entertainment tickets at 10% above face value and banning speculative "ghost" ticket sales. The measure targets third-party resale platforms including SeatGeek, StubHub, and Vivid Seats, and is already funded in the DC budget; barring a mayoral veto it takes effect next year. Live Nation (Ticketmaster's parent) publicly praised the bill, while the Ticket Policy Forum — a trade group representing resale marketplaces — opposed it, arguing the cap will push sales to unregulated channels and that the primary market, controlled by Ticketmaster, is the real driver of high prices.

### Key Points
- DC Council passed the RESALE Act unanimously; author is Councilmember Charles Allen.
- Caps secondary-market resale prices at 10% above original face value.
- Bans speculative ticket sales — advertising or selling tickets the reseller doesn't actually possess.
- Targets SeatGeek, StubHub, and Vivid Seats by name.
- Already funded in the DC budget; would take effect next year absent a veto from Mayor Muriel E. Bowser.
- Live Nation's DC/MD/VA concerts president publicly endorsed the bill as protecting fans and artists.
- The Ticket Policy Forum (resale-platform trade group) called the primary market, not resale, "the real cause of high prices."

### Highlights
- 10% resale price cap plus a speculative-listing ban, unanimously passed — part of a growing wave of resale-restriction laws.
- Live Nation/Ticketmaster publicly endorsed the cap, while the resale-industry trade group (Ticket Policy Forum) opposed it and pointed at Ticketmaster's primary-market pricing power instead.
- The cap applies only to resale; primary ticket prices and fees are untouched by this bill.

### Industry Problem
Another jurisdiction is regulating secondary-market pricing and speculative listings while leaving primary-market pricing and fees — the area a vertically integrated incumbent like Ticketmaster controls — untouched, a structural asymmetry independent resellers say favors the dominant primary seller.

### Proposed Solution
The Act's own fix is a blunt resale price ceiling and a speculative-listing ban; for Etix the actionable move is to monitor how many jurisdictions copy DC's resale-only template as it reshapes secondary-market economics, and confirm any Etix resale/transfer features are cleanly compliant with speculative-listing and price-cap rules as more states follow.

TheTicketingBusiness News

**The Council of the District of Columbia has unanimously passed the RESALE Act which caps the resale price of live entertainment tickets at 10% above their original face value.**

The Restricting Egregious Scalping Against Live Entertainment (RESALE) Amendment Act targets tickets sold on third-party sites such as SeatGeek, StubHub and Vivid Seats.

It also bans speculative ticket sales, making it illegal to advertise or sell tickets that a reseller does not actually possess.

“Today, the DC Council stood with DC’s most beloved and iconic music venues, with our storied theatres, and with every music and live theatre fan who’s tired of getting ripped off and priced out,” said Councilmember Charles Allen, author of the RESALE Act.

“Ticket resellers have used technology and unrestrained profits to turn our live entertainment scene into the wild west – just last week it was revealed StubHub has been running a major ticket scalping operation while they’ve argued they’re just a platform for fans to sell extra tickets.

“People are sick of big tech wringing more and more money out of them. DC is a leader here and I expect many other jurisdictions to step in once they see we can save our residents money and keep more dollars in our local economy instead of propping up big tech.”

Barring a veto from D.C. Mayor Muriel E. Bowser, the Act would take effect next year and has already been funded in the budget.

The pro-consumer bill has been well received by many, including Live Nation which owns Ticketmaster.

“We applaud the D.C. City Council for protecting fans and artists by banning speculative ticketing, reaffirming all in pricing, and capping concert ticket resale prices,” said the company’s president of concerts in D.C., Maryland, and Virginia.

“No one should be able to scam fans by listing tickets they don’t have or by pretending to be legitimate ticket sellers.”

However, not everyone welcomed the RESALE Act.

The Ticket Policy Forum, a trade group representing resale platforms including StubHub and SeatGeek, has argued that a resale price cap will push ticket sales onto unregulated channels and increase fraud.

The group contends that the primary market controlled by Ticketmaster is the real cause of high prices.
