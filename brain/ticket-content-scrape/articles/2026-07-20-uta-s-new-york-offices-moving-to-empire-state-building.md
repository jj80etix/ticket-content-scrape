# UTA’s New York Offices Moving To Empire State Building

## Summary

United Talent Agency is relocating its New York office to the Empire State Building, taking over 100,948 square feet across four floors. The move consolidates UTA's Filmed Entertainment, Music, Sports, Theatre, and other business lines in one iconic location, which the agency's COO framed as a signal of its continued growth and long-term commitment to New York. JLL represented UTA in the lease negotiations, while ESRT and Newmark represented the landlord.

### Key Points
- UTA is moving its New York office to the Empire State Building.
- The new space spans 100,948 square feet across four floors.
- The office houses UTA's Filmed Entertainment, Creators, News, Publishing, Theatre, Music, Speakers, Sports, Brand Advisory, and Executive Search businesses.
- UTA COO Bob Roback cited New York's growing importance to clients and the company's ambitions.
- JLL brokers represented UTA; ESRT and Newmark represented the building owner in lease negotiations.

United Talent Agency will take up residence in the Empire State Building, with the agency announcing it is relocating its New York office to the World’s Most Famous Building.

The new office will feature 100,948 square feet across four floors. UTA’s New York office is home to the agency’s Filmed Entertainment, Creators, News, Publishing, Theatre, Music, Speakers, Sports, Brand Advisory and Executive Search businesses

“Our move to one of New York City’s most iconic landmarks is a reflection of UTA’s continued growth and our long-term commitment to New York as a global epicenter of culture, creativity, and business. As we’ve expanded our integrated talent representation and brand advisory businesses, New York has become an increasingly important center for our clients and our company. This new office reflects the scale of our ambitions and positions us to continue building the future of talent, media, sports, and brand representation,” UTA COO Bob Roback said in a statement.

Mitchell Konsker, Benjamin Bass, and Harrison Potter of JLL represented UTA in the lease negotiations. Jordan Berger, Shanae Ursini, and Kerry Lavelle of ESRT and Scott Klau, Erik Harris, Neil Rubin, Brent Ozarowski, Zachary Weil, and Cole Gendels of Newmark represented the property owner.
