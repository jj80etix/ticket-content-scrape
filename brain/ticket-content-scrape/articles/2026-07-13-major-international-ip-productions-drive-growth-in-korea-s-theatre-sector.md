# Major international IP productions drive growth in Korea’s theatre sector

## Summary

South Korean theatre ticket sales topped 70 billion won in H1 2026 for the first time, per KOPIS data, driven by international IP productions like "Spirited Away" and "Life of Pi" adaptations. Tickets sold rose 26.6% year-on-year to 1.76 million even as musical ticket sales fell 6.1%.

### Key Points
- H1 2026 theatre ticket sales: 75.89bn won, nearly matching all of 2025's 78.13bn won total.
- Tickets sold up 26.6% to 1.76 million; productions staged up from 1,331 to 1,518.
- International IP productions ("Spirited Away," "Life of Pi") were the top grossers, priced 60,000-190,000 won.
- Musical sales fell 6.1% even as classical music sales rose 11.8%; dance sales fell 17.9%.

TheTicketingBusiness News

**Theatre ticket sales in South Korea have surpassed 70 billion won (£35bn/€41bn/$47bn) in the first half of the year for the first time, according to the Korea Performing Arts Box Office Information System (KOPIS).**

Driven by productions based on global intellectual properties, theatre ticket sales reached a total of 75.89 billion won from January to June, marking the highest first-half total since KOPIS began tracking theatre data in 2014.

As reported by the [*Korea Herald*](https://www.koreaherald.com/article/10806146), the first-half theatre ticket sales figure nearly matched last year’s entire total of 78.13bn won.

The figure is the result of a 26.6% increase in the number of tickets sold, with 1.76 million issued – up from 1.39 million the year prior. The number of productions also increased from 1,331 to 1,518.

International productions such as Japan’s ‘Spirited Away’ and the stage adaptation of ‘Life of Pi’, which made its debut in the UK, were the top-grossing offerings in the first half of the year. The two productions were staged at Seoul Arts Center and GS Arts Center, with ticket prices ranging from 90,000 won to 190,000 won for ‘Spirited Away’ and between 60,000 won to 160,000 won for ‘Life of Pi’.

However, musical ticket sales fell 6.1% year-on-year, with sales totalling 223.22 billion won for the first half. Classical music ticket sales increased 11.8% to 40.38 billion won, while ticket sales for traditional Korean music dropped 7.5% to 2.04 billion won. Dance ticket sales also declined 17.9% to 7.67 billion won.
