# Dolly Parton’s ‘DOLLY: A True Original Musical’ Sets Broadway Run

## Summary

Dolly Parton's stage production "DOLLY: A True Original Musical" will begin Broadway previews December 7 at the St. James Theatre, with an official opening night of January 19, 2027 — Parton's 81st birthday. The musical, following its Nashville premiere, tells Parton's life story through her classic catalog plus new songs written for the show.

### Key Points
- Broadway previews begin Dec 7, 2026 at the St. James Theatre; opening night Jan 19, 2027.
- Follows the show's earlier world premiere run in Nashville.
- Features Parton classics ("I Will Always Love You," "Jolene," "9 to 5") plus new original songs.
- Directed by Bartlett Sher; book by Parton and Maria S. Schlatter.

## Transcript

![](https://www.ticketnews.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/06/Hello-Im-Dolly.webp)

Dolly Parton’s upcoming stage production “DOLLY: A True Original Musical” will begin Broadway previews this December at New York City’s St. James Theatre.

The musical will start preview performances on Monday, December 7, with its official opening night scheduled for Tuesday, January 19, 2027. The opening date will also mark Parton’s 81st birthday.

The Broadway production follows the musical’s world premiere in Nashville earlier this year. The show will bring Parton’s personal story to the stage, featuring songs from throughout her career as well as new music written specifically for the production.

“My whole life has been a musical,” Parton said in a statement. “A grand ole opera really, and I can’t wait to present it to you on Broadway. I hope you enjoy watching as much as I’ve enjoyed livin’ it.”

The musical will include some of Parton’s best-known songs, including “I Will Always Love You,” “Jolene,” “Coat of Many Colors,” and “9 to 5.” The production will also feature new songs from Parton as part of its score.

“DOLLY: A True Original Musical” is directed by Bartlett Sher, with a book written by Parton and Maria S. Schlatter. Stephen Oremus serves as music supervisor, Charity Wicks is the music director, and Mandy Moore is responsible for choreography.

Ticket sales for the Broadway production will begin with a presale for registered fans and eligible Capital One cardholders. The fan and Capital One presale will begin Tuesday, July 8, at 10 a.m. ET and continue through Friday, July 10, at 9:59 a.m. ET, unless tickets sell out earlier.
