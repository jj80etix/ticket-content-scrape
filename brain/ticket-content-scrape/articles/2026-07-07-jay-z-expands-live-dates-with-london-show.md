# Jay-Z expands live dates with London show

## Summary

Jay-Z added a single London show at Tottenham Hotspur Stadium (Sept 4) to his 2026 calendar, promoted by Roc Nation, ahead of his triple-header at Yankee Stadium (July 10-12) marking anniversaries of *Reasonable Doubt* and *The Blueprint*. It joins previously announced Paris (Sept 10) and Los Angeles (Oct 23) stadium shows as part of his 30th-anniversary concert series.

### Key Points
- New Tottenham Hotspur Stadium date (Sept 4), his only confirmed UK stop this year, promoted by Roc Nation.
- Joins Paris (Stade de France, Sept 10) and LA (SoFi Stadium, Oct 23) shows.
- Precedes a three-night Yankee Stadium run (July 10-12).
- Brings his total 2026 "Jay-Z 30" appearances to seven; tickets on sale from July 9-10.

## Transcript

The famed rapper has added a show at Tottenham Hotspur Stadium in September, adding to shows in New York, Paris, and Los Angeles

![Jay-Z](https://www.iqmagazine.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/Jay-Z-02-mika.jpg)

Jay-Z

Ahead of his return to the stadium circuit this week, Jay-Z has expanded his 2026 calendar with a new show set for London’s Tottenham Hotspur Stadium in September.

The famed rapper will play a single show at the 62,850-capacity venue on Friday, 4 September, promoted by Roc Nation, slated to be his only UK stop for the year.

London’s new date will slot in ahead of [previously announced performances](https://www.iqmagazine.com/2026/06/jay-z-announces-paris-and-la-stadium-shows/) at Paris’s Stade de France (100,00) on 10 September and Los Angeles’ SoFi Stadium (100,000) on 23 October.

This week, he is due to play a triple header at New York’s Yankee Stadium (50,000) from 10-12 July, marking the anniversaries of *Reasonable Doubt* and *The Blueprint*, two of his most iconic albums.

Earlier this year, he performed at Philadelphia’s Roots Picnic festival on 30 May. With the new London date, his total number of appearances rises to seven.

Taken together, the *Jay-Z 30* concert series is set to celebrate the tastemaker’s three-decade career in music.

Tickets for the new date will be available from Thursday (9 July), with general sale launching the following day.

The last time he played the UK was in 2018, as part of the *On The Run II* tour with Beyoncé, his wife, where the duo performed in stadiums in Cardiff, Manchester, Glasgow, and London. His last solo tour came nearly a decade ago in 2017.

Tottenham Hotspur has become Beyoncé’s venue of choice in the UK capital city, as she has recently brought both the 2025 *Cowboy Carter Tour* and 2023 *Renaissance World Tour* to the venue for six nights and five nights, respectively.

She set the record for the highest-grossing and most-attended set of shows in the venue’s history with *Cowboy Carter*, drawing over 275,000 fans and grossing more than £45 million (€52.7m).
