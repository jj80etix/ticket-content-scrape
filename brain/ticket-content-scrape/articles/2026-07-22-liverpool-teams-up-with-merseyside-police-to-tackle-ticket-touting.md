# Liverpool teams up with Merseyside Police to tackle ticket touting

## Summary

Liverpool FC and Merseyside Police's economic crime unit have jointly seized more than £1.2m in assets tied to organized ticket touting, with three touting cases in the past year yielding two convictions and one pending. The club also disclosed large-scale account-enforcement figures for last season: 67,663 suspected fake accounts closed or blocked, 121,379 more under investigation, 432 lifetime bans, and an 847% jump in matchday stops for suspicious accounts.

### Key Points
- £1.2m+ in touting-linked assets detained under Proceeds of Crime legislation via a club/police joint operation.
- Three touting cases in the past year: two convictions, one pending trial.
- 67,663 suspected fake accounts closed/blocked last season; 121,379 more under investigation.
- 432 lifetime bans and 115 indefinite suspensions issued in 2025-26.
- Matchday suspicious-account stops rose from 381 (2024-25) to 3,608 (2025-26), an 847% increase.
- 3,847 tickets cancelled for suspicious purchase activity and resold as general admission.

### Highlights
- Concrete fraud-enforcement scale: roughly 190K accounts flagged or blocked, 3,608 matchday stops (up 847% year-over-year).
- £1.2m+ in criminal assets detained via a formal police partnership under proceeds-of-crime law.
- Enforcement spans both online account fraud and physical matchday tout activity (e.g., burner-phone detection, up 33%).

### Industry Problem
Organized ticket touting operates at industrial scale (tens of thousands of accounts) and increasingly overlaps with money laundering, requiring ticketing platforms to go beyond account-level fraud detection into law-enforcement partnership and asset-seizure cooperation.

### Proposed Solution
Liverpool's model — a dedicated club investigations team working directly with police economic-crime units, plus heavy investment in on-the-ground detection (burner phones, targeted matchday stops) — is a template for how a primary platform could formalize law-enforcement partnerships as part of its fraud/bot-mitigation stack, not just software-side detection.

**Premier League club Liverpool has teamed up with Merseyside Police to jointly tackle all forms of ticket touting.**

The force’s economic crime unit has been working with the club’s investigations team, helping to detain more than £1.2m (€1.4m/$1.6m) in assets related to organised ticket touts. There are a number of active cases ongoing under Proceeds of Crime legislation, the club said.

Over the last year, there have been three cases of touting linked to Liverpool FC where two have resulted in convictions and another is due before court next month.

The police operation focuses on the laundering of criminal proceeds from illicit ticket sales, while the club continues to crackdown on ticket touting, both online and on the ground.

Last season, the club closed or blocked 67,663 suspected fake accounts, with a further 121,379 accounts currently under investigation. The club also issued 432 lifetime bans and 115 indefinite suspensions during the 2025-26 season.

The club has placed more investigatory resources on the ground on matchdays, resulting in a 33% increase in the number of burner phones either identified, entry into Anfield denied, or seized by Merseyside Police last season.

There was also a significant increase in targeted stops carried out by the club on matchdays, preventing access to the turnstiles for accounts with suspicious activity – rising from 381 across the 2024-25 season to 3,608 last year, an increase of 847%.

Last season also saw 3,847 tickets cancelled for suspicious purchase activity and put back on sale as general admission tickets.

Not only has the club focused on ticket touting in a bid to ensure genuine fans can attend Anfield, but also highlighted 28 cases as part of the Red Together initiative concerning behavioural issues.
