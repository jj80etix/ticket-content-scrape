# UK government unveils music plan with fresh initiatives, funding

## Summary

The UK government announced "Turn It Up: Our Plan for Music," a package boosting grassroots music funding, mentoring programs, and licensing reforms for live events. The plan increases Arts Council funding for the Music Growth Package, funds a Music in Libraries initiative, and reforms event-licensing rules including more Temporary Event Notices and longer festival licences. A new government Music Champion role was also announced.

### Key Points
- £45m Music Growth Package funding over three years via Arts Council England.
- £12.5m for Music in Libraries initiative co-designed with the Ed Sheeran Foundation.
- Temporary Event Notices increased from 15 to 20/year; event days from 21 to 26.
- New minimum 3-year (new events) and 5-year (existing events) festival licence terms.
- Michael Dugher appointed UK's first Music Champion.

TheTicketingBusiness News

**The UK government has committed to backing grassroots music projects, artists and business with a boost to the Music Growth Package, while the industry will benefit from a range of new powers and further investment.**

‘Turn It Up: Our Plan for Music’ features a cash injection of £15m (€17.6m/$20m) from Arts Council England for the Music Growth Package, meaning £45m will be spent on supporting over 2,000 music projects, and at least 40,000 artists and music professionals over three years.

For the first time, the funding will also be used to support mid-career artists, band managers, labels and publishers.

Roughly £12.5m of this funding will be for libraries to be transformed into music lending libraries with free studio spaces and live performances through the Music in Libraries initiative. This will be co-designed by the Ed Sheeran Foundation and other partners to boost the role of libraries in music enrichment, with libraries able to apply for funding for studio spaces, recording booths, mixing desks and state-of-the-art equipment.

The initiative was inspired by Culture Secretary Lisa Nandy’s visit with Ed Sheeran to Brighten the Corners in Ipswich in 2024, which operates three small live music venues.

Meanwhile, at least £10m of dormant assets funding will be dedicated for a Creative Mentoring scheme, which will give thousands of care experienced children and young people the opportunity to receive in-depth mentoring from creative practitioners. Participants will also benefit from opportunities to explore creative disciplines such as music, art, film and drama. This scheme and the Music in Libraries initiative will be delivered by the National Lottery Community Fund.

**Turn It Up**

Reforms to the licensing system have also been announced to help live music and events, including increasing the number of Temporary Event Notices (TENs) from 15 to 20 per year, and the total event days from 21 to 26.

Plans also include longer, more secure licences for festivals and other events, with minimum three-year contracts for new events and five-year contracts for existing events.

Investment will be increased for the UK government’s music trade mission programme by nearly 50%, with the Department for Business and Trade more than doubling its funding to the British Music Embassy at South by Southwest in Austin, supporting UK talent from every nation and region to reach new global audiences.

The government has also announced that Michael Dugher, former chief executive of UK Music, has been appointed as its first Music Champion.

“Music is our shared language. It brings people together, breaks down barriers and gives voice to every community. At a time when too many people feel disconnected, music has never mattered more,” said Culture Secretary Lisa Nandy.

“As the song says, ‘Every generation throws a hero up the pop charts.’ But pop is getting posher, and that must change. Talent is everywhere, but opportunity is not. After a decade of mindless vandalism that stripped culture and creativity from too many classrooms and communities, we are putting music back at the heart of education, investing in creative careers and backing grassroots music across the country.

“We believe music belongs to everyone, not just the privileged few. That’s how we widen opportunity, strengthen our communities and ensure the UK’s incredible fans and extraordinary musical talent continues to inspire the world for generations to come.”
