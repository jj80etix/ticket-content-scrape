# Jay-Z closes historic, star-studded NYC triple header

## Summary

Jay-Z completed a historic three-night run at Yankee Stadium, becoming the first artist to sell out three consecutive shows at the venue and setting its single-concert ticket-sales record with 45,832 tickets on the second night. The closing show was delayed nearly four hours after fans stormed entry points, prompting a multi-hour gate lockdown before Jay-Z took the stage past midnight. The run featured surprise appearances from Beyoncé, Rihanna, Eminem, Usher, and others, and precedes upcoming Jay-Z shows in London, Paris, and Los Angeles.

### Key Points
- Jay-Z became the first artist to sell out three consecutive Yankee Stadium concerts, setting a venue attendance record of 45,832 tickets for one night.
- The finale was delayed nearly four hours after fans stormed entry points, leading to a multi-hour lockdown before doors reopened.
- Roc Nation, Live Nation, and the Yankees issued a joint statement thanking NYPD and stadium security for prioritizing attendee safety.
- Jay-Z's next shows are scheduled for London (Sept. 4), Paris (Sept. 10), and Los Angeles (Oct. 23).


## Transcript

The acclaimed rapper is set to continue solo touring with major shows in London, Paris, and Los Angeles to follow in the coming months

![Jay-Z at Yankee Stadium](https://www.iqmagazine.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/07/Jay-Z-at-Yankee-Stadium-©-Live-Nation.png)

Jay-Z at Yankee Stadium © Roc Nation

Jay-Z wrapped up his historic run at New York’s Yankee Stadium on Sunday (12 July), becoming the first artist to sell out three consecutive shows as he reestablishes his solo touring activity.

Over the weekend (10-12 July), he staged a [triple header](https://www.iqmagazine.com/2026/03/tour-news-jay-z-homecoming-shows-pegged-for-yankee-stadium/) at the 50,000-capacity baseball stadium, marking the anniversaries of *Reasonable Doubt* and *The Blueprint*, two of his most iconic albums. The third, dubbed *Extra Innings*, was added after immense demand.

With it, he set the venue record for the most tickets sold for a concert with 45,832 tickets on Saturday (11 July). Live concerts are rare at the famous Bronx venue, which is primarily a baseball venue.

The run comprised three nights of unique setlists, productions, and surprise guests, which including Beyoncé and Blue Ivy, along with Eminem, Usher, Rihanna, Pharrell Williams, Clipse, Slick Rick, Nas, and Alicia Keys.

The finale of his trio of shows, was delayed by nearly four hours after reports of fans storming the entry points that led to a multi-hour lockdown. As a result, the rapper took to the stage after midnight and played until roughly 3am.

“It was like 10,000 people outside, and we closed all the doors, and somebody rushed the door,” he [reportedly](https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/music/music-news/jay-z-addresses-three-hour-delay-yankee-stadium-concert-1236646285/) said after taking the stage. “They closed the door for you guys’ safety and everyone’s safety outside. There’s 10,000 people outside; I don’t want to start the music and people get trampled. I’m really sorry for the inconvenience, but I had to make sure everyone was OK. I appreciate your patience.”

Following the show, organisers Roc Nation and Live Nation and Yankee Stadium released a joint statement, saying: “The Yankees, Roc Nation and Live Nation express our deep gratitude to the NYPD and to Yankee Stadium security personnel for their leadership and direction throughout the evening, putting the welfare of attendees above all other considerations.”

Despite the challenges, the monumental run marked the acclaimed rapper’s first solo headlining shows in several years, following less than two months behind his return to the stage to headline Philadelphia’s Roots Picnic festival on 30 May.

From here, he is set to continue with his *Jay-Z 30* concert series with dates at London’s Tottenham Hotspur Stadium (4 September), Paris’s Stade de France (10 September), and Los Angeles’ SoFi Stadium (23 October).

All together, he is due to perform seven shows this year.
