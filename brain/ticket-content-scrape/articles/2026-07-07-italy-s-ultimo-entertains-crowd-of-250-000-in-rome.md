# Italy’s Ultimo entertains crowd of 250,000 in Rome

## Summary

Italian singer Ultimo drew 250,000 people to a single show at the University of Rome Tor Vergata on July 4, setting a new record for the largest single-artist concert attendance in Italian music history, surpassing Vasco Rossi's 225,000 mark from 2017. The massive production, promoted by Vivo Concerti, required over 10,000 production workers and 2,500 security staff.

### Key Points
- Ultimo's "La Favola Per Sempre" drew 250,000 attendees, a new Italian concert attendance record.
- Beat the prior record of 225,000 set by Vasco Rossi in 2017.
- Main stage measured 140m wide by 60m high with 38 audio/video towers.
- Production involved 10,000+ crew and 2,500+ security/crowd management staff, plus 500 medical personnel.
- Staged in partnership with the University of Rome Tor Vergata and City of Rome.

TheTicketingBusiness News

**Some 250,000 people came together on July 4 at the University of Rome Tor Vergata for one of the largest single-artist concerts to be held in Europe.**

Italian star Ultimo’s ‘La Favola Per Sempre’ (The Fairy Tale Forever) entertained the monumental crowd, with the show produced and promoted by Vivo Concerti. The concert sold the largest number of tickets in the history of Italian music, with the attendance beating the 225,000 set by fellow Italian artist Vasco Rossi in 2017.

In a post on [Instagram](https://www.instagram.com/p/DaYwi4tEfM4/?utm_source=ig_web_copy_link&igsh=MzRlODBiNWFlZA==), Rossi congratulated Ultimo and the City of Rome, and said: “Every record is meant to be broken.”

The main stage measured 140 metres wide and 60 metres high, with an illuminated structure of Ultimo’s signature above, and a catwalk. To make sure the number of people attending could see the concert, a total of 38 audio and video towers were strategically placed amongst the masses.

More than 10,000 workers aided production, logistics, operations and services, while the event required over 2,500 security and crowd management staff.

To make sure guests remained safe, over 500 medical professionals were present with 10 first aid stations and 20 ambulances. A further 50 members of staff were dedicated to assisting spectators with disabilities, and there was even a dedicated support point for pregnant women.

There were also 20 water tanks supplying 560 free refill points, as well as 46 arena concession stands, 48 food trucks, a kilometre of food and beverage outlets, and 170 roaming vendors.

Vivo Concerti collaborated with the University of Rome Tor Vergata and the City of Rome, while Mayor of Rome Roberto Gualtieri, Councillor for Major Events, Sport, Tourism and Fashion Alessandro Onorato and Rector Nathan Levialdi Ghiron also aided the project.

Following the mega concert, Ultimo announced a stadium tour across Italy in 2027, with stops in Bologna, Milan, Naples, Messina, Florence, Turin and more.
