# Manchester United Fans Furious After Season Ticket Accounts Restricted to Identify ‘Ticket Touts’

## Summary

Manchester United has restricted thousands of season-ticket accounts to investigate suspected ticket touting, triggering backlash from supporter groups after fans received emails saying flagged accounts must submit evidence to avoid losing their tickets. The Manchester United Supporters' Trust says the club is sending suspension and cancellation notices at scale and is calling the process "ban first and ask questions later"; an appeals panel will rule on each case before the Premier League season begins Aug. 22.

### Key Points
- Thousands of Man United season-ticket accounts flagged and restricted pending review for suspected touting.
- Flagged fans must submit evidence to defend their accounts; no tickets revoked yet, appeals panel decides before Aug. 22 kickoff.
- MUST and other supporter groups (The 1958, Red Army) jointly criticized the club's "ban first, ask questions later" approach as lacking due process.
- Dozens of supporter organizations signed a joint statement calling for a pause on suspensions/cancellations during discussions.

### Highlights
- Scale: "thousands" of season-ticket accounts restricted.
- Deadline pressure: appeals must resolve before the Aug. 22 Premier League season opener.
- First time supporter groups across the fanbase have united on a single position, per MUST.

### Industry Problem
Automated fraud/touting detection at scale creates a due-process problem: legitimate long-time season-ticket holders get caught in bot/tout-detection sweeps with limited transparency or recourse, damaging trust even when the underlying anti-touting goal is legitimate.

### Proposed Solution
The club's response (evidence submission + appeals panel) is reactive; a proactive alternative for any primary ticketing platform is transparent, tiered anti-touting detection that shows account holders why they were flagged and offers self-service resolution before any restriction — reducing both touting and false-positive fan anger.

Manchester United fans are voicing their frustrations after the club restricted thousands of season ticket accounts to identify potential ticket touts.

Season ticket holders shared an email with *[BBC Sport](https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/articles/c2elxzrpw4eo)* they received from the club restricting their accounts.

“We have identified patterns linked to your ticketing account that we need to understand more clearly,” the email read. “We need to establish whether there is a straightforward explanation for the activity identified.”

The accounts that were flagged were asked to submit evidence to the club to defend themselves if they feel they were wrongly accused. At this time, no tickets have been revoked; an appeals panel will make a decision on each case before their Premier League season begins on August 22.

Manchester United Supporters’ Trust (MUST) shared in a [social media post](https://x.com/MU_ST) that the club has been “sending out account suspension and season-ticket cancellation notices to thousands of supporters.”

“Long-standing supporters who have followed Manchester United for decades, often across multiple generations of the same family, should never feel they are being treated as suspects without clear evidence, due process or a meaningful opportunity to respond to the allegations made against them,” MUST said in a statement.

“The current approach has created a growing perception among supporters of a “Ban first and ask questions later” process. That is not how trust is built, nor is it consistent with the standards of fairness that loyal supporters should expect from their football club. Investigations should establish the facts before sanctions are imposed, particularly where the potential consequence is the loss of a Season Ticket and relationships with the Club built over many decades.”

MUST called-on the club to pause the issuing of further suspensions and halt the cancellation of season tickets “while meaningful discussions take place.” Dozens of supporter organizations from across the fanbase were added as signatories in a joint statement in support of the Manchester United Fans’ Forum [here](https://www.imust.org.uk/Blog/Entry/joint-statement-in-support-of-the-manchester-united-fans-forum).

Supporters took to social media to air out their frustrations. Many believe that the club is restricting tickets so they can then turn around and sell them for higher prices.

The account restrictions follow a crackdown on ticket touts last season where the club cancelled 22,000 tickets for the first five home games of the season. The club also faced backlash from fans last year for [considering implementing](https://www.ticketnews.com/2025/07/manchester-united-fans-rumored-seat-license-plan/) £4,000 Personal Seat Licenses (PSLs) for their new stadium.
