# Latin Grammy Winner Silvana Estrada Embarking On North American Tour

## Summary

Latin Grammy winner Silvana Estrada announced a North American run mixing headline dates with support slots on Mon Laferte's tour, plus one-off shows with Rubén Blades and Enjambre, running August through December.

### Key Points
- Headline dates in Portland, Vancouver, Miami Beach, and Nashville.
- Supports Mon Laferte across multiple U.S. markets Aug.–Nov.
- Guest appearances with Rubén Blades (Hollywood Bowl) and Enjambre (Boston).

Silvana Estrada USA Tour 2026

Building off a landmark year, Latin Grammy-winning artist Silvana Estrada is embarking on a North American run that includes headline dates along with special guest appearances supporting Mon Laferte’s “Femme Fatale Tour.”

Estrada’s Vendrán Suaves Lluvias was widely recognized as one of the top Latin albums of 2025, cementing her as one of Mexico’s top songwriters of her generation. The 29-year-old star earned a Latin Grammy nomination for the album’s lead single, “Como Un Pájarao,” and she has performed on “Jimmy Kimmel Live!” and “NPR Tiny Desk.”

The young singer will make her way to the States Aug. 8, performing a set at Outside Lands in San Francisco. She will also be headlining shows at Portland’s Wonder Ballroom, Vancouver’s Chan Centre for the Performing Arts, Miami Beach Bandshell in Florida, and Nashville’s City Winery.

Estrada, who won a Latin Grammy in 2022 for Best New Artist, will support Mon Laferte’s tour stops in markets like Charlotte, Detroit, Philadelphia, Austin, Phoenix, Las Vegas and San Jose. She will also perform with Rubén Blades at the Hollywood Bowl in Los Angeles on Sept. 30 and with Mexican rock band Enjambre at Symphony Hall – Boston Pops on Oct. 29. See full routing below.

For tickets to Estrada’s shows, visit her website at [silvanaestrada.com](https://www.silvanaestrada.com/).

The Mexican artist is booked worldwide by United Talent Agency. One of her most successful outings was a three-night stand at Mexico City’s Teatro Metropolitan in January that grossed $501,839 off 9,199 tickets sold, according to Pollstar Boxoffice.
