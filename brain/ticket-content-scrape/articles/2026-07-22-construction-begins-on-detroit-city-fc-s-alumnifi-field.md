# Construction Begins on Detroit City FC’s AlumniFi Field

## Summary

Detroit City FC broke ground on AlumniFi Field, a new 15,000-seat, $150 million soccer-specific stadium in Southwest Detroit expected to open in 2028. The project is the centerpiece of a $200 million-plus mixed-use development that will also include a parking garage and affordable housing, and will be Michigan's only privately owned major sports stadium.

### Key Points
- AlumniFi Field: 15,000-seat, $150M+ stadium, designed by HOK, opening 2028.
- Built on the former Southwest Detroit Hospital site; will be Michigan's only privately owned major sports stadium.
- Broader $200M+ mixed-use development includes a 421-space parking garage and 104-unit residential building (76 units affordable).
- Includes $2.27M in community benefits: neighborhood funding, 3,000 free tickets/year for residents, $17/hour minimum wage, local hiring, and a small-business loan fund.
- Nearly 6,000 seat deposits already placed ahead of the 2028 opening; project approved by Detroit City Council in November 2025.

## Transcript

Detroit City FC recently broke ground on AlumniFi Field, marking the start of construction on the club’s new 15,000-seat soccer stadium in Southwest Detroit.

Club officials were joined by Detroit Mayor Mary Sheffield and project partners for the groundbreaking ceremony at the former Southwest Detroit Hospital site. Designed by HOK, the stadium is expected to open in 2028.

The project is valued at more than $150 million and will become Detroit’s first soccer-specific stadium. It will also be Michigan’s only privately owned major sports stadium.

AlumniFi Field is the centerpiece of a mixed-use development valued at more than $200 million. In addition to the stadium, the project includes a 421-space parking garage and a 104-unit residential building, with 76 units designated as affordable housing. The development is intended to transform the former hospital property into a year-round destination for sports, entertainment and housing.

“As a club built from the ground up by Detroiters, this moment reflects years of work, belief and investment from our supporters and partners,” Detroit City FC co-founder and CEO Sean Mann said during the groundbreaking ceremony. “Breaking ground on AlumniFi Field brings us one step closer to creating a forever home for our supporters and a development that reflects the pride, history and future of this city.”

Construction begins after the Detroit City Council unanimously approved the project in November 2025.

The development includes more than $2.27 million in community benefits. Those commitments include $1.2 million for neighborhood organizations, 3,000 free tickets each year for nearby residents, a $17-per-hour minimum wage, local hiring priorities and a $200,000 revolving loan fund for small businesses.

Supporters have already placed deposits on nearly 6,000 seats ahead of the stadium’s planned opening.

Plans for the site also include recognizing the history of Southwest Detroit Hospital, which opened in 1973 as the first hospital in Detroit to accredit African American doctors and nurses.

Developers said the stadium district will incorporate artistic installations, historical markers and storytelling elements that highlight the hospital’s legacy.

Once completed, AlumniFi Field will serve as Detroit City FC’s permanent home while anchoring the surrounding mixed-use development. The project is expected to bring new housing, public amenities and entertainment opportunities to Southwest Detroit when it opens in 2028.
