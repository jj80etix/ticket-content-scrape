# Mainland Block Party marks million ticket milestone

## Summary

Nigerian block-party brand Mainland Block Party has sold over one million tickets across its events since launching in Lagos in 2018, a milestone organizers say reflects the growing export potential of African live-event IP. The company has expanded from Lagos into Ghana, London, New York, and Houston, and will return to East London on October 26 for its "Last Daze of Summer" event as part of the LDN East dance-music festival.

### Key Points
- Over 1 million tickets sold across Mainland Block Party events since its 2018 Lagos launch.
- Brand has expanded into Ghana, London, New York, and Houston; sold out three New York shows and one Houston show last year.
- Local spin-off formats include Island Block Party (Lagos), Capital Block Party (Abuja), Gardens Block Party (Port Harcourt), Premier Block Party (Ibadan), and Gold Block Party (Ghana).
- Returns to East London Oct. 26 for "Last Daze of Summer" as part of the LDN East dance-music festival.
- Co-founder Tobi Mohammed describes the brand as functioning as a "cultural marketing channel" as much as an event organizer.
- Past performers at Mainland Block Party events include Wale, Tems, Ayra Starr, Omah Lay, and Asake.

## Transcript

As the brand pledges more international editions, organisers say the milestone shows increasing export cachet for African live events

![](https://www.iqmagazine.com/wp-content/smush-webp/2026/07/Mainland-Block-Party-scaled.jpeg.webp)

© Mainland Block Party

Mainland Block Party, popularly known as Nigeria’s biggest block party brand, has announced it has sold more than one million tickets across its events over the past six years, with organisers saying the milestone reflects the increasing export potential of African live event IP.

Unlike traditional concert promoters whose businesses are built around individual headline shows, Mainland Block Party has developed a recurring cultural platform that blends music, youth culture, and community, typically holding monthly festivals across Nigeria as well as overseas.

Its international ambitions have also accelerated in recent years. Following previous periodic editions in London, in addition to last year’s New York Houston shows, the brand will return to East London on 26 October with its one-day *Last Daze of Summer* event as part of the LDN East dance music festival.

Since launching in Mainland Lagos in 2018, the brand has become a fixture on Nigeria’s live entertainment calendar, hosting both Nigerian and international artists including Wale, Tems, Ayra Starr, Omah Lay, Asake, while expanding into multiple editions each year.

From its beginnings as a community-driven event in Mainland Lagos, Mainland Block Party has expanded into Ghana, New York, Houston as well as London, establishing itself among a small but growing group of African-born event brands successfully exporting their live IP.

Announcing the milestone recently, Mainland Block Party co-founder Tobi Mohammed said: “Mainland Block Party has evolved from a place; the Mainland is a feeling. Today, that feeling means two thing: ‘home to everyone’ and ‘come as you are'”.

“We have now built something that sold out three times in New York last year and one time in Houston. We’re going back to New York this year and doing the same thing,” he added.

That evolution has now seen the brand move beyond its original identity. When Mainland Block Party launched in 2018, its founders set out to challenge long-held perceptions of mainland Lagos by creating a cultural experience that demonstrated the area could be just as vibrant and creatively significant as anywhere else in the city.

Community and inclusivity became central to the platform’s identity from the outset, laying the foundation for a brand that would eventually expand far beyond its place of origin. As the platform grew, the event evolved from a geographical reference into a brand identity capable of travelling beyond Lagos.

> “Mainland Block Party has evolved from a place; the Mainland is a feeling”

That repositioning has become central to the company’s expansion strategy, with Mainland Block Party consistently adapting its concept to each market rather than simply replicating the same event across different cities.

Alongside its international editions, it has introduced localised formats including Island Block Party in Lagos’ commercial and more affluent district, Capital Block Party in Abuja, Gardens Block Party in Port Harcourt, Premier Block Party in Ibadan and Gold Block Party in Ghana.

The company’s strategy for entering new markets has centred on adapting to different audiences, forging local partnerships and building communities that extend beyond the Nigerian diaspora. Those partnerships now span media, artists, and commercial brands, including companies across the beverage, financial services, and streaming sectors, many of which are pursuing their own cross-border growth strategies aimed at both local and diaspora audiences.

According to co-founder Mohammed, Mainland Block Party has evolved beyond simply organising events into a platform through which brands engage audiences. In that sense, the company functions as a cultural marketing channel as much as a live event organiser.

The one-million-ticket milestone therefore explains more than audience demand, it also speaks to the growing ability of African live brands to build repeatable event formats, cultivate loyal communities and expand beyond their domestic markets.

As African music continues to travel globally, Mainland Block Party’s trajectory suggests a parallel opportunity not to limit the continent’s exports to artists alone, but to extend it to the live event brands, cultural experiences, and audience communities built around them.
