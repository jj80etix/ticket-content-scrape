# StubHub and CEO face class action lawsuit

## Summary

StubHub and its founder/CEO Eric Baker face a proposed $5m federal class action in the Southern District of New York alleging the company deceptively marketed itself as a neutral fan-to-fan marketplace while Baker's hedge fund, Andro Capital, engaged in large-scale professional ticket resale on StubHub itself. The suit follows StubHub's SEC disclosure of Baker's hedge-fund ties and lands alongside a separate active lawsuit over cancelled FIFA World Cup tickets sold through the platform.

### Key Points
- Plaintiff Louis Sanquini filed the proposed class action in SDNY, alleging deceptive practices and fraudulent misrepresentation; the suit seeks roughly $5m (£3.7m/€4.3m) in class damages.
- Baker disclosed in SEC filings that he runs Andro Capital, a hedge fund engaged in "large-scale resale of millions of dollars' worth of sports and concert tickets" on StubHub.
- The suit claims Sanquini would not have bought tickets on StubHub in 2023 and 2024 had the conflict of interest been disclosed.
- StubHub separately faces a lawsuit from fans whose FIFA World Cup tickets were cancelled at the last minute; StubHub attributes the issue to the event organiser's own ticketing infrastructure and points to its FanProtect Guarantee (replacement tickets or full refund).

### Highlights
- A $5m proposed class action targets both StubHub and CEO Eric Baker personally over alleged concealment of his hedge fund's scalping activity on the platform.
- SEC filings reveal Baker's Andro Capital engages in "large-scale resale of millions of dollars' worth" of tickets via StubHub itself.
- Plaintiff says he would not have transacted on StubHub in 2023-2024 had the conflict been disclosed.
- Comes alongside a separate, still-active lawsuit over cancelled World Cup ticket resales on StubHub.

### Industry Problem
Patrons using resale marketplaces assume they are buying from other fans on a neutral platform, but StubHub's own leadership allegedly holds an undisclosed financial stake in large-scale professional reselling on that same marketplace — undermining the "fan-to-fan" trust premise that secondary-market demand relies on.

### Proposed Solution
The article describes no StubHub remediation (the company is defending, not fixing); for Etix, this reinforces the case for monitoring as litigation and regulatory pressure on secondary marketplaces mounts, and for building primary-side resale/transfer tools with transparent, verifiable seller identity rather than routing fans to third-party marketplaces with opaque incentive conflicts.

TheTicketingBusiness News

**StubHub and its founder, chief executive and chairman Eric Baker are facing a proposed class action lawsuit in the US over the resale marketplace’s alleged ties to large-scale scalpers.**

First reported by [*CBC News*](https://www.cbc.ca/news/world/stubhub-ceo-class-action-scalping-9.7268987), the proposed $5m (£3.7m/€4.3m) class action lawsuit was filed earlier this week by New York resident Louis Sanquini. The lawsuit alleges deceptive practices and fraudulent misrepresentation around StubHub promotion as a neutral ticket marketplace for fans to sell tickets to other fans.

*CBC* reported recently that Baker disclosed in recent filings with the US Securities and Exchange Commission that he runs Andro Capital, which is a hedge fund that engages in “large-scale resale of millions of dollars’ worth of sports and concert tickets” on StubHub.

Sanquini filed the proposed class action in the Southern District of New York, arguing that consumers have been kept in the dark as he believed StubHub was a ‘neutral’ marketplace.

In a statement to CBC News, lead counsel Kevin Steinberg said: “While what StubHub is alleged to have engaged in and perpetrated upon millions of patrons is unfathomable, this case is about transparency and consumer trust. If companies make representations to the public, consumers are entitled to expect that those representations are complete and accurate.”

The lawsuit said that StubHub’s “failure to disclose this conflict of interest, while affirmatively marketing StubHub as a fan-to-fan marketplace, deceived Plaintiff and the Class and caused them to pay prices, and accept terms they would not have accepted had the truth been known”.

Sanquini argued in the suit that if he had known StubHub’s CEO held a financial interest, and that the company was helping finance professional resellers, he would not have used the site to buy tickets in 2023 and 2024.

**World Cup**

StubHub was also recently [sued by fans](https://www.theticketingbusiness.com/2026/07/stubhub-sued-over-cancelled-world-cup-tickets/) that had FIFA World Cup tickets cancelled at the last minute.

In response to the World Cup lawsuit, a StubHub spokesperson said: “Our singular goal is to get fans into events, and if anything goes wrong, our FanProtect Guarantee provides replacement tickets or a full refund.

“The World Cup is no different, and the issues fans have experienced are largely driven by problems with the event organiser’s own ticketing infrastructure.”
