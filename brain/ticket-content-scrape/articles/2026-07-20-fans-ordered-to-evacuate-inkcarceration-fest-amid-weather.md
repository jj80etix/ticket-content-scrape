# Fans Ordered to Evacuate Inkcarceration Fest Amid Weather

## Summary

Inkcarceration, Ohio's music-and-tattoo festival held July 17-19 at The Ohio State Reformatory in Mansfield, ordered fans to evacuate the grounds around 4:30 p.m. on July 18 due to severe weather. The National Weather Service had forecast 50-mph winds and pea-sized hail for the area. The festival reopened by 6:30 p.m. the same day, with headliner Bad Omens still performing, and Sunday's schedule — including headliner Limp Bizkit — proceeded as planned.

### Key Points
- Festival ran July 17-19 at The Ohio State Reformatory, Mansfield, Ohio.
- Evacuation ordered around 4:30 p.m. on July 18 due to forecast 50-mph winds and pea-sized hail.
- Campers were directed to shelter in vehicles or designated shelter locations; other attendees sheltered inside the Reformatory.
- Grounds reopened at 6:30 p.m.; headliner Bad Omens performed as scheduled.
- Sunday lineup proceeded unaffected, including A Day to Remember, Motionless in White, Ice Nine Kills, Lorna Shore, Fit for a King, and headliner Limp Bizkit.

## Transcript

![](https://www.ticketnews.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/inkcarceration.jpg)

[Inkcarceration](https://www.ticketnews.com/2026/01/inkcarceration-limp-bizkit-disturbed-bad-omens/), Ohio’s music and tattoo festival, had to evacuate grounds over the weekend due to weather concerns.

The festival ran from July 17 to 19 at The Ohio State Reformatory in Mansfield. During the second day of the festival on Saturday, July 18, festival organizers ordered fans to evacuate the grounds around 4:30 p.m. The National Weather Service released a statement, noting that “gusty winds could knock down tree limbs and blow around unsecured objects,” adding that “minor hail damage to vegetation is possible.”

Mansfield was expected to receive 50-mph winds and pea-sized hail, NWS said.

“If you camped, please return to the campground via shuttle and shelter in your vehicle or a designated shelter location,” Inkcarceration organizers said in a statement. “Shelter locations are marked on the camping map and with onsite signage. Do not shelter in tents.”

Other attendees were asked to seek shelter inside the Ohio State Reformatory.

The festival reopened at 6:30 p.m., and headliners Bad Omens still took the stage. Sunday’s schedule resumed as normal with performances from acts like A Day to Remember, Motionless in White, Ice Nine Kills, Lorna Shore, and Fit for a King, followed by a headlining set from Limp Bizkit.
