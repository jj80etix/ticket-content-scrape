# BBC Introducing Stage cut from Reading and Leeds

## Summary

Festival Republic is retiring the 18-year-old BBC Introducing stage at Reading and Leeds, replacing it with the in-house "Canopy" stage so any emerging artist — not just BBC-selected acts — can be booked.

### Key Points
- BBC Introducing stage supported roughly 70% of this year's UK lineup artists at some point in their careers.
- Replaced by the expanded, non-BBC-gated Canopy stage.
- Festival Republic says BBC Introducing partnership continues at other festivals (e.g., Latitude).

## Transcript

![TheTicketingBusiness News](https://www.theticketingbusiness.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/04/TBN-LOGO-reversed-white.png)

TheTicketingBusiness News

**The BBC Introducing stage at Reading and Leeds Festivals has been axed after 18 years.**

Since its launch in 2008, the stage has hosted the likes of Ed Sheeran and The 1975, as well as this year’s headliners Florence + The Machine, Charli XCX, Dave, and Raye.

Event organiser Festival Republic confirmed the stage overhaul and claims that it would mean that the festivals would now be open to a “much wider pool of emerging talent”.

As part of the plans, it said it would no longer host a BBC Introducing stage, despite the scheme having supported roughly 70% of all the UK artists on this year’s line-up.

Both Little John’s Farm in Reading and Bramham Park in Leeds will have up to six stages on site, with improvements to the Main Stage now known as The Grid, along with new stages The Gallery and The Warehouse.

Festival Republic has said that the new Canopy stage will be the home for new and breaking acts.

“This year, we launched The Canopy, our new music stage for emerging artists. We have increased both its size and production level, enhancing the live experience for performers and audiences alike,” a spokesperson told [NME](https://www.nme.com/news/music/bbc-introducing-stage-axed-from-reading-leeds-festival-after-18-years-3958200).

“Previously, only artists who had been selected by the BBC were qualified to play the Introducing Stage – now, we are able to open up this important platform to a much wider pool of emerging talent and book any new music artist for the stage.

“The change is fundamentally about supporting a more diverse range of up-and-coming artists, who will hopefully go on to headline the festival in years to come.

“Festival Republic continues to work with BBC Introducing across other festivals in our portfolio, including Latitude Festival this week, where they are hosting a stage featuring a strong lineup of emerging artists, as well as at The Great Escape.”

Reading and Leeds are set to run concurrently from August 27-30 this year.
