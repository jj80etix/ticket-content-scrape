# Olivia Rodrigo Resale Ticket Prices Drop to Less Than £190 for O2 Arena

## Summary

Resale prices for Olivia Rodrigo's 11-night O2 Arena residency (part of her Live Nation-promoted "Unraveled Tour") have fallen below Ticketmaster's primary pricing, with viagogo listing seats from £186-£211 versus roughly £300 on Ticketmaster. This reverses the pattern fans complained about at initial onsale in May, when primary prices ranged from over $200 in upper sections to nearly $800 on the floor and were called "illegal" by frustrated buyers on social media.

### Key Points
- Rodrigo's "Unraveled Tour" runs September 2026 through May 2027 across North America, Europe, and the UK, with 11 nights at London's O2 Arena (April 5-May 10, 2027).
- At initial onsale in May, primary Ticketmaster prices ranged from over $200 (upper sections) to nearly $800 (floor), drawing public backlash.
- Ticketmaster's current O2 pricing runs around £300 per ticket; on resale marketplace viagogo, the lowest listed prices for the same dates are £186-£211.
- The gap illustrates secondary prices falling below primary list price for at least some dates/sections of a high-demand tour, contrary to the usual resale-markup narrative used to explain high consumer costs.

### Highlights
- Viagogo resale listings (£186-£211) currently undercut Ticketmaster primary pricing (~£300) for multiple O2 Arena dates on Rodrigo's residency.
- Initial May onsale primary pricing spanned $200-$800 depending on section, prompting public "illegal" pricing complaints.
- Case involves an 11-night residency at a single venue (The O2, London), a Live Nation-promoted tour, sold via Ticketmaster primary and viagogo secondary.

### Industry Problem
This is a concrete data point on secondary-market price dynamics (Q6): resale isn't uniformly a markup over primary — for some high-demand tours, secondary supply and softening demand can push resale below list price, complicating the industry narrative that blames resale platforms for driving consumer costs.

### Proposed Solution
No solution is proposed in the article itself; this is Monitor-level evidence for the secondary-market state of play. Etix should watch for further instances where resale undercuts primary pricing, since a pattern here would strengthen arguments for more flexible/dynamic primary pricing or refund-and-rebuy flexibility as a client-facing differentiator, without triggering a build response from this single data point alone.

Pop star Olivia Rodrigo is set to perform an 11-night residency at London’s O2 Arena next year, and while tickets initially seemed hard to obtain, seats are now available for under £200 — on resale sites.

While primary markets often point the blame to the resale market for expensive ticket prices, tickets to Rodrigo’s residency at the O2 can be found for cheaper than Ticketmaster, which totals around £300 each. On the resale site viagogo, the lowest-priced tickets start at £186 for her gig at the arena on April 19. Other dates throughout the residency list tickets between £186 and £211.
