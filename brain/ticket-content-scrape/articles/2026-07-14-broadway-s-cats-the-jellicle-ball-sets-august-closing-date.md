# Broadway’s ‘Cats: The Jellicle Ball’ Sets August Closing Date

## Summary

The Ballroom-inspired Broadway revival "Cats: The Jellicle Ball" will close August 8 at the Broadhurst Theatre after opening in April following an Off-Broadway run. The production, co-directed by Zhailon Levingston and Bill Rauch, won three 2026 Tony Awards including Best Direction of a Musical.

### Key Points
- "Cats: The Jellicle Ball" closes August 8 at the Broadhurst Theatre.
- Opened April 7 on Broadway following an Off-Broadway run at PAC NYC.
- Won three 2026 Tony Awards: Direction, Choreography, Costume Design.
- Cast includes André De Shields, Chasity Moore and Junior LaBeija.


## Transcript

![](https://www.ticketnews.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/Cats-The-Jellicle-Ball-1.webp)

Broadway’s reimagined revival of “Cats,” titled “Cats: The Jellicle Ball,” will play its final performance at the Broadhurst Theatre on August 8.

The production began previews March 18 and officially opened April 7 following an earlier Off-Broadway engagement at the Perelman Performing Arts Center.

Producers Michael Harrison and Mike Bosner announced the closing in a statement that praised co-directors Zhailon Levingston and Bill Rauch, the creative team and the cast for developing a new interpretation of Andrew Lloyd Webber’s musical.

“Three years ago, Zhailon Levingston and Bill Rauch began the remarkable process of reimagining ‘Cats’ for a new generation,” the producers said. “They assembled a visionary creative team that fused their passions for Ballroom and theatre to create something thrillingly new.”

“With a truly superhuman cast bringing this vision to life, New York has once again discovered the phenomenon that has become Andrew Lloyd Webber’s ‘Cats: The Jellicle Ball,’” Harrison and Bosner said. “The joy that radiates from the stage each night is unlike anything we’ve experienced in our careers.”

They added, “It has been a true honor to help bring Ballroom to Broadway.”

The revival received three awards at the 2026 Tony Awards. Levingston and Rauch won Best Direction of a Musical, Omari Wiles and Arturo Lyons received Best Choreography and Qween Jean won Best Costume Design of a Musical.

Much of the cast from the Off-Broadway production returned for the Broadway transfer. The company includes André De Shields as Old Deuteronomy, “Tempress” Chasity Moore as Grizabella, Junior LaBeija as Gus and Sydney James Harcourt as Rum Tum Tugger.

**| RELATED:** [**Off-Broadway’s ‘Heathers’ Sets Final Performance Date**](https://www.ticketnews.com/2026/06/off-broadways-heathers-sets-final-performance-date/) **|**

Other returning performers include Jonathan Burke, Baby Byrne, Dava Huesca, Dudney Joseph Jr., Robert “Silk” Mason, Primo Thee Ballerino, Xavier Reyes, Nora Schell, Bebé Nicole Simpson, Sofia, Garnet Williams, Teddy Wilson Jr. and Tara Lashan Clinkscales.

The Broadway cast also features Leiomy as Mavacity, Kya Azeen as Etcetera, Bryson Battle as Jellylorum and Ken Ard as DJ Griddlebone. Ard originated the role of Macavity in the original 1982 Broadway production.

The creative team includes scenic designer Rachel Hauck, lighting designer Adam Honoré, sound designer Kai Harada, projection designer Brittany Bland, hair and wig designer Nikiya Mathis, makeup designer Rania Zohny and music supervisor and director William Waldrop. Casting is by Victor Vazquez and Sujotta R. Pace of X Casting.

A previous Broadway revival ran from 2016 to 2017. “Cats: The Jellicle Ball” is the musical’s second Broadway revival. Michael Harrison and Mike Bosner are the lead producers.
