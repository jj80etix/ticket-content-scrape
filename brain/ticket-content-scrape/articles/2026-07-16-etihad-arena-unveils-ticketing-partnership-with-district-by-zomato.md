# Etihad Arena unveils ticketing partnership with District by Zomato

## Summary

Abu Dhabi's Etihad Arena has named District by Zomato as an official ticketing partner, integrating the discovery-and-dining app — which acquired Paytm Insider's ticketing business in 2024 for $244.1m (~€219m) — into the venue's existing ticketing ecosystem to boost event discovery on Yas Island. The deal is framed as part of Abu Dhabi's broader tourism strategy, as Etihad Arena also faces new competition from a planned $1.7bn Sphere venue confirmed for the same island.

### Key Points
- Etihad Arena (Yas Island, Abu Dhabi) named District by Zomato its official ticketing partner, integrating District with the venue's existing ticketing system.
- District is primarily a dining/attractions/entertainment discovery platform; it acquired Paytm Insider's ticketing business in 2024 for $244.1m.
- Venue GM Marcus Osborne frames the deal as supporting Abu Dhabi's tourism ambitions; District's Kunal Khambhati calls it a milestone for the firm's UAE live-events push.
- Etihad Arena has recently hosted Blackpink, Imagine Dragons, and Disney on Ice, and now faces new competition from a $1.7bn Sphere Entertainment venue confirmed for Yas Island in May 2026.

## Transcript

The move is the latest shift by entertainment aggregator Zomato towards the ticketing sector

![](https://www.iqmagazine.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/District-by-Zomato-Etihad-Arena.png)

Marcus Osborne (left) and Kunal Khambhati (right) © Zomato

Etihad Arena has named District by Zomato as an official ticketing partner, in a move aimed to boost event discovery and visitor footfall to the city’s entertainment epicentre of Yas Island.

The agreement will see District integrate with Etihad Arena’s existing ticketing ecosystem, providing its users with direct access to the venue’s programme. District is primarily a discovery platform covering dining, attractions, and entertainment.

However, in 2024 it [acquired Paytm Insider](https://www.iqmagazine.com/2024/09/india-paytm-insider-acquired-220m/), the fintech company’s entertainment ticketing business, in a deal worth $244.1m (€219m).

Etihad Arena is aiming for the partnership to form part of Abu Dhabi’s wider strategy to grow its tourism and events economy. The venue’s general manager Marcus Osborne noted that the collaboration is intended to support audience development and strengthen the venue’s position within the emirate’s cultural plans.

“As we continue to grow our global audience, partnerships like this play an important role in reaching new audiences and supporting Abu Dhabi’s wider tourism ambitions,” he said, adding: “Together, we can strengthen our offering and continue delivering world-class experiences to audiences in the UAE and beyond.”

District described the agreement as an expansion of its activities in the live events sector. Founder and COO of Events at the firm, Kunal Khambhati, said the collaboration “marks a significant milestone for District as we continue to redefine the live events experience across the UAE”.

He added: “Etihad Arena has established itself as a premier home for exceptional entertainment, and together, we’re committed to delivering world-class, fan-first experiences.”

The partnership also gives Etihad Arena an additional route to engage potential attendees earlier in the discovery process, while offering touring productions another mechanism to reach audiences in the capital, Zomato said.

The development comes as Yas Island continues to build out its live‑entertainment infrastructure, with Abu Dhabi positioning itself to compete more actively for large‑scale tours and international productions.

Etihad Arena has recently hosted a run of large‑scale international productions, including Blackpink, Imagine Dragons, as well as Disney on Ice, alongside regular regional headline shows.

However, the arena is set to face firmer competition for market share after it was [announced](https://www.iqmagazine.com/2026/05/sphere-confirms-1-7bn-abu-dhabi-expansion/) in May that Sphere Entertainment and Abu Dhabi’s Department of Culture and Tourism have confirmed plans to construct a Sphere venue also on Yas Island, at a cost of $1.7 billion (€1.5billion).
