# Music Venue Properties purchases The Pipeline in Brighton

## Summary

Music Venue Properties has acquired The Pipeline, a grassroots music venue in Brighton, UK, marking its 10th venue purchase through the "Own Our Venues" community-ownership initiative. The deal is meant to give operator Thomas Evrenos long-term security against commercial property market pressure so the venue can keep supporting emerging artists and the local alternative music scene. DJ Fatboy Slim, an investor in Music Venue Properties, publicly encouraged musicians and fans to invest in the organization as well.

### Key Points
- Music Venue Properties acquired The Pipeline in Brighton, its 10th grassroots venue purchase via the Own Our Venues initiative.
- Prior acquisitions include venues in Atherton, Preston, Newport, Swansea, Dover, Bristol, Southampton, Leeds and Sheffield.
- The initiative aims to protect grassroots venues from commercial property market volatility through community/investor ownership.
- Fatboy Slim (Norman Cook) is a named investor publicly promoting further investment in the organization.
- Venue operator Thomas Evrenos says the purchase secures the building's future and lets the team focus on programming rather than property risk.

TheTicketingBusiness News

**Music Venue Properties has acquired The Pipeline in Brighton, marking the 10** **<sup>th</sup>** **grassroots music venue it has secured.**

The venue was purchased through the organisation’s Own Our Venues initiative, which aims to provide operators with long-term security while safeguarding cultural spaces.

“The Pipeline has always been more than just a venue,” said the venue’s operator Thomas Evrenos.

“It’s a home for live music, emerging artists and the alternative community that has grown around it.

“I’m very proud that Music Venue Properties chose The Pipeline to become part of its portfolio.

“Everyone here is really happy about this, not just the team at The Pipeline but the wider Brighton music community, our staff, regulars and the musicians who have made this place what it is.

“Knowing the building is now secured means we can focus on what matters most: putting on great shows, supporting grassroots talent and giving people a place to belong.

“Community ownership has given this venue a future, protecting it from the uncertainty of the commercial property market and ensuring The Pipeline can continue to play its part in Brighton’s music scene for years to come.”

Music Venue Properties also previously purchased The Snug in Atherton, The Ferret in Preston, Le Pub in Newport, The Bunkhouse in Swansea, The Booking Hall in Dover, The Croft in Bristol, The Joiners in Southampton, Northern Guitars in Leeds, and Gut Level in Sheffield.

DJ and producer Norman Cook aka Fatboy Slim, who has invested in Music Venue Properties, is encouraging musicians, music fans and investors to support the organisation.

“I have invested a substantial sum and I am proud to be able to help protect the nurturing environments for future musicians and DJs,” he said.

“I urge anyone to join me, whether you are a successful musician wanting to give something back to the lower rungs of the food chain or a music fan who wants to feel part of the wonderful community.

“It’s not a donation, it’s an investment in the future of our wonderful music industry.”
