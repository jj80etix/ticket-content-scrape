# Live Nation’s Munich Arena Cleared for Next Development Phase

## Summary

Live Nation's planned 20,000-capacity Munich Arena received key zoning/planning approval from the City of Freising, clearing the way for the next development phase ahead of a targeted 2029 opening. A Deloitte study projects ~€355m in annual gross value added for the Munich region once operational.

### Key Points
- Planning approval adopts the Zoning Plan and Land Use Plan amendment for the site near Munich International Airport.
- 20,000-capacity venue, targeted for 2029 opening.
- Deloitte estimates ~€355m annual regional gross value added once operational.
- Designed with a "Music First" approach: modern production tech, advanced acoustics, Bavarian-flag-inspired diamond exterior.

## Transcript

![Photo courtesy Live Nation](https://www.ticketnews.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/07/Munich-Arena-Live-Nation.webp)

Photo courtesy Live Nation

Munich Arena has received key planning approval from the City of Freising, allowing the 20,000-capacity venue project to move into its next phase of development ahead of its planned opening in 2029.

The approval completes the city’s planning process and formally adopts the Zoning Plan and related amendment to the Land Use Plan. The project will now continue development at its location near Munich International Airport.

The venue will be designed to accommodate a variety of live entertainment events, including rock, pop, hip-hop and electronic music shows. It will also provide space for promoters, artists and event organizers across different types of productions.

Tom Lynch, President of Venues EMEA at Live Nation, shared, “This approval is an important step forward for Munich Arena and for the future of live entertainment in Bavaria. Munich is already one of Europe’s powerhouse cities, and this arena will add a major new stage, bringing more national and international talent to the region and creating lasting value for artists, fans and the wider community.”

The arena’s exterior design will feature a diamond-shaped structure inspired by the Bavarian flag. Developers said the design will create a new landmark for the region while reflecting the area’s local heritage.

The project is also expected to provide economic benefits for the region. A Deloitte study estimates that Munich Arena will generate approximately €355 million in annual gross value added for Munich and the surrounding area once the venue is operational.

Lorenz Schmid, Managing Partner of Munich Arena, said, “Munich Arena is not simply being developed as a new venue, but as a place where the power of live music creates lifelong memories and brings people together from across Bavaria, Europe and beyond,” Schmid said.

Munich Arena will be built with a “Music First” approach, featuring modern production technology, advanced acoustics and seating arrangements designed to provide strong views from throughout the venue. The project is intended to create an improved experience for both performers and audiences.

Once completed, the venue is expected to strengthen Munich and Bavaria’s role in the European live entertainment market. Developers anticipate that the arena will attract visitors from Germany, Europe and other international markets.

Freising Lord Mayor, Monika Schwind shared, “The concert arena has the potential to provide an important cultural and economic stimulus for our City and the wider region. It represents international visibility, new value creation and a location determined to shape its own future.

“Particularly in economically challenging times, projects such as this can provide a real source of optimism – provided they are carefully planned, transparently supported and responsibly implemented. The decision represents an important milestone on that path.”
