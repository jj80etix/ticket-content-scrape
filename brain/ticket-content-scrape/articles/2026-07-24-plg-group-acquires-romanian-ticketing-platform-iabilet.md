# PLG Group acquires Romanian ticketing platform iaBilet

## Summary

PLG Group has signed an agreement to acquire Romanian ticketing platform iaBilet from Resource Partners, with iaBilet's founders staying on to help run the business. The deal is framed by both sides as a growth play: iaBilet CEO Emil Ionescu called it a chance to "conquer the world," while PLG Group founder Sven Nuutmann pointed to strong long-term growth potential across Central and Eastern European ticketing, noting average ticket prices there remain roughly half those in Western/Southern Europe. It's a small, single-market acquisition rather than a headline-grabbing deal, but it fits a pattern of regional ticketing consolidation.

### Key Points
- PLG Group acquired iaBilet, a Romanian ticketing platform, from prior owner Resource Partners.
- iaBilet's founders (including CEO Emil Ionescu) remain involved, including at board level.
- iaBilet was founded roughly 15 years ago.
- PLG Group cites CEE ticketing as a strong long-term growth market, with average ticket prices ~2x lower than Western/Southern Europe.
- The deal is explicitly framed as expansion strategy tied to inflationary trends and rising audience-experience expectations in the region.

### Highlights
- Acquirer: PLG Group. Target: iaBilet (Romania), sold by Resource Partners.
- Rationale given: CEE ticket prices are ~2x lower than Western/Southern Europe, implying room for growth/monetization.
- Founding team retained at board level post-acquisition — a common structure for cross-border ticketing roll-ups.

### Industry Problem
No stated operational pain point here; this is a consolidation move by an acquirer building a multi-market European ticketing group rather than a response to a specific client or patron complaint.

### Proposed Solution
No product solution described — the "solution" is inorganic growth (roll-up of a national ticketing platform into a larger regional group). Watch for whether PLG-style consolidators start bundling technology/fee models across their acquired platforms in ways that could pressure independent primary platforms competing for European venue/promoter clients; no direct posture change warranted from a single small acquisition.
