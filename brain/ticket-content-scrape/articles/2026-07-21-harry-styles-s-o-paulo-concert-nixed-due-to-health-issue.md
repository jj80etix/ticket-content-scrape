# Harry Styles São Paulo Concert Nixed Due to ‘Health Issue’

## Summary

Harry Styles' July 21 São Paulo concert was cancelled hours before showtime due to a tour health issue; Live Nation says tickets will be refunded through the original purchase channel. His July 24 São Paulo show proceeds as planned, with affected ticketholders offered access to a limited additional ticket batch.

### Key Points
- Cancellation announced the morning of the show via Live Nation social media.
- Refunds issued through original purchase channel; details to follow by email.
- Friday's replacement show has very limited additional capacity for affected fans.

## Transcript

Harry Styles’ São Paulo concert Tuesday night has been abruptly cancelled due to a health issue.

The gig, a part of Styles’ “Together Together” tour, was set to take place at Brazil’s MorumBIS on July 21. However, early Tuesday morning, Live Nation took to [social media](https://x.com/LiveNationBR/status/2079538596982276543) to share that the concert will no longer go on as planned.

“We deeply regret to inform you that the Harry Styles show scheduled for Tuesday, July 21, 2026, at MorumBIS has been canceled due to a health issue on the tour,” Live Nation said. “Tickets for this show will be refunded through the same purchase channel.”

The organizer added that an email with more information will be sent to all ticketholders.

Additionally, Styles’ show on Friday, July 24 is still set to move forward as planned. Those who purchased tickets for the Tuesday, July 21 show will have the opportunity to purchase an “exclusive batch of tickets” for the Friday show while supplies last; ticketholders will receive more information about this option via email.

“We have worked with the event venue to release as many tickets as possible for the Friday show, but please keep in mind that availability is extremely limited,” Live Nation added.

Styles’ “Together Together” tour, which kicked-off in May, is set to run through December. Following the Friday São Paulo show, Styles will head to Mexico City for a pair of shows, and then he’ll appear in New York for a massive residency at New York City’s Madison Square Garden.
