# Don Was And The Pan-Detroit Ensemble Unveil 2026 Fall Dates

## Summary

Don Was and The Pan-Detroit Ensemble announced fall 2026 tour dates supporting their new album "Groove in the Face of Adversity," kicking off Aug. 3 in Phoenix and running through Oct. 18 in Bar Harbor, Maine. The routing includes Telluride Jazz Festival and Willie Nelson's Outlaw Music Festival Tour stops.

### Key Points
- Tour runs Aug. 3 - Oct. 18, 2026, from Phoenix to Bar Harbor, Maine.
- Includes stops at Telluride Jazz Festival and multiple Outlaw Music Festival Tour dates.
- New album "Groove in the Face of Adversity" blends Detroit jazz, blues, and funk influences.
- Set includes a live rendition of the Grateful Dead's "Blues for Allah," marking its 50th anniversary.
- Booked by The Kurland Agency and High Road Touring.

Don Was and The Pan-Detroit Ensemble are hitting the road this fall to promote their new album *Groove in the Face of Adversity*, which channels the soul of Detroit with jazz, blues and funk.

In addition to performing music from the new album, Was and the band will also feature a live rendition of The Grateful Dead’s *Blues for Allah*, honoring the album’s 50 <sup>th</sup> anniversary and Was’ musical association with Bobby Weir.

The Pan-Detroit Ensemble includes top-tier jazz musicians from Was’ hometown, including saxophonist Dave McMurray, trombonist Vincent Chandler, John Douglas on trumpet, drummer Jeff Canady, Mahindi Masai on percussion, guitarist Wayne Gerard and vocalist Steffanie Christ’ian.

“There’s a unique sound and feel to Detroit that permeates the music in a way that resonates all over the globe,” Was said in a statement. “There’s a rawness, a lack of pretension, and an unmistakable underlying groove that reflects the people and culture of the entire city.”

The routing for Don Was and The Pan-Detroit Ensemble festival stops includes Telluride Jazz Festival and Willie Nelson’s Outlaw Music Festival Tour. They kick off their tour Aug. 3 at MIM in Phoenix, and they will also be visiting markets like Boulder Theater in Colorado, Cincinnati’s The Ludlow Garage, Antone’s Nightclub in Austin, The Alluvion in Traverse City, New York’s Le Poisson Rouge and The Sinclair in Boston before wrapping up Oct. 18 at Criterion Theatre in Bar Harbor, Maine.

Don Was and The Pan-Detroit Ensemble is booked by The Kurland Agency and High Road Touring. The band recently performed at High Sierra Music Festival, Big Ears Festival and Waterfront Blues Festival. The band played a sold-out show earlier this year at The Atlantis in Washington, D.C., where they grossed $15,750 from 450 tickets sold, according to Pollstar Boxoffice.
