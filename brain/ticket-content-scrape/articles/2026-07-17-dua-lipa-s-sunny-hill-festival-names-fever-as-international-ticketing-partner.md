# Dua Lipa’s Sunny Hill Festival names Fever as international ticketing partner

## Summary

Kosovo's Sunny Hill Festival, co-founded by Dua Lipa and her father, has named event-discovery platform Fever as its official international ticketing partner for the 2026 edition (July 31-Aug 2), aiming to lower barriers for international travelers and grow the festival's tourism impact. The 2025 edition drew 100,000+ attendees from 60+ countries and generated an estimated €22m in direct economic impact.

### Key Points
- Fever named international ticketing partner for Sunny Hill Festival 2026.
- 2025 edition: 100,000+ attendees, 60+ countries, ~€22m direct economic impact.
- 2026 lineup includes Katy Perry, Martin Garrix, Lewis Capaldi, and others.
- Partnership is framed around discovery, booking, and travel for international fans, not just local ticket sales.

### Highlights
- Fever is positioned explicitly as an international-discovery-to-purchase-to-travel partner, not just a local box office.
- The festival already draws attendees from 60+ countries, a genuine cross-border demand base.

### Industry Problem
Mid-size international festivals need a ticketing partner that also solves audience discovery and cross-border travel funneling, not just point-of-sale — a broader bundle than traditional primary ticketing covers.

### Proposed Solution
Signals a build/improve opportunity in international-discovery and travel-bundling features for primary platforms competing for festival business with meaningful cross-border attendance.

TheTicketingBusiness News

**Sunny Hill Festival, the largest music festival in Kosovo, has selected Fever as its official ticketing partner for 2026.**

The three-day festival in Pristina drew more than 100,000 attendees from over 60 countries in 2025, generated €22m (£18.7m/$25.1m) in direct economic impact, and is set to take place from July 31 to August 2 this year.

The partnership is said to be designed to lower barriers for international travellers, foster cultural exchange and strengthen the festival’s contribution to the regional tourism economy.

“Sunny Hill was always about more than music,” said Dukagjin Dugi Lipa, father of Dua Lipa, who co-founded the event with his daughter.

“For a few days every summer, we bring the world to Kosovo — and the whole country feels it.

“The attention the festival brings is exposure no tourism campaign could ever buy, and the value it creates stays at home: in our visitor economy, in our cultural sector, and in the opportunities we open for young people.

“Fever’s reach and technology will make it easier for fans everywhere to discover Sunny Hill, secure their tickets and make the journey to Prishtina.”

The 2026 lineup includes Katy Perry, Dhurata Dora, Martin Garrix, Lewis Capaldi, Blanco, GJIN, Ledri Vula, Adam Port and Rampa of Keinemusik, PAWSA, Seth Troxler, Carlita, EsDeeKid, MC Kresha, and Manifest, among others.

Founded in 2018, the festival is promoted internationally by three-time Grammy Award winner Dua Lipa.

Previous editions have featured Dua Lipa, Miley Cyrus, Calvin Harris, J Balvin, Shawn Mendes, Stormzy, Burna Boy, Bebe Rexha, DJ Snake, Black Coffee, Peggy Gou, Anyma, and Hardwell.

“Sunny Hill is that rare event where a great festival is also a growth engine for an entire country,” said Mariano Otero, senior vice president at Fever.

“Our role is to make it simple for fans anywhere in the world to discover the festival, book their tickets and travel to Prishtina — so that more people come, and more of the value stays in Kosovo.”
