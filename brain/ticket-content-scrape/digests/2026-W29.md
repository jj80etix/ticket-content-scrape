# Weekly Digest — 2026-W29 (Jul 13–19)

## Regulatory/Legal Watch

- **DC RESALE Act** (Q9, Q11) — DC Council unanimously passed the Restricting Egregious Scalping Against Live Entertainment Act on **July 14**: caps resale at 10% above face value, bans speculative "ghost" listings and surveillance-based pricing, mandates all-in pricing, and requires anyone reselling 50+ tickets/year to license with the District. Heads to Mayor Bowser; **takes effect Jan. 1, 2027** absent a veto. NIVA and independent promoters call it a national template. → [[articles/2026-07-15-dc-has-raised-the-bar-for-ticketing-reform-nationwide|note]]
- **Massachusetts "Great Divide Act"** (Q9, Q10, Q11) — Gov. Healey's closeout supplemental budget proposes a 110%-of-face resale cap and a 10%-of-total-price fee cap, bans speculative tickets, and prohibits deceptive resale sites. Third state (after Maine, Vermont) advancing a resale cap this cycle; **no enactment date yet**. → [[articles/2026-07-17-massachusetts-gov-to-tackle-ticketing-reform-with-the-great-divide-act|note]]
- **Ontario Bill 97 enforcement gap** (Q6, Q11) — resale cap took effect **April 24, 2026** ahead of the verification rules needed to enforce it; StubHub and SeatGeek were placed on Ontario's Consumer Beware list in June (penalties up to CA$250,000 in the enforcement blitz), but no fines confirmed and above-face listings persist. Province is now drafting fix regulations. → [[articles/2026-07-14-ontario-scrambles-to-fix-ticket-resale-cap-months-after-rushing-it-into-law|note]]
- **German court vs. FIFA** (Q9, Q11) — Frankfurt Regional Court issued a **July 13** preliminary injunction (Germany-only, not final) barring FIFA's World Cup ticketing from bait-and-switch "from" pricing, hidden total cost, and coercive countdown timers, and requiring commercial-reseller identity disclosure under the EU Digital Services Act — a precedent applying DSA transparency duties to an organizer-run platform, not just third-party resale. → [[articles/2026-07-15-german-court-orders-fifa-to-halt-deceptive-world-cup-ticketing-practices|note]]
- **New Jersey junk-fees initiative** (Q9) — AG enforcement statement (drip pricing, dark patterns, unbundled/excessive fees) plus Gov. Sherrill's Executive Order 19 targeting all-in pricing; statutory penalties up to $10K/$20K per violation under the NJ Consumer Fraud Act; **agency recommendations due to the governor by September 2026**. → [[articles/2026-07-15-new-jersey-signals-focus-on-junk-fees-regulation-and-enforcement|note]]
- **DOJ v. Live Nation settlement** (Q10) — Tunney Act public comment period opened **July 6**, runs through **Sept. 4, 2026**; a coalition of state AGs has now asked the court for discovery into both the settlement's substance and the disclosed pre-settlement Rapino–Trump meeting. Ruling expected mid-Sept to mid-Oct. → [[articles/2026-07-17-states-seek-discovery-into-doj-live-nation-settlement-citing-weak-remedies-and-a|note]]
- **Enforcement-gap pattern across four jurisdictions** (Q7, Q11) — UK's draft Ticket Tout Ban Bill (unlikely law before 2027-28), Ontario's cap, California's AB 1720 (Fans First Act, in Appropriations), and the EU's Digital Fairness Act (draft expected Q3 2026) are all advancing, but World Cup dynamic pricing still rose ~35% across 95 of 104 matches — Chelsea FC's bot-blocking/ID-verification program is cited as the only credible enforcement template. → [[articles/2026-07-15-new-resale-rules-are-coming-someone-has-to-apply-them|note]]
